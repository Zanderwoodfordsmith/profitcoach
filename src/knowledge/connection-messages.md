# LinkedIn connection messages (Profit Coach playbook)

What Is This
This lesson teaches you how to craft the perfect LinkedIn connection message to engage your ideal clients effectively.
Why Is It Important/Useful
Increase Connection Rates: Personalized and compelling messages boost your acceptance rates.
Build Trust and Credibility: Properly structured messages build confidence in your expertise.
Generate Leads: Effective messaging can turn connections into leads and clients.
Establish Authority: Showcasing your results and systems asserts your authority in the industry.
Save Time: Templates and checklists streamline the process, saving you valuable time.

Most Viewed Offer: This is your most viewed message in your entire business, so it's worth getting right!

The 10 Step Connection message Checklist
Keep It Concise - Is the message under 275 characters?

Personalized Greeting - Uses a short greeting like "Hi {first_name}," to add a personal touch.

Specific Prospect Callout - Mentions the prospect’s business (& possibly location) to make the message feel less automated. "I see you run a UK Engineering company."

Compelling Hook - Use a compelling hook to generate interest, like discussing goals or pain points. "Are you looking to win bigger contracts?"

Justify the Question - Include "I ask because" to explain why you're reaching out. "I ask because...Proof."

Generate Curiosity - Mention a "simple system" to pique curiosity. "Our simple system helped a client XYZ."

Provide Specific Proof - Use specific, industry-related proof to build credibility. "We helped a client go from £75K to £150K in revenue." or what you did in your career.

Relevant Mechanism - Describe the mechanism related to the hook. "By streamlining operations, we improved their margins."

Simple Language - Ensure the message is easy to understand and uses the prospects' language. "We make complex tasks simple, increasing your profits."

Interest CTA - Use a Call-To-Action to gauge interest. "Is this of interest?"

Your message needs to be a 10/10 to for our message to be effective.

Major Red Flags In Messaging
Not personalised - does not call out specific type of business. This is a major problem because it will feel automated and conversion plummets.
Generic language - i.e. any business coach could write it (even if you have called out type of business). This is a major problem when it comes to conversion. Some examples of how to make it more industry relevant:
customers → contracts, retainers, deals, tabs
staff/team → lads (contractors), associates (law firms) etc.
Abstract results - This just leaves prospects without compelling reason to reply. E.g. I used a simple system to double profits instead of Grew profit from £400K → £1.5M t/o in under 2yrs
Selling Coaching This is a conversion killer. Never write anything that says "Would you be interested in how I can support/help you XYZ". This is asking them to make a decision about buying from you, it's way too big. Always make the CTA "Interest" in regards to the benefits. See the research below from gong.

Not Following Template

Is this of interest?

❌ Missing Personalisation line at start - This may make it feel automated which dramatically reduces results
❌ Result is abstract - 127% doesn't mean anything especially if there isn't context. Was that $10 to $27 or $10,000 to £27,000
❌ Could improve method - engagement and value delivery is not terrible but could be better. How would these people describe it in their mind at 3am e.g. "If I could just get people to...use the product more (My guess - Hard to know without being an expert in these core clients)
Selling Immediately
Hi {first_name},

Would be great to connect and see if I can support you boost profits, cut costs and reduce churn?

I've helped firms like Company Name achieve just that—streamlining sales and enhancing customer value swiftly.

Interested to learn more?

Coach Name

❌ Missing Personalisation line at start - This may make it feel automated which dramatically reduces results
❌ Way Too Salesy - This is asking them from the first line for them to decide if they want to be coached by you. It's way too much too soon. You want to highlight benefits and pains not position interest in coaching.
❌ Self Interested - Saying "I've helped..." instead of "I ask because..." like we recommend is not as powerful we don't think. First reason is that because is a very good word to get in for justifying things, people naturally become more when the word is used. Secondly we want to make it about the system and process ideally as it feels more believable than a person being promotional. It's not terrible saying "I helped" but don't think it is ideal.
❌ Missing results - you say the benefit of streamlining but there is no trust built in terms of the actual results achieved.
