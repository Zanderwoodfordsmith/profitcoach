# Brand Narrative Alignment

## What to Do

Ensure your external story reflects your internal mission. Audit every place your brand speaks - website, social media, sales conversations, recruitment ads, investor materials, client onboarding - and check whether the narrative is consistent with the purpose you have defined. Where there are gaps, close them.

This is not a rebranding exercise. It is an alignment exercise. The mission is set. Now make sure every external touchpoint tells the same story.

## Why It Matters

Donald Miller's StoryBrand framework shows that confused messaging costs companies customers. But at Level 5, the cost is bigger than lost sales. Inconsistent narrative confuses your team about what the company stands for. It attracts the wrong talent, the wrong clients, and the wrong partnerships. When what you say externally does not match what you believe internally, people notice - and they stop trusting.

Your brand narrative is how the mission travels beyond the walls of the business. It is the story customers tell about you, the reason talent wants to join, and the lens media and partners use to understand what you do. If that story is misaligned, everything downstream is harder.

## How to Use This

Work through the audit first to see where you stand. Then prioritise the biggest gaps and fix them. You do not need to change everything at once - start with the touchpoints that reach the most people.

---

## Narrative Audit

- [ ] **Read your website homepage as a stranger** - Does it clearly communicate why your company exists, not just what it sells? Can someone understand your mission in 10 seconds?
- [ ] **Review your "About" page** - Does it tell the real story or a corporate filler version? Is the mission front and centre or buried below team photos?
- [ ] **Read your last 10 social media posts** - What story do they collectively tell? Does it reinforce the mission or just promote products and offers?
- [ ] **Listen to a recorded sales call** - What does your sales team say the company is about? Does it match the mission or has it drifted into pure feature-benefit selling?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Update your LinkedIn company description to lead with your mission** - Takes 10 minutes and reaches every person who looks you up.

## Audience Alignment

- [ ] **Check your recruitment messaging** - Job ads and career pages are brand touchpoints. Do candidates understand your mission before they apply? Patagonia attracts mission-aligned talent because every job ad leads with purpose.
- [ ] **Review client onboarding materials** - New clients should understand why your company exists, not just how to use your product. Mission context builds loyalty.
- [ ] **Audit investor or stakeholder communications** - If you report to investors, partners, or a board, is the mission clearly part of how you communicate progress?
- [ ] **Ask 5 customers: "What do you think our company stands for?"** - Their answers reveal what your actual narrative is, regardless of what you intend.

## Closing Gaps

- [ ] **Prioritise the 3 touchpoints with the biggest misalignment** - Fix the ones that reach the most people first. Website and sales conversations usually top the list.
- [ ] **Create a brand narrative one-pager** - A simple document that captures: mission, the change you create, who you serve, and how you are different. Share it with everyone who communicates on behalf of the brand.
- [ ] **Brief your sales team on the mission narrative** - They are your most frequent brand ambassadors. If they cannot connect what they sell to why the company exists, the narrative breaks at the most critical point.
- [ ] **Update your content strategy to reflect the mission** - At least 30% of content should connect back to mission and purpose, not just tactical advice or promotion.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Rewrite your email signature to include a one-line mission statement** - Every email you send becomes a micro-touchpoint for the mission.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Complete audit of all external brand touchpoints against the mission
- Clear picture of where narrative is aligned and where it has drifted
- Brand narrative one-pager that anyone in the company can use
- Sales, marketing, and recruitment messaging updated to reflect mission
- Consistent story across every channel and audience

## Coach Notes

Most owners think their narrative is more consistent than it actually is. The audit often reveals that marketing says one thing, sales says another, and the website says a third. This is normal - it accumulates over years of different people creating content without a shared mission filter.

Do not let this become a perfectionist exercise. The goal is not pixel-perfect brand consistency across every touchpoint. The goal is that anyone who encounters the brand in any context gets the same fundamental message about why the company exists.

If the owner discovers the external narrative does not match the mission they defined, there are two possibilities: the narrative needs updating, or the mission definition was not honest enough. Check both before making changes.
