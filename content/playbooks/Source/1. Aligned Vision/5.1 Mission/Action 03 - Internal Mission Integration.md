# Internal Mission Integration

## What to Do

Build the mission into the operational fabric of your business: hiring, performance reviews, strategic planning, and daily operations. Purpose only matters if it shapes how the business actually runs. This action takes the mission off the wall and puts it into the systems that drive behaviour.

Work through each operational area and identify specific changes that embed the mission into how people are selected, evaluated, rewarded, and directed.

## Why It Matters

Patrick Lencioni's research shows that organisational health - alignment around shared purpose - is the single greatest advantage a company can have. But most companies treat mission as a communications exercise rather than an operational one. They announce the mission then manage the business the same way they always did. Nothing changes because nothing structural changed.

The companies that succeed with mission-driven culture - Patagonia, Southwest Airlines, Richer Sounds - did not just have a good statement. They built systems that made mission-aligned behaviour the default. Hiring filters screened for mission fit. Performance reviews measured mission contribution. Strategy sessions started with "Does this serve our purpose?"

## How to Use This

Work through each operational area in order. For each one, identify what currently exists, what needs to change, and one specific action to take in the next 30 days. Full integration takes 6-12 months, but the first changes can happen immediately.

---

## Hiring & Onboarding

- [ ] **Add mission questions to every interview** - "What about our mission resonates with you personally?" and "Tell me about a time you chose purpose over convenience." Screen for alignment, not just capability.
- [ ] **Rewrite job descriptions to lead with mission** - Before listing requirements, explain why the company exists and why this role matters to the mission. Attract people who care about the purpose, not just the salary.
- [ ] **Build mission into onboarding** - First-week experience should include the mission story, not just HR paperwork and system logins. New hires should understand the "why" before the "how."
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Add one mission-alignment question to your next interview** - Start testing for cultural and purpose fit immediately.

## Performance & Recognition

- [ ] **Include mission contribution in performance reviews** - Not as a vague category but as a specific evaluation: "How has this person's work advanced our mission in the last quarter?" Require examples.
- [ ] **Create a mission recognition programme** - Celebrate people who demonstrate mission-aligned behaviour. Public acknowledgment, awards, or simple shout-outs in team meetings. What gets recognised gets repeated.
- [ ] **Tie a portion of incentives to mission-aligned outcomes** - Not all compensation, but enough that it signals mission matters. If bonuses only reward revenue, revenue is all people will chase.
- [ ] **Review whether any current metrics or incentives conflict with the mission** - Sometimes existing KPIs accidentally reward behaviour that undermines purpose. Find and fix those conflicts.

## Strategic Planning

- [ ] **Start every strategy session with the mission** - Before discussing numbers, markets, or competition, ask: "Are we still true to why we exist? What has changed?" This grounds every strategic conversation in purpose.
- [ ] **Create a mission filter for new opportunities** - A simple yes/no checklist: Does this opportunity align with our mission? Does it strengthen or dilute our focus? Would we pursue it even if it were less profitable? Use it before committing to any major initiative.
- [ ] **Review your current product/service portfolio against the mission** - Is everything you offer connected to the purpose? If something exists purely for revenue with no mission connection, consider whether it belongs.
- [ ] **Include mission impact in your annual planning** - Alongside financial targets, set mission targets. What specific progress toward the mission will you make this year? How will you measure it?

## Daily Operations

- [ ] **Make the mission visible** - Not just a poster. Integrate it into meeting agendas, email templates, internal dashboards, and decision documents. Constant, natural exposure.
- [ ] **Create a "mission check" habit in leadership meetings** - Before closing any significant decision, ask: "Is this consistent with our mission?" Make it a standard agenda item, not an afterthought.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Open your next team meeting by sharing a specific example of the mission in action** - A client outcome, a team decision, a product choice that reflected purpose. Make it real, not abstract.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Mission embedded in hiring, performance, strategy, and daily operations
- Team members can explain the mission and connect their work to it
- Strategic decisions filtered through mission alignment
- Culture that attracts and retains mission-driven people
- Business that operates with purpose whether the owner is present or not

## Coach Notes

Integration is a 6-12 month project, not a single session. Help the client prioritise: hiring and strategic planning usually have the highest impact because they shape who enters the company and what direction it takes.

Watch for surface-level changes that look like integration but are not. Adding a mission slide to the all-hands meeting is not integration. Changing what you hire for, how you evaluate performance, and which opportunities you pursue - that is integration.

The hardest moment comes when mission and profit conflict. A client gets offered a lucrative contract that does not align with purpose. How they handle that moment reveals whether the mission is real or decorative. Coach them through it without judgement, but help them see the long-term cost of compromise.
