## **What This Is**

Mission is what remains when the momentum is gone. It is not your vision, your strategy, or your revenue goals. It is the enduring reason your company exists, the impact that outlasts you, the legacy you are building, the change you are committed to leading in the world.

At Level 5, you have built a business that runs without you. Now the question becomes: what is it actually for? Mission defines your company's contribution beyond profit. It attracts talent who believe in something bigger and guides decisions when the path is unclear.

This playbook is not about writing mission statements for the wall. It is about defining the lasting change your business creates and protecting that intention through everything you build.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Your business succeeds financially but feels hollow. You struggle to explain why your company matters beyond making money. Great people join but leave for businesses with clearer purpose. Strategic decisions feel arbitrary because there is no deeper principle guiding them. You have built something profitable but not something meaningful.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have a sense of purpose but it is not clearly articulated or consistently applied. Sometimes decisions align with your deeper intentions, sometimes they do not. Your team understands the business goals but not the broader mission. You talk about impact but it feels abstract rather than concrete and measurable.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Your mission is clear, specific, and drives real decisions every day. Great talent seeks you out because they want to be part of the change you are creating. Strategic choices become easier because you have a filter for what serves the mission and what does not. Your legacy is being built deliberately, not by accident. People know what your company stands for beyond what it sells.

## **Things to Think About**

What problem will still exist if your company disappeared tomorrow? This is where your mission lives, in the gap between the world as it is and the world as it should be. Your mission is the bridge you are building across that gap, not just for customers but for society.

What change are you actually committed to leading? Beyond growing revenue or expanding markets, what shift in your industry or community are you willing to spend decades driving? Mission requires commitment that outlasts quarterly results and yearly targets.

Does your team understand the bigger picture? When your people explain what the company does to their family or friends, do they talk about features and services or do they talk about impact and change? Mission only matters if it lives in the culture, not just on the website.

Where do profit and purpose intersect? The strongest missions create sustainable competitive advantage. They are not charity, they are the reason customers choose you and stay with you. How does your impact drive your commercial success?

What decisions would change if you filtered them through mission? Product development, hiring, partnerships, market expansion, every strategic choice should serve both profit and purpose. If your mission does not influence real decisions, it is just decoration.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

**Play 1: Mission Definition**

Define the lasting change your company exists to create. This is not about perfect language — it is about clarity of intention that guides decisions and inspires action.

**Play 2: Brand Narrative Alignment**

Ensure your external story reflects your internal mission. What you say to customers, investors, and talent should be consistent with what you believe about your purpose.

**Play 3: Internal Mission Integration**

Build mission into hiring, performance reviews, strategic planning, and daily operations. Purpose only matters if it shapes how the business actually runs.

**Play 4: Mission Protection Framework**

Create clear criteria for decisions that protect your mission from compromise. When growth opportunities conflict with purpose, you need a framework for choosing.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Write one paragraph describing the change your business creates in the world beyond financial results. Make it specific enough that someone could disagree with it.

This week: Ask three of your best people why they joined the company and why they stay. Listen for purpose beyond paycheque.

This week: Review your last three major strategic decisions. Ask whether they served both profit and purpose, or just profit.
