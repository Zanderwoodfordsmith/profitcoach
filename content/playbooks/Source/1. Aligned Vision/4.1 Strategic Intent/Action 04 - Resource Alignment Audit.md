# Resource Alignment Audit

## What to Do

Review how your current allocation of time, money, and talent matches your strategic priorities. Most businesses say one thing is important but spend their resources on something else entirely. This audit closes that gap by mapping where resources actually go, comparing it to where they should go, and creating a plan to redirect.

## Why It Matters

Verne Harnish's work on scaling shows that misaligned resources are one of the primary reasons businesses stall between £1M and £5M. You have enough resources to grow, but they are spread across too many priorities, some of which no longer serve your strategy. Every pound and every hour spent on a low-priority activity is unavailable for a high-priority one. The cost is not just the money. It is the opportunity cost of what you could have done with those resources if they were properly focused.

## How to Use This

Map your current resource allocation across three dimensions: time, money, and talent. Then compare each to your stated strategic priorities. The gaps will be obvious. Closing them is the hard part.

---

## Time Audit

- [ ] **Track your own time for one week** - Not what you planned to do, but what you actually did. Log in 30-minute blocks. Be honest about time spent on email, meetings, reactive tasks, and strategic work.
- [ ] **Calculate the percentage split** - What percentage of your time goes to: (1) Strategic/growth work, (2) Operational/delivery work, (3) Administrative/reactive work, (4) Low-value or habitual tasks?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Identify your biggest time leak this week** - The single activity that consumed the most time relative to its strategic importance. What would happen if you stopped or delegated it?
- [ ] **Map your team's time allocation** - Ask each direct report to estimate their split across the same four categories. Where is strategic capacity being consumed by operational demands?

## Financial Audit

- [ ] **List your top 10 expense categories** - Include staff costs, marketing spend, technology, professional services, premises, and any other significant line items.
- [ ] **Tag each expense as strategic, operational, or legacy** - Strategic expenses directly serve your growth priorities. Operational expenses keep the business running. Legacy expenses serve a priority that is no longer current.
- [ ] **Calculate the ratio** - What percentage of your total spend is genuinely strategic versus maintaining the status quo? Most businesses discover 70-80% of spend is operational or legacy, leaving very little fuel for growth.
- [ ] **Identify legacy expenses you can redirect** - These are the easiest wins. You are already spending the money. You just need to point it somewhere better.

## Talent Audit

- [ ] **Map your team's skills against your strategic priorities** - Do you have the right people in the right roles to execute your strategy? Where are the gaps?
- [ ] **Identify your most strategically valuable people** - Who on your team has the greatest impact on your strategic priorities? Are they spending their time on strategic work, or are they buried in operations?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Free up 5 hours per week for your most strategic person** - What could you take off their plate so they spend more time on what matters most? Even a small shift compounds quickly.
- [ ] **Assess whether hiring plans match strategic needs** - Is your next hire the person your strategy needs, or the person who fills the most obvious operational gap? These are often different people.

## Realignment Plan

- [ ] **Rank your strategic priorities** - Force a strict ranking, not a tier. Number one gets the most resources. Number five gets what is left. If everything is equally important, nothing is.
- [ ] **Define specific resource shifts** - For each priority, state what time, money, or talent will be redirected to it and where those resources are coming from. Every increase must have a corresponding decrease somewhere.
- [ ] **Set a 90-day reallocation target** - You cannot restructure overnight. Pick the 2-3 most impactful shifts and commit to completing them within 90 days.
- [ ] **Schedule a quarterly resource review** - Alignment drifts naturally as the business changes. Build a quarterly check to compare allocation against priorities and adjust.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear, honest picture of where your resources actually go today
- Visible gaps between what you say matters and what you fund with time, money, and talent
- Specific reallocation decisions that shift resources toward strategic priorities
- Identification of legacy spending that can be redirected immediately
- A quarterly rhythm for maintaining alignment as priorities evolve

## Coach Notes

This action is often a wake-up call. Most owners believe their resources are well-allocated until they do the actual audit. The time tracking exercise is particularly revealing - expect clients to be surprised and slightly embarrassed by how little time goes to strategic work. Do not let them skip it or estimate. Actual tracking for one week is essential. The financial audit can be sensitive, especially if the business is tight. Focus on reallocation rather than reduction. You are not asking them to spend less, you are asking them to spend better. The biggest resistance usually comes from cutting legacy activities. There is always a reason something started, and emotional attachment to past decisions makes it hard to stop.
