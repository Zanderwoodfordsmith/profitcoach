# Strategic Trade-Off Framework

## What to Do

Build a clear, reusable decision filter for what your business will and will not pursue. Strategy is fundamentally about trade-offs. Every yes has a cost. This framework turns your strategic intent into a practical tool you can use every time an opportunity, partnership, or new idea lands on your desk. Instead of evaluating each opportunity from scratch, you run it through your filter and get a clear answer.

## Why It Matters

The biggest threat to a growing business is not lack of opportunity. It is too much opportunity. Every new idea that "could work" pulls resources, attention, and energy away from what is already working. Michael Porter argues that the essence of strategy is choosing what not to do. Greg McKeown calls it "the disciplined pursuit of less." Without a trade-off framework, you end up with a bloated product line, an overstretched team, and a business that does many things adequately but nothing exceptionally.

## How to Use This

Define your strategic filters once, then apply them to every significant opportunity or initiative. The framework should make most decisions obvious and fast, leaving only genuinely difficult calls for deeper analysis.

---

## Define Your Filters

- [ ] **Write your strategic intent in one sentence** - What are you building, for whom, and why? This is the anchor that every trade-off is tested against.
- [ ] **List your 3-5 "always yes" criteria** - What characteristics must an opportunity have for you to consider it? Examples: serves existing customer segment, builds on current capability, generates recurring revenue, strengthens competitive advantage.
- [ ] **List your 3-5 "always no" criteria** - What characteristics automatically disqualify an opportunity? Examples: requires a new team capability you do not have, competes with your core offering, depends on a single customer, has margins below X%.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Apply your filters to the last opportunity you said yes to** - Does it pass? If not, you may already be drifting from your strategy.

## Build the Decision Tool

- [ ] **Create a simple scoring card** - List your "always yes" criteria down the left. For each opportunity, score it 1-5 against each criterion. Set a minimum total score for a "yes."
- [ ] **Add a resource impact check** - Even if an opportunity scores well, ask: "What will we stop doing or deprioritise to pursue this?" If the answer is "nothing - we will just add it on top," that is a warning sign.
- [ ] **Define your "not now" process** - Good opportunities that fail the filter today might be right later. Create a parking list and review it quarterly so nothing valuable gets permanently lost.
- [ ] **Build in a "regret test"** - For borderline decisions, ask: "If we say no to this and a competitor does it successfully, will we regret it?" If the answer is no, decline confidently.

## Apply to Current Commitments

- [ ] **Audit your current initiatives** - List everything you are actively investing time and resources in. Run each one through your filter. How many would you say yes to if they were proposed today?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Identify one current activity that fails your filter** - Start the conversation about stopping, reducing, or handing it off. Sunk cost is not a reason to continue.
- [ ] **Review your pipeline of ideas and opportunities** - Apply the filter to everything you have been considering. Clear out the ones that do not pass so they stop consuming mental energy.
- [ ] **Check for commitment creep** - Are there projects that started small but gradually expanded beyond their original scope? These are particularly dangerous because they grow without a conscious decision.

## Team Communication

- [ ] **Share the framework with your leadership team** - They need to understand not just what the filters are, but why each one matters. Context prevents workarounds.
- [ ] **Empower your team to use it** - If someone brings you an opportunity that clearly fails the filter, they should be able to see that themselves before it reaches you.
- [ ] **Celebrate good "no" decisions** - Saying no is harder than saying yes. When someone on your team identifies and declines an off-strategy opportunity, recognise it publicly.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A one-page decision filter you can apply to any opportunity in under 5 minutes
- Faster, more consistent strategic decisions across the business
- Fewer off-strategy commitments draining resources
- Your team learns to self-filter, reducing the number of decisions that reach you
- Greater focus on the initiatives that genuinely build your competitive position

## Coach Notes

Clients often struggle with the "always no" list because it feels like closing doors. Reframe it: every door you close focuses more energy on the doors you have chosen to walk through. The most powerful coaching moment in this action is the current commitments audit. Most owners discover they are already invested in 2-3 things that would fail their own filter. Helping them gracefully exit or wind down those commitments frees up significant capacity. Watch for clients who create filters so broad that everything passes. If the filter does not force hard choices, it is not doing its job. Push for specificity until the filter actually says no to things.
