# Competitive Advantage Assessment

## What to Do

Identify and test the sustainable advantages you can build and defend over time. This is not about listing what you are good at today. It is about finding the advantages that will compound over years and become increasingly difficult for competitors to replicate. You need to know what makes you genuinely different, whether that difference matters to customers, and whether you can protect it.

## Why It Matters

Michael Porter's research is clear: the only way to sustain above-average profitability is through durable competitive advantage. Without it, you are competing on price, and someone will always be willing to go lower. Most business owners confuse temporary advantages (a good location, a loyal customer base, being first to market) with sustainable ones. Temporary advantages erode. Sustainable advantages compound. The difference between the two determines whether your business grows or gets slowly commoditised.

## How to Use This

Work through the assessment honestly. Score each potential advantage. Then focus your investment on the 1-2 advantages that score highest and can genuinely be defended.

---

## Advantage Identification

- [ ] **List everything you believe makes you different** - Products, services, people, processes, relationships, reputation, methodology, technology, culture. Get it all out without filtering.
- [ ] **For each item, ask: "Could a competitor replicate this within 12 months?"** - If the answer is yes, it is not a sustainable advantage. It might be valuable today, but it will not protect you long-term. Mark these as temporary.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Circle the 2-3 items that would be hardest for anyone to copy** - These are your candidates for sustainable advantage. They usually involve some combination of time, relationships, proprietary knowledge, or compounding effects.
- [ ] **Categorise your advantages** - Sort them into: (1) Cost advantages - you can deliver at lower cost without sacrificing quality. (2) Differentiation advantages - you deliver something customers value that others cannot match. (3) Network advantages - your value increases as your customer base or ecosystem grows.

## Customer Validation

- [ ] **Ask your best customers why they stay** - Not why they bought initially, but why they have not left. The answer to "why do you stay?" reveals your real advantage, which is often different from what you think it is.
- [ ] **Ask lost prospects why they chose someone else** - This is uncomfortable but essential. The reasons you lose deals reveal where your advantages are not strong enough.
- [ ] **Test willingness to pay** - Would your customers pay more for what makes you different? If your advantage does not translate into pricing power, it is not as valuable as you think.
- [ ] **Compare your self-assessment to customer perception** - Where do they match? Where do they diverge? Customer perception is reality, regardless of what you believe internally.

## Sustainability Testing

- [ ] **Rate each advantage on three dimensions** - (1) Value: How much do customers care? (2) Rarity: How few competitors can match it? (3) Durability: How long will it take to erode? Score each 1-10. Multiply the three scores for a composite ranking.
- [ ] **Identify what feeds your advantage** - Sustainable advantages have reinforcing loops. A strong reputation attracts better talent, which produces better work, which strengthens the reputation further. Map the loop for your top advantages.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Identify one thing you could do this month to strengthen your top advantage** - Small, consistent investment in your advantage compounds faster than occasional big moves.
- [ ] **Assess vulnerability** - What could erode or destroy each advantage? A technology shift, a regulatory change, a competitor with deep pockets, key person dependency? Name the threats so you can monitor them.

## Investment Plan

- [ ] **Decide where to concentrate resources** - Pick 1-2 advantages to invest in heavily. Spreading investment across five advantages means none of them become dominant.
- [ ] **Define how you will measure advantage strength over time** - Customer retention, pricing power, win rate against specific competitors, time to replicate. Pick metrics that tell you whether your advantage is growing or shrinking.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear, honest view of what actually makes you different
- Separation of temporary advantages from sustainable ones
- Customer-validated understanding of why people choose and stay with you
- A focused investment plan for strengthening your 1-2 most defensible advantages
- Early warning indicators for when advantages are eroding

## Coach Notes

Most clients overestimate their competitive advantage. They believe their service quality or customer care is genuinely different when, in reality, their competitors say the exact same thing. Push for specificity and evidence. The question "Would your customers say the same thing?" is a powerful reality check. The most common sustainable advantages for SMEs are: deep expertise in a narrow domain, proprietary methodology or IP, long-standing customer relationships with high switching costs, and employer brand that attracts talent competitors cannot get. Help the client identify which of these they can realistically build and defend.
