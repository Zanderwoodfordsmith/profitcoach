## **What This Is**

Purpose is your filter. Without it, every opportunity looks the same, every decision feels hard, and every day feels heavy. You say yes to things that drain you and no to things that matter because you have no clear line to judge them against.

This playbook is not about finding your life's calling or writing a mission statement that sounds good on a website. It is about defining what you are actually willing to commit to, the problem you want to solve, the change you want to create, and the people you want to serve. Once that is clear, choices become simple.

Purpose at Level 1 is practical, not philosophical. It answers: what am I building this business to achieve, and is what I am doing today moving me towards that or away from it?

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Everything feels equally important and equally pointless. You take on clients or projects you do not enjoy because they pay the bills, but you resent the work. You change direction constantly based on the latest opportunity or advice. You cannot explain why someone should choose your business over a competitor beyond price or convenience. Every day feels like pushing a boulder uphill.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have a rough sense of what you want to achieve but it changes based on your mood or the latest challenge. Sometimes you feel aligned with your work, other times it feels like a grind. You can explain what you do but struggle to explain why it matters beyond making money. You make decisions based on gut feel rather than clear criteria.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You can clearly explain the change you exist to create and why it matters to you personally. You know exactly who you serve and who you do not serve, and you are comfortable saying no to good money from the wrong people. Your daily work feels connected to something bigger. When opportunities arise, you can quickly decide if they align with your purpose or distract from it.

## **Things to Think About**

What transformation are you truly committed to delivering? Not what you think sounds impressive, but what you actually want to see happen in the world. If you strip away the revenue, the recognition, and the external validation, what change would you still want to create?

Who are you unwilling to build this business around? Purpose becomes real when you define who you will not serve. Which clients, customers, or market segments would you turn down even if they offered good money? This boundary tells you more about your purpose than any mission statement.

If income were guaranteed, would this still matter to you? Imagine you had enough money to never work again. Would you still care about the problem your business solves? If the answer is no, you might be solving the wrong problem, or you might need to reframe why it matters to you.

Is this a problem you want to solve for the next decade? Purpose is not a sprint, it is the thing you are willing to commit to long term. Can you see yourself still caring about this problem in ten years, or are you just solving it because you happen to be good at it right now?

Where is your daily behaviour misaligned with your stated purpose? Look at how you actually spend your time, which clients you take on, and which projects get your best energy. If there is a gap between what you say matters and how you actually behave, either your purpose needs refining or your choices need changing.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

**Play 1: The Purpose Statement**

Define the specific change you exist to create in clear, practical language. This is not a fluffy mission statement, it is the filter you will use to make every business decision going forward.

**Play 2: Serve & Swerve Analysis**

Identify exactly who you want to serve and who you want to avoid. Create clear boundaries around your ideal clients and the ones you will turn down, even when money is tight.

**Play 3: The Purpose Test**

Build a simple framework for testing whether opportunities, projects, and decisions align with your purpose. Every choice gets measured against the same bar instead of being decided by mood or desperation.

**Play 4: Alignment Audit**

Review your current business activities, client base, and daily work to identify where you are aligned with your purpose and where you are drifting. Then create a plan to move towards alignment without destroying cash flow.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Write down the last three business decisions you made and ask: "Did I make this choice because it aligned with what I want to achieve, or because I felt I had to?"

This week: List your current clients or customers. Circle the ones you genuinely enjoy working with. Ask yourself what they have in common, that is a clue to your purpose.

This week: Complete this sentence: "If I could fix one problem in my industry forever, it would be..." Your first answer is usually the most honest.
