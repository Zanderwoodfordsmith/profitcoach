# The Alignment Audit

## What to Do

Review your current business activities, client base, and daily work to identify where you are living your purpose and where you are drifting from it. Then create a plan to move towards alignment without destroying cash flow in the process.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners have a gap between what they say matters and how they actually spend their time. That gap is where energy leaks, motivation dies, and burnout builds. This audit makes the gap visible — and once you can see it, you can close it. The goal is not overnight transformation. It is an honest look at where you are, so you can make deliberate choices about where you are heading.

## How to Use This

Work through each section with your Purpose Statement and Serve & Swerve criteria beside you. Score honestly — nobody sees this but you (and your coach, if you have one).

---

## Time Alignment

- [ ] **Look at your calendar from last week** — Categorise every block of time: purpose-aligned work, necessary admin, reactive firefighting, or misaligned activity. What percentage of your time went to purpose-aligned work?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Identify your biggest time thief** — The single activity that consumed the most time while contributing the least to your purpose. Can you eliminate, delegate, or reduce it this week?
- [ ] **Check your ratio** — If less than 30% of your time is purpose-aligned, something structural needs to change. If it is 30-60%, you are on track to improve. Above 60%, you are in good shape.

## Revenue Alignment

- [ ] **List your revenue sources** — Break down where your money comes from by client type, service type, or product type.
- [ ] **Mark each as Aligned, Neutral, or Misaligned** — Aligned means it directly serves your purpose and ideal clients. Misaligned means it pays bills but pulls you away from where you want to go. Neutral is somewhere in between.
- [ ] **Calculate the percentage** — What share of your revenue comes from aligned work? If misaligned revenue is over 40%, you have a strategic problem, not just a time management problem.

## Energy Alignment

- [ ] **List the 5 activities that give you energy at work** — The things that make you lose track of time, feel proud, or look forward to Monday. These are purpose clues.
- [ ] **List the 5 activities that drain your energy** — The things you procrastinate on, dread, or feel resentful about. These are misalignment signals.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Can you delegate or eliminate one energy-draining activity this month?** — Not all of them. Just one. Start building momentum towards a more aligned workload.

## The Alignment Plan

- [ ] **Pick your biggest misalignment** — The one area (time, revenue, or energy) where the gap between purpose and reality is largest.
- [ ] **Set a 90-day target** — What would "better" look like in 90 days? Not perfect — just measurably better. Example: "Move from 20% aligned revenue to 40% by taking on 2 ideal clients and winding down 1 misaligned one."
- [ ] **Identify the one change that starts the shift** — Not a complete business overhaul. One deliberate change you can make this month that moves the needle.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear, honest picture of how aligned your business currently is with your purpose
- Specific areas where you are drifting and the cost of that drift
- A 90-day plan to increase alignment without blowing up your revenue
- Renewed clarity about what to pursue, what to tolerate temporarily, and what to actively eliminate

## Coach Notes

This action often reveals uncomfortable truths — clients discover that 60%+ of their revenue comes from work they do not enjoy or clients they do not want. The coaching moment is helping them create a transition plan rather than either ignoring the problem or making drastic changes that kill cash flow.

Revisit this audit every quarter. Alignment is not a one-time fix — it is an ongoing practice of choosing to close the gap between what you say matters and how you actually operate.

The sequence matters: Purpose Statement → Serve & Swerve → Alignment Audit. Do not run this audit without the first two, or they will not know what they are auditing against.
