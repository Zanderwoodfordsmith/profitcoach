# Vision Alignment Audit

## What to Do

Review every current strategy, project, and priority against your 3-year vision. Score each one for alignment. Kill anything that does not serve the bigger picture. Start anything that is missing. This is a ruthless pruning exercise that clears the path between where you are now and where you said you want to be.

Do this after completing The 3-Year Picture and Scale & Ambition Clarity. Without a clear vision, there is nothing to audit against.

## Why It Matters

Most businesses accumulate projects, priorities, and commitments the way a house accumulates clutter. Each one seemed like a good idea at the time. But collectively, they pull the business in ten directions instead of one. Strategy is as much about what you stop doing as what you start doing.

Michael Porter said it clearly: "The essence of strategy is choosing what not to do." This audit forces that choice. Every project that does not serve your vision is consuming resources, time, energy, and attention that could go toward something that does.

## How to Use This

Gather your list of current projects, commitments, and priorities before starting. If you do not have a written list, that is your first task. Then work through the scoring and decision framework below.

---

## List Everything on Your Plate

- [ ] **Write down every active project in the business** - Product development, marketing campaigns, system implementations, hiring plans, partnerships. Everything that is consuming time and resources right now.
- [ ] **List every recurring commitment** - Regular meetings, reports, memberships, subscriptions, retainers, partnerships. Anything that takes time or money on a repeating basis.
- [ ] **Include "someday" projects** - Things you have been planning or talking about but have not started. They are taking up mental space even if they are not taking up calendar space.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Count the total number of items on your list** - If it is more than 15, you are almost certainly spread too thin. The number itself is diagnostic.

## Score Each Item for Vision Alignment

- [ ] **Rate each item 1-5 for alignment with your 3-year picture** - 5 = directly serves the vision. 3 = somewhat related. 1 = no connection at all.
- [ ] **Rate each item 1-5 for current ROI (time and money invested vs. results)** - 5 = excellent return. 1 = consuming resources with little to show for it.
- [ ] **Multiply alignment x ROI for a composite score out of 25** - This gives you a quick ranking. Items scoring below 10 are prime candidates for cutting.
- [ ] **Flag anything scoring 5 or below** - These are actively working against your vision. They need to stop immediately unless there is a contractual or legal reason they cannot.

## Make the Hard Decisions

- [ ] **Kill list: Write down everything you will stop doing** - Projects to cancel, meetings to end, commitments to wind down. Be specific about when each one stops.
- [ ] **Keep list: Confirm what stays and why** - For every item you keep, write one sentence explaining how it serves the vision. If you cannot, it belongs on the kill list.
- [ ] **Start list: What is missing?** - Look at your 3-year picture. What do you need to start doing that you are not currently doing? This is where the freed-up resources go.
- [ ] **Communicate changes to your team** - If you are killing projects, the people working on them need to know. Explain the reasoning. Connect it to the vision.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Cancel or decline one low-scoring commitment today** - A meeting, a project, a subscription. Start the pruning immediately.

## Build an Alignment Check Habit

- [ ] **Before saying yes to anything new, run the alignment test** - "Does this serve my 3-year picture? What is the expected ROI? What will I stop doing to make room for it?"
- [ ] **Schedule this full audit quarterly** - Projects drift. New commitments creep in. A quarterly audit keeps the business focused on what matters.
- [ ] **Share your framework with key team members** - When everyone understands the vision alignment filter, they start filtering their own ideas and requests. This is how strategy cascades through an organisation.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A scored list of every current project and commitment ranked by vision alignment
- A clear kill list of projects and commitments to end
- A start list of missing initiatives to begin
- A simple alignment test to apply to every future decision
- A quarterly audit habit that prevents strategic drift

## Coach Notes

This is often an emotional exercise. Clients have invested time, money, and identity into projects that score poorly. Killing a project can feel like admitting failure. Reframe it: stopping something that does not serve your vision is not failure. Continuing it is. The sunk cost fallacy is powerful here.

The most common resistance is "but we have already invested so much in X." Remind them that past investment is irrelevant. The only question is: does continuing this serve where you are going? If not, every additional hour and pound spent on it is waste. Watch for clients who score everything highly to avoid making hard choices. If every project scores 20+, they are not being honest. Push back and force genuine prioritisation.
