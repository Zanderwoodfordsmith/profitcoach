# Non-Negotiables Framework

## What to Do

Identify the absolute standards, values, and lifestyle requirements that you will never compromise for the sake of growth. These are your hard lines. The things that, if you cross them, make the business not worth running even if it is profitable.

Write them down, share them with your team, and use them as a filter every time a decision tempts you to bend.

## Why It Matters

Growth creates pressure to compromise. A big client wants to push your boundaries. A new revenue stream requires working weekends. A cost-saving measure lowers quality. Without clearly defined non-negotiables, these compromises happen gradually. No single decision feels like a betrayal. But after two years of small compromises, you wake up running a business you do not recognise.

Jim Collins found that enduringly great companies had a set of core values they refused to sacrifice regardless of external pressure. The same principle applies at any scale. Your non-negotiables are the walls of the container. Everything else can flex. These cannot.

## How to Use This

Work through each category below. Not every category will have a non-negotiable for you. That is fine. The goal is to identify the handful (usually 5-8) that genuinely matter, not to fill in every box.

---

## Quality Standards

- [ ] **Define your quality floor** - What is the minimum standard of work you will deliver, regardless of time pressure or budget? Describe it specifically enough that anyone on your team could recognise when work falls below it.
- [ ] **Identify quality compromises you have already made** - Be honest. Where have you let standards slip? What triggered it? This reveals where you are most vulnerable.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Name one quality standard you want to restore right now** - Something that used to be better. Commit to bringing it back this week.

## Ethical Lines

- [ ] **Write down practices you refuse to engage in** - Misleading marketing, aggressive upselling, copying competitors, underpaying suppliers, making promises you cannot keep. Be specific.
- [ ] **Define how you treat people who cannot fight back** - Suppliers, junior staff, unsuccessful applicants, departing employees. Your ethics are tested when there are no consequences for bad behaviour.
- [ ] **Identify any current grey areas** - Anything you are doing that you feel slightly uncomfortable about. Bring it into the light and decide: is this acceptable or does it need to change?

## Lifestyle Requirements

- [ ] **Set your working hours boundary** - Maximum hours per week. Days you do not work. Times you are unavailable. Write them as rules, not guidelines.
- [ ] **Define your family and personal commitments** - School pick-ups, date nights, holidays, exercise, hobbies. What is protected time that business cannot touch?
- [ ] **Set your minimum personal income** - Below this number, the business is not serving you. You are serving it. What is the floor?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Block one personal commitment in your calendar this week and treat it as unmovable** - Show yourself (and your team) that non-negotiables are real.

## Relationship Standards

- [ ] **Define the client you will not work with** - What behaviour, attitude, or situation makes someone a bad fit regardless of how much they pay? Describe the red flags clearly.
- [ ] **Set your team culture standards** - What behaviour is unacceptable from team members? Dishonesty, disrespect, blaming, gossip? Name it and enforce it.
- [ ] **Write your "walk away" criteria** - Under what circumstances would you fire a profitable client, decline a lucrative deal, or let a high-performer go? Pre-deciding makes it easier when the moment arrives.

## Compile and Share

- [ ] **Write your non-negotiables on one page** - Maximum 8 items. Each stated clearly in one sentence. This becomes your reference document.
- [ ] **Share them with your team** - Non-negotiables only work if everyone knows them. Discuss them in a team meeting. Invite questions.
- [ ] **Review them quarterly** - Not to weaken them. To check whether you are living them. Are there any you have already compromised? Fix it.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear, written list of 5-8 non-negotiable standards and values
- Specific enough to be actionable, not vague platitudes
- Shared with your team so they can uphold them too
- A decision-making filter that prevents slow-creep compromise
- Quarterly review habit to maintain accountability

## Coach Notes

The power of this action is in specificity. "We deliver great service" is a platitude, not a non-negotiable. "Every client gets a response within 4 hours during working days" is a non-negotiable. Push clients past vague statements into concrete, testable standards.

The lifestyle non-negotiables are often the hardest for driven owners to commit to. They feel guilty about setting boundaries. Remind them that a burned-out owner is useless to their business, their team, and their family. Boundaries are not weakness. They are what make sustained high performance possible. If a client has already compromised a non-negotiable and feels bad about it, that is a coaching opportunity. Help them restore it and build the discipline to protect it going forward.
