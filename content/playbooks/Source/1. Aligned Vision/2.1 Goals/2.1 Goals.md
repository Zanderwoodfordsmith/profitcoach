## **What This Is**

Goals turn intention into action. Without them, you are working hard but not necessarily making progress. You are busy but not building. You may have a vague sense of where you want to go, but no measurable way to know if you are getting there.

This playbook is about translating your ambition into specific, measurable targets that actually drive decision-making. It covers both business and personal goals, because if the business is not funding the life you want, something needs to change.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You have ambitions and ideas but no clear targets or deadlines. You know roughly what you want but could not write down what success looks like in twelve months. You set goals at New Year then forget about them. You are working harder than ever but struggle to see measurable progress towards anything meaningful.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have some goals written down but they are either too vague or you rarely review them. You know what you want to achieve this year but have not broken it into quarterly milestones. Personal and business goals feel disconnected, you are building a business that does not support the life you actually want.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You have clear, measurable twelve-month outcomes for both business and personal life. These are broken into quarterly targets with specific milestones. You review progress monthly and adjust course when needed. Your business goals align with your personal goals, the business is funding the life you are designing, not consuming it.

---

## **Things to Think About**

What must be true by this time next year? Not what you hope might happen, what absolutely must be different for you to feel the year was successful. This is your North Star, the outcome that everything else serves.

Are you tracking activity or results? There is a difference between "I will post on social media five times a week" and "I will generate fifty qualified leads per month." One measures effort, the other measures outcome. Guess which drives better decisions.

Is your business funding the life you actually want? Many business owners build successful businesses that trap them in lifestyles they never chose. Your business goals should serve your personal goals, not replace them.

How often do you formally assess whether you are winning? Monthly goal reviews are not overhead, they are the difference between drifting and driving. If you are not reviewing, you are not really committed.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

**Play 1: Twelve-Month Outcomes**

Define clear, specific outcomes for both business and personal life over the next twelve months. What will be measurably different by this time next year?

**Play 2: Quarterly Breakdown**

Break your twelve-month outcomes into quarterly milestones so you know if you are on track every ninety days, not just at year end.

**Play 3: Leading Indicators**

Identify the metrics that predict success in your goals so you can course-correct early rather than wait for results.

**Play 4: Monthly Review Rhythm**

Install a simple monthly review process to track progress, celebrate wins, and adjust targets based on what you are learning.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Write down your single most important twelve-month outcome for the business. Make it specific enough that a stranger could measure whether you achieved it.

This week: List three personal outcomes you want this year. Check whether your current business goals support or conflict with them.

This week: Pick one goal you set months ago but never review. Block thirty minutes to assess where you stand and what needs to change.
