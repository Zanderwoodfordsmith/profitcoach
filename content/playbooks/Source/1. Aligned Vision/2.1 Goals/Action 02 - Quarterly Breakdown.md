# Quarterly Breakdown

## What to Do

Take your twelve-month outcomes and break them into four quarterly milestones. Each quarter should have clear targets that, if hit, confirm you are on track for the annual goal. This turns "what I want in a year" into "what I need to achieve in the next 90 days."

## Why It Matters

Twelve months is too long to wait before checking progress. By the time you realise you are off track, you have lost months you cannot get back. Quarterly milestones create natural checkpoints where you can celebrate wins, catch problems, and adjust your approach. They also make big goals feel manageable - you are not trying to grow revenue by £150K this year, you are trying to add £35-40K this quarter.

## How to Use This

Start with your Twelve-Month Outcomes (Action 1). For each one, work backwards: if the goal is X by month 12, what needs to be true at month 9? Month 6? Month 3? Then fill in the detail for this quarter.

---

## Quarterly Milestone Mapping

### For Each Twelve-Month Outcome:

- [ ] Write the annual outcome at the top of a page
- [ ] Define the Q4 milestone (month 10-12): what does "nearly there" look like?
- [ ] Define the Q3 milestone (month 7-9): what does "well on track" look like?
- [ ] Define the Q2 milestone (month 4-6): what does "building momentum" look like?
- [ ] Define the Q1 milestone (month 1-3): what does "started and moving" look like?
- [ ] Check the progression: does each quarter build logically on the previous one?

### This Quarter Deep Dive

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: For your #1 outcome, write down the single most important thing that must happen THIS QUARTER to stay on track
- [ ] List 3-5 specific targets for this quarter with numbers attached (revenue, leads, hires, products launched, etc.)
- [ ] For each target, identify the key activities that will drive it. What do you need to DO to make the number happen?
- [ ] Identify dependencies: what needs to happen first before other targets become possible?
- [ ] Identify risks: what could derail this quarter's targets? What is your plan B?

### Connect to Weekly Action

- [ ] For each quarterly target, identify what needs to happen this month
- [ ] For this month's priorities, identify what needs to happen this week
- [ ] Set a weekly review question: "Am I on track for this quarter's milestones based on what I achieved this week?"

---

## Expected Outcome

- Each twelve-month outcome broken into 4 quarterly milestones
- This quarter's milestones detailed with specific targets and activities
- A clear line from annual goals to quarterly milestones to monthly priorities to weekly actions
- Dependencies and risks identified for the current quarter
- A weekly self-check that keeps you accountable to the quarterly pace

## Coach Notes

This is where goals become real or stay theoretical. The biggest mistake clients make: setting quarterly milestones that are front-loaded with easy wins and back-loaded with the hard work. Challenge this - if the difficult stuff is all in Q3 and Q4, they will not do it. Also watch for clients who set the annual goal and then make Q1 targets that are clearly insufficient to stay on pace. If the annual revenue goal requires £150K of growth and Q1 only targets £15K, the maths does not work. Use this exercise at the start of each quarter to reset milestones based on actual progress.
