# Leading Indicators

## What to Do

Identify the metrics that predict whether you will hit your goals before the results come in. Leading indicators are the activities and early signals that tell you if you are on track this week, rather than waiting until quarter-end to find out you missed.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners only track lagging indicators: revenue, profit, customer count. These tell you what already happened. By the time revenue drops, the problem started weeks or months ago. Leading indicators give you early warning. If your goal is 10 new clients per quarter and you track weekly sales calls, you will know by week 3 if you are on pace - not at the end of week 12 when it is too late to fix.

## How to Use This

Take your quarterly targets (from Action 2) and for each one, identify 1-2 leading indicators that predict the result. Then set up simple tracking.

---

## Identify Your Leading Indicators

### For Each Quarterly Target:

- [ ] Write the target (the lagging indicator): e.g., "10 new clients this quarter"
- [ ] Ask: what activity, if done consistently, would make this result almost inevitable? That is your leading indicator.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Pick your single most important quarterly target and identify one number you can track THIS WEEK that predicts success
- [ ] Set a weekly target for each leading indicator (e.g., "15 outreach calls per week" or "3 proposals sent per week")

### Common Leading vs. Lagging Pairs

Use this table to find your leading indicators:

- [ ] **Revenue goal** - Leading: proposals sent, sales calls booked, pipeline value, leads generated
- [ ] **Customer growth** - Leading: enquiries received, consultations held, trial sign-ups, referral requests made
- [ ] **Profit improvement** - Leading: cost reviews completed, pricing changes implemented, upsell conversations held
- [ ] **Team development** - Leading: training sessions delivered, one-to-ones held, feedback conversations completed
- [ ] **Process improvement** - Leading: processes documented, audits completed, tools implemented

## Set Up Tracking

- [ ] Create a simple weekly tracker (spreadsheet, notebook, or whiteboard) with your 3-5 leading indicators
- [ ] Set weekly targets for each indicator
- [ ] Record actuals every Friday during your weekly review
- [ ] Add a traffic light: Green (on or above target), Amber (within 20% of target), Red (more than 20% below target)
- [ ] For any indicator in Red for 2+ consecutive weeks, diagnose the cause and adjust

## Use the Data

- [ ] Review leading indicators weekly, not monthly - the whole point is early warning
- [ ] When a leading indicator drops, investigate immediately: is it a capacity issue, a motivation issue, or a priority issue?
- [ ] When leading indicators are consistently green but the lagging result is not following, your indicator might be wrong - reassess the connection
- [ ] Share your leading indicators with your coach or accountability partner so they can challenge you when numbers slip

---

## Expected Outcome

- 1-2 leading indicators identified for each quarterly target
- Weekly targets set for each leading indicator
- A simple tracking system that takes less than 5 minutes per week to update
- Early warning when goals are at risk, with enough time to course-correct
- Confidence that you are on track (or clear evidence that you are not) every single week

## Coach Notes

Leading indicators are one of the most powerful coaching tools you have. They shift the conversation from "how did last month go?" (too late) to "are you on track this week?" (actionable). The most common mistake: choosing indicators that are too easy to game. "Number of emails sent" is easy to hit but does not predict revenue. "Number of proposals sent to qualified prospects" is harder but much more predictive. Push clients toward indicators that measure quality activity, not just volume. Review indicators quarterly and change them if they are not predicting the result.
