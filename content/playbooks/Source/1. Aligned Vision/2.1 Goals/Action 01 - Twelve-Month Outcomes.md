# Twelve-Month Outcomes

## What to Do

Define clear, specific, measurable outcomes for both your business and your personal life over the next twelve months. Not vague hopes. Not a list of things to try. Concrete results that a stranger could measure and confirm whether you achieved them.

## Why It Matters

Without specific outcomes, you default to "busy." You work hard, things happen, but twelve months later you cannot point to what actually changed. Twelve-month outcomes give you a filter for every decision: "Does this move me toward my outcomes or away from them?" They also make it obvious when you are off track - early enough to do something about it, not in December when it is too late.

## How to Use This

Block 45-60 minutes of uninterrupted time. Bring your BOSS diagnostic results if you have them. Work through the business outcomes first, then personal. The two must connect.

---

## Business Outcomes

- [ ] Write down the single most important business outcome for the next 12 months. Make it specific: "Increase revenue from £350K to £500K" not "grow the business."
- [ ] Add 2-3 supporting outcomes that would need to be true for the main outcome to happen (e.g., "Launch second service line generating £5K/month by September", "Hire a sales person by June who is generating leads independently by August")
- [ ] For each outcome, define the measurement: how will you know you have achieved it? What number, milestone, or observable change proves success?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Write your #1 outcome on a card and put it where you see it every day - desk, bathroom mirror, phone wallpaper. Visibility drives focus.
- [ ] Pressure-test each outcome: Is it ambitious enough to matter? Is it realistic enough to be achievable? Would you be genuinely proud if you hit it?

## Personal Outcomes

- [ ] Write down 3 personal outcomes for the next 12 months across different areas: health, relationships, lifestyle, or personal growth
- [ ] For each, define what measurable success looks like (e.g., "Exercise 4x per week consistently for 6+ months" not "get fitter")
- [ ] Check alignment: does each personal outcome support or conflict with your business outcomes? If your business goal requires 70-hour weeks and your personal goal is more family time, something needs to change.
- [ ] Identify any personal outcome that the business should be funding or enabling (e.g., "Take 4 weeks of proper holiday" requires the business to function without you)

## Alignment Check

- [ ] Read your business and personal outcomes together. Do they paint a picture of a life you actually want, or just a bigger business?
- [ ] Identify conflicts: where does achieving one outcome make another harder? Resolve the tension now, not later.
- [ ] Share your outcomes with someone who will hold you accountable: coach, partner, business partner, or mentor
- [ ] Set a calendar reminder to review these outcomes on the first of every month

---

## Expected Outcome

- A maximum of 4-5 specific, measurable business outcomes for the next 12 months
- 3 personal outcomes that align with your business direction
- Clear measurement criteria for each outcome
- Conflicts between business and personal goals identified and addressed
- A visible reminder system and monthly review commitment

## Coach Notes

This is foundational for the entire coaching engagement. Without clear outcomes, every session risks becoming reactive problem-solving. Do this in the first or second session. The most common issue: clients write outcomes that are too vague ("improve marketing") or too numerous (a list of 15 goals). Push for specificity and force them to choose their top 3-5. If they cannot prioritise, that is a signal that purpose and strategy work (from Level 1) may need revisiting. The personal outcomes section often surfaces the real motivation behind their business goals - pay attention to what they say here.
