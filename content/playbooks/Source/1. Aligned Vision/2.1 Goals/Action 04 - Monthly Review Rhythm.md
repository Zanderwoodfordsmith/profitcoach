# Monthly Review Rhythm

## What to Do

Install a simple monthly review process that takes 30-45 minutes and keeps you connected to your goals. This is not a big strategic retreat. It is a focused check-in: What did I achieve? Am I on track? What needs to change? Done consistently, it is the difference between drifting through the year and driving toward your outcomes.

## Why It Matters

Goals without reviews are just wishes. The monthly review creates a forcing function: once a month, you sit down and honestly assess whether your actions are producing results. It catches drift early (before a bad month turns into a bad quarter), celebrates progress (which fuels motivation), and forces adjustments (which keeps you relevant to changing circumstances). Most business owners who set goals and miss them did not fail at execution - they failed at review.

## How to Use This

Schedule a recurring 45-minute block on the last Friday of every month. Protect it like a client meeting. Work through the review framework below.

---

## Setup

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Open your calendar now and block "Monthly Review" on the last Friday of each month for the next 12 months. Recurring, non-negotiable.
- [ ] Gather your materials before each review: your twelve-month outcomes (Action 1), quarterly milestones (Action 2), leading indicators tracker (Action 3), and last month's review if you have one
- [ ] Choose a location that feels different from your normal workspace - a quiet room, a coffee shop, or even a park bench with a notebook

## The Monthly Review Framework (30-45 Minutes)

### Part 1: Score the Month (10 minutes)

- [ ] Review each quarterly milestone: are you on track, ahead, or behind based on this month's progress?
- [ ] Rate the month 1-10 on overall progress toward your outcomes
- [ ] List your top 3 wins from the month - what went right that you should celebrate or repeat?
- [ ] List the top 3 misses - what did not happen that should have?

### Part 2: Diagnose (10 minutes)

- [ ] For each miss: was it a priority issue (wrong focus), a capacity issue (not enough time/resource), or an execution issue (knew what to do, did not do it)?
- [ ] Review your leading indicators: what do they tell you about next month's likely results?
- [ ] Identify one thing you should START doing next month
- [ ] Identify one thing you should STOP doing next month
- [ ] Identify one thing you should CONTINUE doing because it is working

### Part 3: Plan Next Month (10 minutes)

- [ ] Set your top 3 priorities for next month based on what the quarterly milestones require
- [ ] For each priority, define one specific action you will take in week 1
- [ ] Identify any decisions you have been avoiding that need to be made this month
- [ ] Update your leading indicator targets if circumstances have changed
- [ ] Set your monthly review date for next month (should already be in the calendar)

### Part 4: Personal Check (5 minutes)

- [ ] Review your personal outcomes: how did this month serve or hinder your personal goals?
- [ ] Rate your energy, motivation, and satisfaction for the month (1-5 each)
- [ ] If any personal scores are consistently below 3, flag this as something to address - the business should fund the life you want, not consume it

---

## Expected Outcome

- A consistent monthly rhythm that keeps you connected to your goals
- Early detection of drift before it becomes a serious problem
- A record of wins and progress that builds confidence over time
- Clear monthly priorities that tie directly to quarterly and annual outcomes
- A simple, repeatable process that takes less than an hour and makes the rest of the month more focused

## Coach Notes

The monthly review is your most reliable coaching anchor point. If the client does this consistently, the coaching sessions become dramatically more productive because they arrive knowing where they stand. The biggest risk: clients skip it when they are busy (the exact moment they need it most). Build accountability by asking "When is your monthly review this month?" in every session. If they miss one, do it together in the coaching session - they will feel the value and be less likely to skip the next one. Over time, the review notes become a powerful record of progress that you can reference when clients feel stuck or discouraged.
