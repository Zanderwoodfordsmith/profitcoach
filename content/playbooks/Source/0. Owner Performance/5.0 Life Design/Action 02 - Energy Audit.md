# Energy Audit

## What to Do

Track your energy levels across every activity in your week, then redesign your commitments around what gives you energy and what drains it. For one week, rate every meeting, task, and interaction on a simple scale: gives energy (+), neutral (0), or drains energy (-). At the end of the week, sort the results and make decisions about what stays, what goes, and what changes.

At Level 5, managing energy matters more than managing time. You have probably already optimised your calendar. The next breakthrough comes from optimising what fills it.

## Why It Matters

Jim Loehr's research on high performers shows that energy, not time, is the fundamental currency of performance. A business owner running on depleted energy makes worse decisions, damages relationships, and cannot think strategically. You can have 4 free hours in your diary, but if you arrive at them exhausted from energy-draining activities, those hours are wasted.

Most owners tolerate energy drains because they feel obligated - "That is just part of running a business." At Level 5, you have the resources and team to eliminate or delegate most of those drains. The question is whether you give yourself permission to do it.

## How to Use This

Carry a simple tracking sheet (or use notes on your phone) for one full working week. Log every activity and rate its energy impact. Then sit down with the data and make three lists: amplify, redesign, or eliminate.

---

## Week-Long Energy Tracking

- [ ] **Create a simple energy log** - Three columns: Activity, Duration, Energy Impact (+, 0, -). Keep it with you all week.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Rate yesterday's activities from memory right now** - You will immediately spot patterns you already know but have not acted on.
- [ ] **Log every activity for 5 full working days** - Meetings, calls, admin, creative work, travel, social interactions, even lunch. Miss nothing.
- [ ] **Note the time of day for each entry** - Some activities drain you at 8am but energise you at 2pm. Time context matters.
- [ ] **Include personal commitments too** - Exercise, family time, hobbies, social events. Energy does not reset at 5pm.

## Analysis (End of Week)

- [ ] **Sort all activities into three columns: Energisers, Neutral, and Drains** - Count them. Most owners discover 30-40% of their week is spent on draining activities.
- [ ] **For each energy drain, ask: Can this be eliminated, delegated, or redesigned?** - Not "Should I keep doing this?" but "Is there any way I do not have to?"
- [ ] **Identify your peak energy windows** - When during the day are you at your best? Your most important work should live in those windows, not your most draining.
- [ ] **Look for hidden drains** - Some activities seem fine but leave you flat. Certain people, meeting formats, or task types that subtly deplete you over time.
- [ ] **Identify energy amplifiers you are underusing** - Activities that leave you energised but rarely make it into your week. Exercise, creative work, mentoring, strategic thinking.

## Redesign Commitments

- [ ] **Move your top 3 energisers into your weekly schedule as non-negotiables** - Protect them the way you protect client meetings.
- [ ] **Eliminate or delegate your top 3 energy drains this month** - Start with the easiest one to remove. Build momentum.
- [ ] **Restructure your day around energy patterns** - High-energy work in peak windows. Admin and routine tasks in low-energy slots. Never put draining meetings before important work.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Cancel or shorten one recurring meeting that drains you** - You probably already know which one.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Clear map of what gives and takes energy across your entire week
- At least 3 energy drains eliminated or delegated within 30 days
- Daily schedule restructured around natural energy patterns
- More hours spent in flow states on work that energises you
- Better decisions because you are making them with full energy, not fumes

## Coach Notes

This action often produces an "I already knew that" reaction. The owner knows what drains them. The value is in making it visible and giving them permission to act on it. Many owners feel guilty about eliminating things that drain them, especially if those things involve people or long-standing commitments.

Push back gently on "I cannot delegate that." At Level 5, the owner has a team. If they are still doing energy-draining work that someone else could handle, the issue is trust or control, not capability.

Re-run the energy audit every quarter. What drains you changes as the business evolves. New drains creep in and old energisers get crowded out if you are not watching.
