## **What This Is**

Life Design is where the journey comes full circle. You built a business that can run without you. Now the question is: what do you want to do with that freedom?

This is not about retirement. It is about intentional living. The business serves your life, not the other way around. You choose your level of involvement based on what energises you, not what the business demands. You design your days, your years, and your legacy around what matters most to you.

This playbook is for owners who have created something bigger than themselves and now want to live deliberately within that success.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Success feels hollow. You built something remarkable but you are not sure why. Your calendar is full of things you should do rather than things you want to do. You have financial freedom but no clear vision of what that freedom is for. The business runs itself but you still feel trapped by expectations, your own and others'.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have more freedom than most but you have not fully embraced it. Some weeks you feel in control of your time, others you get pulled back into old patterns. You know what you do not want but you are still figuring out what you do want. The infrastructure for independence is there but your mindset has not fully shifted to use it.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Your life is designed by intention, not accident. You are clear on what brings you energy and what drains it. Your involvement in the business comes from choice, not obligation. You have structured your time, relationships, and commitments around what truly matters to you. You feel fully aligned between who you are and how you spend your days.

## **Things to Think About**

What does your ideal Tuesday look like? Not your ideal vacation or retirement, your ideal working Tuesday. How involved are you in the business? What time do you start? What brings you energy? Design from the day up, not the year down.

If money was no object, what would you do differently? You have built the wealth. Now what? Many owners discover that the habits that built the business no longer serve the life they want. Security thinking needs to become abundance thinking.

Where do you add the most unique value, in the business and beyond? At this level, your time is your most precious resource. You can afford to be ruthless about where you spend it. What can only you do? What lights you up? What would the world lose if you did not do it?

What legacy do you want to leave? Not the business legacy, that is running without you. The personal legacy. How do you want to be remembered? What impact do you want to have on the people around you? What would make you proud to look back on this chapter?

How do you want to be needed? Most owners struggle with not being needed by the business anymore. But being needed is different from being wanted. How do you want to contribute? Where do you want people to seek your wisdom, experience, or involvement?

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

**Play 1: Life Vision Canvas**

Map out what your ideal life actually looks like across all dimensions: work, relationships, health, contribution, adventure. This is your personal strategic plan.

**Play 2: Energy Audit**

Identify what gives you energy and what drains it, then redesign your commitments around your energy patterns. At this level, managing energy matters more than managing time.

**Play 3: Involvement by Design**

Define your ideal relationship with the business: how much time, in what capacity, on what timeline. Turn "I should be involved" into "I choose to be involved."

**Play 4: Freedom Structure**

Design the systems and boundaries that protect your freedom while maintaining the relationships and responsibilities that matter to you. Freedom without structure becomes chaos.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Block out one day next week where you do nothing business-related unless it genuinely excites you. Notice what that feels like.

This week: List three things you are currently involved in out of habit or obligation, not choice. Pick one to delegate or eliminate.

This week: Write down your ideal average day five years from now. Include where you are, what you are doing, who you are with, and how you feel.
