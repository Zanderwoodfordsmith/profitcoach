# Life Vision Canvas

## What to Do

Map out your ideal life across six dimensions: work, relationships, health, finances, contribution, and adventure. For each dimension, describe what "great" looks like in 3 years and 10 years. Be specific - not "be healthy" but "run a half marathon, sleep 7 hours a night, eat well 5 days a week." This is your personal strategic plan, and the business exists to fund it.

Most owners build their life around the business. This reverses it. You define the life first, then check whether the business is designed to deliver it. If there is a gap, that gap becomes your strategic priority.

## Why It Matters

A business without a life vision behind it becomes a trap. You hit revenue targets and still feel empty because you never defined what the money was for. Dan Sullivan calls this "the gap" - measuring yourself against an undefined ideal instead of concrete progress. The canvas gives you something real to measure against.

Tim Ferriss asks: "What would you do with $10 million in the bank?" Most owners cannot answer clearly. That lack of clarity means the business drifts toward "more" instead of "enough." Enough is the most powerful number in business because it lets you make decisions from abundance, not anxiety.

## How to Use This

Set aside 90 minutes with no distractions. Work through each dimension honestly. Share it with your coach - the conversation about gaps between current reality and your vision is where the real value sits.

## Life Dimensions

### Work & Career
- [ ] **Define your ideal working week** - How many hours? Which days? What type of work? Be honest about what energises you versus what you do out of habit.
- [ ] **Describe your ideal role in the business** - Are you the operator, the strategist, the chair, or gone entirely? There is no wrong answer, but there must be an answer.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Write down 3 things you do in the business that you would happily never do again** - This is your first delegation shortlist.

### Relationships & Family
- [ ] **Describe your ideal family life** - How present are you? What activities do you share? What milestones do you refuse to miss?
- [ ] **Assess your key relationships honestly** - Partner, children, friends, parents. Which are thriving? Which are on autopilot? Which need repair?
- [ ] **Define non-negotiable commitments** - School runs, date nights, family holidays. Put them in the calendar before business commitments.

### Health & Energy
- [ ] **Set specific health targets** - Weight, fitness level, sleep quality, stress management. Vague goals produce vague results.
- [ ] **Identify your current health risks** - What are you ignoring? Skipped check-ups, poor sleep, no exercise, too much alcohol. Name it honestly.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Book any overdue health appointments this week** - GP check-up, dentist, optician. Takes 5 minutes to book.

### Finances & Wealth
- [ ] **Define your "enough" number** - What annual passive income would mean you never need to work again? Write it down. This drives your wealth-building strategy (see [[5.4 Wealth Building]]).
- [ ] **Map where you are today versus that number** - No judgement, just clarity. The gap is your financial roadmap.

### Contribution & Legacy
- [ ] **Define the impact you want to have beyond profit** - Mentoring, charity, community, industry change. What matters to you when the business chapter ends?
- [ ] **Identify one contribution you could start now** - You do not need to wait until you are "successful enough." Start small.

### Adventure & Growth
- [ ] **List experiences you want to have** - Travel, learning, creative projects, challenges. Things that make life feel full, not just productive.
- [ ] **Schedule one adventure in the next 90 days** - Not "someday." A specific trip, experience, or challenge with a date attached.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A written personal vision across all six life dimensions
- Clear picture of gaps between current reality and ideal future
- Specific, measurable targets rather than vague aspirations
- A foundation that every business decision can be tested against: "Does this move me toward or away from my life vision?"
- Motivation grounded in something deeper than revenue targets

## Coach Notes

This is often an emotional session. Many owners have not thought about what they actually want in years. They have been so focused on the business that personal vision has atrophied. Give them space and time.

Watch for two traps. First, the owner who designs a vision that requires selling the business tomorrow - help them build a realistic timeline. Second, the owner who cannot think beyond the business at all - they have merged identity with the company and need help separating the two. The question "Who are you without the business?" is uncomfortable but essential.

Revisit the canvas quarterly. Life vision is not static - it evolves as circumstances change. The value is in the regular check-in, not the initial exercise.
