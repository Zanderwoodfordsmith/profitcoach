# Emotional Intelligence Audit

## What to Do

Honestly assess how you handle pressure, conflict, stress, and high-stakes situations as a leader. Then build specific practices to respond rather than react. This is not therapy. It is a practical review of the emotional patterns that help or hurt your business, followed by a concrete plan to manage them better.

## Why It Matters

Your emotional state sets the tone for your entire business. When you are calm and focused, your team feels safe to perform. When you are reactive, anxious, or short-tempered, your team spends energy managing you instead of doing their work. Daniel Goleman's research shows that emotional intelligence accounts for nearly 90% of what sets high performers apart from peers with similar technical skills. For a business owner, the cost of poor emotional regulation shows up in staff turnover, bad decisions made under pressure, damaged client relationships, and a culture of fear or avoidance.

## How to Use This

Work through the self-assessment honestly. Score yourself, identify your patterns, then pick 2-3 specific practices to build. This is not about perfection. It is about awareness and incremental improvement.

---

## Self-Assessment

- [ ] **Rate your self-awareness (1-10)** - How quickly do you notice when your emotional state is shifting? Can you name what you are feeling in the moment, or do you only recognise it after the fact?
- [ ] **Rate your self-regulation (1-10)** - When you feel frustrated, anxious, or angry, how well do you control your response? Do you snap first and apologise later, or can you pause before reacting?
- [ ] **Rate your empathy (1-10)** - How well do you read other people's emotional states? Can you tell when a team member is struggling before they tell you? Do you genuinely consider how decisions affect others?
- [ ] **Rate your social skills (1-10)** - How effectively do you navigate difficult conversations, resolve conflict, and build trust? Do people come to you with problems, or do they avoid you?
- [ ] **Rate your motivation under pressure (1-10)** - When things go wrong, do you rise to the challenge or withdraw? How quickly do you recover from setbacks?

## Pattern Recognition

- [ ] **Identify your top 3 emotional triggers** - What situations consistently cause you to react badly? Common ones: feeling disrespected, losing control, being blindsided, perceived incompetence in others, financial pressure.
- [ ] **Map your stress response** - What do you actually do when triggered? Some owners go quiet and withdraw. Others get loud and dominant. Others micromanage. Name your pattern honestly.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Ask two trusted people: "What am I like when I am stressed?"** - Their answer will be more accurate than your self-assessment. Listen without defending.
- [ ] **Review your last 3 difficult conversations** - How did you handle them? What would you do differently? Where did emotion override logic?

## Building Better Responses

- [ ] **Create a personal pause protocol** - Define what you will do when you feel a trigger. A 10-second breathing pause, leaving the room, writing your thoughts before speaking. Pick something simple that buys you time between stimulus and response.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Practise naming your emotions out loud this week** - "I am feeling frustrated right now" is remarkably powerful. It moves you from reacting to observing, which gives you choice.
- [ ] **Schedule weekly reflection time** - 10 minutes reviewing how you handled difficult moments that week. Not to judge yourself, but to notice patterns and track improvement.
- [ ] **Identify one relationship that needs repair** - If your emotional patterns have damaged a relationship with a team member, client, or partner, name it. Repairing it is both the hardest and most impactful thing you can do.
- [ ] **Build a pre-meeting reset habit** - Before any high-stakes meeting or conversation, take 2 minutes to check your emotional state. Are you calm enough to listen well? If not, what do you need to do first?

---

## Expected Outcome

- Clear understanding of your emotional triggers and default reactions
- Honest assessment from people you trust, not just your own view
- 2-3 specific practices that help you respond instead of react
- Improved team dynamics because your emotional state is more predictable
- Better decisions under pressure because you are not being hijacked by emotion

## Coach Notes

This is one of the most sensitive actions in the entire system. Many business owners have never examined their emotional patterns with any rigour, and some will resist strongly. Do not push too hard too fast. Start with the self-assessment and let the numbers speak. If a client rates themselves 9/10 on everything, gently challenge it - ask for specific examples. If they rate themselves low across the board, normalise it - most founders struggle here precisely because the job is stressful. The goal is awareness, not perfection. Help them pick one or two practices and stick with them rather than trying to overhaul their entire emotional wiring at once.
