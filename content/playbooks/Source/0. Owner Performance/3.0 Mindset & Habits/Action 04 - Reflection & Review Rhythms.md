# Reflection & Review Rhythms

## What to Do

Build systematic reflection into three time horizons: daily, weekly, and monthly. Each rhythm has a different purpose. Daily reflection captures lessons while they are fresh. Weekly reflection spots patterns. Monthly reflection connects dots across weeks and drives strategic adjustments.

This is not journaling for the sake of journaling. It is a structured extraction process that turns raw experience into usable insight.

## Why It Matters

Experience alone does not create wisdom. Reflected experience does. Two business owners can go through the same challenge. One learns nothing because they move straight to the next fire. The other pauses, examines what happened, and adjusts their approach. After a year, the gap between them is enormous.

Most owners repeat the same mistakes because they never stop long enough to see the pattern. They lose the same type of client for the same reason. They hire the same wrong profile. They avoid the same conversation. Reflection breaks the cycle by making the invisible visible.

## How to Use This

Start with the daily rhythm for one week. Add the weekly rhythm in week two. Add the monthly rhythm at the end of the first month. Building all three at once usually means none of them stick.

---

## Daily Reflection (5-15 Minutes, End of Day)

- [ ] **Answer three questions in writing every evening** - What went well today? What did I learn? What will I do differently tomorrow? Keep answers to 2-3 sentences each.
- [ ] **Choose a consistent time and place** - Right after shutting down the computer. On the train home. Before dinner. Attach it to an existing habit so it becomes automatic.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Do your first daily reflection tonight** - Do not wait for the perfect notebook or app. Use whatever is in front of you.
- [ ] **Note any decisions you are avoiding** - Avoidance patterns show up clearly in daily reflection. If the same unresolved issue appears three days in a row, it needs action.

## Weekly Reflection (30 Minutes, Friday or Sunday)

- [ ] **Review the week's daily reflections** - Look for themes. What kept showing up? What surprised you? What drained your energy versus what gave you energy?
- [ ] **Score the week 1-10 for progress on your top priorities** - Not effort. Progress. You can work hard all week and score a 3 if you were busy on the wrong things.
- [ ] **Identify one pattern from this week** - A pattern in client behaviour, team dynamics, your own energy, sales conversations. One pattern, clearly stated.
- [ ] **Decide one adjustment for next week** - Based on what you learned, what will you do differently? One specific, actionable change.
- [ ] **Celebrate one win** - Business owners are terrible at acknowledging progress. Name one thing that went well and let yourself feel it.

## Monthly Reflection (60 Minutes, Last Day of Month)

- [ ] **Review the month's weekly reflections** - Four weeks of patterns and adjustments. What stuck? What did you try and abandon? What themes keep recurring?
- [ ] **Compare actual results to goals** - Revenue, clients, projects completed, team performance. Where are you ahead? Where are you behind? Why?
- [ ] **Identify your biggest lesson from the month** - One sentence. Force yourself to distil it. "I learned that..." This becomes part of your personal operating manual.
- [ ] **Assess your energy and wellbeing trajectory** - Are you trending up or down? Burnout does not arrive overnight. It creeps in over months. Catch it early.
- [ ] **Set or adjust priorities for next month** - Based on everything you have reviewed, what matters most in the next 30 days?

---

## Expected Outcome

- A daily habit that captures lessons before they fade
- Weekly pattern recognition that spots issues early
- Monthly strategic adjustment based on accumulated insight
- A written record that shows growth over time
- Reduced repetition of the same mistakes

## Coach Notes

The biggest resistance to this action is "I do not have time." Reframe it: you do not have time NOT to reflect. The owner who spends 15 minutes reflecting saves hours by not repeating avoidable mistakes. Start with daily only. If a client tries to implement all three rhythms at once, they will likely drop all three within two weeks.

In sessions, ask to see their reflection notes. The quality of the reflection matters more than the quantity. A client who writes "good day, got stuff done" every night is ticking a box, not reflecting. Push for specificity. "I noticed that every time I price on the spot I discount by 20%. Next time I will say I will send a proposal tomorrow." That is a reflection that changes behaviour.
