# Priority Filter System

## What to Do

Install a decision framework that replaces urgency-driven behaviour with priority-driven action. You will build a simple filter that every task, request, and opportunity passes through before getting your time. The goal is to stop reacting to whatever shouts loudest and start choosing what matters most.

This is not a productivity system or a to-do list app. It is a thinking tool that changes how you decide what deserves your attention.

## Why It Matters

Urgency is a terrible guide for action. The things that feel most urgent are rarely the most important. A client complaint feels urgent. Building a referral system does not. Answering emails feels urgent. Hiring the right person does not. But the second item in each pair is worth ten times more over the long run.

Eisenhower's insight was simple: important and urgent are different things. Most owners spend their days in the urgent-but-not-important quadrant and wonder why the business never changes. A priority filter forces you to see the difference before you commit your time.

## How to Use This

Build your filter in one session, then use it daily. Refine it monthly as your priorities shift.

---

## Build Your Priority Filter

- [ ] **Define your top 3 priorities for the current quarter** - Not 7. Not 10. Three. If everything is a priority, nothing is. Write them in a single sentence each.
- [ ] **Create your "Does it serve the three?" test** - For every incoming task, request, or idea, ask: "Does this directly serve one of my three priorities?" If yes, it gets attention. If no, it waits.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **List the 5 things you spent time on yesterday that did NOT serve your top 3 priorities** - This gap between intention and action is where your time leaks.
- [ ] **Set a daily "filter moment"** - Before you start work each day, look at your task list and run everything through the filter. Move non-priority items to a "later" list.

## Categorise Incoming Demands

- [ ] **Priority + Urgent = Do now** - These are rare. Genuine emergencies that also align with your priorities. Handle immediately.
- [ ] **Priority + Not Urgent = Schedule** - This is where the gold is. Strategic work that moves the business forward. Block time for it this week.
- [ ] **Not Priority + Urgent = Delegate or contain** - Someone else's emergency. A client wanting an immediate response. Delegate it, handle it in minimal time, or set expectations about timing.
- [ ] **Not Priority + Not Urgent = Decline or park** - This is where most of your time currently goes. Meetings that "might be useful," nice-to-have projects, favours. Say no or add it to a someday list.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Identify one recurring commitment that falls into "Not Priority + Not Urgent" and cancel it this week** - A meeting, a report, a call. Free up the time immediately.

## Defend Your Filter Against Common Attacks

- [ ] **Write down your top 3 "yes triggers"** - What makes you say yes to things that do not serve your priorities? Guilt? Fear of missing out? People-pleasing? Knowing your weakness helps you catch it.
- [ ] **Create a standard "not right now" response** - A polite, repeatable way to say no or defer. "That sounds interesting, but I am focused on X this quarter. Can we revisit in April?"
- [ ] **Review your filter every Monday** - Are the three priorities still right? Has anything changed? A filter only works if it reflects current reality.
- [ ] **Track your filter compliance for one week** - At the end of each day, estimate what percentage of your time went to priority items. The number will surprise you.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Three clear priorities that guide every decision about where your time goes
- A daily habit of filtering tasks before starting work
- Reduced time on low-value activities that feel busy but produce nothing
- A polite, consistent way to say no to non-priority requests
- Over time, a dramatic shift from reactive to intentional use of your working hours

## Coach Notes

Most clients will struggle to narrow down to three priorities. They will want five or seven. Push back firmly. The constraint is the point. If they cannot choose three, they have a clarity problem that needs solving before this tool works.

Watch for the client who builds a perfect filter but never uses it. The daily "filter moment" is the critical habit. Without it, this becomes another document that gathers dust. In sessions, ask: "What did you say no to this week?" If they cannot answer, the filter is not running. Also watch for clients who filter well for tasks but still accept every meeting invitation. Meetings are where most priority leaks happen.
