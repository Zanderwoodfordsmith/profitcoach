# Energy Audit and Recovery Rituals

## What to Do

Track what boosts and what drains your energy throughout the day, then develop short recovery rituals (10-30 minutes) for different types of fatigue. Not all tiredness is the same. Physical, mental, and emotional fatigue need different recovery approaches. This action helps you identify your specific energy patterns and build targeted rituals to recharge.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners push through fatigue until they crash. They treat energy as a single battery that runs down and needs a holiday to recharge. In reality, you have multiple energy systems (physical, mental, emotional, creative) and each one drains and recovers differently. A 15-minute walk can restore physical energy. A 10-minute meditation can restore emotional energy. Knowing which type of fatigue you are experiencing and having a ready-made ritual to address it means you recover faster and crash less.

## How to Use This

Do the energy audit first to understand your patterns. Then build 3-4 recovery rituals and start using them daily.

---

## Energy Audit

- [ ] For 5 working days, note your energy level (1-5) at four points: morning start, midday, mid-afternoon, end of day
- [ ] After each rating, tag the type of energy: Physical (body tiredness), Mental (brain fog, cannot think clearly), Emotional (drained from people, conflict, or stress), Creative (out of ideas, everything feels flat)
- [ ] Note what happened before each dip: a long meeting? A difficult conversation? Skipped lunch? Screen time?
- [ ] Identify your biggest energy drains by frequency and severity
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Identify the one activity that most reliably drains your energy and schedule a 10-minute recovery ritual immediately after it this week

## Build Your Recovery Rituals

### For Physical Fatigue

- [ ] **Movement ritual (10-15 min):** A short walk outside, basic stretches, or a few minutes of exercise. Physical fatigue responds to movement, not rest.
- [ ] **Posture reset (5 min):** Stand up, stretch your shoulders, walk to get water, and return to your desk with a fresh position.

### For Mental Fatigue

- [ ] **Brain break ritual (10-15 min):** Step away from screens. Do something that requires zero cognitive effort: make a cup of tea, look out the window, listen to music.
- [ ] **Task switch (15-20 min):** When deep thinking stops producing results, switch to a low-cognitive task (organising, routine admin) and let your subconscious process the hard problem.

### For Emotional Fatigue

- [ ] **Decompression ritual (10-15 min):** After difficult conversations, conflict, or emotionally heavy work - journaling, a walk, or just sitting quietly.
- [ ] **Connection ritual (10-15 min):** When isolation or stress builds up - call someone who energises you, have a non-work conversation, or spend a few minutes with family.

### For Creative Fatigue

- [ ] **Stimulus change (15-30 min):** Leave your normal environment. Work from a different room, go to a coffee shop, or take a notebook outside. Creative energy responds to new inputs.
- [ ] **Input ritual (15-20 min):** Read something unrelated to your current work, listen to a podcast, or browse inspiring examples in a different industry.

## Make It Stick

- [ ] Create a "recovery menu" - a simple list of 3-4 rituals you can choose from based on what type of fatigue you are feeling
- [ ] Schedule at least one recovery ritual per day (not optional - treated like a meeting)
- [ ] Track the impact: after each ritual, rate your energy again. Which rituals produce the biggest recovery?
- [ ] After 2 weeks, refine your menu to the 2-3 rituals that work best for you

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear map of what drains your energy and what type of fatigue you experience most
- A personalised "recovery menu" of 3-4 rituals that work for you
- Faster recovery from energy dips throughout the day
- Fewer afternoon crashes and end-of-day exhaustion
- A sustainable practice of proactive recovery rather than pushing through until burnout

## Coach Notes

This is a powerful exercise for owners who say "I am tired all the time" but cannot explain why. The audit usually reveals that one or two types of fatigue dominate. Often it is emotional fatigue for owners dealing with team issues or difficult clients, or mental fatigue for those in constant decision-making mode. Help them build rituals specific to their dominant fatigue type. The key coaching insight: recovery is not laziness, it is performance management. Elite athletes recover deliberately - business owners should too.
