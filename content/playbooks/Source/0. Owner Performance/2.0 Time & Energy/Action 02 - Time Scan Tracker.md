# Time Scan Tracker

## What to Do

Track how you spend every hour of the week across the major categories of your life: sleep, commute, meals, admin, client work, strategic work, family, exercise, and downtime. At the end of the week, total each category and see where your 168 hours actually go.

## Why It Matters

You have 168 hours every week. Most business owners cannot account for more than 60-70 of them. The rest disappears into transitions, distractions, low-value habits, and activities that feel productive but are not. This tracker makes the invisible visible. Most people find 15-25 hours per week they did not realise were being consumed by low-value activities.

## How to Use This

Use a simple spreadsheet, notebook, or phone app. At the end of each day (takes about 5 minutes), log how you spent your time in broad categories. Do not try to track in real-time on day one - just reconstruct at the end of each day. After 7 days, total everything up.

## Setup Checklist

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Create a simple time log with these categories: Sleep, Commute/Travel, Meals/Breaks, Admin/Email, Client/Delivery Work, Sales/Marketing, Strategic/Planning, Family/Personal, Exercise/Health, Entertainment/Downtime, Other
- [ ] Decide your tracking method: spreadsheet with hours per category per day, notebook, or a simple app
- [ ] Set a daily reminder for the end of your workday to log that day's time

## Week-End Analysis

- [ ] Total each category for the full week
- [ ] Calculate percentage of 168 hours for each category
- [ ] Identify the category that surprised you most - where did more time go than you expected?
- [ ] Identify the category that got less time than it deserves - where are you underinvesting?
- [ ] Highlight any category consuming more than 15 hours per week that is not sleep, client delivery, or family
- [ ] Compare your actual strategic/planning hours to what you thought they were before tracking

---

## Expected Outcome

- A complete picture of where your 168 hours go each week
- Specific categories where time is being wasted or poorly allocated
- Data to support delegation, elimination, and restructuring decisions
- A baseline to measure against when you redesign your weekly schedule
- The shock factor that motivates real change (almost everyone is surprised by what they find)

## Coach Notes

Assign this in week 1 or 2 of the coaching engagement. The data it produces fuels the next several sessions. When reviewing results, do not judge - help the client see the gap between their current allocation and their goals. The most common finding: business owners spend 2-3x more time on admin and email than they estimated, and 50-70% less time on strategic work than they believed. Use the results to build their Power Plan (Action 5).
