# Time Transformer Assessment

## What to Do

Complete a diagnostic assessment that reveals the gap between how you currently spend your time and how you should be spending it. Score yourself across three dimensions: what you love doing, what generates the highest return, and how your time splits between strategic work and day-to-day operations.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners have never honestly evaluated how their time maps to their strengths, their enjoyment, and their impact. You end up spending 80% of your week on work that drains you, pays poorly, or could be done by someone else. This assessment makes that gap visible so you can start closing it deliberately rather than drifting through another year of busy-but-unproductive work.

## How to Use This

Set aside 30-45 minutes of uninterrupted time. Be brutally honest with your answers. The value of this tool comes from accuracy, not optimism.

---

## The Assessment

### Part 1: Love vs. Loathe

- [ ] List the 10 activities you spend the most time on each week
- [ ] Rate each one from 1-5 on how much you enjoy it (1 = dread it, 5 = love it)
- [ ] Rate each one from 1-5 on how good you are at it (1 = poor, 5 = excellent)
- [ ] Highlight any activity where enjoyment is 1-2 AND skill is 1-3 - these are your immediate delegation or elimination targets
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Identify the single activity you enjoy least and spend the most time on - this is your first candidate to remove from your plate

### Part 2: Return on Time

- [ ] For each of the 10 activities, estimate the hourly value it generates for the business (directly or indirectly)
- [ ] Compare that to what you could pay someone else to do it - calculate the gap
- [ ] Identify activities where your cost (based on what you should be earning per hour) is more than 3x what you could pay someone else
- [ ] Flag any activity where you are the ONLY person who could do it vs. activities where you are just the person who currently does it
- [ ] Calculate total hours per week spent on work that could be done by someone earning less than half your target hourly rate

### Part 3: Strategic vs. Operational Split

- [ ] Review your calendar for last week and categorise every block as Strategic (building the business) or Operational (running the business)
- [ ] Calculate the percentage split between strategic and operational work
- [ ] Set a target split for 90 days from now (most Level 2 owners should aim for at least 30% strategic)
- [ ] Identify the three biggest operational time blocks that could be moved off your plate to shift the ratio
- [ ] Write down what you would do with 5 extra strategic hours per week - make it specific

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear picture of where your time goes and what it produces
- Visible gap between current time allocation and ideal time allocation
- A prioritised list of activities to delegate, eliminate, or automate
- A target strategic/operational split to work towards over the next quarter
- Specific activities identified for each category: love and keep, tolerate but delegate, eliminate entirely

## Coach Notes

This is an excellent first-session tool for any client scoring red or amber on Owner Performance. Walk through it together rather than assigning as homework - the conversation it generates is as valuable as the assessment itself. Pay attention to emotional reactions: when a client says "I know I should delegate this but..." that resistance is where the real coaching happens. Revisit the assessment at 90 days to show progress.
