# Delegation Candidate Identification

## What to Do

Systematically identify the work that can and should be moved off your plate. This is not about dumping tasks on someone else. It is about recognising which activities do not require your specific skills, preparing those tasks for handoff, and building the confidence to let go. This action gives you a concrete delegation plan, not a vague intention to "delegate more."

## Why It Matters

Most business owners know they should delegate but get stuck on three things: not knowing what to delegate, not trusting anyone else to do it, and not having the time to train someone. This exercise solves the first problem directly and gives you the structure to address the other two. Every hour you spend on work someone else could do is an hour you are not spending on work only you can do.

## How to Use This

Use your Task Value Analysis (Action 4) as the starting point. If you have not done that, start by listing everything you do in a typical week. Then work through the framework below.

---

## Identify Delegation Candidates

- [ ] Pull your task list from the Task Value Analysis (Action 4) - focus on everything rated Medium or Low value
- [ ] For each task, answer: "Does this genuinely require MY specific knowledge, relationships, or decision-making authority?" If no, it is a delegation candidate.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Find one task you are doing today that has a clear, documented process and could be handed to someone else by Friday
- [ ] Group delegation candidates into categories:
  - **Admin & operations:** scheduling, data entry, filing, expenses, booking travel
  - **Communication:** routine emails, standard client updates, supplier correspondence
  - **Content & marketing:** social media posting, newsletter formatting, basic design work
  - **Financial:** invoicing, chasing payments, basic bookkeeping entries
  - **Client delivery:** parts of your service that follow a standard process

## Assess Readiness

- [ ] For each candidate, rate delegation readiness: **Ready** (clear process, someone available), **Almost** (needs brief documentation, someone could learn), **Not Yet** (complex, no one available, needs hiring)
- [ ] For "Ready" tasks: name the specific person who will take them on
- [ ] For "Almost" tasks: note what needs to happen first (write a brief, record a video walkthrough, spend 30 minutes training)
- [ ] For "Not Yet" tasks: decide if this is a future hire need, a contractor opportunity, or an automation candidate

## Prepare for Handoff

- [ ] For your top 3 delegation candidates, write a simple one-page brief: what the task is, how to do it, what good looks like, common mistakes to avoid
- [ ] Decide on the check-in rhythm: daily review for the first week, then weekly, then monthly as confidence builds
- [ ] Set the standard: "80% as good as I would do it" is good enough for most delegated tasks. Perfect is the enemy of delegated.
- [ ] Plan the handoff conversation: walk them through the brief, do it together once, let them do it while you watch, then let them fly

## Track and Expand

- [ ] Set a 30-day target: how many hours per week will you reclaim through delegation?
- [ ] Schedule a review in 2 weeks to check: is the delegated work being done? To what standard? What needs adjusting?
- [ ] Once the first batch is stable, identify the next 3 tasks to delegate
- [ ] Keep a running "delegation log" showing what you have handed off, to whom, and how it is going

---

## Expected Outcome

- A prioritised list of tasks ready for delegation with specific people assigned
- Simple briefs for each task that make handoff smooth
- A clear plan for moving 5-10 hours per week off your plate within 30 days
- Confidence that delegation is working, built through structured check-ins
- A growing habit of asking "should I be doing this?" before accepting any new task

## Coach Notes

Delegation resistance is one of the most common coaching challenges at Level 2. The two main blockers: "No one can do it as well as me" (perfectionism) and "It is faster to just do it myself" (short-term thinking). For the first, reframe: their job is not to do it as well as you, it is to do it well enough that you are freed for higher-value work. For the second, point out the maths: 2 hours training someone now saves 2 hours per week forever. That is 100+ hours per year. Challenge clients to delegate one thing this week - not next month, this week. Success breeds confidence.
