# 15-Minute Time Tracker

## What to Do

For one working week, log what you are doing every 15 minutes. Set a timer and every time it goes off, write down exactly what you were working on. This is the most granular time-tracking exercise in the system and it will be the most productive week you have ever had - because measuring what you do changes how you do it.

## Why It Matters

The Time Scan Tracker (Action 2) shows you the big picture. This shows you the detail. It catches the micro time-wasters: the 15 minutes checking social media between tasks, the 30-minute email rabbit hole, the meetings that run 20 minutes over. These small leaks add up to hours per day. You cannot fix what you cannot see, and this tool shows you everything.

## How to Use This

Use a simple sheet with time slots every 15 minutes from when you start work to when you finish. Set a recurring 15-minute timer on your phone. When it goes off, write one line describing what you were doing. Do not edit or justify - just record. Do this for 5 working days.

---

## Setup

- [ ] Create a tracking sheet with 15-minute slots covering your working hours (e.g., 7:00am to 6:00pm = 44 slots per day)
- [ ] Set a recurring 15-minute timer on your phone or use an interval timer app
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Start tomorrow - do not wait for the "right" week. There is no perfect week. Any week is the right week.
- [ ] Tell your team what you are doing so they understand if you pause briefly to log

## Daily Tracking

- [ ] **Each 15-minute block:** Write one line describing what you were doing when the timer went off
- [ ] At the end of each day, review and tag each entry: High Value (H), Medium Value (M), or Low Value (L)
- [ ] Note any blocks where you were interrupted or switched tasks mid-block

## End-of-Week Analysis

- [ ] Count total blocks per day and per week
- [ ] Calculate percentage tagged as High, Medium, and Low value
- [ ] Identify your top 3 recurring low-value activities by frequency
- [ ] Identify the times of day when you do your best high-value work
- [ ] Identify the times of day when low-value work clusters (this is usually mid-afternoon or just after lunch)
- [ ] Count the number of task switches per day - how often did you jump between different activities?
- [ ] Look for patterns: what triggers low-value time? (usually: after meetings, after checking email, or when energy dips)
- [ ] Calculate your "productive hours" - blocks tagged H that were uninterrupted

---

## Expected Outcome

- Granular data on exactly where your working hours go
- A clear picture of your high-value vs. low-value time ratio
- Specific time-of-day patterns showing when you are at your best and worst
- A short list of recurring low-value activities to eliminate or delegate
- The experience of a highly productive week (the act of tracking makes you more intentional)

## Coach Notes

This is intense - warn the client it will feel annoying for the first day but becomes second nature by day 3. The real value is in the end-of-week analysis session. Do this together. Look for the pattern of task-switching (most owners switch tasks 30+ times per day) and the gap between perceived productive time and actual productive time. Use results to design their energy-based calendar (Action 6) and deep work blocks (Action 7). If the client resists doing a full week, even 2-3 days produces useful data.
