# Sleep Optimisation Protocol

## What to Do

Audit your current sleep habits and implement the changes that have the biggest impact on sleep quality. This is not a comprehensive sleep science course. It is the ten highest-impact strategies that business owners can implement this week to wake up sharper, think more clearly, and sustain energy through the day.

## Why It Matters

Sleep is the foundation of everything else in this playbook. Poor sleep destroys decision-making, creativity, emotional regulation, and physical energy. A business owner running on 5-6 hours of broken sleep is operating at roughly 70% cognitive capacity. You would not accept 70% performance from your team. Do not accept it from yourself. Fixing sleep is the highest-ROI investment most overworked owners can make.

## How to Use This

Start with the audit. Then pick 2-3 strategies to implement this week. Do not try to overhaul everything at once. Small, consistent changes compound.

---

## Sleep Audit

- [ ] Track your sleep for 5 nights: what time you go to bed, what time you fall asleep (estimate), what time you wake, and how you feel on waking (1-5 scale)
- [ ] Calculate your average sleep duration and quality score
- [ ] Note any patterns: do you sleep better/worse on certain days? After certain activities? With or without screens?
- [ ] Identify your biggest sleep disruptor: is it getting to sleep, staying asleep, or waking too early?

## The Ten Strategies (Pick 2-3 to Start)

- [ ] **⚡ Quick Win: Set a consistent wake time 7 days a week.** This is the single most impactful change. Your body clock anchors to wake time, not bedtime. Pick a time and stick to it, including weekends.
- [ ] **Get 15+ minutes of natural light within 30 minutes of waking.** Go outside. This resets your circadian rhythm and improves sleep onset 14-16 hours later. Artificial light does not count.
- [ ] **Stop caffeine by 2pm.** Caffeine has a half-life of 5-6 hours. Your 3pm coffee is still 50% active at 9pm. Switch to decaf or water after lunch.
- [ ] **Create a 30-minute wind-down routine before bed.** No screens, no work, no stimulating conversation. Read, stretch, journal, or do breathing exercises.
- [ ] **Make your bedroom a sleep sanctuary.** Dark (blackout curtains or an eye mask), cool (16-19C), and quiet (earplugs or white noise if needed). Remove all screens.
- [ ] **Stop eating 2-3 hours before bed.** Late meals disrupt sleep quality even if they do not prevent you falling asleep.
- [ ] **Limit alcohol.** Alcohol helps you fall asleep but destroys sleep quality in the second half of the night. Even 1-2 drinks measurably reduces restorative sleep.
- [ ] **Exercise regularly, but not within 3 hours of bedtime.** Exercise improves sleep quality significantly, but late-night workouts raise your core temperature and can delay sleep onset.
- [ ] **Use the "worry dump" technique.** If racing thoughts keep you awake, spend 5 minutes before bed writing down everything on your mind and your next action for each item. Get it out of your head and onto paper.
- [ ] **If you cannot sleep after 20 minutes, get up.** Go to another room, do something boring, and return when drowsy. Lying in bed frustrated trains your brain to associate bed with stress.

## Track Progress

- [ ] After implementing your chosen strategies for 2 weeks, re-do the sleep audit
- [ ] Compare your average quality score before and after
- [ ] If improvement is noticeable, add 1-2 more strategies
- [ ] If no improvement, try different strategies from the list or consider whether a medical issue needs investigating

## Expected Outcome

- A baseline understanding of your current sleep patterns and quality
- 2-3 specific, implementable changes made this week
- Measurable improvement in sleep quality within 2 weeks
- Better daytime energy, clearer thinking, and improved decision-making
- A sustainable sleep routine that supports long-term performance

## Coach Notes

This can feel like an unusual topic for a business coaching session, but it is one of the most impactful. Many Level 2 owners are running on broken sleep and compensating with caffeine and adrenaline. Frame it as a performance investment, not a lifestyle lecture. The consistent wake time is the biggest lever - push hard on this one. If a client is resistant, challenge them: "If your top performer told you they were operating at 70% capacity and you could fix it with one habit change, would you ignore it?"
