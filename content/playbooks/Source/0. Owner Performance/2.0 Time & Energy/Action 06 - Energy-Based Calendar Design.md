# Energy-Based Calendar Design

## What to Do

Map your daily energy patterns and align your most important work with your peak hours. Most people waste their best energy on their lowest-value tasks because they schedule by convenience rather than biology. This action restructures your day around when you think best, not when slots happen to be free.

## Why It Matters

Your brain does not perform equally across the day. Most people have 2-4 hours of peak cognitive capacity, a longer stretch of moderate capacity, and several hours of low capacity. If your highest-value strategic work lands in your low-energy hours while email and admin consume your peak hours, you are systematically underperforming. Matching task type to energy level is one of the simplest changes that produces the biggest results.

## How to Use This

If you have done the 15-Minute Time Tracker (Action 3), you already have data on when your best work happens. If not, spend 3 days noticing when you feel sharpest and when you start flagging. Then reorganise your calendar accordingly.

---

## Energy Mapping

- [ ] Track your energy levels for 3-5 days: rate yourself every 2 hours on a simple 1-3 scale (1 = low, 2 = moderate, 3 = high)
- [ ] Identify your peak energy window - the 2-4 hours where you consistently score 3
- [ ] Identify your moderate window - the hours where you score 2 (good for structured but less demanding work)
- [ ] Identify your low energy window - the hours where you score 1 (meetings, admin, routine tasks)
- [ ] Note what affects your energy: meals, exercise, caffeine, sleep quality, specific types of work

## Calendar Restructure

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Move your single most important task to your peak energy window tomorrow. Just one change.
- [ ] Assign task types to energy levels:
  - **Peak (score 3):** Strategic thinking, creative work, complex problem-solving, important writing, high-stakes decisions
  - **Moderate (score 2):** Meetings, phone calls, structured tasks, team management, reviews
  - **Low (score 1):** Email, admin, data entry, routine communications, filing, expenses
- [ ] Restructure your default day to match: peak work first, moderate work second, low-value work in low-energy slots
- [ ] Move all email and Slack checking to moderate or low energy windows - never peak
- [ ] If you have meetings, batch them into your moderate energy window rather than scattering them across the day

## Protect the Pattern

- [ ] Set "focus mode" on your devices during peak hours (no notifications, no email, no Slack)
- [ ] Brief your team on your new schedule: "I am not available for calls or questions between [peak hours] unless it is genuinely urgent"
- [ ] Create a visual cue for yourself: green/amber/red blocks on your calendar matching your energy zones
- [ ] Review weekly: did your actual schedule match your energy-based design, or did the old pattern creep back?

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear map of your daily energy pattern
- Your most important work scheduled during your highest-energy hours
- Low-value work contained in low-energy slots where it cannot do damage
- Measurable improvement in the quality and speed of your strategic work
- A sustainable daily rhythm that works with your biology, not against it

## Coach Notes

This connects directly to the Power Plan (Action 5) - the energy map informs when to place each type of block. Most clients discover their peak window is 7-11am but they have been filling that time with email and meetings for years. The resistance point is usually meetings: "I cannot control when other people want to meet." Challenge this - most meetings can be moved. Start with just protecting the first 90 minutes of the peak window and expand from there.
