# Nutrition for Performance

## What to Do

Assess your current eating habits and implement simple changes that reduce energy crashes, support sustained focus, and keep you performing at your best through the working day. This is not a diet plan. It is a set of practical eating principles for business owners who need reliable energy.

## Why It Matters

What you eat directly affects how you think. A carb-heavy lunch creates an afternoon crash. Skipping meals leads to poor decisions fuelled by low blood sugar. Inconsistent eating patterns mean inconsistent energy. Most business owners treat food as fuel they grab between meetings rather than as a performance tool. Small changes to what and when you eat can eliminate the afternoon slump and extend your productive hours.

## How to Use This

Start with the audit, then pick 2-3 changes to implement this week. This is about progress, not perfection.

---

## Current Habits Audit

- [ ] Track what you eat and when for 5 working days - just a quick note, not calorie counting
- [ ] Note your energy level 1 hour after each meal (1-5 scale)
- [ ] Identify meals that consistently cause energy crashes (usually high-sugar or high-carb lunches)
- [ ] Note how many hours you go without eating during your working day
- [ ] Count your daily caffeine intake (cups of coffee, energy drinks, etc.)

## Performance Eating Principles (Pick 2-3 to Start)

- [ ] **⚡ Quick Win: Eat a protein-rich breakfast or first meal.** Eggs, Greek yoghurt, nuts, or a protein shake. Protein stabilises blood sugar and sustains energy far longer than toast or cereal.
- [ ] **Reduce refined carbs at lunch.** Swap the sandwich, pasta, or chips for a meal built around protein and vegetables. Save the carbs for dinner when the crash does not matter.
- [ ] **Stay hydrated throughout the day.** Dehydration causes fatigue, poor concentration, and headaches before you even feel thirsty. Keep water at your desk and aim for 2-3 litres per day.
- [ ] **Eat at consistent times.** Your body performs best on a predictable schedule. Erratic meal times create erratic energy levels.
- [ ] **Prepare meals in advance.** When you are busy, you eat whatever is fastest, which is usually the worst option. Spending 30 minutes on Sunday preparing lunches for the week saves time and protects your energy.
- [ ] **Snack strategically.** If you need something between meals, choose nuts, fruit, or protein-based snacks rather than biscuits, crisps, or sweets. The sugar spike from junk food is followed by a crash 30-60 minutes later.
- [ ] **Consider your eating window.** Some people perform well with intermittent fasting (eating within an 8-10 hour window). Others need regular meals. Experiment and track your energy to find what works for you.
- [ ] **Reduce alcohol on work nights.** Beyond sleep disruption, even moderate drinking reduces next-day cognitive performance. If you drink, keep it to weekends or limit to 1-2 units on work nights.

## Track and Adjust

- [ ] After 2 weeks of changes, re-do the energy tracking (rate energy 1 hour after meals)
- [ ] Compare before and after - are you crashing less? Is afternoon energy more stable?
- [ ] If improvement is clear, add 1-2 more changes
- [ ] If certain changes are not sustainable, swap them for others from the list

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear picture of how your current eating habits affect your energy
- 2-3 practical changes implemented this week
- Fewer energy crashes during the working day
- More consistent focus and decision-making capacity
- A sustainable approach to eating that supports performance without requiring a complete lifestyle overhaul

## Coach Notes

Tread carefully - food is personal and emotional. Frame this entirely as a performance conversation, not a health or weight conversation. Avoid being prescriptive about specific diets. The goal is energy management, not nutrition coaching. The most common win for business owners: switching from a carb-heavy lunch to a protein-and-vegetable lunch eliminates the 2-3pm crash that kills productive afternoons. If a client pushes back, focus only on the hydration and lunch changes - these two alone make a noticeable difference within a week.
