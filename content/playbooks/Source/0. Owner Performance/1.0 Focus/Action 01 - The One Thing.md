# The One Thing

## What to Do

Each week, identify the single most important outcome that will move your business forward. Each day, identify the single most important task that contributes to that outcome. Do that task first, before anything else touches your attention.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners work on 10 things at 10% instead of one thing at 100%. The result is constant motion with no completion. Greg McKeown calls this the "undisciplined pursuit of more" — saying yes to everything means nothing gets your best effort. One completed priority beats five half-finished projects every time.

## How to Use This

Set up a weekly and daily rhythm. This is not a productivity hack — it is a decision-making filter that changes how you spend every hour.

---

## Weekly Setup (Every Monday, 10 Minutes)

- [ ] **Ask yourself: "If I could only accomplish one thing this week, what would create the most progress?"** — Write it down. This is your Weekly One Thing.
- [ ] **Check it against your 90-day priority** — Does this week's focus actually move the needle on your biggest goal? If not, you are being busy, not strategic.
- [ ] **Write it somewhere visible** — Sticky note, whiteboard, phone wallpaper. You need to see it every morning before the day pulls you in ten directions.
- [ ] **Identify what you will say no to this week** — What meetings, requests, or projects will you decline or defer to protect time for your One Thing? Saying no is not optional — it is the mechanism that makes this work.

## Daily Execution (Every Morning, 2 Minutes)

- [ ] **Before opening email or messages, ask: "What is the one task today that moves my Weekly One Thing forward?"** — Write it down. This is your Daily One Thing.
- [ ] **Do it first** — Before email. Before calls. Before anything reactive. Your best energy goes to your most important work.
- [ ] **When you finish it, everything else is a bonus** — This reframes your day. You have already won. Everything after is extra.

## Weekly Review (Every Friday, 5 Minutes)

- [ ] **Did you complete your Weekly One Thing?** — Yes or no. Be honest.
- [ ] **If no, what got in the way?** — Was it a genuine emergency, or did you let distractions win? The pattern will tell you something important about how you work.
- [ ] **What is next week's One Thing?** — Set it now so Monday morning is clear.

---

## Expected Outcome

- You start each week knowing exactly what matters most
- You start each day with one clear priority, not a chaotic to-do list
- Completion rate goes up because you are finishing fewer, more important things
- Decision-making gets simpler — "Does this serve my One Thing?" becomes your filter
- Over time, you build a track record of weekly wins that compound

## Coach Notes

This is the foundational action for the entire Owner Performance area. If a client can only do one thing from the entire Profit System, it should be this. The power is in the simplicity — resist the urge to add complexity.

In coaching sessions, review their Weekly One Thing from the previous week before anything else. Did they complete it? If not, why? The pattern of what derails them is where the real coaching happens.

Watch for clients who pick a Weekly One Thing that is too small (comfortable but not impactful) or too big (ambitious but not completable in a week). Help them calibrate.
