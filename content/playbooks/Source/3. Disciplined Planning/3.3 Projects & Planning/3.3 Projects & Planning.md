## **What This Is**

Projects are where strategy meets execution. They are the vehicles that carry your business from where it is to where it wants to go. But without structure, projects become chaos. Multiple initiatives compete for attention, timelines slip, and resources get spread thin across too many things that never quite get finished.

This playbook is about bringing discipline to project delivery. It covers prioritising what matters most, sequencing initiatives for maximum impact, and building visibility so nothing falls through the cracks. It is not about complex project management software or endless Gantt charts. It is about a simple system that ensures the right projects get done at the right time.

The line: this is not about the day-to-day execution of individual projects. That sits in 1.3 Execution Playbook. This is about managing multiple projects across time horizons and making sure they add up to something meaningful.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You have a dozen half-finished initiatives and no clear sense of which ones actually matter. Projects start enthusiastically but fizzle out when something more urgent appears. You commit to too many things at once and deliver none of them properly. There is no shared view of what projects are active, who owns what, or when things are supposed to be finished. Strategic initiatives get stuck in endless discussion while urgent tasks dominate your time.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have some project discipline but it is inconsistent. Big projects sometimes get planned properly, smaller ones fly by the seat of your pants. You know what you are working on this quarter but the connection between projects and longer-term goals is unclear. Resource conflicts happen regularly because nobody is looking at the bigger picture. Some projects succeed, others quietly disappear, and you are not always sure why.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You have a clear view of all active projects with defined outcomes, timelines, and owners. New project requests get evaluated against strategic priorities before being approved. Resources are allocated thoughtfully across initiatives so nothing gets starved of attention. You conduct regular project reviews and adjust priorities based on what you learn. Projects connect clearly to quarterly goals, which connect to annual plans, which connect to your 3-year vision. When projects finish, you capture the lessons and apply them to what comes next.

## **Things to Think About**

Which project would move the needle most if you could only finish one this quarter? That is your real priority. Everything else is supporting cast. Most businesses dilute their impact by spreading effort across too many moderately important things instead of concentrating on the one thing that unlocks everything else.

What must happen first to unlock other initiatives? Some projects are dependencies — they clear the path for everything that comes after. Others are nice-to-haves that can wait. Sequencing matters as much as selection. Build the foundation before you build the house.

Who actually has time to deliver this? The biggest project killer is not poor planning — it is assuming people who are already at capacity can somehow take on more work. Every project needs real time from real people. If the math does not add up on paper, it will not add up in reality.

Can you see clearly what is actually happening? Projects die in the shadows. Without regular visibility — status updates, milestone reviews, blockers identified and addressed — momentum leaks away and you wake up three months later wondering where the time went.

What did your last few projects teach you? Every project is a learning opportunity about your planning, your team, your processes. If you are not capturing these lessons and applying them to future initiatives, you are doomed to repeat the same mistakes over and over.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Strategic Project Prioritisation — A framework for evaluating and ranking potential projects against impact, effort, and strategic alignment. Stop saying yes to everything and start choosing what actually matters.

Play 2: Project Planning & Sequencing — A system for mapping out project timelines, identifying dependencies, and sequencing initiatives for maximum impact. Build the roadmap before you start the journey.

Play 3: Resource Allocation & Capacity Planning — Match project demands to actual team capacity so nothing gets starved of attention or sets people up to fail. Realistic planning based on what people can actually deliver.

Play 4: Project Tracking & Reviews — Simple dashboards and regular review processes to keep projects visible and on track. Catch problems early and adjust course before small issues become big failures.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: List every active project or initiative currently running in your business. Circle the three that would have the biggest impact if finished properly.

This week: Pick your most important project and identify the single biggest blocker preventing progress. Schedule time this week to address it directly.

This week: Set up a simple project tracker — a spreadsheet or basic tool where you can see all active projects, their status, and who owns them. Update it before your next team meeting.
