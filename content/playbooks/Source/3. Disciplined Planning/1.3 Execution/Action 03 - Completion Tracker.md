# Completion Tracker

## What to Do

Set up a simple tracking system that measures what you committed to versus what you actually finished. Your completion rate — the percentage of planned work you actually complete — is the single most important metric for an overwhelmed business owner.

## Why It Matters

You cannot improve what you do not measure. Most business owners measure effort (hours worked, tasks started) rather than completion (work finished, outcomes delivered). The Completion Tracker makes the gap visible. When you can see that you planned 10 things and finished 4, you either need to plan fewer things or remove the obstacles to finishing. Both are better than continuing to overcommit and underdeliver.

## How to Use This

Choose one of the tracking methods below. Keep it dead simple — the moment tracking becomes a project in itself, you will stop doing it.

---

## Setup

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Choose your format** — Spreadsheet, notebook, whiteboard, or app. It does not matter. What matters is that you will actually use it every week. A notebook on your desk beats a fancy app you never open.
- [ ] **Create four columns: Planned, Completed, Carried Forward, Dropped** — That is the whole system. Each week, list what you planned (from your Monday Plan), then mark each as completed, carried forward, or dropped on Friday.
- [ ] **Add a "Completion Rate" row** — Completed ÷ Planned × 100 = your weekly percentage. Track this number every week.

## Weekly Tracking (Every Friday, 2 Minutes)

- [ ] **Mark each planned item: Completed, Carried Forward, or Dropped** — Be honest. "Almost done" is not completed. Either it is finished or it is not.
- [ ] **Calculate your completion rate** — Write it down alongside the week number. You want to see the trend over time, not just any single week.
- [ ] **If carrying forward, ask: is this still the right priority?** — Items that carry forward for 3+ weeks are either not important enough to do or too big to finish. Break them down or drop them.

## Monthly Pattern Review (10 Minutes)

- [ ] **Look at your last 4 weeks of completion rates** — What is your average? What is the trend? Improving, stable, or declining?
- [ ] **What types of work do you consistently complete?** — These are your strengths. Lean into them.
- [ ] **What types of work do you consistently carry forward or drop?** — These are either not aligned with your strengths, not important enough, or blocked by something you have not addressed. This is where the insight lives.
- [ ] **Adjust next month's planning based on reality, not optimism** — If your average completion rate is 50%, plan 5 things, not 10. Better to plan 5 and finish 5 than plan 10 and finish 4.

---

## Benchmarks

| Rate | What It Means |
|------|--------------|
| Below 40% | Significantly overcommitting. Reduce planned items or remove blockers. |
| 40-60% | Common starting point. Room to improve through better planning and fewer commitments. |
| 60-75% | Healthy range. You are stretching but realistic. |
| 75-90% | Strong execution. Make sure you are still challenging yourself. |
| Above 90% | Either exceptional or not stretching enough. Try adding one more ambitious item per week. |

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear, visible measure of your execution effectiveness
- Self-correcting behaviour — just tracking completion improves it
- Better planning because you calibrate commitments against real data
- A trend line that shows progress over weeks and months
- Evidence for coaching conversations — "your completion rate dropped, what changed?"

## Coach Notes

The Completion Tracker is the accountability mechanism for everything in this playbook. If a client is doing their 90-Day Deliverables and Weekly Execution Rhythm but not tracking completion, they are guessing about their effectiveness.

The first month is the hardest — most clients will see a completion rate of 30-50% and feel discouraged. Reframe it: "You've been operating blind. Now you can see the gap. That's the first step to closing it." Most clients improve to 60%+ within 4-6 weeks just from awareness.

Ask to see their tracker in every session. It takes 30 seconds to review and it tells you more about their progress than anything they say.
