# 90-Day Deliverables

## What to Do

Define exactly what will be completed in the next 90 days — not a wish list, not a rough direction, but specific, measurable outcomes with clear deadlines and single-person ownership. If you cannot describe what "done" looks like, you are not ready to start.

## Why It Matters

Ray Dalio's 5-Step Process is: Goals → Problems → Diagnosis → Design → Execution. Most business owners jump straight to execution without clearly defining what they are executing towards. The result is constant activity with no completion. 90-day cycles work because they are long enough to achieve something meaningful but short enough to maintain urgency. A year is too abstract. A week is too tactical. 90 days is the sweet spot for turning strategy into finished work.

## How to Use This

Do this at the start of every quarter. Review weekly. The discipline is in the review, not just the planning.

---

## Setting Your 90-Day Deliverables

- [ ] **Ask: "If I could only complete 3 things in the next 90 days, which 3 would move the business forward most?"** — Not start. Complete. The distinction matters. Write them down.
- [ ] **For each deliverable, define what "done" looks like in one sentence** — "Improve marketing" is not a deliverable. "Launch a lead generation campaign that produces 20 qualified leads per month" is. If you cannot describe done, you are not ready to start.
- [ ] **Assign one owner per deliverable** — Not a team. Not "shared responsibility." One person whose name is on it and who will explain what happened if it does not get done. If you are a solo operator, all three are yours — but be honest about capacity.
- [ ] **Set a deadline for each** — Within the 90-day window. Not all three need the same deadline. Sequence them if one depends on another.
- [ ] **Identify the first action for each deliverable** — David Allen's GTD principle: every project must have a defined "next action." What is the very first thing that needs to happen? Make it specific and physical — not "think about marketing" but "write the brief for the landing page."

## The "Not Now" List

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Write down everything else you want to do that did not make the top 3** — This is your "Not Now" list. It is not a rejection — it is a deliberate decision to focus. Put it somewhere visible so you can stop carrying it in your head.
- [ ] **Review the Not Now list at the end of the quarter** — Some items will have resolved themselves. Some will become next quarter's priorities. Some will never matter. All of that is fine.

## Weekly Check (Every Friday, 15 Minutes)

- [ ] **For each deliverable: am I on track to complete this by the deadline?** — Green (on track), amber (at risk), red (behind). Be honest. Amber is where coaching intervention happens — do not wait until it is red.
- [ ] **What is the next action for each deliverable this coming week?** — Keep the momentum moving. Every week should have at least one concrete step towards each deliverable.
- [ ] **Is anything blocking progress?** — Name it specifically. A vague sense of "it's not moving" is not useful. "I need a decision from X" or "I don't know how to do Y" are actionable blockers.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Three clear, defined deliverables for the quarter with deadlines and owners
- A "Not Now" list that frees your mind from everything else
- A weekly rhythm that keeps you honest about progress
- At the end of 90 days, a track record of completion that builds confidence and momentum
- The foundation for more sophisticated planning at higher levels (Projects & Planning, Meetings & Reviews)

## Coach Notes

The biggest trap is clients who set 7-10 deliverables because "they're all important." They are not. If everything is priority one, nothing is priority one. Push them to choose three — maximum. The discipline of choosing is where the value lives.

If a client consistently fails to complete their 90-day deliverables, the problem is usually one of three things: too many deliverables (reduce), unclear definition of "done" (sharpen), or no weekly review rhythm (install). Diagnose which one before adding more.
