# Weekly Execution Rhythm

## What to Do

Build a consistent weekly cycle: plan on Monday, execute through the week, review on Friday. This rhythm turns good intentions into completed work by creating accountability within each week, not just at quarterly milestones.

## Why It Matters

David Allen's GTD research shows that the weekly review is the single most important productivity habit — more important than any app, system, or tool. Without it, tasks pile up, priorities drift, and you lose track of what matters. The weekly rhythm is not about working harder. It is about working deliberately — starting each week with a clear plan and ending it with an honest review of what got done.

## How to Use This

Set up the Monday Plan and Friday Review as recurring calendar blocks. Non-negotiable. If you skip these, everything else degrades within 2-3 weeks.

---

## Monday Plan (30 Minutes)

- [ ] **Review your 90-day deliverables** — Where does each one stand? What needs to move forward this week to stay on track?
- [ ] **Identify your top 3 outcomes for this week** — Not tasks. Outcomes. "Write the proposal" is a task. "Send the completed proposal to the client for approval" is an outcome. Outcomes have an end state.
- [ ] **Assign your top 3 to specific days** — Do not leave them floating. "I'll do the proposal on Wednesday morning" is a commitment. "I'll do the proposal this week" is a hope.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Identify your One Thing for today** — What is the single most important thing you can do today to start the week with momentum? Do it before anything else.
- [ ] **Check for conflicts** — Look at your calendar. Do you have enough unblocked time to do the work you have planned? If not, move or cancel something now, not when it is too late.

## Daily Execution (2 Minutes Each Morning)

- [ ] **Before opening email, confirm today's priority** — What is the one thing that moves the week forward? Write it down. Do it first.
- [ ] **At the end of the day, note what got done** — 30 seconds. Just a quick mark: done, partially done, or not started. This feeds your Friday review.

## Friday Review (15 Minutes)

- [ ] **Review your 3 weekly outcomes — did you complete them?** — Be honest. Completed means finished, not "mostly done" or "I worked on it."
- [ ] **Calculate your completion rate** — How many of your planned outcomes did you actually complete? Track this number weekly. Your completion rate is more important than how many hours you worked.
- [ ] **What got in the way?** — If something was not completed, why? Was it unrealistic? Did something unexpected consume your time? Did you avoid it? The pattern across weeks reveals the real problem.
- [ ] **What carries forward?** — Anything not completed either carries to next week (if still important) or gets dropped (if it was not important enough to finish). Do not let unfinished items accumulate indefinitely.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Set your top 3 outcomes for next week** — Do this now, not Monday morning. Starting Monday with a clear plan means you hit the ground running instead of spending your best energy deciding what to do.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Every week starts with a clear plan and ends with an honest review
- Your completion rate becomes visible — and it will improve just from tracking it
- You stop confusing "busy" with "productive" because you are measuring outcomes, not hours
- Problems surface early (Friday review) instead of festering until the quarterly review
- The compounding effect: 48 weeks of deliberate execution per year transforms a business

## Coach Notes

The Friday review is where coaching gets traction. Ask the client to share their completion rate each week. A consistent 60-70% completion rate is healthy. Below 50% means they are overcommitting. Above 90% means they are not stretching enough.

If a client says "I didn't have time for the review," that IS the problem. The review takes 15 minutes. If they cannot find 15 minutes to assess whether their week was productive, their time management is the first thing to address — which loops back to 1.0 Focus.

This pairs directly with 90-Day Deliverables (which sets the quarterly targets) and The One Thing from 1.0 Focus (which drives the daily priority).
