# Annual Planning Retreat

## What to Do

Run a structured annual process for setting direction, priorities, and resource allocation for the coming year. This is your opportunity to step fully out of the business and think about the business - where it has been, where it is going, and what needs to be true for the next 12 months to be successful. A retreat is not a luxury. It is the foundation that makes every other planning rhythm effective.

## Why It Matters

A business without an annual plan is a business running on momentum. That works when the market is stable and growth is easy. It breaks when conditions change, competition intensifies, or the business hits a complexity ceiling. Keith Cunningham's "Thinking Time" approach emphasises that the quality of your annual plan determines the quality of your year. Every quarter, month, and week should be working toward something defined, measurable, and meaningful. Without an annual plan, your quarterly sessions have no anchor and your weekly meetings have no strategic context.

## How to Use This

Block a full day (or two half-days) at least 6 weeks before your financial year starts. This gives you time to plan, communicate, and prepare before the year begins.

## Pre-Retreat Preparation

- [ ] **Complete a year-in-review analysis** - Revenue and profit actual vs. plan. Customer acquisition and retention. Team changes. Key wins and key failures. What happened and why?
- [ ] **Gather team input** - Survey or interview each department head. What worked this year? What frustrated them? What would they change? What do they need? Aggregate the themes.
- [ ] **Review your strategic fundamentals** - Your arena definition, competitive advantages, and growth strategy from the Profit System. Are they still accurate? Has anything shifted?
- [ ] **Prepare external market data** - Industry trends, competitor moves, regulatory changes, economic outlook. Not an exhaustive research project. Just enough context to plan realistically.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Send a "pre-read" pack to all attendees one week before** - Include the year-in-review summary, team feedback themes, and market context. This prevents the first 2 hours of the retreat being a data download.

## Retreat Agenda (Full Day)

- [ ] **Year in review (90 minutes)** - Walk through performance, celebrate wins, honestly assess failures and missed goals. What did you learn? What patterns do you see? What would you do differently with the benefit of hindsight?
- [ ] **Strategic direction check (60 minutes)** - Does your strategic arena still hold? Has your competitive advantage strengthened or weakened? Are there new opportunities or threats that require a strategic shift? This is the "big picture" conversation.
- [ ] **Annual goals (90 minutes)** - Define 3-5 annual goals. Each must be specific, measurable, and connected to your strategic direction. Common categories: revenue/profit target, customer/market goal, operational improvement, team/culture goal.
- [ ] **Resource allocation (60 minutes)** - For each goal, define the budget, people, and time it needs. If total resource demand exceeds supply (it always does), force prioritisation. What gets full funding and what gets deferred?
- [ ] **Q1 priorities and actions (60 minutes)** - Break the annual goals into first-quarter deliverables. What must happen in the first 90 days to set the year up for success?

## Quality Standards

- [ ] **Hold the retreat off-site** - Get physically away from the office. No popping back to check on things. No "just 5 minutes" interruptions. Full immersion.
- [ ] **Limit attendance to 3-6 people** - The owner, key leaders, and optionally a coach or facilitator. More than 6 people and you get presentations and politics instead of honest strategy.
- [ ] **Start with reflection, not planning** - The temptation is to jump straight into next year. Resist it. Understanding this year thoroughly is the foundation for planning next year well.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Hire a facilitator or use your coach** - Having someone else run the agenda frees the owner to think and participate rather than managing the room. Even one facilitated retreat is more productive than five self-run ones.

## Post-Retreat Execution

- [ ] **Document the annual plan within one week** - A clear, concise document. Annual goals, quarterly milestones, resource commitments, and Q1 priorities. Maximum 3 pages.
- [ ] **Communicate to the full team within two weeks** - Everyone needs to know what the business is aiming for and what their role is. If your team does not know the plan, you do not have a plan.
- [ ] **Connect to your quarterly, monthly, and weekly rhythms** - The annual plan feeds quarterly sessions. Quarterly priorities feed monthly reviews. Monthly outcomes feed weekly meetings. If these rhythms are not connected, the plan lives in a document nobody reads.
- [ ] **Set the date for the next annual retreat immediately** - Protect it now. If you wait until "closer to the time," it will not happen.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear, written annual plan with 3-5 specific goals
- Resource allocation that matches priorities rather than spreading thin
- First quarter priorities defined and ready to execute from day one
- The full team aligned around the same direction and goals
- A connected planning rhythm from annual to quarterly to monthly to weekly

## Coach Notes

The annual retreat is where you have the most strategic impact as a coach. Many clients have never done one properly. The most common issue is setting too many goals. Push hard for 3-5 maximum. Each additional goal dilutes focus and resources. Better to achieve 3 goals completely than partially progress on 7. Watch for goals that are actually activities rather than outcomes. "Launch a new marketing campaign" is an activity. "Increase qualified leads by 40%" is an outcome. The activity might be part of the plan, but the goal must be the outcome. If a client resists the time commitment, remind them that one day of strategic thinking directs 250+ days of execution. The return on investment is enormous.
