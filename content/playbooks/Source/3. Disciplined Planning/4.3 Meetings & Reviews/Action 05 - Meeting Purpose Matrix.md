# Meeting Purpose Matrix

## What to Do

Create a framework for deciding what type of meeting is needed based on the outcome you want. Different outcomes require fundamentally different meeting structures. Information sharing needs a different format than decision making. Problem solving needs a different format than brainstorming. Most meeting frustration comes from mismatched format and purpose - running a decision meeting when you needed information sharing, or running a brainstorm when you needed a decision.

## Why It Matters

The average business owner spends 35-50% of their working week in meetings. Most of those meetings are poorly structured, too long, or unnecessary. Patrick Lencioni argues that the problem is not too many meetings. It is too many bad meetings. A bad meeting is not just wasted time. It is an active drain on morale, energy, and trust. When people sit in meetings that go nowhere, they learn to disengage. A purpose matrix solves this by ensuring every meeting has a clear type, structure, and expected outcome before it starts.

## How to Use This

Build the matrix once. Then before scheduling any meeting, consult it. The right format for the right purpose transforms meeting culture.

---

## Define Meeting Types

- [ ] **Information sharing meetings** - Purpose: distribute information. Format: one-to-many presentation with Q&A. Duration: 15-30 minutes. Key rule: if it could be an email, make it an email. Only meet when questions, context, or nuance require live discussion.
- [ ] **Decision meetings** - Purpose: make a specific decision. Format: present options, discuss trade-offs, decide. Duration: 30-45 minutes. Key rule: the decision must be stated at the end. If you leave without a decision, the meeting failed.
- [ ] **Problem-solving meetings** - Purpose: diagnose and resolve a specific issue. Format: define the problem, explore root causes, generate solutions, agree on actions. Duration: 45-60 minutes. Key rule: start with the problem definition, not the solution.
- [ ] **Brainstorming meetings** - Purpose: generate ideas without judgement. Format: open exploration, divergent thinking, capture everything. Duration: 30-60 minutes. Key rule: no evaluation during the brainstorm. Separate idea generation from idea selection.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Categorise your next 5 scheduled meetings by type** - If you cannot identify the type, the meeting probably does not have a clear purpose. Cancel or restructure it.

## Build the Matrix

- [ ] **Create a one-page reference** - Four columns: Meeting Type, When to Use, Standard Format, Expected Output. This becomes your team's guide for planning any meeting.
- [ ] **Define default durations for each type** - Information: 20 minutes. Decision: 30 minutes. Problem-solving: 45 minutes. Brainstorming: 45 minutes. Set these as starting points and only extend with justification.
- [ ] **Define required preparation for each type** - Decision meetings need options prepared in advance. Problem-solving meetings need the problem clearly stated. Brainstorming needs a focused question. If preparation is not done, postpone the meeting.
- [ ] **Define attendee guidelines for each type** - Information: anyone who needs the information. Decision: only people with relevant input or authority. Problem-solving: people closest to the problem. Brainstorming: diverse perspectives. Smaller is almost always better.

## Apply to Current Meeting Calendar

- [ ] **Audit all recurring meetings** - For each one, identify the type and check whether the format matches. A weekly meeting that tries to be all four types at once will be mediocre at all of them.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Cancel or restructure the one meeting that everyone dreads** - Every business has at least one meeting that people avoid or zone out in. Fix it or kill it.
- [ ] **Introduce the "meeting purpose statement"** - Every meeting invite must include one sentence: "The purpose of this meeting is to [inform/decide/solve/brainstorm] [specific topic]." If the organiser cannot write this sentence, the meeting is not ready.
- [ ] **Set a "no agenda, no meeting" rule** - If there is no agenda shared at least 24 hours before, the meeting is automatically cancelled. This single rule eliminates most unnecessary meetings.
- [ ] **Track meeting hours monthly** - How much total time does your team spend in meetings each month? If it is more than 30% of total working hours, something needs to change.

## Team Adoption

- [ ] **Share the matrix with your team** - Walk them through each meeting type and explain the expected behaviours. Give them permission to challenge meetings that do not have a clear purpose.
- [ ] **Model the behaviour** - Apply the matrix to every meeting you call. Your team will follow your lead.
- [ ] **Review and refine after 30 days** - Is the matrix being used? Are meetings improving? What needs adjustment?

---

## Expected Outcome

- Every meeting in your business has a clear type and purpose before it starts
- Meeting duration matches purpose rather than defaulting to 60 minutes for everything
- The number of unnecessary meetings decreases significantly
- Meeting quality improves because format matches objective
- Team engagement in meetings increases because people know why they are there

## Coach Notes

The quickest win in meeting culture is the "no agenda, no meeting" rule. Clients are often surprised how many meetings simply disappear when this rule is enforced. The ones that survive are the ones that matter. The harder change is getting the owner to model the behaviour. If the owner continues to call ad-hoc meetings without agendas or holds 90-minute rambling sessions, no matrix will fix the culture. Start with the owner's own meetings before rolling out to the team. Also watch for the "everything is a decision meeting" pattern. Some owners turn every discussion into a decision, which exhausts the team. Help them distinguish between "I need a decision today" and "I need to think about this for a few days."
