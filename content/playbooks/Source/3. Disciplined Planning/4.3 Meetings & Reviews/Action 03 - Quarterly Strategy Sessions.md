# Quarterly Strategy Sessions

## What to Do

Protect a half-day every quarter to step back from operations and review progress against your annual goals, assess what is working and what is not, and adjust course for the next 90 days. This is not an extended version of your weekly meeting. It is a fundamentally different conversation - zoomed out, strategic, and focused on direction rather than execution.

## Why It Matters

Businesses that plan quarterly outperform those that only plan annually. Annual plans lose relevance within weeks as conditions change. Quarterly reviews create a rhythm of assessment and adjustment that keeps you moving toward your goals even as the landscape shifts. Verne Harnish's Rockefeller Habits framework identifies the quarterly planning rhythm as one of the three most critical disciplines for scaling companies. Without it, you are navigating by a map you drew 12 months ago in a landscape that has changed significantly.

## How to Use This

Block the session at the start of each quarter. Prepare the data in advance so the session focuses on thinking and deciding, not on looking things up.

---

## Session Preparation (One Week Before)

- [ ] **Gather quarterly performance data** - Revenue, profit, customer metrics, pipeline, team metrics. Compile into a simple one-page summary. No one should be seeing numbers for the first time in the session.
- [ ] **Review progress against annual goals** - For each annual goal, note the current status. On track, behind, ahead, or abandoned. Be honest.
- [ ] **Collect input from department heads** - Ask each one: "What worked, what did not work, and what should change for next quarter?" Prepare a summary.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Send the data pack to all attendees 3 days before the session** - This ensures everyone arrives informed and ready to discuss, not needing a 30-minute data walkthrough.

## Session Agenda (Half Day)

- [ ] **Quarter in review (45 minutes)** - Walk through performance data. What goals did you hit? Which ones did you miss and why? What surprised you, positively or negatively? This is fact-finding, not blame-finding.
- [ ] **What is working and what is not (30 minutes)** - For each major initiative, product, or market: Is it delivering? Should you continue, adjust, or stop? Candid assessment, not wishful thinking.
- [ ] **External landscape check (20 minutes)** - Has anything changed in your market, competitive environment, or customer needs since last quarter? Any trends or threats you need to respond to?
- [ ] **Strategic adjustments (30 minutes)** - Based on everything reviewed, what needs to change? New priorities? Shifted resources? Abandoned initiatives? Specific adjustments to the annual plan?
- [ ] **Next quarter priorities (45 minutes)** - Define 3-5 priorities for the coming quarter. Each must have: a clear outcome, an owner, a deadline, and metrics for success. These become the focus of your weekly and monthly rhythms for the next 90 days.

## Quality of Thinking

- [ ] **Get out of the office** - Run the session somewhere different. A meeting room at a hotel, a co-working space, even someone's home. Physical separation from daily operations helps shift thinking from tactical to strategic.
- [ ] **Limit attendance** - Only people who have strategic input should be there. 3-6 people maximum. More than that and you get presentations, not conversations.
- [ ] **Ban devices for the discussion sections** - Laptops and phones pull attention back to operational details. The whole point is to think beyond the day-to-day.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Start the session with a 10-minute "clear the decks" round** - Everyone dumps their most pressing operational concern. Write them on a board. Promise to address them after the session. Then set them aside and focus on strategy.

## Follow-Through

- [ ] **Document quarterly priorities and share within 48 hours** - A clear one-page document showing the 3-5 priorities, owners, deadlines, and success metrics.
- [ ] **Cascade to the team** - Within one week of the session, communicate the quarterly priorities to the entire team. Everyone should know what the business is focused on and why.
- [ ] **Connect to weekly and monthly rhythms** - Quarterly priorities should show up in weekly meeting priority checks and monthly department reviews. If they do not, they are just a document nobody reads.
- [ ] **Schedule the next session immediately** - Before you leave, the next quarterly session is in the calendar. Protect it.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear view of where you stand against annual goals at each quarter mark
- Honest assessment of what is working and the courage to stop what is not
- 3-5 focused priorities for the next 90 days with clear owners and metrics
- A team that understands the direction and their role in achieving it
- A rhythm of strategic reflection that prevents the business from drifting

## Coach Notes

The most common failure is treating this as an operational meeting with a strategy label. Push the client to think at a higher level. "What should we stop doing?" is often more valuable than "What should we start?" Most businesses are doing too much, not too little. Help them resist the temptation to add new priorities without removing old ones. If a client consistently skips or postpones quarterly sessions, that tells you something about their relationship with strategic thinking. Some owners are uncomfortable with it and default to operations because it feels more productive. Help them see that 4 hours of strategic thinking can redirect hundreds of hours of execution.
