# Monthly Department Reviews

## What to Do

Conduct a structured monthly review with each department head (or function owner in smaller businesses) to examine performance, resource needs, upcoming challenges, and progress against quarterly goals. This is deeper and more strategic than the weekly operations meeting. It is where you understand what is really happening beneath the surface numbers and help each area of your business stay on track.

## Why It Matters

Weekly meetings keep the trains running. Monthly reviews ensure the trains are going to the right destination. Without them, departments drift. Problems that are too nuanced for a weekly check-in accumulate until they become significant. Team members who need support, resources, or direction do not get it until something visibly fails. Andy Grove's management philosophy at Intel centred on regular one-to-one reviews as the most important management tool available. For a business owner, monthly department reviews are the mechanism that prevents surprises and builds genuine understanding of your business.

## How to Use This

Set up a recurring schedule so each department or function gets a dedicated review once per month. Use the same structure every time so both parties know what to prepare.

---

## Schedule and Preparation

- [ ] **Book monthly reviews for each department** - If you have 4-5 department heads, spread them across the month rather than cramming them into one day. One review per week keeps the load manageable.
- [ ] **Send a standard prep template 3 days before** - Give the department head a simple template to complete: wins from the month, metrics update, challenges, resource requests, and priorities for next month. This ensures the meeting is productive from minute one.
- [ ] **Block 90 minutes** - 60 minutes for the review itself. 30 minutes buffer for overflow or immediate follow-up actions.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Start with your most important department this month** - Do not wait until you have everything perfect. Pick one department, run the review, and learn from it before rolling out to others.

## Review Agenda

- [ ] **Performance review (20 minutes)** - Walk through the key metrics for this department. What is on track? What is behind? For anything behind, what is the root cause and what is the plan to recover? Focus on understanding, not blame.
- [ ] **Wins and achievements (10 minutes)** - What went well this month? What should be recognised or celebrated? This is not optional. If you only discuss problems, you train people to hide problems.
- [ ] **Challenges and obstacles (20 minutes)** - What is getting in the way? Resourcing issues, cross-functional blockers, skill gaps, process breakdowns, external factors. Your job is to listen, ask questions, and remove obstacles the department head cannot remove themselves.
- [ ] **Resource review (10 minutes)** - Does this department have what it needs for the coming month? People, budget, tools, time. If not, what is the specific request and what is the business case?
- [ ] **Next month priorities (10 minutes)** - Agree on the 2-3 most important outcomes for the coming month. These should connect to the quarterly goals. Write them down so there is no ambiguity about what success looks like.

## Getting Honest Information

- [ ] **Ask "What am I not seeing?"** - This is the most powerful question in a monthly review. It gives permission to surface issues that the department head might otherwise keep quiet.
- [ ] **Ask about team health** - How is morale? Is anyone struggling? At risk of leaving? Overloaded? These human factors drive performance more than any process or tool.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **End every review by asking: "What is the one thing I could do to make your job easier?"** - Then actually do it. This builds trust faster than any management technique.
- [ ] **Watch for patterns across departments** - If multiple departments raise the same issue (slow decision-making, unclear priorities, resource constraints), that is a leadership or systems problem, not a department problem.

## Follow-Through

- [ ] **Document agreed actions and share within 24 hours** - Each review should produce 3-5 specific actions with owners and deadlines. Share them immediately.
- [ ] **Track actions month to month** - Open the previous month's review at the start of each new one. Were actions completed? If not, why? Accountability without follow-through is meaningless.
- [ ] **Adjust the format based on feedback** - After 3 months, ask each department head: "Is this review useful? What would make it more valuable?" The format should serve them, not just you.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Deep understanding of what is happening in each area of your business
- Problems caught and addressed before they become crises
- Department heads who feel heard, supported, and accountable
- Clear monthly priorities aligned to quarterly goals
- A running record of actions, progress, and patterns that informs strategic decisions

## Coach Notes

The quality of these reviews depends entirely on whether the department head feels safe being honest. If they think the review is about being judged, they will present a polished version of reality and hide the real problems. Help the owner set the right tone: curious, supportive, and direct. Not interrogating, not soft. The "What am I not seeing?" question is transformative but only works if the owner genuinely wants the answer and responds constructively when they hear something uncomfortable. If a client says they do not have time for monthly reviews, ask them how much time they spend on firefighting. Monthly reviews prevent the fires.
