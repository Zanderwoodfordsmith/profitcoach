# Customer Journey Map

## What to Do

Draw out how customers experience your business from first contact to completion (and beyond). Map every touchpoint, every handoff, every moment where they wait, get confused, or feel friction. The goal is to see your business through your customer's eyes and identify where the experience breaks down.

## Why It Matters

You experience your business from the inside. Your customers experience it from the outside, and those two views rarely match. What feels efficient internally often feels confusing externally. A customer journey map reveals the gaps: where leads fall through the cracks, where new clients feel lost, where service delivery creates frustration, and where you lose people without ever knowing why. You cannot fix friction you cannot see.

## How to Use This

Get a large piece of paper, a whiteboard, or a digital tool. Walk through the journey as if you were a brand-new customer who has never heard of you before. Be brutally honest about what actually happens, not what is supposed to happen.

## Map the Journey

### Stage 1: Discovery (How They Find You)

- [ ] List every way a potential customer discovers your business (website, social media, referral, ad, networking, etc.)
- [ ] For each channel, what is the first impression? What do they see, read, or experience?
- [ ] How long does it take from "I have heard of this business" to "I have a way to contact them or learn more"?
- [ ] Flag any channel where the first impression is unclear, outdated, or inconsistent with your brand

### Stage 2: First Contact (How They Reach Out)

- [ ] What happens when someone enquires? (Email, phone, form, DM, etc.)
- [ ] How quickly do they get a response? Is this consistent, or does it depend on who is available?
- [ ] What information do you ask for, and is it the right amount? (Too much = friction, too little = wasted follow-up)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Check your enquiry channels right now. Is there anything waiting for a response? Reply today.

### Stage 3: Evaluation (How They Decide)

- [ ] What does the sales process look like from the customer's perspective? How many steps?
- [ ] Where do they have to wait? For proposals, for callbacks, for information?
- [ ] Is the pricing clear, or do they have to ask?
- [ ] At what point do most prospects drop off? (This is your biggest conversion leak)

### Stage 4: Onboarding (How They Start)

- [ ] What happens in the first 48 hours after someone becomes a client?
- [ ] Do they receive a welcome email, an introduction call, a clear explanation of next steps?
- [ ] How quickly do they get their first result or "win" from working with you?
- [ ] Is the onboarding the same every time, or does it depend on who handles it?

### Stage 5: Delivery (How They Experience the Work)

- [ ] How do you communicate progress during the engagement?
- [ ] Where do clients typically get frustrated or confused?
- [ ] Are handoffs between team members smooth, or does work get lost?
- [ ] How does the client know when something is complete?

### Stage 6: Completion and Beyond

- [ ] How does the engagement end? Is there a clear wrap-up, or does it just fizzle out?
- [ ] Do you ask for feedback? A testimonial? A referral?
- [ ] What happens after the project or contract ends? Do you stay in touch?
- [ ] Is there a clear path to continue working together (repeat purchase, upsell, ongoing support)?

## Identify Friction Points

- [ ] Mark every point on your map where the customer has to wait, repeat information, or figure something out themselves
- [ ] Mark every handoff where work passes between people
- [ ] Mark any moment where the experience is inconsistent (different depending on the day, the person, or the channel)
- [ ] Prioritise: which 3 friction points are costing you the most in lost customers, wasted time, or poor experience?

---

## Expected Outcome

- A visual map of your complete customer journey from discovery to completion
- Every touchpoint, handoff, and waiting point identified
- The top 3 friction points that are hurting your business
- A clear picture of where the experience is strong and where it needs work
- A foundation for improving onboarding (2.6), retention, and referral systems

## Coach Notes

Do this exercise on a whiteboard in the coaching session if possible - the visual element makes it much more powerful than filling in a form. Have the client walk you through the journey as if you are a customer. Ask "then what?" at every step until the full picture is clear. The most revealing moments: when the client says "well, it depends" (inconsistency), "I think..." (lack of clarity), or long pauses (gaps they have never thought about). This map becomes a reference document for several other playbooks, especially 2.6 Customer Experience and 1.6 Fulfilment.
