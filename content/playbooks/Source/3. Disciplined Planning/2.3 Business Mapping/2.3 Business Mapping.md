## **What This Is**

Before you can optimise anything, you need to see how it actually works. Business mapping is about getting the invisible visible — turning the tangled mess of "how things get done" into clear, visual diagrams that show you where the problems are.

Most business owners run on instinct and memory. They know roughly how a customer journey works or how their team delivers a project, but they have never drawn it out. The problem is you cannot fix what you cannot see. Every bottleneck, every handoff, every point where things break down — it is all hidden until you map it.

This playbook is about creating simple visual maps of how work flows through your business. Not detailed process manuals. Not complex flowcharts. Just clear pictures that help you understand where the problems are before you start trying to solve them.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You know things are inefficient but you cannot pinpoint where the problems are. Work gets stuck in mysterious places and no one knows why. Team members have different ideas about how the same process works. You spend time fixing the same problems over and over because you treat symptoms, not causes. When someone asks "how does this work?" you struggle to explain it clearly.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have a rough sense of how your main processes work but nothing is written down or visualised. Some team members understand the flow, others are guessing. You can see obvious problems but the subtle inefficiencies stay hidden. You have tried to document processes before but they sit in folders unused and quickly become outdated.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Your key business processes are mapped out visually so anyone can understand them at a glance. You can see exactly where work gets stuck, where handoffs fail, and where duplication happens. New team members learn faster because they can see how their role fits into the bigger picture. When problems arise, you know exactly which part of the process to examine and fix.

---

## **Things to Think About**

Can you draw your customer journey from first contact to final delivery? Not the idealised version you wish existed — the messy reality of how things actually happen. Every handoff, every delay, every point where customers have to wait. If you cannot draw it, you cannot improve it.

Where does work get stuck most often? There are always a few predictable places where things slow down or break. These bottlenecks are costing you time, money, and customer satisfaction. Mapping helps you see them clearly instead of just feeling their effects.

Who actually owns each step? Mapping reveals the uncomfortable truth about unclear accountability. When something goes wrong, everyone points to someone else. Clear process maps show exactly whose responsibility each stage is — and where ownership gaps exist.

What work is being done twice? Duplication loves to hide in plain sight. Different people doing the same task, information being re-entered multiple times, customers being asked for the same details repeatedly. Mapping exposes these hidden inefficiencies that everyone just accepts as "how we do things."

Which handoffs regularly fail? Every time work passes from one person to another, there is a risk something gets lost, delayed, or misunderstood. Mapping shows you these critical handoff points so you can strengthen the weak links.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Customer Journey Map — Draw how customers experience your business from first contact to completion. See your business through their eyes and identify every friction point.

Play 2: Core Process Flow — Map your most important internal process step by step. Usually your main delivery process or your sales process — whichever drives the most revenue.

Play 3: Handoff Analysis — Identify every point where work transfers between people or departments and assess what regularly goes wrong at each handoff.

Play 4: Bottleneck Identification — Find the constraint points where work consistently gets stuck and analyse what causes the slowdown.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Pick one process that frustrates you regularly. Get a whiteboard or large piece of paper and draw out every step from start to finish. Do not worry about making it perfect — just get it visible.

This week: Walk through your customer journey as if you were a new customer. Note every point where they have to wait, repeat information, or feel confused. Mark these on your map.

This week: Ask three team members to describe the same process. Compare their answers. Where they disagree, that is where your process clarity needs work.
