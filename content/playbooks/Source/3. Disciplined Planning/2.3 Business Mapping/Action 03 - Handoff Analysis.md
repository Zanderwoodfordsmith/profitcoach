# Handoff Analysis

## What to Do

Identify every point in your business where work transfers between people or departments, then assess what regularly goes wrong at each handoff. Handoffs are where most process failures occur - information gets lost, deadlines get missed, and accountability gets blurred. This action maps them all and fixes the worst ones.

## Why It Matters

Every time work passes from one person to another, there is a risk of something going wrong. The message gets garbled. The context gets lost. One person assumes the other has done something they have not. In a business with 5-10 handoffs in a typical process, even a 90% success rate at each handoff means only 35% of work makes it through cleanly. Fixing your handoffs is one of the fastest ways to improve quality, speed, and client satisfaction.

## How to Use This

Use your Customer Journey Map (Action 1) and Core Process Flow (Action 2) as inputs. If you have not done those yet, start by listing the main handoffs you can think of.

---

## Identify All Handoffs

- [ ] List every point where work passes from one person to another in your main processes
- [ ] For each handoff, note: who gives, who receives, what information transfers, and what medium is used (email, verbal, system, Slack, etc.)
- [ ] Include external handoffs: supplier to you, you to client, freelancer to team
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Ask your team today - "Where does work get stuck or lost when it passes between people?" Their answers will identify your worst handoffs immediately.

## Assess Each Handoff

For your top 5-8 handoffs (the most frequent or most important):

- [ ] **Clarity:** Does the receiver always know exactly what they need to do? (1-5 scale)
- [ ] **Completeness:** Does the information transfer include everything needed, or does the receiver have to chase missing details? (1-5 scale)
- [ ] **Timeliness:** Does the handoff happen on time, or is there usually a delay? (1-5 scale)
- [ ] **Accountability:** Is it clear who is responsible after the handoff? (Yes/No)
- [ ] **Failure frequency:** How often does this handoff cause a problem? (Rarely / Sometimes / Often)

## Fix the Worst Handoffs

- [ ] Rank handoffs by failure frequency - start with the ones that go wrong most often
- [ ] For the top 3 problem handoffs, diagnose the root cause:
  - Information loss: what is not being communicated?
  - Format mismatch: is the giver sending information in a way the receiver cannot easily use?
  - Timing issues: is the handoff happening too early, too late, or inconsistently?
  - Accountability gap: does nobody clearly own the work during the transition?
- [ ] For each, design a simple fix:
  - A standard handoff template or checklist
  - A shared system where both parties can see the status
  - A clear trigger ("when X is complete, Y starts")
  - An explicit confirmation step ("received and understood")
- [ ] Implement fixes one at a time and monitor for 2 weeks before tackling the next

---

## Expected Outcome

- A complete inventory of handoffs across your main processes
- Each handoff assessed for clarity, completeness, timeliness, and accountability
- The top 3 problem handoffs identified with root causes diagnosed
- Simple, practical fixes designed and implementation started
- Fewer dropped balls, less chasing, and smoother workflow between people

## Coach Notes

Handoff analysis often surfaces team communication issues that the owner was not fully aware of. Approach this constructively - the goal is to fix the system, not blame individuals. A powerful exercise: have two people involved in the same handoff describe the process independently, then compare their descriptions. Where they differ, that is where the handoff is failing. Common fixes are surprisingly simple: a shared checklist, a standard email template, or a 2-minute verbal confirmation. The improvement is often dramatic for minimal effort.
