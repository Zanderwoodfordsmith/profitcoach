# Follow-Up Engine

## What to Do

Build a systematic nurture process that converts "not now" prospects into future customers. Most sales are not lost. They are just not won yet. This action creates a structured follow-up sequence for prospects who showed interest but did not buy, so they stay warm until the timing is right. You will design touchpoints across days, weeks, and months that keep you top of mind without being annoying.

## Why It Matters

Research consistently shows that 80% of sales require five or more follow-ups, yet most salespeople stop after one or two. The reason is simple: follow-up feels awkward. Without a system, you rely on memory and motivation, both of which fade quickly. A Follow-Up Engine removes the personal discomfort by making follow-up automatic and value-driven rather than "just checking in." The revenue sitting in your unconverted pipeline is often larger than the revenue you need from new leads. Activating it is the cheapest growth strategy available.

## How to Use This

Categorise your unconverted prospects, then build a follow-up sequence for each category.

---

## Step 1: Categorise Your Unconverted Prospects

- [ ] Pull a list of every prospect who engaged but did not buy in the last 6 months
- [ ] Sort them into categories:
  - Hot: interested but timing or budget was wrong (follow up within 1-2 weeks)
  - Warm: engaged but not convinced (follow up within 2-4 weeks)
  - Cool: early-stage interest, did not progress (monthly nurture)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Identify the 5 hottest unconverted prospects right now. Send each one a personal message today. At least one will re-engage.
- [ ] Remove anyone who explicitly said no or asked not to be contacted

## Step 2: Build Your Short-Term Follow-Up (Days 1-14)

- [ ] Day 1 after the call: send a thank-you message summarising what you discussed and the offer
- [ ] Day 3: share a relevant piece of content, case study, or testimonial related to their situation
- [ ] Day 7: check in with a specific question, not "just following up." Ask about something they mentioned on the call.
- [ ] Day 14: make a direct but low-pressure offer. "I have a slot opening next week. Want to revisit this?"
- [ ] If no response after Day 14, move to the medium-term sequence

## Step 3: Build Your Medium-Term Follow-Up (Weeks 3-12)

- [ ] Every 2-3 weeks, send one touchpoint that provides value rather than asks for a sale
- [ ] Touchpoint types: useful article, industry insight, relevant case study, event invitation, tool or resource
- [ ] Every third touchpoint, include a soft call-to-action: "If your situation has changed, I would love to pick up where we left off"
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Create a library of 10-12 follow-up content pieces you can rotate. Build it once, use it for every prospect.
- [ ] Track opens and responses. If someone re-engages, move them back to the short-term sequence.

## Step 4: Build Your Long-Term Nurture (3-12 months)

- [ ] Monthly touchpoint: newsletter, LinkedIn content, or personal message
- [ ] Quarterly check-in: a brief personal note referencing their situation. "Hi [Name], we spoke in January about X. Has anything changed on that front?"
- [ ] Annual triggers: end of financial year, budget planning season, New Year, industry events
- [ ] Keep the tone helpful and patient. Long-term nurture is about being present when they are ready, not pressuring them to be ready now.

## Step 5: Automate What You Can

- [ ] Set up email sequences for the short and medium-term follow-up in your CRM or email tool
- [ ] Use calendar reminders for personal touchpoints that cannot be automated
- [ ] Create templates for each follow-up type so sending takes minutes, not hours
- [ ] Review your follow-up pipeline weekly: who is in which stage, who has re-engaged, who needs a personal touch

---

## Expected Outcome

- Every unconverted prospect categorised and in a follow-up sequence
- A documented short-term sequence (Days 1-14) for recent near-misses
- A medium-term value-driven nurture flow (Weeks 3-12)
- A long-term monthly nurture keeping you top of mind for 3-12 months
- Automated sequences for repeatable touchpoints
- A content library of 10-12 follow-up assets

## Coach Notes

Follow-up is where most clients feel uncomfortable, and that discomfort is costing them serious money. Reframe follow-up as a service, not an imposition. If you genuinely believe your offer helps people, following up is the responsible thing to do. The prospect is busy and forgetful, not uninterested. The biggest implementation barrier is building the content library. Help clients create it in a single session rather than trying to build it one piece at a time. Once the library exists, follow-up becomes easy. Track "reactivation rate" as a key metric: what percentage of nurtured prospects eventually buy. Even 10-15% reactivation from a systematic follow-up engine represents significant revenue from prospects who would otherwise have been lost.
