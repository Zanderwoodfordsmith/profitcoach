# 5-Stage Sales Pipeline

## What to Do

Map your prospect journey through five clear stages - Lead, Booked, Showed, Proposal, Won/Lost - with defined entry criteria, exit criteria, and actions for each stage. This gives you a visual pipeline showing exactly where every prospect sits and what needs to happen next to move them forward.

## Why It Matters

Without a pipeline, sales is a guessing game. You do not know how many prospects you have, where they are stuck, or what your real forecast looks like. A structured pipeline turns sales from an art into a process. It shows you where deals die (so you can fix the bottleneck), how long your sales cycle takes (so you can forecast revenue), and whether you have enough prospects at the top to hit your targets. Every business that scales beyond the owner doing all the selling needs this foundation.

## How to Use This

Work through each stage below. Define what qualifies a prospect for that stage and what moves them to the next one. Then map your current prospects into the pipeline.

---

## Stage 1: Lead

- [ ] Define what qualifies someone as a lead (e.g. expressed interest, downloaded something, was referred, responded to outreach)
- [ ] List all sources that generate leads for your business
- [ ] Define the action that moves a lead to "Booked" (e.g. they agree to a call, they fill in a booking form)
- [ ] Set a maximum time a lead can sit without action before it is archived or re-engaged (e.g. 14 days)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: List every prospect you are currently "talking to" but have not booked a call with. Those are leads that need an action today.

## Stage 2: Booked

- [ ] Define what "Booked" means specifically (date and time confirmed, calendar invite sent, reminder sequence active)
- [ ] Set up a confirmation and reminder process for booked calls (see Action 8: Show Rate System)
- [ ] Define the action that moves a booked prospect to "Showed" (they turn up to the call or meeting)

## Stage 3: Showed

- [ ] A prospect enters this stage when they attend the scheduled call or meeting
- [ ] Define what happens during the call (see Action 4: Diagnostic Call Framework)
- [ ] The exit action is either: a proposal is made/sent, or the prospect is disqualified
- [ ] Track how many booked calls result in shows. This is your show rate.

## Stage 4: Proposal

- [ ] Define what counts as a proposal (verbal offer on the call, written proposal sent, quote delivered)
- [ ] Set a maximum follow-up window (e.g. proposals not responded to within 7 days get a follow-up sequence)
- [ ] The exit is Won or Lost. Every proposal must resolve to one of these.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Check right now - how many outstanding proposals do you have with no next action scheduled? Follow up on all of them today.

## Stage 5: Won / Lost

- [ ] Won: Define the handoff process from sales to delivery/onboarding
- [ ] Lost: Record the reason for every lost deal (price, timing, competitor, no decision, not qualified)
- [ ] Review lost reasons monthly to identify patterns you can fix
- [ ] For "not now" losses, move to a nurture sequence (see Action 9: Follow-Up Engine)

## Build Your Pipeline View

- [ ] Choose a tool for tracking your pipeline (CRM, spreadsheet, Kanban board - anything visual)
- [ ] Enter all current prospects into the correct stage
- [ ] Set a daily or weekly review rhythm to update pipeline status
- [ ] Calculate your current conversion rates: Lead→Booked, Booked→Showed, Showed→Proposal, Proposal→Won

---

## Expected Outcome

- Five clearly defined pipeline stages with entry and exit criteria
- Every current prospect mapped to a stage
- A visual pipeline view in your chosen tool
- Conversion rates calculated between each stage
- A review rhythm to keep the pipeline current

## Coach Notes

The pipeline is useless if it is not kept up to date. Push clients to make pipeline review a non-negotiable weekly habit, ideally Monday morning. The most common mistake is letting prospects sit in a stage with no next action. Every prospect needs a next action with a date. If a client says "I will follow up when they are ready," that means the deal is dead - teach them to set a specific follow-up date instead. The conversion rate data is gold. If the bottleneck is Booked→Showed, fix the reminder system. If it is Showed→Proposal, fix the sales call. If it is Proposal→Won, fix the offer or follow-up.
