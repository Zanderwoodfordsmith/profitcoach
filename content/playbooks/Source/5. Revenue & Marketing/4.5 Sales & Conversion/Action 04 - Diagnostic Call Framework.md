# Diagnostic Call Framework

## What to Do

Build one master sales call script that works across phone, Zoom, and in-person meetings. The framework follows five phases: open the conversation, diagnose the problem, paint the desired future, make the offer, and secure commitment. This is not about being scripted or pushy. It is about having a reliable structure so every sales conversation covers the right ground in the right order.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners wing their sales calls. Some talk too much about their product. Some never ask for the sale. Some diagnose the problem brilliantly but forget to make an offer. A framework gives you consistency. It ensures you spend most of the call listening (not pitching), that you understand the prospect's real problem before offering solutions, and that every call ends with a clear next step. The diagnostic approach also positions you as an expert rather than a vendor, because you are leading the conversation rather than reacting to it.

## How to Use This

Work through each phase below. Write your version of each section in your own words. Then practise the full flow at least three times before using it live.

---

## Phase 1: Open (5 minutes)

- [ ] Write your opening: set the agenda, explain how the call will work, and get permission to ask questions
- [ ] Include a rapport-building question that is genuine, not formulaic (avoid "so, how's the weather?")
- [ ] State the outcome of the call clearly: "By the end, you will know whether we can help and what that looks like"
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: The single most powerful opening move is to give the prospect control. "I want to make sure this is useful for you. What would make this call a good use of your time?" This immediately shifts the dynamic.

## Phase 2: Diagnose (15-20 minutes)

- [ ] Prepare 5-7 diagnostic questions that uncover the prospect's current situation, challenges, and root causes
- [ ] Questions should move from surface to depth: What is happening? How long has it been happening? What have you tried? What is it costing you?
- [ ] Listen for the emotional driver underneath the logical problem. People buy to solve pain or achieve desire, not to acquire features.
- [ ] Take notes during this phase. Repeating their own words back to them later is the most powerful selling technique that exists.
- [ ] Do not offer solutions during this phase. Diagnose first. Prescribe later.

## Phase 3: Paint the Future (5-10 minutes)

- [ ] Summarise what you have heard: "So if I understand correctly, the situation is X, and what that means is Y"
- [ ] Ask the prospect to describe what success looks like: "If we were having this conversation 12 months from now and things had gone well, what would be different?"
- [ ] Bridge the gap between their current situation and their desired future. This gap is what your offer fills.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Let the prospect sell themselves. The more they talk about what they want, the more committed they become before you even make an offer.

## Phase 4: Make the Offer (5-10 minutes)

- [ ] Present your solution in terms of their specific problems and desired outcomes, not your features
- [ ] Structure the offer clearly: what they get, what it costs, what happens next
- [ ] Address the obvious objection before they raise it (usually price or time commitment)
- [ ] Use their own words from Phase 2 to connect the offer to their situation
- [ ] Include social proof relevant to their situation (a similar client, a relevant result)

## Phase 5: Secure Commitment (5 minutes)

- [ ] Ask for the decision directly. Do not hint or hope. "Based on everything we have discussed, would you like to go ahead?"
- [ ] If yes: define the immediate next step (payment, contract, onboarding call) and complete it on the call if possible
- [ ] If not now: set a specific follow-up date and action. "No problem. When would be a good time to revisit this? I will send you X in the meantime."
- [ ] If no: thank them, ask what would need to change for them to reconsider in the future, and add to nurture
- [ ] Never end a call without a defined next step. "I will be in touch" is not a next step.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A written call framework covering all five phases
- 5-7 diagnostic questions tailored to your prospects
- A clear offer presentation structure
- Confidence to ask for the sale directly
- Every call ending with a defined next step regardless of outcome

## Coach Notes

The diagnosis phase is where most clients need the most coaching. Business owners love talking about their solution but hate asking probing questions because it feels intrusive. Reframe it: you cannot help someone you do not understand. A doctor who prescribes without diagnosing is negligent. The same applies in sales. Role-play this framework with clients before they use it live. Record a few practice runs so they can hear how they sound. The most common mistake is rushing through diagnosis to get to the pitch. If anything, slow down the diagnosis and speed up the offer. A well-diagnosed prospect practically closes themselves.
