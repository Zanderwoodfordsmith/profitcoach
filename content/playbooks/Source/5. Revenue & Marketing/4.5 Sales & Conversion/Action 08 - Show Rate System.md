# Show Rate System

## What to Do

Build a reminder and engagement system that turns booked calls into attended calls. You will design a sequence of touchpoints between booking and the call that increases perceived value, builds anticipation, and makes it easy for the prospect to show up. The target is a show rate of 80% or higher.

## Why It Matters

Every no-show is wasted pipeline. You generated the lead, had the conversation, booked the call, and then the prospect did not turn up. If your show rate is 60%, you are losing 40% of your qualified pipeline to a problem that is almost entirely solvable with systems. A 20-percentage-point improvement in show rate has the same revenue impact as generating 20% more leads, but it costs almost nothing. This is one of the highest-leverage fixes in any sales process.

## How to Use This

Map the time between booking and the call. Then build touchpoints at each gap.

---

## Step 1: Measure Your Current Show Rate

- [ ] Pull data from the last 30-60 days: how many calls were booked vs. how many were attended?
- [ ] Calculate your show rate: (Attended / Booked) x 100
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: If you do not track this, start today. Even a simple tally on a notepad gives you the number within two weeks.
- [ ] Set a target show rate (80% is good, 85%+ is excellent)

## Step 2: Build Your Confirmation Sequence

- [ ] Immediately after booking: send a confirmation email or message with date, time, link/location, and what to expect
- [ ] Include one sentence on the value of the call: what they will get from showing up
- [ ] 24 hours before the call: send a reminder via the channel they are most likely to see (email, SMS, or DM)
- [ ] 1 hour before the call: send a short "looking forward to speaking" message
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Add a personal touch to the 24-hour reminder. "Hi [Name], looking forward to our call tomorrow at 2pm. I have been thinking about [specific thing they mentioned] and have some ideas." Personalisation dramatically increases show rates.

## Step 3: Increase Perceived Value

- [ ] Send a short piece of content between booking and the call (article, video, case study) that relates to their situation
- [ ] Frame it as "I thought this might be useful before our conversation"
- [ ] This positions you as helpful before they have paid anything and increases their investment in the relationship
- [ ] If appropriate, send a brief pre-call questionnaire (2-3 questions max) so they start thinking about their problem before the call

## Step 4: Reduce Friction

- [ ] Make the call link or location easy to find in every message (do not make them search for it)
- [ ] If using Zoom, include the link in the calendar invite, the confirmation email, and the reminder
- [ ] Offer easy rescheduling rather than cancellation. Include a reschedule link in your reminders.
- [ ] If a prospect reschedules, treat it as a positive signal, not a negative one. They are still engaged.

## Step 5: Handle No-Shows

- [ ] If someone does not show, wait 5 minutes then send a message: "Hi [Name], I had us down for a call just now. No worries if something came up. Here is my calendar link to rebook."
- [ ] Follow up once more 24 hours later if no response
- [ ] After two no-shows with no communication, move to nurture rather than continuing to chase
- [ ] Track no-show reasons when given. If multiple people cite the same reason, there is a systemic issue to fix.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A documented reminder sequence covering booking confirmation through to call time
- A pre-call engagement touchpoint that increases perceived value
- Easy access to call links and rescheduling options in every communication
- A no-show follow-up process that recovers missed calls
- Show rate tracked weekly with a clear improvement target

## Coach Notes

Show rate problems are almost always system problems, not lead quality problems. If the prospect was interested enough to book, they are interested enough to attend. They no-show because they forgot, something came up, or they did not feel the call was important enough to prioritise. Your system needs to solve all three: reminders for forgetting, easy rescheduling for schedule conflicts, and value framing for priority. The pre-call content is underused by most businesses. A client who arrives on the call having read or watched something you sent is significantly more engaged and converts at a higher rate. Push clients to automate this sequence so it runs without manual effort.
