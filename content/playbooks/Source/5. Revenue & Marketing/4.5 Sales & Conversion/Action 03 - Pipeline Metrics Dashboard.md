# Pipeline Metrics Dashboard

## What to Do

Build a simple dashboard tracking conversion rates at every stage of your sales pipeline. The purpose is to identify the single constraint limiting your growth right now so you can focus improvement efforts on the one number that matters most, rather than trying to fix everything at once.

## Why It Matters

Sales is a chain of conversions. Leads become booked calls. Booked calls become attended calls. Attended calls become proposals. Proposals become wins. If you improve the weakest link by even a small percentage, the impact flows through to revenue. But if you do not measure each link, you will either guess wrong about where the problem is or try to improve everything simultaneously and improve nothing. Eli Goldratt's Theory of Constraints applies perfectly to sales: find the bottleneck, fix it, then find the next one.

## How to Use This

You need your 5-Stage Sales Pipeline (4.5 Action 2) set up first. Pull data from the last 30-90 days if available, or start tracking from today.

---

## Define Your Core Metrics

- [ ] Lead Volume: How many new leads enter the pipeline per week/month?
- [ ] Lead→Booked Rate: What percentage of leads book a call?
- [ ] Show Rate: What percentage of booked calls are attended?
- [ ] Proposal Rate: What percentage of attended calls result in a proposal or offer?
- [ ] Close Rate: What percentage of proposals convert to paying customers?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Calculate your overall conversion rate (leads to customers). Even if the stage-by-stage data is rough, knowing your end-to-end rate immediately tells you whether you have a volume problem or a conversion problem.

## Identify Your Revenue Formula

- [ ] Write out your revenue equation: Leads x Conversion Rate x Average Deal Value = Revenue
- [ ] Calculate each number using your current data
- [ ] Work backwards from your revenue target: how many leads do you need to hit your monthly number?
- [ ] If the lead volume required is unrealistic, your conversion rates need to improve before you scale lead generation

## Build Your Dashboard

- [ ] Choose a format: spreadsheet, CRM dashboard, whiteboard, or printed scorecard
- [ ] Track weekly numbers for each pipeline metric
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: A simple spreadsheet with one row per week and columns for each metric is enough. Do not over-engineer this.
- [ ] Include a rolling 4-week average so you can spot trends rather than reacting to individual weeks
- [ ] Add your target numbers alongside actuals so gaps are immediately visible
- [ ] Set up a visual indicator (red/amber/green) for each metric against target

## Find Your Single Constraint

- [ ] Compare conversion rates across stages. The stage with the biggest drop-off is your current bottleneck.
- [ ] Common bottlenecks and their fixes:
  - Low Lead→Booked: messaging, targeting, or speed of follow-up
  - Low Show Rate: reminder system, perceived value of the call, ease of booking
  - Low Proposal Rate: sales call structure, qualification, or offer-market fit
  - Low Close Rate: pricing, objection handling, follow-up, or proposal format
- [ ] Choose ONE metric to focus on improving this month
- [ ] Set a specific improvement target (e.g. "increase show rate from 60% to 75%")

---

## Expected Outcome

- A working dashboard tracking conversion rates at every pipeline stage
- Your revenue formula calculated and documented
- A clear picture of how many leads you need to hit your revenue target
- Your single biggest constraint identified
- A specific improvement target set for this month

## Coach Notes

Resist the urge to build a complex dashboard. A single sheet of paper on the wall with five numbers updated weekly is more effective than a sophisticated CRM dashboard nobody looks at. The constraint focus is critical. Clients who try to improve all five metrics simultaneously make no progress. Clients who focus on one metric for 4-6 weeks see real movement. If the data does not exist yet, start tracking from today and use the first 30 days as a baseline. Do not wait for perfect data to start making decisions. Connect this to the Leading Indicators action (2.1) so pipeline metrics feed into the broader goal-tracking system.
