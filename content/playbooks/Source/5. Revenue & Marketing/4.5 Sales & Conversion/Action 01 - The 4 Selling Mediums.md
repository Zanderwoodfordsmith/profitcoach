# The 4 Selling Mediums

## What to Do

Understand the four channels through which you can sell - in-person, phone, video call (Zoom), and asynchronous (email, DM, text) - and decide which combination works best for your business, your offer, and your buyers. Each medium has different strengths, conversion rates, and scalability. Most businesses default to one without considering whether it is actually the best fit.

## Why It Matters

The medium you sell through affects your conversion rate, your reach, and your time investment. In-person meetings convert highest but scale worst. Async messages scale best but convert lowest. Choosing the wrong medium for your offer means either leaving money on the table (selling a high-ticket offer over email) or burning time unnecessarily (doing face-to-face meetings for a low-ticket product). Getting this right is a leverage decision that affects every sale you make.

## How to Use This

Review each medium below and assess honestly which ones you currently use, which you should use, and where the gaps are.

---

## Audit Your Current Sales Mediums

- [ ] List every way you currently sell (in-person meetings, phone calls, Zoom calls, emails, DMs, proposals sent without a call, etc.)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: For each medium, estimate your conversion rate. Even a rough guess will reveal which channels actually work and which waste time.
- [ ] Note how much of your selling time goes to each medium
- [ ] Identify whether you chose these mediums strategically or just fell into them by habit

## Evaluate Each Medium for Your Business

### In-Person

- [ ] Assess: Is your offer high enough value to justify the time and travel cost of face-to-face selling?
- [ ] Consider where in-person adds value others cannot: complex demonstrations, relationship-heavy industries, high-trust purchases
- [ ] If you use in-person selling, define a minimum deal size that justifies the time investment

### Phone

- [ ] Assess: Could your current in-person meetings work just as well as phone calls?
- [ ] Phone works well for: quick qualification, warm leads, follow-ups, relationship maintenance, and selling to time-poor buyers
- [ ] Note: phone removes visual cues but adds personal connection compared to async

### Video Call (Zoom)

- [ ] Assess: Is video call your primary selling channel? If not, should it be?
- [ ] Video gives you most of the benefits of in-person (visual cues, rapport, screen sharing, demonstrations) with none of the travel cost
- [ ] For most service businesses selling to other businesses, video call is the optimal primary channel

### Asynchronous (Email, DM, Text)

- [ ] Assess: Are you trying to close deals through messaging that would close faster on a call?
- [ ] Async is best for: initial outreach, qualification, follow-up nurture, sending proposals after a live conversation
- [ ] Async is worst for: handling objections, building trust, closing high-value deals for the first time

## Design Your Sales Medium Strategy

- [ ] Choose your primary selling medium (the channel where most deals close)
- [ ] Choose your supporting mediums (channels that feed into or follow the primary)
- [ ] Map the typical path: e.g. DM → phone qualifier → Zoom close → email follow-up
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: If you are currently trying to close over email or DM, switch one prospect to a call this week and compare the result.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear understanding of which selling mediums you currently use and their conversion rates
- A strategic choice of primary and supporting sales channels
- A mapped prospect journey across mediums
- Eliminated or reduced use of low-converting channels for high-value sales

## Coach Notes

Most business owners have never thought about this consciously. They sell the way they started selling and never question it. The biggest unlock is usually moving from trying to close through async channels to using async for qualification and calls for closing. For high-ticket offers (£2,000+), live conversation is almost always necessary for the first sale. For repeat purchases from existing customers, async works fine. Push clients to track conversion rates by medium for 30 days. The data usually makes the right strategy obvious.
