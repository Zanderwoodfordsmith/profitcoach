# Call Recording and Coaching

## What to Do

Set up a system to record every sales conversation, review wins and losses against your Diagnostic Call Framework, and improve one specific behaviour per week per salesperson. This is not surveillance. It is the same deliberate practice approach that elite performers use in every field: record, review, identify one thing to improve, practise it, repeat.

## Why It Matters

Sales skill improves through reviewed repetition, not just raw experience. A salesperson who makes 100 calls without reviewing any of them will repeat the same mistakes for years. A salesperson who reviews 5 calls per week and fixes one specific issue each time will be dramatically better within a month. Recording also eliminates the "I think the call went well" problem. Memory is unreliable. The recording tells you exactly what happened, and it is often very different from what you remember.

## How to Use This

Set up recording technology, then build a weekly review habit. Follow the steps below in order.

---

## Step 1: Set Up Recording

- [ ] Choose a recording tool: Zoom records natively, phone calls can use apps like Otter.ai or dedicated call recording software
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: If you use Zoom, turn on cloud recording now. It takes 30 seconds and captures every call automatically.
- [ ] Ensure you comply with recording laws in your jurisdiction (in the UK, inform the other party at the start of the call)
- [ ] Create a simple filing system: date, prospect name, outcome (won/lost/pending)
- [ ] Set up automatic transcription if your tool supports it (makes review faster)

## Step 2: Build Your Review Framework

- [ ] Create a scorecard based on your Diagnostic Call Framework (Action 4) with key behaviours to rate:
  - Did you open by setting an agenda and getting permission?
  - Did you ask diagnostic questions before presenting solutions?
  - Did you listen more than you talked? (Aim for 70/30 prospect/seller split)
  - Did you uncover the emotional driver, not just the logical problem?
  - Did you present the offer in terms of their specific situation?
  - Did you ask for the decision directly?
  - Did the call end with a clear next step?
- [ ] Rate each behaviour 1-5 on every reviewed call
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Review your last recorded call right now against this scorecard. You will immediately spot 2-3 things you do well and 1-2 that need work.

## Step 3: Establish the Weekly Review Rhythm

- [ ] Block 30 minutes per week for call review (Friday afternoon works well)
- [ ] Review at least 2 calls per week: one win and one loss
- [ ] For wins: identify what you did well so you can repeat it deliberately
- [ ] For losses: identify the moment the deal started slipping and what you could have done differently
- [ ] If you have a sales team, review one call per salesperson per week as a coaching exercise

## Step 4: Focus on One Behaviour at a Time

- [ ] From your review, identify the single behaviour that would make the biggest difference if improved
- [ ] Make that behaviour your focus for the entire week. Before every call, remind yourself of the one thing you are working on.
- [ ] Track whether the behaviour improves across the week
- [ ] Next week, pick a new behaviour or continue with the same one if it is not yet consistent
- [ ] Keep a log of behaviours worked on and progress made

---

## Expected Outcome

- Every sales call recorded and filed consistently
- A scorecard for evaluating calls against your framework
- A weekly review habit with at least 2 calls reviewed per week
- One specific improvement focus per week per salesperson
- A log showing skill progression over time

## Coach Notes

Recording calls is one of the most resisted and most effective coaching tools available. Clients hate hearing themselves on tape. Push through the discomfort. The insight is worth it. Keep the review session positive: start with what went well, then identify one improvement area. Never review a call by listing everything wrong. That destroys confidence. The one-behaviour-per-week rule is essential. Trying to fix three things simultaneously means fixing none of them. If you coach a client's sales team, use call recordings in group sessions. Hearing a colleague's great call teaches faster than any training manual. Just make sure the culture is supportive, not judgemental. Connect this to the Diagnostic Call Framework (Action 4) so the review scorecard matches the call structure.
