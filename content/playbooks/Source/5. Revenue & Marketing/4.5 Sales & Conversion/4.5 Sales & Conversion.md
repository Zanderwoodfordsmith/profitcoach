## **What This Is**

Sales and conversion is where marketing effort becomes revenue. It is the bridge between having prospects and having customers. Get this right and you multiply the value of every lead, every conversation, every pound spent on marketing. Get it wrong and you are burning money on leads that never convert.

This playbook is about building a systematic sales process that works consistently across all mediums — whether you are selling in person, on the phone, over Zoom, or through digital channels. It covers the complete journey from initial conversation to signed contract, with clear metrics so you know exactly where deals are won and lost.

This is not about learning sales tricks or persuasion techniques. It is about creating a repeatable system that converts prospects into customers predictably, scales beyond just you, and improves every month.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Your conversion rates vary wildly depending on your mood, the prospect, or what you had for breakfast. You wing every sales conversation with no clear process. Some deals close, others disappear, and you have no idea why. You cannot explain to someone else how to sell your product because you do not have a system — just instinct and hope.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have some sales processes but they are different for phone calls versus Zoom versus email. Your close rate is decent but inconsistent across your team. You know roughly how many leads convert but you cannot pinpoint where most deals are lost. Some months are great, others are disappointing, and you cannot predict which will be which.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You have one core sales process that adapts to any medium — phone, Zoom, in-person, or digital. Every conversation follows the same diagnostic framework so you are comparing like with like. You know your exact conversion rates at every stage and can spot problems immediately. New salespeople can learn your system and hit targets within 90 days. Revenue becomes predictable because conversion is systematic.

---

## **Things to Think About**

Do you have one sales process or four different approaches? Most businesses sell differently on the phone versus Zoom versus email versus in-person. This creates inconsistency, makes training impossible, and means you never get good at any single approach.

Can you explain exactly where deals are lost? If you cannot tell the difference between prospects who do not show up, prospects who show but do not buy, and prospects who want to think about it, you cannot improve. Each problem needs a different solution.

Would your sales process work if you recorded every conversation? The test of a real system is whether it sounds professional when played back. If you would be embarrassed by a recording, you do not have a process — you have improvisation.

Do you handle objections or do they handle you? The same five objections come up in 80% of sales conversations. If you do not have prepared, practiced responses that feel natural, you are losing deals you should win.

Is your sales training "shadow me once" or something more systematic? Most SMEs train new salespeople by having them listen to a few calls and then hope for the best. Real sales training means scripts, role-play, recorded calls, and weekly coaching sessions.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

This is a larger playbook organised into four sections. When you open it in the Profit System, you will find:

### **Sales Architecture & Pipeline**

Building the foundation for systematic selling.

Play 1: The 4 Selling Mediums — Understand when to use in-person, phone, Zoom, and async channels, and how they work together rather than competing with each other.

Play 2: 5-Stage Sales Pipeline — Map every prospect journey through Lead → Booked → Showed → Proposal → Won/Lost, with clear entry and exit criteria for each stage.

Play 3: Pipeline Metrics Dashboard — Track conversion rates at every stage so you can identify the single constraint limiting your growth and focus your improvement efforts there.

### **Core Sales Process**

The systematic approach that works across all mediums.

Play 4: Diagnostic Call Framework — One master script that adapts to phone, Zoom, or in-person: open the conversation, diagnose the problem, paint the desired future, make the offer, and secure commitment.

Play 5: Risk Reversal & Guarantees — Use guarantees strategically to remove the biggest objection — "what if it does not work" — without creating operational nightmares or attracting problem customers.

Play 6: Objection Handling System — Prepare and practice responses to the five objections that appear in 80% of sales conversations, including which ones to handle before the call and which ones to address live.

### **Conversion Optimization**

Improving show rates, close rates, and deal sizes.

Play 7: Async-to-Call Flow — Use DM, email, and text to start conversations, qualify prospects, and book calls — without trying to close deals through messaging.

Play 8: Show Rate System — The reminder sequences, incentives, and personal touches that turn booked calls into attended calls, because you cannot close people who do not show up.

Play 9: Follow-Up Engine — What happens to prospects who do not buy immediately: the systematic nurture process that converts maybes into yeses over time.

### **Sales Team Development**

Scaling sales beyond just the founder.

Play 10: Call Recording & Coaching — Record every sales conversation, review wins and losses against your framework, and improve one specific behaviour per week per salesperson.

Play 11: Sales Training System — Onboard new salespeople with scripts, role-play scenarios, and a 90-day development plan so they can hit targets quickly.

Play 12: Setter & Closer Structure — When you are ready to scale beyond doing all the selling yourself, how to split lead qualification from deal closing and route the best prospects to your best closers.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Map your last 20 prospects through the 5-stage pipeline. Count how many progressed from each stage to the next. Your lowest conversion rate shows you where to focus first.

This week: Write down the exact words you use to open every sales conversation. If it changes depending on your mood, you need a script. If you do not have one, create a 30-second opening that works for phone, Zoom, or in-person.

This week: List the five objections you hear most often. Write a 60-second response to each one. Practice them out loud until they sound natural, not rehearsed.
