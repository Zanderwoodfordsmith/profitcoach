# Async-to-Call Flow

## What to Do

Build a structured process for using DMs, email, and text messages to start conversations, qualify prospects, and book calls - without trying to close deals through messaging. Async channels are brilliant for opening doors and filtering prospects. They are terrible for closing. This action maps the handoff from written conversation to live call so you get the right people on calls and waste less time on unqualified ones.

## Why It Matters

Two mistakes dominate async selling. First, trying to close through DMs or email, which leads to long message threads that go nowhere because you cannot read tone, handle objections, or build trust in real time. Second, jumping to "let's get on a call" too early, which feels pushy and scares off prospects who are not ready. The async-to-call flow solves both problems. It gives you a sequence: start the conversation casually, establish relevance, qualify interest, then transition to a call when the prospect is warm and ready.

## How to Use This

Map your async flow for each channel you use (DM, email, text). Follow the steps below in order.

---

## Step 1: Define Your Async Channels

- [ ] List every async channel where you interact with prospects (LinkedIn DM, Instagram DM, email, SMS, WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger, etc.)
- [ ] For each channel, note the typical conversation style (formal vs. casual, long vs. short messages)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Pick your highest-volume async channel and build the flow for that one first. Add others later.
- [ ] Identify which channels generate the most booked calls currently

## Step 2: Build Your Opening Sequence

- [ ] Write 3-5 opening message templates for each channel (not copy-paste scripts, but frameworks you personalise)
- [ ] First message purpose: start a relevant conversation, not pitch or sell
- [ ] Good openers: react to something they posted, ask a genuine question, share something useful, reference a mutual connection
- [ ] Bad openers: "Hi, I help businesses like yours..." or anything that sounds like it was sent to 500 people
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Go through your last 10 successful conversations that led to calls. What did the opening message look like? Use that pattern.

## Step 3: Build Your Qualification Flow

- [ ] After the conversation is flowing, ask 2-3 qualifying questions through messaging:
  - What is their situation? (Are they a fit for what you offer?)
  - What have they tried? (Are they problem-aware and solution-seeking?)
  - What is their timeline? (Are they ready to act or just browsing?)
- [ ] If qualified, transition to a call. If not, either nurture or gracefully exit.
- [ ] Write your transition phrase: the message that moves from async chat to booked call

## Step 4: Design the Call Transition

- [ ] Write 2-3 transition messages that naturally move from messaging to a call
- [ ] Good transitions: "This is getting interesting - would it be easier to jump on a quick call to dig into this properly?" or "I think I can help but I would need to understand more. Fancy a 20-minute call this week?"
- [ ] Include a direct booking link or propose specific times (reduce friction)
- [ ] If they hesitate, offer a low-commitment framing: "No pitch, just a conversation to see if it makes sense"
- [ ] Once booked, send a confirmation with date, time, and what you will cover

## Step 5: Handle Common Async Objections

- [ ] "Can you just send me more info?" - Respond with a short summary and restate the call offer. Do not send a full proposal over message.
- [ ] "I'm not ready yet" - Respect it, offer something useful (article, resource), and set a future follow-up date
- [ ] "What does it cost?" - Give a range if appropriate, then pivot: "The exact investment depends on your situation, which is why the call is useful"
- [ ] Radio silence after initial response - Send one follow-up after 3-5 days, then move to nurture

---

## Expected Outcome

- A documented async-to-call flow for your primary channel
- Opening message templates you can personalise quickly
- A qualification framework that filters before the call, not during it
- Transition messages that move warm prospects to booked calls naturally
- Fewer dead-end message threads and more qualified calls on your calendar

## Coach Notes

The biggest mindset shift for clients is accepting that the purpose of messaging is to book the call, not to sell. Many business owners write long, detailed DMs trying to convince someone to buy. That almost never works. Keep messages short, conversational, and focused on getting to a live conversation. The qualification step is critical. Without it, clients end up on calls with people who were never going to buy. Teach clients to see async as a filter, not a sales channel. The metric that matters is "messages sent to calls booked" ratio. If it takes 50 DMs to book one call, the opening messages need work. If calls are booked but prospects are unqualified, the qualification questions need work.
