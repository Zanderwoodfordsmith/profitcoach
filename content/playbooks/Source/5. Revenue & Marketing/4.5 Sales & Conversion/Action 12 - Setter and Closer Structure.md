# Setter and Closer Structure

## What to Do

Design the structure for splitting lead qualification (setting) from deal closing when you are ready to scale beyond doing all the selling yourself. A setter qualifies prospects, books calls, and ensures only the right people reach the closer. A closer runs the diagnostic call, makes the offer, and wins the deal. This division lets you handle more volume, reduces wasted time on unqualified prospects, and creates a clear career path for your sales team.

## Why It Matters

In early-stage businesses, the owner does everything: finds leads, qualifies them, books calls, runs calls, closes deals, and follows up. That works until it does not. The ceiling is typically 15-20 sales conversations per week before quality drops. Beyond that, you need to split the role. The setter-closer model is the most common and effective way to do this. It works because each role requires different skills. Setting requires speed, volume, and friendly persistence. Closing requires depth, empathy, and commercial acumen. Few people excel at both.

## How to Use This

Only move to a setter-closer model when you have a documented sales process (Actions 1-6) and enough lead volume to justify the split. Work through the checklist below to design the structure.

---

## Assess Readiness

- [ ] Confirm you have a documented sales process that someone else can follow
- [ ] Calculate your current weekly sales call volume. If it is under 10 per week, you probably do not need this yet.
- [ ] Calculate how much of your selling time goes to qualification vs. closing. If more than 40% is qualification, the split will create immediate leverage.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Track your time for one week. Log every sales-related activity and categorise it as setting (qualification, booking, admin) or closing (running calls, proposals, negotiation). The ratio tells you exactly where the bottleneck is.

## Define the Setter Role

- [ ] Write a clear job description: what setters do, what they do not do, and how they are measured
- [ ] Core setter activities: respond to inbound leads, conduct outbound outreach, qualify using 2-3 key questions, book calls for closers
- [ ] Setters should NOT: make offers, discuss pricing, send proposals, or try to close
- [ ] Define qualification criteria: what makes a prospect qualified to be booked with a closer?
- [ ] Set setter KPIs: number of conversations started, qualification rate, calls booked per week, show rate of booked calls

## Define the Closer Role

- [ ] Write a clear job description: what closers do, what they do not do, and how they are measured
- [ ] Core closer activities: run diagnostic calls, present offers, handle objections, close deals, manage proposal follow-up
- [ ] Closers should NOT: prospect, respond to initial enquiries, or handle admin
- [ ] Set closer KPIs: calls attended, proposal rate, close rate, average deal value, revenue per month

## Design the Handoff Process

- [ ] Define the information the setter passes to the closer before each call (prospect name, company, situation summary, key pain points, qualification notes)
- [ ] Choose a format: CRM notes, shared document, Slack message, or handoff form
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Create a simple one-page handoff form with 5 fields. Keep it fast for the setter to complete and useful for the closer to review.
- [ ] Set a standard: closers review the handoff notes before every call so they walk in prepared
- [ ] Build a feedback loop: closers tell setters when a booked prospect was not actually qualified, so qualification criteria improve over time

## Set Compensation Structure

- [ ] Decide how setters are paid: base salary + bonus per qualified booking, or base + commission on deals that close from their bookings
- [ ] Decide how closers are paid: base salary + commission on closed deals, or higher base with smaller bonus
- [ ] Align incentives: setters should be rewarded for quality (bookings that show up and convert), not just volume
- [ ] Closers should be rewarded for revenue, not just close rate (prevents cherry-picking easy deals)

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear decision on whether you are ready for the setter-closer split
- Written role definitions for both setter and closer positions
- Qualification criteria that filter prospects before they reach a closer
- A documented handoff process ensuring closers are prepared for every call
- A compensation structure that aligns incentives with business outcomes

## Coach Notes

Timing matters. Clients who split too early create overhead without enough volume to justify it. Clients who split too late burn out and their close rate drops because they are exhausted from doing everything. The sweet spot is usually when the owner is consistently doing 15+ sales conversations per week and growth is limited by their available time. The first hire should usually be a setter, not a closer. It is easier to find and train someone to qualify and book calls than to close. The owner stays as the closer until the second sales hire. Common mistakes: setters who over-qualify (filtering out good prospects) and closers who blame setters for lead quality rather than improving their own conversion. The feedback loop between roles is what makes the system work. Without it, setter and closer end up in conflict rather than collaboration.
