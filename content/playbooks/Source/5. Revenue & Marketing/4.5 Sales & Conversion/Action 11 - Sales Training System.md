# Sales Training System

## What to Do

Build an onboarding and development system for new salespeople that includes scripts, role-play scenarios, and a 90-day development plan. The goal is to get a new salesperson from zero to competent in 90 days without relying on "shadow me and figure it out." This system makes your sales knowledge transferable so you are not the only person who can sell effectively.

## Why It Matters

Most small businesses hire salespeople and throw them in the deep end. "Here is a phone, here are some leads, go sell." The result is slow ramp-up, inconsistent performance, and high turnover. A structured training system solves all three. It compresses the learning curve from 6-12 months to 90 days. It creates consistency because everyone learns the same framework. And it reduces turnover because people who feel supported and competent stay longer than those who feel lost and failing.

## How to Use This

You need your Diagnostic Call Framework (Action 4), Objection Handling System (Action 6), and Call Recording system (Action 10) in place before building this. Work through the sections below.

---

## Step 1: Document Your Sales Knowledge

- [ ] Write out your complete sales script based on the Diagnostic Call Framework
- [ ] Document your product knowledge: what you sell, who it is for, what problems it solves, pricing, FAQs
- [ ] Create a "competitor cheat sheet" covering the top 3-5 alternatives prospects might consider and how you compare
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Record yourself doing a mock sales call using your framework. This single recording becomes the most valuable training asset you own.
- [ ] Compile 3-5 examples of great calls (from your recordings) that new hires can study

## Step 2: Build Role-Play Scenarios

- [ ] Create 5-7 realistic prospect scenarios covering different industries, objections, and personality types
- [ ] For each scenario, write a one-paragraph brief the role-player uses to "become" the prospect
- [ ] Include at least one scenario per universal objection (price, timing, authority, stall, scepticism)
- [ ] Build in a "difficult prospect" scenario for advanced practice (hostile, dismissive, or constantly interrupting)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Create one role-play scenario based on your most common prospect type. You can use this in hiring interviews to assess sales potential.

## Step 3: Design the 90-Day Development Plan

### Week 1-2: Foundation

- [ ] Product knowledge training: what you sell, how it works, who it helps, results achieved
- [ ] Study the sales script and Diagnostic Call Framework
- [ ] Listen to 10 recorded calls (mix of wins and losses)
- [ ] Shadow 5 live calls with the business owner or senior salesperson

### Week 3-4: Guided Practice

- [ ] Daily role-play sessions (15-20 minutes each) working through all scenarios
- [ ] First supervised live calls with coaching debrief after each one
- [ ] Begin handling inbound enquiries with support available
- [ ] Weekly review against the call scorecard (Action 10)

### Month 2: Supervised Selling

- [ ] Full call volume with weekly call review and coaching
- [ ] Focus on one skill improvement per week
- [ ] Track conversion metrics against team benchmarks
- [ ] Introduce objection handling practice using real scenarios from their calls

### Month 3: Independent Selling

- [ ] Full autonomy with continued weekly review
- [ ] Performance compared against targets with clear feedback
- [ ] Identify whether the new hire is on track, needs additional support, or is not a fit
- [ ] End-of-90-days review: decision to confirm, extend probation, or part ways

---

## Expected Outcome

- A documented sales training package covering scripts, product knowledge, and competitor information
- 5-7 role-play scenarios for practice and assessment
- A structured 90-day onboarding plan with clear milestones
- Faster ramp-up time for new salespeople
- Consistent sales approach across the team

## Coach Notes

The hardest part for business owners is documenting what they do instinctively. They have sold successfully for years but cannot explain their process because it feels intuitive. The recording exercise (recording themselves doing a mock call) forces the tacit knowledge into explicit form. Push for it. Role-play resistance is also common. Adults feel silly pretending to be prospects. Normalise it by doing it yourself first. The 90-day timeline is a guideline, not a rigid rule. Some people ramp faster, some slower. The key is having clear milestones so you know whether someone is on track rather than guessing. If a new hire is not showing improvement by the end of Month 2, it is usually a fit issue rather than a training issue.
