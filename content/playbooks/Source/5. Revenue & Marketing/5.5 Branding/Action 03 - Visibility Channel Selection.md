# Visibility Channel Selection

## What to Do

Map 3-5 channels where your ideal customers spend time and develop a strategic presence on each. Instead of trying to be everywhere and being mediocre at all of it, pick the channels that match your strengths and your audience, then dominate them. This action builds on your authority positioning by deciding where that authority gets distributed.

## Why It Matters

Visibility without strategy is exhausting. Most business owners post sporadically across six platforms, attend networking events they do not enjoy, and dabble in SEO without committing. The result: no platform builds momentum because none gets enough consistent attention. The businesses that grow fastest pick a small number of channels and go deep. They become known in those spaces. The key insight: your customers do not care how many platforms you are on. They care whether you show up consistently in the one or two places they actually look.

## How to Use This

Score each potential channel against five criteria, then build a simple presence plan for your top selections.

---

## Map All Potential Channels

- [ ] **List every channel where your ideal customers might discover you** - Include both online and offline:
  - Social media (LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, X, TikTok, YouTube)
  - Content platforms (blog/SEO, podcast, newsletter, Medium, Substack)
  - Paid advertising (Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads)
  - Referral and partnership channels
  - Events (conferences, local networking, speaking, trade shows)
  - Directories, listings, and industry platforms
  - Community spaces (forums, Slack groups, Facebook groups, Reddit)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Cross off any channel where your ideal customer does not spend time. Be honest. If your customers are CFOs, TikTok is probably not the answer.

## Score Each Channel

For each remaining channel, score 1-5 on these five criteria:

- [ ] **Audience match** - How many of your ideal customers are active here? (1 = very few, 5 = core audience lives here)
- [ ] **Content fit** - Does the channel suit your strengths and your brand promise? (1 = poor fit, 5 = natural match)
- [ ] **Effort required** - How much time and resource to maintain presence? (1 = very high effort, 5 = manageable)
- [ ] **Competition** - How crowded is this channel in your niche? (1 = extremely crowded, 5 = wide open)
- [ ] **Measurability** - Can you track results from this channel? (1 = impossible to measure, 5 = clear metrics available)
- [ ] **Total each channel's score** - Rank from highest to lowest. Your top 3-5 are your visibility channels.

## Build Your Channel Plan

- [ ] **For each selected channel, define your role** - Are you an educator, commentator, entertainer, curator, or connector? Pick one per channel.
- [ ] **Set a minimum viable presence for each** - What is the least you can do consistently? One LinkedIn post per week. One networking event per month. One blog post per fortnight. Define the floor, not the ceiling.
- [ ] **Assign ownership** - Who manages each channel? If you are doing everything yourself, reduce to 2-3 channels maximum. Spreading yourself thin defeats the purpose.
- [ ] **Create a 30-day test period** - Commit to your minimum viable presence for 30 days before judging results. Most people quit channels before they have given them a fair test.
- [ ] **Set review criteria** - After 30 days, what would "working" look like? Leads generated, conversations started, profile views, or engagement? Define this now so you are not guessing later.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A scored list of all potential visibility channels
- 3-5 channels selected based on evidence, not habit
- A clear minimum viable presence defined for each
- Ownership assigned and review criteria set
- A 30-day commitment to test the plan before making changes

## Coach Notes

The hardest part of this action is getting clients to drop channels. They feel guilty about stopping something, even if it produces nothing. Frame it this way: every hour spent on a channel that does not work is an hour stolen from a channel that could.

Watch for clients who pick channels based on what they enjoy rather than where their customers are. Enjoying a platform is a bonus, not a reason. Also watch for clients who pick channels based on what everyone else is doing. The best channel is often the one competitors are ignoring.

If a client has zero current presence anywhere, start with just one channel. Build consistency before adding a second.
