# Authority Positioning Strategy

## What to Do

Identify where and how to demonstrate credibility so that your ideal customers trust you before they ever speak to you. Choose the platforms, topics, and formats that align with where your market pays attention and what they need to believe before buying. Stop trying to be an expert at everything and start owning one specific territory.

## Why It Matters

In a crowded market, the business that is seen as the authority wins. Not the cheapest, not the biggest, not the one with the most features. The one people trust. Authority is not about bragging or self-promotion. It is about consistently showing up with useful, relevant knowledge in the places where your ideal customers are already looking. When someone sees your name associated with a specific topic enough times, you become the default choice when they need help with that thing.

## How to Use This

Work through each section to define your authority territory, choose your primary format, and build a 90-day plan to establish presence.

---

## Define Your Authority Territory

- [ ] **Name the single topic you want to own in your market** - What subject should people automatically associate with your business? This should connect directly to your brand promise.
- [ ] **List 5-7 subtopics within that territory** - These become your content pillars. Enough variety to stay interesting, narrow enough to stay focused.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Google your topic + your industry. Who currently shows up? These are your authority competitors. Study what they do well and where the gaps are.
- [ ] **Identify your unique angle** - What perspective do you bring that others do not? Industry experience, a specific methodology, contrarian opinion, real-world results? This is your differentiator.
- [ ] **Write your "I help [who] with [what] so they can [result]" statement** - This is your authority positioning in one line.

## Choose Your Authority Platforms

- [ ] **List where your ideal customers go to learn and solve problems** - LinkedIn, YouTube, industry publications, podcasts, conferences, trade groups, online communities, Google search.
- [ ] **Rank these by three factors: where you can reach the most ideal customers, where you are most comfortable creating, and where competition for attention is lowest** - The sweet spot is high audience overlap, medium comfort, and low competition.
- [ ] **Select one primary platform and one secondary** - You will go deep on one before adding another. Spreading across five platforms means you build authority on none.
- [ ] **Research the format that works best on your primary platform** - Long-form articles on LinkedIn, short videos on Instagram, detailed guides for SEO, interviews for podcasts. Match the platform's native format.

## Build Your 90-Day Authority Plan

- [ ] **Commit to a publishing frequency you can sustain for 90 days without burning out** - Weekly is ideal. Fortnightly is fine. Monthly is the minimum. Consistency beats volume.
- [ ] **Map your first 8-12 content topics from your subtopic pillars** - You should never sit down wondering "what should I write about?" The topics should already be decided.
- [ ] **Create one cornerstone piece of content** - A definitive guide, framework, or resource that demonstrates your depth on your core topic. This becomes your anchor that everything else links back to.
- [ ] **Identify 2-3 places to contribute as a guest** - Podcasts, publications, events, or communities where you can reach new audiences without building from scratch.
- [ ] **Set a 90-day review date** - What will you measure? Reach, engagement, inbound enquiries, or direct mentions? Pick one lead metric and track it.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clearly defined authority territory with specific subtopics
- One primary platform chosen with a format that matches
- A 90-day content plan with topics already mapped
- Guest contribution opportunities identified
- A cornerstone content piece planned or created

## Coach Notes

The biggest resistance here is clients who want to be known for everything. "I am a business coach" is not an authority position. "I help service businesses fix their cash flow" is. The narrower the territory, the faster authority builds.

Watch for clients who skip straight to tactics (posting on LinkedIn) without defining their territory first. Content without a clear positioning is just noise. Make sure the territory is defined before any content gets created.

If a client says "but my competitors already own that topic," remind them that authority is local and contextual. You do not need to be the world's top expert. You need to be the most visible, most consistent voice for your specific audience.
