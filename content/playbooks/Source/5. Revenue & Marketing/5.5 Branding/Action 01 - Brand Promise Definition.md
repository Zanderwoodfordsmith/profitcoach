# Brand Promise Definition

## What to Do

Clarify the single most important commitment your business makes to every customer. This is not a tagline or a slogan. It is the one result, feeling, or experience that people can count on every time they buy from you. Once defined, this promise becomes the filter for every decision - from what you sell to how you answer the phone.

## Why It Matters

Most businesses have a vague sense of what they stand for, but nothing specific enough to guide real decisions. The result is inconsistency. One team member prioritises speed, another prioritises quality, another prioritises price. Without a clear promise, your brand is whatever each employee happens to feel like delivering that day. Companies like Volvo (safety), FedEx (overnight, guaranteed), and Ritz-Carlton (personalised luxury) built empires on one clear promise that everyone in the business could understand and deliver against. You need the same clarity.

## How to Use This

Work through these sections in order. By the end, you should have a single sentence that captures what your business promises, and a simple test to check whether you are keeping it.

---

## Gather Your Evidence

- [ ] **List the top 5 reasons customers choose you over competitors** - Ask real customers if you can. If not, review testimonials, feedback, and your own experience from sales conversations.
- [ ] **Review the last 10 positive reviews or testimonials** - What patterns emerge? What words come up again and again?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Ask 3 current customers: "What is the one thing you count on us for?" Their language is often better than anything you would write yourself.
- [ ] **List 3-5 things you would never compromise on, even under pressure** - These reveal your real values, not the ones on a poster.
- [ ] **Identify what you do differently from your nearest competitors** - Not better. Different. What would customers miss if you disappeared?

## Draft Your Promise

- [ ] **Write 5-10 versions of a single sentence that completes: "When you work with us, you can always count on..."** - Do not filter. Get quantity first.
- [ ] **Eliminate anything generic** - "Great service," "high quality," and "customer focus" mean nothing because everyone claims them. Cross these out.
- [ ] **Eliminate anything you cannot consistently deliver** - A promise you break is worse than no promise at all.
- [ ] **Circle the 2-3 drafts that feel most true and most distinctive** - True meaning you already deliver this. Distinctive meaning competitors cannot easily claim the same thing.
- [ ] **Test each finalist: Could a new employee understand this well enough to make daily decisions based on it?** - If not, simplify.
- [ ] **Choose one. Write it down as a clean, single sentence.** - This is your Brand Promise.

## Validate Your Promise

- [ ] **Share it with your team and ask: "Does this match what we actually do?"** - If there is a gap between the promise and reality, you either need to change the promise or change the operations.
- [ ] **Check it against 3 recent customer complaints** - Did those complaints happen because the promise was broken? If so, you have identified the right promise but need to deliver it more consistently.
- [ ] **Run the "hard decision" test** - Think of a recent tough business decision. Would this promise have made the right answer obvious? If yes, it is a useful filter. If no, it may be too vague.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A single, clear sentence that captures what customers can always count on from your business
- Evidence that this promise matches what customers actually value
- A filter that makes difficult business decisions simpler
- A foundation that the rest of this playbook builds on - authority positioning, visibility, and brand integrity all flow from this promise

## Coach Notes

Most clients will try to make their brand promise a paragraph. Push them to one sentence. The discipline of choosing one thing is the whole point. If they say "we promise great quality AND fast delivery AND personal service," they have not done the work yet. Help them choose.

Watch for promises that describe what the business does instead of what the customer gets. "We provide bespoke consulting" is a description. "You get a clear plan within 48 hours" is a promise. Keep redirecting toward the customer's experience.

If a client struggles, go back to customer language. The promise is usually hiding in the best testimonials.
