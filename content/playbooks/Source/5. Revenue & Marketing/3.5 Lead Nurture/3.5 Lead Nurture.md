## **What This Is**

Not everyone buys on the first visit. The best prospects often need time, trust, and multiple touchpoints before they are ready to commit. A structured nurture system turns cold leads into warm prospects, and warm prospects into customers who choose you because they already know, like, and trust you.

This playbook is about building relationships at scale. It bridges the gap between someone raising their hand and someone reaching for their wallet. It is the systematic follow-up that most businesses know they should do but struggle to do consistently.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Leads come in and disappear into a black hole. You have no systematic follow-up beyond maybe a single phone call or email. Most prospects go cold because you do not stay in touch. When you do remember to follow up, it is sporadic and generic. You have no idea who is warming up, who is ready to buy, or who has gone cold completely.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have some follow-up sequences but they are inconsistent. Sometimes leads get nurtured, sometimes they do not. Your messaging is generic rather than segmented. You track opens and clicks but do not really know what they mean or what to do with that information. Some leads convert over time but you cannot predict which ones or when.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Every lead enters a structured nurture sequence immediately. Your follow-up is consistent, valuable, and personalised to where they are in their journey. You can see who is engaging, who is warming up, and who is ready for a conversation. Conversion rates from your nurture sequences are predictable. Prospects often reach out to you because your nurturing has built enough trust and urgency for them to act.

---

## **Things to Think About**

Are you tracking the full journey from first contact to sale? Most businesses only measure leads and sales, missing everything in between. The nurture phase is where the real work happens — building trust, overcoming objections, and staying top of mind until they are ready to buy.

Is your follow-up valuable or just persistent? There is a difference between nurturing and nagging. Every touchpoint should give them something useful — insight, tools, case studies, or solutions to problems they did not know they had. If you would not want to receive your own emails, neither do they.

Do you know the difference between hot, warm, and cold leads? Different temperatures need different approaches. A hot lead who just downloaded your pricing guide needs a different message than a cold lead who signed up for a general newsletter six months ago. Segment by behaviour, not just demographics.

How do you handle prospects who go quiet? Every nurture system needs reactivation campaigns for leads who have disengaged. Sometimes people are just busy. A well-timed "we miss you" campaign can bring dormant leads back to life.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Nurture Journey Mapping — Map out the stages prospects go through from first contact to purchase decision. Define what each stage looks like and what they need to hear to move to the next one.

Play 2: Content Cadence System — Build a consistent schedule of valuable touchpoints that keeps you top of mind without overwhelming them. Email sequences, social media, direct mail — whatever channels your prospects actually use.

Play 3: Lead Scoring & Segmentation — Create a simple system to identify who is warming up based on their behaviour. Website visits, email engagement, content downloads — the digital body language that tells you they are getting ready to buy.

Play 4: Reactivation Campaign Builder — Design automated sequences to re-engage leads who have gone cold. Different approaches for different reasons they stopped engaging — too busy, not ready, wrong timing.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Set up a simple 5-email sequence that goes out automatically to every new lead. Educational content first, then social proof, then a soft call-to-action.

This week: Review your CRM or lead list. Identify 10 leads who showed interest but went quiet in the last 90 days. Send them a personal "check-in" email asking how you can help.

This week: Look at your best customers. What questions did they ask or objections did they raise before buying? Turn those into nurture content that addresses those concerns proactively.
