# Nurture Journey Mapping

## What to Do

Map the stages a prospect goes through from the moment they first encounter your business to the point they become a paying client. For each stage, define what the prospect is thinking, what they need to hear, and what action moves them to the next stage. This becomes the blueprint for every piece of nurture content, every email sequence, and every touchpoint you build.

Most businesses treat the gap between "lead captured" and "sale made" as a black box. This tool makes it visible and manageable.

## Why It Matters

Allan Dib's 1-Page Marketing Plan breaks the customer journey into three phases: before, during, and after. The nurture journey lives in the "during" phase, and it is where most businesses lose the highest volume of potential clients. Ryan Deiss calls this the "relationship escalation" - you cannot jump from stranger to customer in one step. Each stage in the journey is a micro-commitment that builds trust and moves the prospect closer to a buying decision. Without a mapped journey, your marketing is a series of disconnected touchpoints that hope for the best.

## How to Use This

Work from your real client data. Think about your last 10-20 clients and how they actually moved from first contact to purchase. The journey you map should reflect reality, not an idealised marketing funnel from a textbook.

---

## Step 1: Define Your Stages

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Identify your typical entry points** - How do prospects first find you? Website, social media, referral, event, direct outreach? List the top three. These are the starting points of your journey.
- [ ] **Map 4-6 stages from awareness to purchase** - A simple version: Aware (they know you exist), Interested (they are consuming your content), Considering (they are evaluating you as an option), Ready (they want to buy and need a reason to act now). Adjust to match your actual sales cycle.
- [ ] **Define what the prospect is thinking at each stage** - At "Aware" they are thinking "Who is this?" At "Considering" they are thinking "Can I trust them to deliver?" Write out the internal questions for each stage.
- [ ] **Define what they need to hear at each stage** - At "Aware" they need proof you understand their problem. At "Considering" they need case studies and social proof. Match the message to the mindset.

## Step 2: Map the Touchpoints

- [ ] **List every current touchpoint you have** - Emails, social posts, blog articles, webinars, calls, meetings, proposals, free resources. Everything that makes contact with a prospect.
- [ ] **Assign each touchpoint to a stage** - Where does this touchpoint sit in the journey? Some will cluster in one stage (usually awareness) while other stages have almost nothing. The gaps are your priorities.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Identify the biggest gap** - Which stage has the fewest touchpoints? That is where prospects are falling out of your funnel. Prioritise building content for that stage first.
- [ ] **Define the transition triggers** - What action or signal shows that a prospect has moved from one stage to the next? Downloading a guide, booking a call, replying to an email, visiting a pricing page. These triggers tell you when someone is warming up.

## Step 3: Design the Experience

- [ ] **Set a timeline for each stage** - How long does a typical prospect spend in each stage? Some journeys take days, others take months. Knowing this shapes how often you make contact and how patient you need to be.
- [ ] **Define the ideal next action at each stage** - What do you want the prospect to do next? Read an article, watch a video, book a call, download a guide? Every touchpoint should have one clear next step.
- [ ] **Plan for drop-off points** - Where do prospects typically disappear? After the first email? Before the sales call? Build specific re-engagement content for those moments.
- [ ] **Document the full journey on one page** - A visual map showing stages, touchpoints, triggers, and timelines. This becomes the reference document for all nurture activity.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear, documented map of the prospect journey from first contact to purchase
- Each stage defined with prospect mindset, key messages, and touchpoints
- Gaps in the current journey identified and prioritised for action
- Transition triggers defined so you know when prospects are moving forward
- A single-page reference document that guides all nurture content creation

## Coach Notes

Most clients will try to map an idealised journey rather than their actual one. Push them to be honest about what really happens. If 80% of their clients come through referrals and skip straight to "Considering," that is the journey to optimise, not a theoretical seven-stage funnel.

The most valuable output of this exercise is usually the gap analysis. Clients almost always discover they have plenty of awareness-stage content but almost nothing for the "Considering" and "Ready" stages. That is where sales are being lost.

If the client has a short sales cycle (under 2 weeks), keep the journey to 3-4 stages. If they have a long cycle (3-6 months), they may need 5-6 stages with more touchpoints in the middle. Match the map to reality.
