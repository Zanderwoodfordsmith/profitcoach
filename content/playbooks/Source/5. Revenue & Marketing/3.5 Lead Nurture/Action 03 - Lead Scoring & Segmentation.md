# Lead Scoring & Segmentation

## What to Do

Create a simple system to identify which prospects are warming up and which have gone cold, based on their behaviour. Every time a prospect visits your website, opens an email, downloads a resource, or engages with your content, they are giving you signals. Lead scoring turns those signals into a number that tells your sales team who to call first. Segmentation groups prospects by characteristics so you can send the right message to the right audience.

You do not need enterprise software for this. A spreadsheet and some basic email tracking will get you 80% of the value.

## Why It Matters

Ryan Deiss's traffic temperature model (cold, warm, hot) is the simplest version of this idea: different people need different messages depending on where they are in the relationship. Sending a sales pitch to someone who just discovered you is like proposing on a first date. Lead scoring prevents that mistake by giving you data on readiness. Allan Dib reinforces that the purpose of nurture is to move people from cold to warm to hot, and scoring is how you measure whether that is actually happening.

## How to Use This

You need access to your email marketing data (open rates, click rates), website analytics (if available), and your CRM or contact list. Start simple and add complexity only when the basic system is working.

---

## Step 1: Define Your Scoring Criteria

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **List the behaviours that signal buying intent** - Email opens, link clicks, website visits, pricing page views, resource downloads, webinar attendance, social media engagement, direct replies, booking requests. Write down every signal you can track.
- [ ] **Assign point values to each behaviour** - Low-intent signals (email open, social like): 1-2 points. Medium-intent signals (link click, resource download, blog visit): 3-5 points. High-intent signals (pricing page view, booking request, reply to email): 8-10 points.
- [ ] **Set scoring thresholds** - Cold: 0-10 points. Warm: 11-25 points. Hot: 26+ points. Adjust based on your sales cycle and data. The thresholds matter less than the consistency of using them.
- [ ] **Add decay** - Points should reduce over time. A website visit from yesterday is more meaningful than one from three months ago. Simple approach: halve all scores every 30 days.

## Step 2: Build Your Segments

- [ ] **Segment by temperature** - Cold, warm, and hot leads get different content and different levels of sales attention. This is the minimum segmentation every business should have.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Segment by source** - Where the lead came from (referral, social, organic, paid, event) tells you something about their mindset and intent. Referral leads are typically warmer than cold traffic. Treat them accordingly.
- [ ] **Segment by interest or need** - If you sell multiple products or services, track which ones each prospect has shown interest in. Someone who downloaded a guide on pricing is a different prospect from someone who watched a video on operations.
- [ ] **Segment by fit** - Not all leads are good leads. If you have qualifying criteria (company size, revenue, industry), tag prospects early so your sales team focuses on the ones most likely to buy and succeed.
- [ ] **Keep segments simple** - 3-5 segments is enough to start. Temperature (cold/warm/hot) plus one additional dimension (source or interest) covers most businesses. Do not build 20 segments that nobody maintains.

## Step 3: Put Scoring to Work

- [ ] **Create a weekly "hot leads" report** - Every Monday, pull a list of prospects who crossed the hot threshold in the past 7 days. This is your sales team's priority call list.
- [ ] **Trigger actions at thresholds** - When a lead crosses from cold to warm, they enter an automated nurture sequence. When they cross from warm to hot, a salesperson gets notified. Define these triggers and automate them.
- [ ] **Send different content to different segments** - Cold leads get educational content. Warm leads get case studies and social proof. Hot leads get direct offers and calls to action. Your Content Cadence System should map to these segments.
- [ ] **Review and calibrate monthly** - Check whether your scoring is accurate by comparing scores to actual conversions. If people who score "hot" are not buying, your point values need adjusting. If people who score "cold" are converting, you are missing signals.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A documented lead scoring system with defined behaviours, point values, and thresholds
- At least three prospect segments receiving tailored content and attention
- A weekly hot leads report driving sales activity
- Automated triggers that move prospects between segments based on behaviour
- Monthly calibration habit that keeps scoring accurate

## Coach Notes

Start with the simplest possible version. A Google Sheet with columns for name, email, last activity date, and a manual temperature tag (cold/warm/hot) is a perfectly valid lead scoring system for a business doing under £1M. Do not let the client get distracted by CRM features they do not need yet.

The biggest win from this exercise is usually the realisation of how many warm leads are being ignored. Most small businesses have prospects who have engaged multiple times but never received a follow-up because nobody was tracking behaviour. That list of warm, neglected leads is immediate revenue opportunity.

If the client has no email marketing or tracking in place, this action depends on the Content Cadence System being set up first. You need touchpoints generating data before you can score anything.
