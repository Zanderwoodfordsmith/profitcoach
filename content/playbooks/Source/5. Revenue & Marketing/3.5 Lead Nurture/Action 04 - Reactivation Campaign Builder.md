# Reactivation Campaign Builder

## What to Do

Design automated sequences to re-engage leads who have gone cold. These are people who showed interest at some point - downloaded a resource, attended an event, had a sales conversation, or engaged with your content - but then stopped responding. A reactivation campaign gives them a reason to come back and a clear path to re-engage.

Most businesses focus all their energy on generating new leads while sitting on a database of old ones who already know them. Reactivation is almost always cheaper and faster than new lead generation.

## Why It Matters

Deiss and Henneberry's Digital Marketing framework positions reactivation as one of the highest-ROI activities in any marketing system. These prospects already cleared the hardest hurdle: they know you exist and they showed some level of interest. The cost of re-engaging them is a fraction of the cost of acquiring a new lead. Dean Jackson's "9-word email" technique has demonstrated that even the simplest reactivation approach can generate responses from leads that have been dormant for months or years. You are not starting from zero with these people. You are restarting a conversation.

## How to Use This

You need your contact database, any available engagement history, and a basic email tool. Work through this process to design 2-3 reactivation campaigns for different scenarios.

---

## Step 1: Identify and Segment Your Cold Leads

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Pull a list of all contacts who have not engaged in 60+ days** - No email opens, no website visits, no replies, no purchases. This is your reactivation pool. For most businesses, it is larger than they expect.
- [ ] **Segment by how they went cold** - Did they drop off after a sales conversation (high intent, something stopped them)? After downloading a resource (early stage, never warmed up)? After being a client (lapsed customer)? Each group needs a different approach.
- [ ] **Segment by original source** - Referral leads who went cold might respond to a personal touch. Ad-generated leads might need a different offer. Match the reactivation approach to how they arrived.
- [ ] **Remove genuinely dead contacts** - Bounced emails, wrong numbers, people who asked to be removed. Clean the list before you start so you are working with real prospects.

## Step 2: Design Your Reactivation Sequences

- [ ] **Build a "simple check-in" sequence for warm drop-offs** - 3 emails over 2 weeks. Email 1: Short, personal, no selling. "Hi [name], are you still looking at [problem you solve]?" Email 2: Share something useful (article, insight, case study). Email 3: Direct offer or invitation to reconnect.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Write a 9-word email** - "[Name], are you still interested in [outcome]?" No formatting, no images, no footer. Just a plain text question. This technique works because it feels personal and requires almost no effort to respond to.
- [ ] **Build a "new value" sequence for early-stage drop-offs** - These prospects never warmed up, so give them a reason to. Share something new: a fresh case study, a new resource, a recent result. 4 emails over 3 weeks, each offering genuine value.
- [ ] **Build a "win-back" sequence for lapsed clients** - Different tone: you have a relationship here. Acknowledge the gap, share what has changed or improved, and make a specific return offer. 3 emails over 2 weeks plus a personal phone call from the account owner.
- [ ] **Include a clear unsubscribe or "not interested" option** - Let people opt out cleanly. This improves your list quality and keeps you compliant. A smaller, engaged list is worth more than a large, unresponsive one.

## Step 3: Launch, Measure, and Iterate

- [ ] **Test with a small batch first** - Send your first reactivation sequence to 50-100 contacts before rolling it out to the full list. Check for deliverability issues, reply patterns, and unsubscribe rates.
- [ ] **Track the right metrics** - Reply rate matters more than open rate for reactivation. A reply (even "not interested right now") is a success because it confirms the contact is real and gives you information.
- [ ] **Set up a response handling process** - When someone replies, who responds and how quickly? Reactivation replies need a fast, personal response. If they wait 48 hours for a reply, you have lost them again.
- [ ] **Schedule reactivation campaigns quarterly** - This is not a one-time exercise. New contacts go cold every month. Run reactivation sequences on a rolling quarterly basis to keep your pipeline active.
- [ ] **Feed results back into your scoring system** - Contacts who re-engage move back from cold to warm in your lead scoring. Contacts who do not respond after two reactivation attempts get moved to a long-term nurture list or removed.

## Expected Outcome

- A segmented list of cold leads ready for reactivation
- 2-3 tailored reactivation sequences designed for different drop-off scenarios
- A tested, repeatable process for re-engaging dormant contacts quarterly
- Response handling procedures so re-engaged leads get prompt, personal follow-up
- An ongoing rhythm that treats reactivation as a standard part of marketing operations

## Coach Notes

Reactivation campaigns are one of the quickest wins in the entire Profit System because the leads already exist. A client with 500 cold contacts who reactivates even 5% has 25 warm conversations without spending a penny on advertising.

The most common mistake is sending a sales pitch as the reactivation email. Cold leads went cold for a reason. Lead with curiosity or value, not a discount. The 9-word email works precisely because it asks a question rather than making an offer.

If a client has never done reactivation before, start with just the simple check-in sequence and the 9-word email. These take 30 minutes to set up and typically generate replies within 48 hours. Use those early wins to build momentum for the more structured sequences.

For lapsed clients, always recommend a phone call alongside the email sequence. The personal touch carries weight with someone who already knows and trusted you.
