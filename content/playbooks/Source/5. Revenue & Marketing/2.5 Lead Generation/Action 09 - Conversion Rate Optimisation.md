# Conversion Rate Optimisation

## What to Do

Test and improve how many website visitors, content viewers, or prospects become actual leads by optimising your landing pages, forms, and calls-to-action. A small improvement in conversion rate has a multiplying effect on your entire lead generation system without increasing traffic or spend.

## Why It Matters

If 100 people visit your website and 2 become leads (2% conversion), doubling your conversion to 4% doubles your leads without spending a penny more on marketing. Most SME websites convert at 1-3%, but well-optimised pages convert at 5-10%+. The difference is not about design or technology - it is about clarity, relevance, and removing friction. Conversion optimisation is the highest-leverage marketing activity because it makes everything else work harder.

## How to Use This

Audit your current conversion points, identify the biggest leaks, and fix them one at a time.

---

## Audit Your Conversion Points

- [ ] List every place where a visitor can become a lead: website contact form, landing pages, social media DMs, phone calls, email sign-ups, booking pages
- [ ] For each, find or estimate the current conversion rate (visitors or viewers / leads captured)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Check your website's contact page right now. Is the form simple (name + email + one question)? Or does it ask for 8 fields that nobody wants to fill in? Reduce to the minimum.
- [ ] Identify your highest-traffic conversion point - this is where optimisation will have the biggest impact

## Common Conversion Killers (Check Each)

- [ ] **Unclear headline:** Does the visitor know within 5 seconds what you do, who you help, and what they should do next?
- [ ] **Too many options:** Does your page give one clear action, or does it offer 5 different things to click?
- [ ] **Weak call-to-action:** Does your CTA tell them what happens next? "Submit" is weak. "Get your free template" is strong.
- [ ] **Too much friction:** Are you asking for too much information? Every additional form field reduces conversion by 5-10%.
- [ ] **No social proof:** Are there testimonials, client logos, or results visible near the conversion point?
- [ ] **Slow page load:** Does your page load in under 3 seconds? Slow pages lose visitors before they even see your offer.
- [ ] **Mobile unfriendly:** Does the page work well on a phone? (50%+ of visitors are on mobile)

## Fix and Test

- [ ] Prioritise: fix the biggest conversion killer first
- [ ] Make one change at a time so you can measure the impact
- [ ] Run each change for 2-4 weeks before evaluating (unless the sample size is very small)
- [ ] Track before and after: what was the conversion rate before the change, and what is it now?
- [ ] If the change improved conversion, keep it and move to the next fix. If not, revert and try something different.

## Ongoing Optimisation

- [ ] Review conversion rates monthly as part of your lead tracking routine
- [ ] Test one new element per month: headline, CTA, form length, testimonial placement, page layout
- [ ] Set a conversion rate target for your main landing page (aim for at least 5% as a starting goal)
- [ ] As you add new lead magnets or landing pages, apply the same principles from the start
- [ ] Document what works so you build a "conversion playbook" for your business over time

---

## Expected Outcome

- All conversion points identified and current rates measured
- The biggest conversion killers identified and fixed
- Measurable improvement in how many visitors become leads
- An ongoing testing habit that continuously improves conversion
- More leads from the same traffic, without increasing marketing spend

## Coach Notes

Conversion optimisation is often overlooked because it is less exciting than launching a new marketing campaign. But the maths is compelling: a client spending £2,000/month on ads with a 2% landing page conversion gets 40 leads. Improving to 4% conversion gives them 80 leads for the same spend. That is equivalent to doubling their ad budget. Start with the simplest fixes first - reducing form fields, clarifying the headline, and adding one testimonial near the CTA. These three changes alone often produce a 30-50% improvement in conversion rate. Connect to Lead Magnets (Action 5) for the content side and Channel Testing (Action 3) for the traffic side.
