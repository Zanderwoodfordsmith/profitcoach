# Content Strategy

## What to Do

Develop a focused content plan that consistently addresses your ideal customer's problems and builds authority in your niche. This is not about posting for the sake of posting. It is about creating content that attracts the right people, demonstrates your expertise, and moves them toward becoming a lead.

## Why It Matters

Random content produces random results. Posting about your holiday one week, sharing an industry article the next, and writing a tip the week after creates no momentum. A content strategy focuses your effort: same audience, same problems, same message, repeated consistently until you own that space in your prospect's mind. The businesses that generate leads through content are not the ones with the best content - they are the ones with the most consistent, focused content.

## How to Use This

Define your content pillars, create a simple publishing rhythm, and commit to 90 days of consistency.

---

## Define Your Content Foundation

- [ ] **Who are you creating content for?** Write a one-sentence description of your ideal reader/viewer (use your ICP from 1.5 Ideal Customer)
- [ ] **What problem do you solve?** List the top 5 problems your ideal customer faces that your business addresses
- [ ] **What is your unique angle?** Why should someone listen to you rather than anyone else on this topic? (Experience, methodology, results, perspective)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Write down the 10 questions clients ask you most often. Each one is a piece of content waiting to be created.

## Choose Your Content Pillars (3-5 Topics)

- [ ] Select 3-5 recurring themes that you will create content about. Every piece should fit within one of these pillars.
- [ ] Each pillar should address a specific problem or aspiration your ideal customer has
- [ ] Example pillars for a business coach: "Cash flow management", "Time as a business owner", "Hiring and team", "Growth strategy", "Owner mindset"
- [ ] Test each pillar: could you create 20+ pieces of content about this topic without running out of things to say?

## Set Your Publishing Rhythm

- [ ] Choose your primary content format: written posts, short videos, long-form articles, podcast, or a mix
- [ ] Set a realistic publishing frequency you can maintain for 90 days (consistency beats volume - 2 great posts per week beats 7 mediocre ones)
- [ ] Create a simple content calendar for the next 4 weeks: topic + pillar + format for each piece
- [ ] Block time in your weekly schedule for content creation (batch creating is more efficient than daily scrambling)
- [ ] Decide who creates the content: you, a team member, or a combination

## Content That Generates Leads (Not Just Likes)

- [ ] Every piece of content should include one of these:
  - A direct call to action ("DM me 'cashflow' for our forecast template")
  - A link to a lead magnet or resource
  - An invitation to a conversation ("If this sounds like your business, let's talk")
  - A demonstration of results that prompts enquiries
- [ ] Create one signature piece of content per month: a deep-dive post, guide, or case study that showcases your expertise
- [ ] Repurpose: every long piece can become 3-5 shorter pieces. One article becomes a LinkedIn post, a tweet thread, an email, and a quote graphic.

## Track and Refine

- [ ] Track which posts get the most engagement and which generate actual leads (these are often different)
- [ ] After 30 days, review: which pillar resonates most? Which format performs best?
- [ ] Double down on what works. Stop doing what does not.
- [ ] At 90 days, assess: is content generating measurable leads? If not, adjust the strategy or the call-to-action.

---

## Expected Outcome

- 3-5 content pillars defined and aligned with your ideal customer's problems
- A realistic publishing rhythm committed to for 90 days
- A 4-week content calendar created
- Every piece of content designed to move readers toward becoming leads
- A tracking system to measure what generates leads, not just engagement

## Coach Notes

Content strategy overwhelm is real. Clients hear "you need to be on LinkedIn, YouTube, email, and have a blog" and freeze. Simplify: one channel, one format, 2-3 times per week. That is enough. The most important thing is consistency over 90 days. Most businesses quit content after 6 weeks because they do not see immediate leads. Content compounds - the first 60 days build authority, leads typically start flowing after 90 days of consistent output. Hold them accountable to the rhythm, not the results, for the first quarter.
