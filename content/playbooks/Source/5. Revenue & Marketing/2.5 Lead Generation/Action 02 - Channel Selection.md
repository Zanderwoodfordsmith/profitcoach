# Channel Selection

## What to Do

Choose 2-3 primary lead generation channels to focus on based on where your ideal customers actually spend time and where you can realistically compete. This is about strategic focus, not spreading thin. Pick your channels, commit to them, and ignore everything else until they are working.

## Why It Matters

You cannot be great at every channel. LinkedIn, Facebook, Google Ads, YouTube, email, networking, SEO, referrals, cold outreach - each one is a full-time job to do well. The businesses that generate consistent leads are not on every channel. They are excellent on 2-3 channels that match their audience, their strengths, and their budget. Channel selection is the strategic decision that determines whether your marketing effort produces results or just produces activity.

## How to Use This

Use your Channel Audit (Action 1) data as the starting point. Then apply the selection criteria below to choose your primary channels.

---

## Selection Criteria

For each potential channel, assess:

- [ ] **Audience presence:** Are your ideal customers actually here? (A LinkedIn strategy is pointless if your customers are tradespeople who do not use LinkedIn)
- [ ] **Proven results:** Has this channel already produced leads for you, or are you hoping it will?
- [ ] **Competitive landscape:** Can you realistically stand out here? (Competing with huge brands on Google Ads might not be viable on a small budget)
- [ ] **Skill and resource fit:** Do you or your team have the skills to execute well on this channel? Can you commit the time it requires?
- [ ] **Cost viability:** Can you afford to invest consistently for 3-6 months before expecting significant returns?
- [ ] **Scalability:** If it works, can you scale it up? (Networking produces great leads but does not scale. Paid ads scale but cost money.)

## Make Your Selection

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: If your Channel Audit showed one channel clearly outperforming the rest, that is your primary channel. Start there.
- [ ] Choose your **Primary Channel** (the one you invest the most time and money in): ___
- [ ] Choose your **Secondary Channel** (your backup and diversification): ___
- [ ] Choose your **Opportunity Channel** (one you are testing with limited investment): ___
- [ ] For each channel, define: what does "doing it well" look like? (Posting frequency, ad budget, outreach volume, etc.)

## Channel Commitment

- [ ] Set a minimum commitment period: 90 days of consistent effort before evaluating results
- [ ] Define weekly activities for each channel (e.g., "3 LinkedIn posts, 10 connection requests, 2 comments on prospect posts")
- [ ] Assign a specific person responsible for each channel (even if that person is you)
- [ ] Set monthly KPIs: leads generated, engagement metrics, cost per lead
- [ ] Agree on what "not working" looks like so you can make a data-driven decision at 90 days

## What to Ignore

- [ ] List the channels you are NOT going to focus on for the next 90 days
- [ ] For each, set a reminder to reassess at the 90-day mark
- [ ] Give yourself permission to say no to new channel ideas for the next 90 days ("That sounds interesting, I will evaluate it at the next review")
- [ ] Unsubscribe from marketing emails and courses about channels you are not using - they create FOMO and distraction

---

## Expected Outcome

- 2-3 channels selected with clear rationale for each choice
- Weekly activity targets defined for each channel
- A 90-day commitment to consistent execution before re-evaluation
- Non-selected channels parked with review dates
- Focused effort that produces results instead of scattered activity that produces noise

## Coach Notes

The hardest part of channel selection is what you decide NOT to do. Owners see competitors on TikTok and panic, or hear a podcast about webinars and want to try it. Your job is to protect their focus. The 90-day commitment is critical - most channels need 8-12 weeks of consistent effort before producing reliable results. Clients who switch channels every month never give any channel enough time to work. Common winning combinations for SMEs: referrals + LinkedIn, SEO + email marketing, or local networking + Google Ads. The best channel combination depends on the business type, audience, and owner's strengths.
