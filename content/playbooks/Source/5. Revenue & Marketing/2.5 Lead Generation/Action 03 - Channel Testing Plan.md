# Channel Testing Plan

## What to Do

Set up simple, structured tests for your selected lead generation channels so you can measure what works, scale the winners, and kill the losers quickly. This is not about gut feel - it is about running small experiments, tracking the numbers, and letting the data decide.

## Why It Matters

Even the right channel can fail if executed poorly. A Channel Testing Plan lets you try different approaches within your chosen channels and find what resonates with your audience. Testing one ad headline against another, one content format against another, or one outreach script against another compounds over time. Small improvements in conversion at each stage multiply across your entire funnel.

## How to Use This

For each of your selected channels (from Action 2), design 2-3 simple tests to run over the next 30-60 days. Keep tests small, measurable, and time-bound.

---

## Design Your Tests

For each primary channel:

- [ ] Identify one variable to test first (headline, offer, audience, format, timing, or call-to-action)
- [ ] Create Version A (your current approach or best guess) and Version B (one change)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Pick the simplest test you can start this week. For example: try a different subject line on your next email, a different hook on your next LinkedIn post, or a different headline on your landing page.
- [ ] Define the success metric: what number will tell you which version won? (Click rate, response rate, lead conversion rate, cost per lead)
- [ ] Set a time frame: how long will you run each test? (Usually 2-4 weeks or until you have enough data)

## Test Framework

- [ ] **Test 1:** Channel: ___ | Variable: ___ | Version A: ___ | Version B: ___ | Metric: ___ | Duration: ___
- [ ] **Test 2:** Channel: ___ | Variable: ___ | Version A: ___ | Version B: ___ | Metric: ___ | Duration: ___
- [ ] **Test 3:** Channel: ___ | Variable: ___ | Version A: ___ | Version B: ___ | Metric: ___ | Duration: ___
- [ ] Run only one test per channel at a time (testing multiple variables simultaneously makes it impossible to know what caused the change)

## Track and Evaluate

- [ ] Record results weekly: what happened with each test?
- [ ] At the end of each test period, declare a winner based on the data
- [ ] Keep the winner, discard the loser, and design the next test
- [ ] Document what you learned: build a "what works for us" reference over time
- [ ] After 90 days of testing, you should have a clear picture of what works on each channel

## Scaling Winners

- [ ] Once a test produces a clear winner, increase investment: more budget, more time, more frequency
- [ ] Track whether results hold as you scale (sometimes what works small breaks at scale)
- [ ] Set a rule: only scale after 2+ weeks of consistent results
- [ ] When a channel stops producing despite good execution, it may be saturated - test a new approach before abandoning it

## Expected Outcome

- Structured tests running on each of your primary channels
- Data-driven decisions about what works and what does not
- Continuous improvement in lead generation performance over time
- A documented "playbook" of what works for your specific business and audience
- Confidence that your marketing spend is being optimised, not guessed

## Coach Notes

Testing discipline separates businesses that grow from businesses that stay stuck. Most SME owners try something once, decide it "did not work," and move on. Push for proper tests: enough volume, enough time, and only one variable changed. The biggest mistake is testing too many things at once and learning nothing. Start with the highest-impact variable - usually the headline/hook/offer, not the colour of the button. For clients new to testing, start with just one simple A/B test and build the habit.
