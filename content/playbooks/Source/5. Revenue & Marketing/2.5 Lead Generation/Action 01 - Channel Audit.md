# Channel Audit

## What to Do

Review every way leads currently find your business, measure which channels are actually working, and eliminate the ones that drain time without delivering results. This gives you a clear, data-driven picture of your current lead generation so you can double down on winners and stop wasting effort on losers.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners are spread across too many channels. A bit of social media here, some networking there, occasional ads, maybe a blog post when they remember. The result: mediocre results from everything and great results from nothing. A channel audit reveals the truth. Usually 1-2 channels produce 80% of your leads and the rest are noise. Once you know which is which, you can redirect all that scattered effort into what actually works.

## How to Use This

Look at the last 3-6 months of data. If you do not have formal tracking, reconstruct from memory, your CRM, your inbox, or by asking new clients "how did you find us?"

---

## Inventory Your Channels

- [ ] List every channel that has generated at least one lead in the last 6 months:
  - Referrals / word of mouth
  - Website organic search (SEO)
  - Social media (list each platform separately)
  - Paid advertising (list each platform)
  - Email marketing
  - Content marketing (blog, podcast, YouTube)
  - Networking events / speaking
  - Partnerships / joint ventures
  - Directories / listings
  - Cold outreach (email, phone, LinkedIn)
  - Other: ___
- [ ] For each channel, estimate or count: how many leads per month?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Ask your last 5 new clients "How did you first hear about us?" The answers often reveal your best channel.

## Measure Each Channel

For each active channel:

- [ ] **Lead volume:** How many leads per month (average over last 3-6 months)?
- [ ] **Lead quality:** What percentage of leads from this channel become paying customers?
- [ ] **Cost:** What do you spend on this channel monthly (include your time at your hourly rate)?
- [ ] **Cost per lead:** Total cost / number of leads
- [ ] **Cost per customer:** Total cost / number of customers acquired
- [ ] **Time investment:** How many hours per week do you or your team spend on this channel?

## Rank and Decide

- [ ] Rank channels by cost per customer (lowest = best efficiency)
- [ ] Rank channels by lead quality (highest conversion rate = best quality)
- [ ] Rank channels by volume (most leads = best scale)
- [ ] Identify your top 2-3 channels that score well across multiple rankings
- [ ] Identify channels that score poorly on all three: these are candidates for elimination
- [ ] For each channel, decide: **Double down** (invest more), **Maintain** (keep current effort), **Reduce** (scale back), or **Eliminate** (stop completely)

## Action Plan

- [ ] Eliminate or pause the bottom-performing channels this week - free up the time
- [ ] Redirect freed-up time and budget to your top 2-3 channels
- [ ] Set up proper tracking for your top channels if it does not already exist (so the next audit is data-driven, not estimated)
- [ ] Schedule a channel audit every 6 months to stay focused on what works

---

## Expected Outcome

- A complete inventory of every lead generation channel with performance data
- Your top 2-3 channels identified with clear evidence of why they work
- Underperforming channels eliminated or reduced
- Time and budget redirected to the highest-performing channels
- A tracking system in place for future measurement

## Coach Notes

The most common finding: referrals are the best channel but get zero systematic effort, while social media gets hours of attention but generates few actual leads. Challenge clients to be honest about time spent vs. results produced. The hardest part is eliminating channels - owners feel like they should be everywhere. Push back: "Being average on 7 channels is worse than being excellent on 2." The channel audit is the foundation for everything else in this playbook. Do it first.
