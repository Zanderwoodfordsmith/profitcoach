# Weekly Lead Targets

## What to Do

Calculate how many leads you need each week to hit your revenue goals and set up a simple system to track progress against those targets. This turns "I need more leads" (vague) into "I need 12 leads per week" (specific and measurable).

## Why It Matters

Without a lead target, you cannot tell whether your marketing is working until the revenue shows up (or does not) weeks or months later. A weekly lead target gives you an early warning system. If you need 12 leads per week and you are getting 6, you know NOW that you will miss your revenue target in 60-90 days - with enough time to do something about it. This is one of the simplest and most powerful metrics in your business.

## How to Use This

Work backwards from your revenue goal to calculate the exact number of weekly leads you need.

---

## The Calculation

- [ ] **Step 1: Monthly revenue target.** What monthly revenue do you need to hit your annual goal? £___/month
- [ ] **Step 2: Average deal value.** What is the average value of a new customer? £___
- [ ] **Step 3: New customers needed per month.** Monthly target / Average deal value = ___ customers per month
- [ ] **Step 4: Conversion rate.** What percentage of leads become customers? ___% (If you do not know, use 10-20% as a starting estimate)
- [ ] **Step 5: Monthly leads needed.** Customers needed / Conversion rate = ___ leads per month
- [ ] **Step 6: Weekly lead target.** Monthly leads / 4.3 = ___ leads per week

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Do this calculation right now. Write the weekly number on a Post-It and stick it where you see it every day.

## Example

If your monthly revenue target is £50,000, your average deal is £5,000, and your conversion rate is 20%:
- 50,000 / 5,000 = 10 customers needed
- 10 / 0.20 = 50 leads needed per month
- 50 / 4.3 = ~12 leads per week

## Set Up Tracking

- [ ] Add your weekly lead target to your Lead Tracking System (Action 7)
- [ ] Every Friday, count new leads this week and compare to target
- [ ] Use a simple traffic light: Green (on or above target), Amber (within 25% of target), Red (more than 25% below)
- [ ] If you are Red for 2 consecutive weeks, treat it as urgent: diagnose the cause and adjust your lead generation activities immediately

## Refine Over Time

- [ ] After 3 months of tracking, replace estimated conversion rates with your actual numbers
- [ ] Recalculate the lead target with real data - it may change significantly
- [ ] If your conversion rate improves (through better sales process or lead quality), your lead target drops
- [ ] If your average deal value increases (through pricing or upselling), your lead target drops
- [ ] Review and adjust quarterly alongside your revenue goals

---

## Expected Outcome

- A specific weekly lead target calculated from your revenue goals
- A simple tracking system showing whether you are on pace each week
- Early warning when lead flow drops below what is needed
- A clear connection between marketing activity and revenue outcomes
- A number your whole team can rally around and be accountable for

## Coach Notes

This is one of the most impactful calculations you can do in a coaching session. Most clients have never worked backwards from revenue to leads. The number is often either surprisingly manageable ("I only need 8 leads a week?") or surprisingly high ("I need 40 leads a week and I'm getting 5?"). Either way, it creates clarity. If the number seems unreachable, look at the levers: can you improve conversion rate? Increase average deal value? Both are often easier than dramatically increasing lead volume. Review this monthly and celebrate weeks when the target is hit.
