# Referral System

## What to Do

Build a simple, repeatable system to generate referrals from existing customers, past clients, and your network. Referrals are the highest-quality lead source most businesses completely ignore. This action turns "I get referrals sometimes" into "I systematically generate referrals every month."

## Why It Matters

Referred leads convert at 3-5x the rate of cold leads, spend more, and stay longer. Despite this, most business owners leave referrals to chance - they hope happy clients will mention them but never actually ask. A referral system does not need to be complex. It needs to be consistent. When you make asking for referrals a habit and make it easy for people to refer, the volume increases dramatically.

## How to Use This

Design your referral system, then implement it over the next 30 days.

---

## Build the System

### When to Ask

- [ ] Identify the natural "ask moments" in your client journey: when clients are happiest with your work (after a win, after a deliverable, at a milestone, during a renewal conversation)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Contact 3 of your happiest current clients this week and ask: "Is there anyone you know who might benefit from what we do?" That is a referral system in its simplest form.
- [ ] Set triggers: after every positive client interaction, ask. Make it reflexive, not random.

### How to Ask

- [ ] Script your referral request (keep it natural, not salesy):
  - "You mentioned you found [specific result] really valuable. Do you know anyone else facing a similar challenge?"
  - "We are looking to work with more businesses like yours. Who in your network might be a good fit?"
  - "I have space for [X] new clients this quarter. If you know anyone who could benefit, I would love an introduction."
- [ ] Make it specific: instead of "anyone who needs help," say "business owners doing £200K+ who are struggling with [specific problem]"
- [ ] Offer to make it easy: "I can send you a short message you could forward, or I'm happy for you to just share my name and number"

### What to Offer

- [ ] Decide whether to offer a referral incentive:
  - **No incentive:** Works well in professional services where the referral itself is a favour between trusted contacts
  - **Reciprocal value:** Offer something valuable to the referrer (a free session, a discount on future work, access to an exclusive resource)
  - **Financial incentive:** A commission or gift for successful referrals (works best for product businesses or structured programmes)
- [ ] Whatever you choose, make the offer clear and consistent

### How to Follow Up

- [ ] When you receive a referral, contact them within 24 hours (speed matters - the warm introduction cools quickly)
- [ ] Always reference the referrer: "Sarah mentioned you might be looking at improving your cash flow management"
- [ ] Update the referrer on what happened (within appropriate confidentiality): "I spoke with James - great connection, thank you"
- [ ] Regardless of outcome, thank the referrer personally

## Systematise It

- [ ] Create a referral tracking list: who referred whom, when, and what happened
- [ ] Add "ask for referral" as a step in your client delivery process (not a one-off effort, a built-in habit)
- [ ] Set a monthly target: [X] referral asks per month
- [ ] Review monthly: how many asks, how many referrals received, how many converted?
- [ ] Send a quarterly thank-you to your top referrers (handwritten note, small gift, or simply a genuine message)

---

## Expected Outcome

- A repeatable referral process built into your client journey
- A scripted, natural way to ask for referrals at the right moments
- A clear incentive structure (even if the incentive is simply gratitude)
- Monthly referral targets being tracked and hit
- A growing source of high-quality, pre-qualified leads from trusted introductions

## Coach Notes

Referral reluctance is almost universal. Clients say "I do not want to seem pushy." Reframe: if your service genuinely helps people, not asking for referrals is doing their network a disservice. The key insight: the best time to ask is right after a win, when the client is most delighted. Also push for specificity in the ask - "Do you know any business owners?" is too vague. "Do you know any business owners doing £500K+ who are frustrated by their cash flow?" gives the referrer a specific person to think of. Track referrals carefully - they often become your highest-ROI lead source by far.
