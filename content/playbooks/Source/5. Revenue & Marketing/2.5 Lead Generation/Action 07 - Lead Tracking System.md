# Lead Tracking System

## What to Do

Set up simple tracking so you know exactly how many leads each channel generates, what they cost, and which ones convert to paying customers. This does not require expensive software. A well-structured spreadsheet beats a fancy CRM that nobody uses.

## Why It Matters

Without tracking, your marketing is a black box. You spend money, you do activities, and customers appear - but you have no idea which activities produced which customers. Tracking connects the dots: this channel produced this lead, this lead became this customer, this customer generated this revenue. Once you can see those connections, you can invest in what works and stop wasting money on what does not.

## How to Use This

Start with a simple system. You can upgrade later once the habit is established.

---

## Setup

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Create a spreadsheet with these columns: Date, Lead Name, Source (how they found you), Status (New / Contacted / Meeting Booked / Proposal Sent / Won / Lost), Value (if won), Notes
- [ ] Add every current lead and opportunity to the tracker
- [ ] Set up a "source" dropdown with your active channels: Referral, Website, LinkedIn, Google Ads, Email, Networking, Cold Outreach, Other
- [ ] Make this your single source of truth for leads - everything goes in here

## Daily/Weekly Habits

- [ ] Add every new enquiry or lead to the tracker the day it comes in (takes 30 seconds per lead)
- [ ] Update statuses as leads progress through your pipeline
- [ ] Every Friday, spend 5 minutes reviewing: How many new leads this week? From which channels? What stage is each at?
- [ ] Flag any lead that has been in the same status for more than 2 weeks - it needs attention or a decision

## Monthly Metrics

At the end of each month, calculate:

- [ ] **Total new leads** this month
- [ ] **Leads by source:** How many from each channel?
- [ ] **Conversion rate:** What percentage of leads became customers?
- [ ] **Conversion rate by source:** Which channel produces the highest-quality leads?
- [ ] **Average time to close:** How long from first contact to paying customer?
- [ ] **Cost per lead by channel:** Total channel cost / leads from that channel
- [ ] **Cost per customer by channel:** Total channel cost / customers from that channel

## Use the Data

- [ ] Compare this month to last month: are lead numbers improving, declining, or flat?
- [ ] Identify your best channel by cost per customer (not just volume - quality matters)
- [ ] Identify bottlenecks in your pipeline: where are leads getting stuck?
- [ ] Share the numbers with your coach or accountability partner monthly
- [ ] Adjust your marketing spend and effort based on what the data shows, not what you feel

---

## Expected Outcome

- A single, simple system tracking every lead from source to outcome
- Weekly visibility on pipeline health
- Monthly metrics that show what is working and what is not
- Data-driven decisions about where to invest marketing time and money
- A foundation for more sophisticated tracking as the business grows

## Coach Notes

Start simple. A Google Sheet beats a CRM for most businesses under £1M if the alternative is a CRM that nobody uses. The critical habit is adding every lead the day it arrives and updating statuses weekly. If the client says "I will catch up on the tracker later," it will never happen. Build the habit before upgrading the tool. The most powerful number: cost per customer by channel. When a client sees they are paying £500 per customer from paid ads but £0 per customer from referrals (which they never systematically pursue), the strategic implication is obvious.
