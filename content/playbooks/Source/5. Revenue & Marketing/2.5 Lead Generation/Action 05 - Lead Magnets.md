# Lead Magnets

## What to Do

Create one or more valuable resources that prospects exchange their contact details for. A lead magnet bridges the gap between "someone who is interested" and "someone whose details I have." It should solve a specific problem, demonstrate your expertise, and make the prospect want to learn more.

## Why It Matters

Content creates awareness. Lead magnets create leads. Without a lead magnet, your content marketing is just brand building - nice but hard to measure. A good lead magnet captures contact details so you can follow up, nurture, and eventually convert. It also pre-qualifies prospects: someone who downloads your "Cash Flow Forecast Template" is signalling they have a cash flow problem you can solve.

## How to Use This

Choose a lead magnet format, create it, and set up a simple delivery mechanism. Start with one. You can add more later based on what works.

---

## Choose Your Lead Magnet

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Think about the tool, template, or checklist you wish you had when you started. That is probably a great lead magnet.
- [ ] Select a format that matches your audience and your strengths:
  - **Checklist or cheat sheet** (quick to consume, easy to create - great first lead magnet)
  - **Template or worksheet** (practical, high perceived value)
  - **Calculator or assessment** (interactive, highly engaging)
  - **Short guide or PDF** (5-10 pages max, focused on one problem)
  - **Video training or webinar replay** (good for demonstrating expertise)
  - **Swipe file or example collection** (saves the prospect time)

## Design for Conversion

- [ ] Focus on ONE specific problem your ideal customer has - not a broad topic
- [ ] The title should promise a clear outcome: "The 13-Week Cash Flow Forecast Template" not "Guide to Finance"
- [ ] It should deliver value in under 15 minutes of the prospect's time - if it takes longer, they will not use it
- [ ] Include a subtle bridge to your paid offering: "This template covers the basics. For a full diagnostic of your business finances, [link/CTA]"
- [ ] Make it look professional: clean design, your branding, well-formatted

## Create and Launch

- [ ] Create the lead magnet content
- [ ] Set up a simple landing page with: headline, 3-4 bullet points of what they will get, email capture form, and a clear button
- [ ] Set up automatic email delivery: when someone submits their details, they immediately receive the resource
- [ ] Create a thank-you page that appears after sign-up (this is a great place for a secondary CTA - book a call, join a community, etc.)
- [ ] Test the entire flow yourself before promoting it

## Promote and Measure

- [ ] Add the lead magnet link to your email signature, social media bio, and website
- [ ] Reference it in your content: "I created a free [resource] that helps with exactly this - link in comments"
- [ ] Consider running a small paid ad to the landing page to test conversion ($5-10/day for 2 weeks)
- [ ] Track: landing page visits, sign-up rate, email delivery rate, and how many sign-ups become leads or customers
- [ ] If conversion rate is below 20%, test a different headline. If it is above 30%, the magnet is working well.

---

## Expected Outcome

- One lead magnet created, designed, and live
- A landing page and automated email delivery system set up
- The lead magnet promoted across your content and channels
- Contact details being captured from interested prospects
- A measurable conversion path from content viewer to captured lead

## Coach Notes

The biggest mistake with lead magnets: making them too broad or too long. "The Ultimate Guide to Running a Business" is too generic. "The 5-Minute Daily Cash Check for Business Owners" is specific and actionable. Push clients toward the narrowest, most practical option. The lead magnet does not need to be perfect - it needs to be useful. A simple, well-targeted checklist outperforms a beautifully designed 40-page guide that nobody reads. Also ensure the follow-up sequence is in place - capturing an email and doing nothing with it wastes the entire effort. Connect to Lead Nurture (3.5) for what happens after capture.
