# Objection Playbook

## What to Do

Document the predictable objections your ideal customers raise before buying, then build a prepared response for each one. This turns sales from improvisation into a repeatable system where you handle concerns before they become deal-breakers.

## Why It Matters

Every market has standard objections — and price is almost never the real one. Cialdini's research on influence shows that objections are usually about risk, trust, or timing, not money. The business owner who says "it's too expensive" usually means "I'm not confident this will work for me." When you know the real objection, you can address it proactively in your marketing and sales conversations — before the prospect even raises it. That is the difference between closing 2 in 10 and closing 5 in 10.

## How to Use This

Collect objections from real sales conversations, then build your response for each. This document should live next to your desk and get updated every time you hear a new objection.

---

## Collect the Objections

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **List every reason a prospect has said no or hesitated in the last 6 months** — Even vague ones like "I need to think about it." Write them all down without filtering.
- [ ] **Group them into categories** — Most objections fall into 5 buckets:
  - **Price** — "It's too expensive" / "I can't afford it right now"
  - **Timing** — "Not the right time" / "Maybe next quarter"
  - **Trust** — "How do I know this will work?" / "I've been burned before"
  - **Authority** — "I need to check with my partner/accountant/team"
  - **Need** — "I'm not sure I need this" / "We're managing OK"
- [ ] **Identify your top 3 most common objections** — These are the ones you need bulletproof responses for. The rest can be handled more loosely.

## Build Your Responses

For each of your top 3 objections:

- [ ] **Name the real concern behind the words** — "Too expensive" usually means "I don't see enough value to justify the cost." "Not the right time" usually means "This isn't painful enough yet." Identify the real objection underneath the polite version.
- [ ] **Write a response that addresses the real concern, not the surface words** — Do not argue with the objection. Acknowledge it, then redirect to the real issue. Example: "I completely understand. Before we talk about the investment, can I ask — if the cost were not a factor, would this be the right solution for you?" If yes, the objection is value communication, not price.
- [ ] **Add proof that reduces the risk** — Case studies, testimonials, guarantees, or data that answers "will this work for someone like me?" The more specific the proof, the more powerful. "We helped a similar business increase revenue by 40% in 6 months" beats "our clients love us."
- [ ] **Write a one-line version for marketing** — Address the objection before they raise it. Example in marketing copy: "You might be thinking 'I've tried consultants before and nothing changed.' Here's why this is different..."

## Integrate Into Your Sales Process

- [ ] **Address the top 3 objections proactively in your sales conversation** — Do not wait for them to raise it. Bring it up yourself: "Most business owners I speak to have two concerns at this point..." This demonstrates confidence and removes the awkwardness.
- [ ] **Add objection-handling to your marketing content** — FAQ sections, email sequences, social proof placement — all designed to address concerns before the sales call happens.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Update your proposal or sales page to pre-empt your #1 objection** — Whatever concern comes up most often, address it directly in your sales materials. If it is trust, add case studies. If it is timing, add urgency. If it is price, add ROI calculation.

## Expected Outcome

- A documented playbook of objections and prepared responses you can use in every sales conversation
- Proactive objection handling in your marketing that reduces resistance before the sales call
- Higher close rates because concerns are addressed rather than ignored
- A living document that improves every time you hear a new objection
- Confidence in sales conversations because you have heard it all before

## Coach Notes

Role-play objection handling in coaching sessions. Most clients know the right response intellectually but freeze when a real prospect pushes back. Practice makes the response feel natural rather than scripted.

If a client gets the same objection repeatedly and their response is not working, the problem is usually their offer, not their sales technique. If every prospect says "too expensive," the value communication or the value itself needs work — loop back to Value Definition.

This is a living document. After every lost deal, the client should add the objection and their planned response for next time. Over 6 months, this becomes one of their most valuable business assets.
