# Build Your Leadership Potential Matrix

## What to Do

Create a structured assessment to identify who in your team has the judgement, character, and capability to become a leader. This goes beyond current job performance. A brilliant technician isn't necessarily a future leader. You're looking for people who think beyond their own role, take responsibility without being asked, and make good decisions under pressure.

The output is a simple matrix plotting each person's current performance against their leadership potential.

## Why It Matters

Most businesses promote based on technical skill. The best salesperson becomes sales manager. The best developer becomes tech lead. And half the time, it's a disaster - you lose a great specialist and gain a mediocre manager. A leadership potential matrix separates "good at their job" from "could lead others." It prevents costly promotion mistakes and ensures you're investing development time in the right people.

## How to Use This

Assess each team member against the criteria below, then plot them on a 2x2 grid. Be honest - this is a private working document, not a public ranking.

---

## Leadership Potential Assessment

- [ ] **List every team member** - Include everyone, even those you wouldn't initially consider for leadership. Sometimes potential hides in unexpected places.
- [ ] **Rate current performance (1-5)** - Based on their actual output in their current role. Are they delivering what's expected, exceeding it, or falling short? Use evidence, not impressions.
- [ ] **Assess judgement quality** - When this person faces an ambiguous situation, do they generally make sensible decisions? Do they weigh trade-offs or jump to conclusions? Rate 1-5.
- [ ] **Assess ownership mentality** - Do they take responsibility for outcomes, or just for tasks? When something goes wrong in their area, do they fix it or wait to be told? Rate 1-5.
- [ ] **Assess people influence** - Do others naturally follow this person? Do they bring people together or create friction? Can they persuade without relying on authority? Rate 1-5.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Ask yourself "who would I trust to run things if I was away for two weeks?"** - The names that come to mind are your starting point for leadership potential. The gap between those names and your current management team tells you where to focus.
- [ ] **Assess learning agility** - When faced with something new, do they figure it out or freeze? Do they seek feedback and adjust, or defend and resist? Leaders need to keep growing. Rate 1-5.
- [ ] **Assess emotional steadiness** - How do they handle pressure, conflict, and uncertainty? Leaders who spiral under stress create chaos. Leaders who stay calm create confidence. Rate 1-5.
- [ ] **Plot the 2x2 matrix** - X-axis: Current performance (low to high). Y-axis: Leadership potential (low to high). Place each person on the grid.
- [ ] **Identify your "high potential" quadrant** - Top right: high performance AND high leadership potential. These are your priority development candidates.
- [ ] **Watch for the "hidden gems"** - Top left: high potential but currently underperforming. They might be in the wrong role, bored, or lacking development. Worth investigating before writing them off.
- [ ] **Be cautious about the "star performers"** - Bottom right: high performance but low leadership potential. Brilliant specialists. Promote them into leadership and you may lose what makes them great. Consider senior individual contributor paths instead.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Share your assessment with a trusted colleague or advisor** - Get a second opinion. Your blind spots are real. Someone who sees the team differently can challenge your ratings.

---

## Expected Outcome

- You have a clear, evidence-based view of leadership potential across your team
- Promotion decisions are based on leadership capability, not just technical performance
- You know exactly who to invest development time and resources in
- Hidden potential gets surfaced before it walks out the door
- You avoid the common mistake of promoting great doers into bad leaders

## Coach Notes

This is a sensitive exercise. Handle it privately. Never share the full matrix with the team - it would create politics and resentment. Use it as a working tool between owner and coach to guide development conversations and succession planning. Reassess every 6-12 months. People grow. Some people who show low potential now may develop rapidly with the right support. Others who look promising may plateau. The matrix is a snapshot, not a sentence.
