# Create a Leadership Development Plan

## What to Do

Build an individualised development path for each emerging leader covering the four core areas: communication, decision-making, conflict resolution, and strategic thinking. This isn't a generic training programme. Each person gets a plan tailored to their specific gaps, strengths, and the leadership role they're growing into.

## Why It Matters

"Develop your leaders" sounds obvious. But without a specific plan, development becomes a vague intention that never happens. People attend a workshop, feel inspired for a week, and go back to old habits. A structured plan with specific activities, timelines, and milestones turns intention into progress. It also shows your emerging leaders that their development is a priority, not an afterthought.

## How to Use This

Use the potential matrix and 360 feedback to identify each person's priorities. Then build a plan using the framework below. Review progress monthly.

---

## Development Plan Framework

- [ ] **Start with their biggest gap** - From their assessment and 360 feedback, identify the one area that would make the most difference if they improved. That's where the plan starts. Not four areas at once. One.
- [ ] **Set a 90-day development goal for that area** - Specific and observable. "Improve communication" is too vague. "Run the weekly team meeting solo for 8 consecutive weeks and collect team feedback" is a development goal.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Pair the goal with a real business challenge** - Don't create artificial exercises. Find a real project, conversation, or decision that lets them practice the skill in context. Learning by doing always beats learning by studying.

## Communication Development

- [ ] **Practice: Lead one team meeting per week** - Running meetings builds confidence in public communication, agenda management, and facilitation.
- [ ] **Practice: Deliver feedback to a team member (with coaching)** - Have the emerging leader prepare feedback, rehearse it with you, deliver it, and debrief afterwards.
- [ ] **Practice: Present a recommendation to you or the leadership team** - Forces them to structure their thinking, anticipate questions, and communicate concisely.

## Decision-Making Development

- [ ] **Practice: Make one decision per week that would normally come to you** - Start with low-risk decisions and gradually increase stakes. Debrief each decision: what did you consider? What would you do differently?
- [ ] **Practice: Write a decision memo** - For one significant decision, have them write: the decision, the options considered, the trade-offs, and their recommendation. This builds rigour.
- [ ] **Practice: Handle an ambiguous situation without clear answers** - Give them a real business problem with no obvious right answer. See how they navigate uncertainty.

## Conflict Resolution Development

- [ ] **Practice: Observe you handling a difficult conversation, then debrief** - Model the behaviour first. Discuss what you did, why, and what you were thinking.
- [ ] **Practice: Handle a minor team disagreement independently** - With your support available but not in the room. Debrief afterwards.
- [ ] **Study: Learn one conflict framework** - Something simple like "describe the behaviour, explain the impact, agree the change." Give them a tool, not just encouragement.

## Strategic Thinking Development

- [ ] **Practice: Join one strategic discussion or planning session** - Let them observe how decisions are made at a higher level. Ask for their input.
- [ ] **Practice: Analyse a competitor or market trend and present findings** - Forces them to look beyond day-to-day operations and think about the bigger picture.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Ask them "what would you do if you were running this area?" once per month** - This single question shifts their mindset from executor to leader faster than any training programme.

## Review and Adjust

- [ ] **Monthly development check-in (30 minutes)** - What have they practiced? What's improved? What's still hard? Adjust the plan based on real progress.
- [ ] **Quarterly milestone review** - Are they on track for the 90-day goal? Set the next one.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Each emerging leader has a specific, practical development plan
- Development happens through real work, not just training courses
- Progress is visible and measurable within 90 days
- Emerging leaders feel supported and invested in
- You build a bench of capable leaders ready for increasing responsibility

## Coach Notes

The biggest risk is overloading the plan. Most owners want to develop all four areas simultaneously. Push back. One area per quarter is plenty. Deep development in one skill beats shallow exposure to four. Also watch for the "too busy to develop" trap. Development activities should be woven into regular work, not added on top. If someone is too busy to run one meeting per week as a development exercise, the issue is workload, not development. Address that first.
