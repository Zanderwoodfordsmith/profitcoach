## **What This Is**

Your legacy is not what you build yourself. It is what others can build without you. Developing leaders means turning your best people into capable decision-makers who can own outcomes, solve problems, and run parts of the business independently.

This is about creating leadership depth beyond the founder. Strong organisations have multiple people who can think strategically, manage through others, and maintain standards when the owner steps back. It strengthens resilience, improves succession readiness, and unlocks performance stability across the business.

This playbook covers the full journey: identifying who has leadership potential, intentionally developing their capability, and gradually expanding their authority. It is not about promoting everyone or creating titles for the sake of it. It is about building genuine leadership capacity in the people who have earned it.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You are the only person who can make decisions or solve complex problems. Team members constantly bring you issues they should handle themselves. When you go away, things either wait for your return or fall apart without your input. You have titles like "manager" but they are just senior doers, not genuine leaders who can think and act independently.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have identified a few people with leadership potential but their development is informal and inconsistent. Some team members can handle more responsibility, others still need constant direction. You delegate authority occasionally but often step back in when things get difficult. There is emerging leadership capability but it is not systematic or reliable.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You have a clear pipeline of leadership talent developing at different stages. Your best people can make decisions within their areas without checking with you first. They handle problems, manage conflict, and maintain standards independently. When you delegate responsibility, it stays delegated because they have the capability to own it. The business can run and grow through the strength of multiple leaders, not just you.

## **Things to Think About**

Who demonstrates good judgement when things go wrong? Leadership potential shows up most clearly under pressure. Look for the people who stay calm, think clearly, and make sensible decisions when everything else is chaos. Those are your future leaders, not necessarily your highest performers in normal conditions.

Is leadership development happening by accident or by design? Most SMEs hope leadership emerges naturally. It rarely does. The people with potential need structured mentorship, deliberate skill-building, and increasing responsibility over time. If you are not being intentional about it, you are leaving leadership development to chance.

Are you creating decision-makers or permission-seekers? There is a difference between delegating tasks and delegating authority. True leaders need to be trained to think independently, weigh options, and make judgement calls. If your people still come to you for every decision, you are not developing leaders — you are developing better assistants.

What would happen if your strongest leader left tomorrow? This is the succession depth test. If losing one key person would create a leadership vacuum, you have not built deep enough capability. Strong organisations have multiple people who can step up, not just one obvious successor.

How are you measuring leadership growth? Leadership development needs clear milestones and measurement. What specific capabilities are you building? How do you know when someone is ready for more responsibility? Without measurement, development becomes wishful thinking.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

This is a larger playbook organised into three sections. When you open it in the Profit System, you will find:

### **Identifying & Assessing Potential**

Spotting the people who can become leaders and evaluating their readiness.

Play 1: Leadership Potential Matrix — A structured assessment to identify who has the judgement, character, and capability to become a leader. Look beyond current performance to future potential.

Play 2: 360-Degree Leadership Review — Gather feedback from peers, direct reports, and customers to understand how someone is perceived as a potential leader. Leadership is as much about influence and trust as it is about capability.

Play 3: Readiness Conversation — A direct discussion with potential leaders about their interest in leadership, career aspirations, and readiness to take on broader responsibility.

### **Building Leadership Capability**

Developing the skills, mindset, and confidence to lead effectively.

Play 4: Leadership Development Plan — Create an individualised development path covering communication, decision-making, conflict resolution, and strategic thinking. Make it specific and measurable.

Play 5: Mentorship & Coaching — Establish regular one-to-one sessions focused on leadership challenges, decision-making practice, and skill development. This is where real leadership growth happens.

Play 6: Leadership Challenge Projects — Give emerging leaders specific projects that require them to influence without authority, manage complexity, and deliver results through others.

Play 7: Cross-Functional Exposure — Rotate potential leaders through different areas of the business so they understand how everything connects and can think strategically about the whole organisation.

### **Strategic Delegation & Authority**

Gradually expanding responsibility and creating genuine decision-making authority.

Play 8: Authority Mapping — Define clear boundaries around what decisions each leader can make independently, what requires consultation, and what needs your approval. Remove ambiguity.

Play 9: Graduated Responsibility — Systematically increase the scope and complexity of what each leader is accountable for, building their confidence and capability progressively.

Play 10: Leadership Accountability System — Install regular review meetings where leaders report on their outcomes, decisions, and challenges. Hold them accountable for results, not just effort.

Play 11: Succession Planning Matrix — Map out who could step into key roles if needed, identify skill gaps, and create development plans to fill them. Always have a Plan B.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: List your top 3–5 performers and ask yourself: who shows good judgement when problems arise? That is your starting point for leadership development.

This week: Pick one decision you make regularly that someone else could handle. Give it to them with clear boundaries and authority to act.

This week: Schedule a career conversation with your most promising potential leader. Ask what they want to achieve and how leadership fits into their plans.
