# First Week Plan

## What to Do

Create a structured first-week plan covering introductions, how the business works, how their role fits in, and what they need to know to start contributing. The first week should feel purposeful and well-organised, not chaotic and improvised.

## Why It Matters

A new hire's first week sets the tone for their entire employment. A structured week tells them: this business is organised, your role matters, and we have thought about how to get you up to speed. An unstructured week tells them: figure it out yourself. New hires who receive a structured first week reach full productivity 30-50% faster than those left to work things out on their own.

## How to Use This

Plan the week before they start. Share it with the new hire and anyone who has a role in their onboarding.

---

## Day 1: Welcome and Orientation

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Start with a 30-minute one-to-one with their manager. Cover: "Welcome, here is what this week looks like, here is what you need to know today, and here is who you will meet."
- [ ] Tour of the workspace (physical or virtual): where things are, who sits where, how to find what they need
- [ ] Introductions to immediate team members (schedule these, do not leave them to chance)
- [ ] Set up any remaining technology (should be minimal if Pre-Start Prep was done)
- [ ] Lunch with the team or their buddy
- [ ] End of day: 15-minute check-in. "How was day one? Any questions?"

## Day 2: Understanding the Business

- [ ] Overview of the business: what you do, who you serve, how you make money, and where you are headed
- [ ] Explain the team structure: who does what and how their role connects
- [ ] Share key documents: client list, product/service overview, pricing, current projects
- [ ] If relevant, shadow a colleague doing the kind of work they will be doing
- [ ] End of day: brief check-in

## Day 3: Understanding Their Role

- [ ] Deep dive into their specific role: responsibilities, expectations, and how success is measured
- [ ] Review the Role Scorecard (Action 1) with them: "Here is what winning looks like"
- [ ] Walk through the tools and systems they will use daily
- [ ] Introduce them to the key processes relevant to their role
- [ ] Give them their first small task: something achievable that produces a visible output

## Day 4: Starting to Contribute

- [ ] They should be working on real tasks today, with support available
- [ ] Check in mid-day: "Do you have everything you need? What questions have come up?"
- [ ] Introduce them to any clients or external contacts they will work with
- [ ] Review their first task output together: feedback, guidance, and encouragement

## Day 5: Reflection and Planning

- [ ] 30-minute end-of-week review with their manager:
  - "What went well this week?"
  - "What are you unclear about?"
  - "What do you need from me?"
  - "Here is what next week looks like"
- [ ] Share the 30-60-90 day plan (Action 11) and walk through the milestones
- [ ] Ask for their feedback on the onboarding: "What would have made this week even better?"
- [ ] Celebrate: "You have had a great first week. Here is what you have already contributed."

---

## Expected Outcome

- A new hire who feels welcomed, informed, and ready to contribute by Friday
- Clear understanding of the business, the team, and their role
- First real tasks completed with feedback received
- A 30-60-90 day plan shared and understood
- Problems, confusion, and unmet needs surfaced early through daily check-ins

## Coach Notes

The first week plan is one of the cheapest, highest-impact investments in a new hire. Most SMEs leave new starters to "settle in" which really means "figure it out alone." The daily check-ins are critical - they are only 5-15 minutes each but they catch misunderstandings early and make the new hire feel supported. Push clients to prepare this plan in advance and share it with the new hire before day one. Common mistake: overloading day 1 with information. Spread it across the week. Day 1 should be about belonging, not learning everything at once.
