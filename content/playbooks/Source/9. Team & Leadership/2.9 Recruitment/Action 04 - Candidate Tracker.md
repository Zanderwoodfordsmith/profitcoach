# Candidate Tracker

## What to Do

Set up a simple system to track who has applied, where they are in the process, and what happens next. This prevents candidates falling through the cracks, keeps the process moving, and gives you a clear view of your recruitment pipeline at any point.

## Why It Matters

Without a tracker, recruitment quickly becomes chaotic. CVs sit in your inbox unreviewed. You forget who you have spoken to and who you have not. Candidates wait too long for a response and accept other offers. A simple tracker keeps you organised, responsive, and professional throughout the process. Candidates judge your business by how you run the hiring process - a disorganised process signals a disorganised company.

## How to Use This

Create a simple spreadsheet or board and update it as candidates move through each stage.

---

## Setup

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Create a spreadsheet with these columns: Name, Source (where they found the ad), Date Applied, Phone Screen (date/status), Interview (date/status), Task (date/status), Decision, Notes
- [ ] Or use a simple Trello/Notion board with columns for each stage: Applied > Phone Screen > Interview > Task > Offer > Hired / Rejected
- [ ] Add every applicant the day they apply - even if you know immediately they are not a fit

## Track Each Stage

- [ ] **Applied:** Log every application with name, source, and date received
- [ ] **Screened:** Mark CVs as Yes (proceed), Maybe (review again), or No (reject) within 48 hours of application
- [ ] **Phone Screen:** Record date scheduled, date completed, and outcome (proceed or reject)
- [ ] **Interview:** Record date, interviewers, and outcome
- [ ] **Task:** Record date sent, date returned, and evaluation
- [ ] **Decision:** Offer made / Rejected / On hold
- [ ] **Notes:** Key observations at each stage (useful for comparing candidates later)

## Communication Standards

- [ ] Respond to every applicant within 48 hours, even if just to acknowledge receipt
- [ ] Reject candidates promptly and politely - do not leave people hanging
- [ ] Confirm next steps after every stage: "You will hear from us by [date] about [next step]"
- [ ] If delays happen, communicate proactively: "We are taking a bit longer than expected. I will update you by [date]."

## Post-Hire Review

- [ ] After the hire is made, review the tracker: which channels produced the best candidates? How long did the process take? Where did it get stuck?
- [ ] Keep the tracker for future hires - it becomes your recruitment data over time
- [ ] Note any candidates who were strong but not selected - they may be right for a future role

---

## Expected Outcome

- Every candidate tracked from application to decision
- No applications lost or forgotten
- A clear, responsive process that reflects well on your business
- Data on which channels and stages work best
- A reusable system for every future hire

## Coach Notes

The tracker is simple but surprisingly few SMEs use one. Most rely on memory and an overflowing inbox. Even a basic spreadsheet dramatically improves the quality of the hiring process. The communication standards are especially important: a candidate who gets a prompt, professional rejection will still speak positively about your business. A candidate who never hears back will tell everyone. Push clients to set the 48-hour response rule and stick to it. If they are too busy to review applications within 48 hours, they are not ready to hire.
