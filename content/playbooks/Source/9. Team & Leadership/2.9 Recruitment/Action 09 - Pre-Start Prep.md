# Pre-Start Prep

## What to Do

Get everything ready before day one so the new hire walks into a business that is prepared for them. Contracts signed, equipment ready, logins created, first-week schedule planned, and a welcome message sent. First impressions count both ways - how you prepare for them signals how seriously you take their role.

## Why It Matters

Nothing kills new-hire enthusiasm faster than arriving on day one to find that nobody expected them. No desk, no login, no plan for the day. It tells them: "We did not prepare for you, so you are probably not that important." The opposite is equally powerful. Walking into a business that has everything ready, a welcome message, a clear first-week plan, and someone genuinely glad to see them, builds immediate trust and commitment. Pre-start prep is a 1-2 hour investment that shapes the entire first impression.

## How to Use This

Complete this checklist before the new hire's start date. Ideally, start 1-2 weeks before they arrive.

---

## Administrative Prep

- [ ] Contract signed and returned (do not leave this to day one)
- [ ] Employee details collected: bank details, emergency contact, ID for right-to-work check
- [ ] Added to payroll system
- [ ] Added to pension scheme if applicable
- [ ] Employee handbook or key policies sent in advance (so they can read before day one, not during it)

## Equipment and Access

- [ ] Computer/laptop set up, tested, and ready
- [ ] Email account created
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Send them their email login and any essential system access 2-3 days before they start. Let them set up passwords from home so day one is not spent in IT setup.
- [ ] Software access: create accounts for every tool they will use (project management, CRM, communication tools, etc.)
- [ ] Physical setup: desk, chair, phone, stationery, access cards/keys
- [ ] Shared drive access with relevant folders

## Communication

- [ ] Send a personal welcome message from their manager (email or text, not a formal letter): "Looking forward to having you. Here is what your first day looks like..."
- [ ] Send the first-week schedule in advance so they know what to expect
- [ ] Tell the existing team: who is starting, what their role is, and when. Ask someone to be their buddy for the first week.
- [ ] If the role involves client contact, prepare a brief introduction to key clients

## First-Day Prep

- [ ] Print their first-week schedule and leave it on their desk
- [ ] Prepare a simple welcome pack: key passwords, who to ask for what, where things are, Wi-Fi details
- [ ] Book a lunch for day one (even a simple team coffee creates belonging)
- [ ] Clear your calendar for the first 30 minutes of their first day - you should be the first person they see

---

## Expected Outcome

- Everything administrative, technical, and logistical ready before day one
- The new hire feels expected, valued, and prepared
- Day one is spent learning and connecting, not filling in forms and setting up passwords
- The team is briefed and ready to welcome the new person
- A professional first impression that builds immediate commitment

## Coach Notes

Pre-start prep is where most SMEs fall down because it feels like admin. But the ROI is enormous: a new hire who feels prepared and welcome ramps up faster, integrates with the team more quickly, and is less likely to regret accepting the offer. The personal welcome message is the highest-impact, lowest-effort item on this list - a 2-minute text from the hiring manager makes the new hire feel personally valued. Push clients to complete this checklist at least 3 days before the start date, not the night before.
