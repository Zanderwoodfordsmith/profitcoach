# 30-60-90 Day Plan

## What to Do

Create clear goals, training milestones, and KPIs for each phase of the new hire's first 90 days. This gives them a roadmap to follow, gives you a way to measure progress, and creates natural checkpoints to course-correct if something is off track.

## Why It Matters

Without a plan, new hires drift. They do whatever feels most urgent rather than building toward the outcomes you hired them for. A 30-60-90 day plan creates accountability on both sides: the new hire knows what they need to achieve, and you know what to evaluate at each checkpoint. It also prevents the common pattern where everything seems fine for 3 months and then suddenly it becomes clear the person is not performing - by which point you have wasted a quarter.

## How to Use This

Build the plan from your Role Scorecard (Action 1). Share it with the new hire during their first week.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn and Understand

The first month is about getting up to speed, not delivering results.

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Write the single most important thing the new hire should understand by day 30. That is the goal for the first month.
- [ ] **Learning goals:**
  - Understand the business: who you serve, what you sell, how you deliver
  - Understand their role: responsibilities, expectations, tools, and processes
  - Build relationships: meet everyone they will work with regularly
  - Learn the systems: become competent with daily tools and processes
- [ ] **Milestone tasks:**
  - Complete [specific training or reading]
  - Shadow [specific colleague or process] at least [X] times
  - Complete [first deliverable] independently (with review)
- [ ] **Day 30 checkpoint:** Manager review. "Are you on track? What gaps remain? What support do you need for month 2?"

## Days 31-60: Contribute and Build

The second month is about producing real output with decreasing supervision.

- [ ] **Performance goals:**
  - Take ownership of [specific responsibility] with minimal oversight
  - Produce [specific output] at the expected quality level
  - Identify and flag [specific issues or improvements] proactively
- [ ] **Milestone tasks:**
  - Deliver [key project or output] independently
  - Handle [client interaction or process] without manager involvement
  - Propose one improvement to a process or approach
- [ ] **Day 60 checkpoint:** Manager review. "Are you meeting expectations? What is working well? Where do you need to improve?"

## Days 61-90: Own and Deliver

The third month is about operating at the expected level with full ownership.

- [ ] **Performance goals:**
  - Consistently deliver the outcomes from the Role Scorecard
  - Operate independently in the core responsibilities
  - Demonstrate the values and behaviours expected
- [ ] **Milestone tasks:**
  - Hit [specific KPI or target] for at least 2 consecutive weeks
  - Handle [complex situation or edge case] with good judgment
  - Be able to explain their role and how it connects to business goals
- [ ] **Day 90 checkpoint:** Formal review. "Have the 90-day outcomes been met? Is this person confirmed in the role?" This is the decision point: confirm, extend probation, or part ways.

## Build the Plan

- [ ] Write specific, measurable goals for each phase
- [ ] Share the plan with the new hire in week 1 so they can see the full picture
- [ ] Print or share digitally - make it visible and referenced regularly
- [ ] Adjust the plan if role requirements change, but do not lower the bar without good reason
- [ ] Use the checkpoints as structured coaching conversations, not just performance reviews

---

## Expected Outcome

- Clear expectations set from day one that both parties understand
- Natural checkpoints at 30, 60, and 90 days to assess progress
- Problems identified early enough to address or make a change
- New hires reaching full productivity faster through structured goals
- A fair, evidence-based decision about role confirmation at 90 days

## Coach Notes

The 30-60-90 plan is the bridge between recruitment and performance management. Without it, the first 3 months are a black hole where nobody knows whether things are going well until it is too late. The day 90 checkpoint is the most important: this is where you decide if the hire is confirmed. Many managers avoid this conversation, but it is essential. If performance is not where it needs to be at 90 days, delaying the conversation does not improve it. Push clients to have honest, kind conversations at each checkpoint. "Here is what is going well. Here is what needs to improve. Here is the support we will provide."
