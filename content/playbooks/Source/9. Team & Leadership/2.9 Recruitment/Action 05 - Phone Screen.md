# Phone Screen

## What to Do

Run a short 15-20 minute call with shortlisted candidates to check motivation, logistics, and basic fit before investing time in a full interview. The phone screen is a filter: it identifies candidates who should definitely progress and candidates who should definitely not, saving hours of unnecessary interviews.

## Why It Matters

A full interview is a 45-60 minute investment from you and potentially your team. Phone screens prevent wasting that time on candidates who look good on paper but are wrong in practice - maybe they cannot start for 3 months, maybe they are only interested at double your budget, maybe their communication style is immediately wrong for the role. A 15-minute call reveals these issues before you commit to a full process.

## How to Use This

Schedule phone screens for your top 8-10 applicants. Use the same questions for every candidate so you can compare fairly.

---

## Before the Call

- [ ] Review their CV/application and note 1-2 things you want to clarify
- [ ] Have your Role Scorecard (Action 1) in front of you
- [ ] Block 20 minutes in your calendar (15 for the call, 5 for notes)

## The Call Script (15 Minutes)

- [ ] **Introduction (2 minutes):** "Thanks for applying. I would like to spend 15 minutes getting to know you and telling you more about the role. Is now still a good time?"
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Ask this first: "What interested you about this role specifically?" Their answer tells you immediately whether they read the ad carefully or are applying to everything.

### Check Motivation (5 minutes)
- [ ] "What are you looking for in your next role?"
- [ ] "What do you know about our company?"
- [ ] "Why are you leaving (or looking to leave) your current position?"

### Check Logistics (3 minutes)
- [ ] "When would you be available to start?"
- [ ] "The salary range is £X-£Y. Does that work for you?" (Do not waste time on candidates outside your range)
- [ ] "This role is [location/hours]. Does that work for your situation?"

### Check Fit (3 minutes)
- [ ] "Tell me briefly about your most relevant experience for this role."
- [ ] "What would you say is your biggest strength for this type of work?"
- [ ] One role-specific question from your scorecard (e.g., "Have you managed a team before?" or "Are you comfortable with cold outreach?")

### Close (2 minutes)
- [ ] "Do you have any questions about the role or the company?"
- [ ] "Here is what happens next: I am speaking to a few candidates this week and will be in touch by [date] about the next stage."
- [ ] Thank them for their time.

## After the Call

- [ ] Rate the candidate immediately (while the conversation is fresh): Strong Yes / Yes / Maybe / No
- [ ] Note 2-3 key observations in your Candidate Tracker (Action 4)
- [ ] If No: send a polite rejection within 48 hours
- [ ] If Yes or Strong Yes: schedule for the full interview (Action 6)
- [ ] If Maybe: compare against other phone screens before deciding

---

## Expected Outcome

- The candidate pool narrowed from 8-10 to 3-5 for full interviews
- Basic fit (motivation, logistics, salary) confirmed before investing interview time
- Consistent data on each candidate for fair comparison
- A professional candidate experience that reflects well on your business
- Hours saved by not interviewing clearly wrong candidates

## Coach Notes

Phone screens are the most efficient part of the recruitment process. Encourage clients to use them for every hire, even if they only have 4-5 applicants. The salary question is crucial - many clients avoid it and then discover at offer stage that the candidate expects 40% more. Get it out of the way early. If a candidate cannot answer "What interested you about this role?" with anything specific, that tells you they are applying to everything and have no genuine interest. Move on.
