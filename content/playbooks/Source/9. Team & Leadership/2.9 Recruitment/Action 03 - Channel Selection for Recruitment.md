# Channel Selection for Recruitment

## What to Do

Pick 3-5 places your ideal candidate actually spends time and focus your recruitment effort there. Stop posting everywhere and hoping. A targeted approach on the right channels beats a scattered approach on every job board.

## Why It Matters

Different roles attract candidates through different channels. A junior admin role fills well through Indeed. A senior marketing hire is more likely found through LinkedIn or industry networks. A specialist developer might only be reachable through niche job boards or referrals. If you post your ad on the wrong channels, you get either no applications or the wrong applications. Matching the channel to the candidate is as important as writing a great ad.

## How to Use This

Consider the role, the seniority, and where your ideal candidate would be looking, then choose your channels.

---

## Channel Options

Review each and tick the ones that make sense for your current role:

### Job Boards
- [ ] **Indeed** - High volume, good for operational and admin roles, free listings available
- [ ] **LinkedIn Jobs** - Best for professional, managerial, and specialist roles. Paid listings get more reach.
- [ ] **Reed / Totaljobs** - Established UK boards with good reach for mid-level roles
- [ ] **Industry-specific boards** - Check if your sector has a specialist board (e.g., Charity Job, Escaped)

### Network and Referral
- [ ] **Your personal network** - Post on LinkedIn, email your contacts, ask your team. Often produces the best candidates.
- [ ] **Employee referrals** - Ask your team if they know anyone. Offer a referral bonus if appropriate (£250-500 is common).
- [ ] **Industry groups** - LinkedIn groups, Facebook groups, Slack communities relevant to the role
- [ ] **Professional associations** - Many have job boards or mailing lists (e.g., CIM for marketing, CIPD for HR)

### Direct Approach
- [ ] **LinkedIn search and outreach** - For senior or specialist roles, approach candidates directly. This is how recruiters work.
- [ ] **Freelancer platforms** - Upwork, Fiverr, PeoplePerHour. Good for testing a role as a freelance project before committing to a full hire.

### Recruitment Support
- [ ] **Recruitment agency** - Typically charges 15-25% of salary. Worth it for hard-to-fill or senior roles. Not worth it for straightforward hires.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Before paying for any channel, post on LinkedIn personally and ask your network. This is free and often produces the highest-quality candidates.

---

## Expected Outcome

- 3-5 targeted channels selected based on where your ideal candidate actually is
- A clear budget and timeline for the recruitment effort
- No wasted money on channels that do not match the role
- A higher-quality applicant pool because you are fishing in the right pond
- A plan to assess and adjust channels if initial results are poor

## Coach Notes

Most SME owners default to Indeed for every role because it is familiar. Challenge them to think about where a great candidate for this specific role would be. A talented marketing person is probably on LinkedIn, not scanning Indeed. A skilled tradesperson might respond better to a local Facebook group than a national job board. For senior roles, direct outreach (the client personally messaging potential candidates on LinkedIn) often works better than any job ad. If using a recruiter, insist on seeing the shortlist criteria to make sure they are aligned with the scorecard.
