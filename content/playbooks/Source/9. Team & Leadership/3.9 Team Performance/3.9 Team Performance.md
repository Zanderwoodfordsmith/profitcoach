## **What This Is**

Team performance is about turning your people from task-completers into results-deliverers. It is the difference between a team that shows up and a team that shows up and gets things done. When performance is working, everyone knows what winning looks like, gets the feedback they need to improve, and has a clear path to grow.

This playbook moves you beyond hoping your team performs well to ensuring they do. It covers setting measurable outcomes, building structured feedback loops, and creating a culture where candid conversations about performance are normal and helpful.

This is not about micromanaging or performance management theatre. It is about creating clarity so good people can do their best work — and helping everyone else get better or move on.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Nobody really knows what "good" looks like in their role. Performance conversations happen reactively when something goes wrong, or not at all. People drift along doing OK work without clear feedback or development. You avoid difficult conversations and hope performance issues fix themselves. Team members who want to grow have no clear path forward.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have some performance measures in place but they are inconsistent across roles. Reviews happen annually — if you remember to do them. Some people get good feedback, others do not, depending on their manager. You know who your top performers are but you are not sure how to develop the rest. Development conversations are ad hoc rather than systematic.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Every role has clear, measurable success criteria that people understand and track. Performance feedback is regular, specific, and actionable. People know exactly where they stand and what they need to do to improve or advance. Development paths are clear and intentional. Performance issues are addressed quickly and fairly. Your best people stay challenged and growing.

## **Things to Think About**

Is success actually measurable in each role? If you cannot point to specific numbers or observable outcomes that define good performance, then "performance management" is just subjective opinion. Revenue, quality scores, customer satisfaction, projects completed — what gets measured gets managed.

Are your performance conversations data or feelings? "You need to communicate better" is not feedback — it is a vague complaint. "In the last three client meetings, two customers had to repeat their requirements because you missed key details" is feedback you can act on. Facts beat feelings every time.

Do people know how to grow in your business? If someone wants to advance their career, can they see the specific skills, experiences, and results they need to get there? Without clear development paths, your best people will find them somewhere else.

Is feedback frequent enough to be useful? Annual reviews are archaeological exercises — you are digging up ancient history. Monthly or quarterly feedback keeps development conversations current and actionable. Problems get fixed before they become performance issues.

Do your incentives actually reward the behaviour you want? If you say collaboration matters but only reward individual achievement, guess what you will get more of? Check whether your recognition, pay rises, and promotions are reinforcing the right things.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Role Success Scorecard — Define measurable outcomes for each role so everyone knows exactly what winning looks like. Clear targets make everything else possible.

Play 2: Performance Review System — Build a structured, consistent process for regular performance discussions. Same framework, same frequency, same quality of conversation for everyone.

Play 3: Development Planning — Create clear pathways for skill building and career advancement. Map the specific capabilities, experiences, and results needed for each person to reach their next level.

Play 4: Feedback Culture Toolkit — Install the systems and skills that make candid, helpful feedback a normal part of how your team operates. Train managers to give feedback that drives improvement, not defensiveness.

Play 5: Performance Incentive Alignment — Review and redesign how recognition, rewards, and advancement decisions get made to ensure they reinforce the behaviours and results you actually want.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Pick your most important role and write down three specific, measurable outcomes that define success in that position. Make them numbers or observable behaviours, not personality traits.

This week: Schedule 30-minute one-to-ones with each of your direct reports for the next two weeks. Ask: "What is clear about your role? What is confusing? What do you need from me?"

This week: Look at your last three recognition or reward decisions. Were they based on measurable results or gut feel? Make a note to track this going forward.
