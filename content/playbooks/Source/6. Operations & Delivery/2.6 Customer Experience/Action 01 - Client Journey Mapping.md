# Client Journey Mapping

## What to Do

Identify every touchpoint from first contact to project completion and beyond, then spot where friction appears. This is similar to the Customer Journey Map in 2.3, but focused specifically on the client experience: how they feel at each stage, where they get frustrated, and where you can delight them.

## Why It Matters

Clients do not experience your business as a series of processes. They experience it as a series of moments - and the moments that frustrate, confuse, or disappoint them are the moments that cost you renewals and referrals. Mapping the journey from the client's perspective reveals friction you have been blind to because you are too close to the business to see it.

## How to Use This

Walk through the journey as if you were a client. Better yet, ask a recent client to walk you through their experience.

---

## Map Every Touchpoint

- [ ] List every interaction a client has with your business from first contact to completion:
  - First enquiry / first impression
  - Response to enquiry
  - Discovery call or sales conversation
  - Proposal or quote
  - Contract / sign-up
  - Welcome / onboarding
  - Kickoff meeting or first session
  - Regular delivery interactions
  - Progress updates or check-ins
  - Issue resolution (when things go wrong)
  - Project completion or contract end
  - Follow-up / ongoing relationship
- [ ] For each touchpoint, note: who handles it, how it happens (email, call, meeting, automated), and how consistent it is

## Rate the Experience

For each touchpoint:

- [ ] **How does the client feel?** (Excited, confident, confused, frustrated, ignored, valued?)
- [ ] **Is there waiting involved?** How long? Is the wait communicated or does the client wonder what is happening?
- [ ] **Is it personalised or generic?** Does the client feel known, or do they feel like account #47?
- [ ] **Is it consistent?** Does the same thing happen every time, or does it depend on who handles it?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Identify the single touchpoint where clients are most likely to feel confused or ignored. Fix that one this week.

## Find the Friction

- [ ] Circle every touchpoint where clients have to: wait without explanation, repeat information, figure something out themselves, or chase you for an update
- [ ] Identify the three worst friction points by impact on client satisfaction
- [ ] For each, ask: is this a process problem (no system), a people problem (inconsistent execution), or a communication problem (client does not know what to expect)?

## Design Improvements

- [ ] For each of the top 3 friction points, design a simple fix:
  - Missing communication? Create a template email or message for that stage
  - Inconsistent experience? Write a brief checklist for whoever handles it
  - Long wait? Set an expectation upfront ("You will hear from us within 48 hours")
- [ ] Implement one improvement this week and the other two within 30 days
- [ ] After implementing, ask the next 3 clients who go through that touchpoint: "How was that experience?"

---

## Expected Outcome

- A complete map of every client touchpoint with experience ratings
- The top 3 friction points identified and diagnosed
- Simple fixes designed and implementation started
- A foundation for ongoing experience improvement
- Better client retention and referrals through a smoother journey

## Coach Notes

If possible, get the client to interview 2-3 of their own clients about their experience. The owner's perception of the journey and the client's actual experience are often very different. The most common gap: the space between "they signed up" and "we started work." This is where new clients feel most uncertain and where many businesses go silent. A simple welcome email with clear next steps can transform the experience. This action overlaps with the Customer Journey Map in 2.3 but is focused on feelings and experience rather than process efficiency.
