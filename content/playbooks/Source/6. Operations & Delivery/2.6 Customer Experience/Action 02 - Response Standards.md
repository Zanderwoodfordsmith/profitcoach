# Response Standards

## What to Do

Set clear expectations for how quickly you respond to different types of client communication and build simple systems to hit those standards consistently. Response time is one of the most visible indicators of how much a business values its clients. Make it deliberate, not accidental.

## Why It Matters

Slow or inconsistent responses are the number one silent killer of client relationships. A client who waits 3 days for a reply to an important question does not complain - they just start looking at alternatives. Fast, consistent response builds trust and confidence. It signals "we are organised, we care, and you are a priority." The standard does not need to be "instant." It needs to be defined, communicated, and consistently met.

## How to Use This

Define your standards, set up the systems to meet them, and communicate them to your clients and team.

---

## Define Your Standards

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Set one rule right now and tell your team: "Every client email gets a reply within [X hours]." Even an acknowledgement ("Got this, will respond properly by [date]") counts.
- [ ] Set response time targets for different communication types:
  - **Urgent issues** (service down, major problem): ___ hours
  - **Standard client emails**: ___ hours (recommendation: same business day or within 24 hours)
  - **Proposals / quotes**: ___ business days
  - **General enquiries from prospects**: ___ hours (speed matters most here - first responder often wins)
  - **Social media messages**: ___ hours
  - **Phone calls / voicemails**: ___ hours for callback

## Build Systems to Hit Them

- [ ] Assign clear ownership: who monitors each communication channel and when?
- [ ] Set up notifications so nothing sits unread (but batch-process at defined times, do not react to every ping)
- [ ] Create template responses for common situations: "Thanks for your message, I will get back to you by [date]" - a fast acknowledgement buys you time for a proper response
- [ ] If you have a team, create a simple rota: who is covering email/phone during business hours?
- [ ] Set up an auto-responder for out-of-hours enquiries: "We have received your message and will respond within [X hours] on the next business day"

## Communicate and Measure

- [ ] Tell clients what to expect: include response times in your welcome pack, on your website, or in your email signature
- [ ] Track your actual response times for 2 weeks: are you hitting the standards?
- [ ] If you consistently miss a standard, either improve the system or adjust the standard to something realistic
- [ ] Review monthly: ask your team (or yourself) - did any client wait too long this month? What caused it?

---

## Expected Outcome

- Clear, defined response time standards for every type of client communication
- Systems in place to meet those standards consistently
- Clients informed of what to expect
- Measurable tracking of whether standards are being met
- Improved client confidence and satisfaction through reliable responsiveness

## Coach Notes

Response standards are deceptively simple but surprisingly powerful. Many clients resist formalising them ("I just reply when I can"). Push back with: "If your clients could choose between 'whenever you can' and 'within 4 hours, guaranteed,' which would they pick?" The standard creates accountability. The most important standard for growth: prospect enquiry response time. Research shows that responding to a new enquiry within 5 minutes vs. 30 minutes increases contact rates by 100x. For service businesses, speed of first response is often the differentiator between winning and losing the sale.
