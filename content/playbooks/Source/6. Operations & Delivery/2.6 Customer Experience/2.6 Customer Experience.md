## **What This Is**

Customer experience is what happens between "hello" and "goodbye" — and every moment in between. It influences whether customers renew, refer, or just disappear quietly. Small friction points compound over time, turning good customers into ex-customers.

This playbook is about deliberately designing client interactions instead of hoping they work out. It covers the entire journey: how they first encounter you, how quickly you respond, how clearly you onboard them, and how personally you treat them along the way.

When customer experience is broken, you lose clients without knowing why. When it is working, customers stay longer, buy more, and bring their friends.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You hear about unhappy customers through complaints or cancellations, never through proactive feedback. Response times are inconsistent — sometimes fast, sometimes forgotten. New clients are left to figure things out on their own. Every interaction feels generic and rushed. You have no idea why customers leave because no one ever asks them.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

Some parts of the client journey work well, others create friction. You respond quickly when you remember to check messages, but there is no system. Onboarding happens differently each time depending on who handles it. You collect feedback occasionally but do not use it consistently to improve. Most clients are satisfied but few are delighted.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Every client interaction is designed, not accidental. Response times are fast and consistent because there are systems in place. New clients have a structured onboarding experience that gets them results quickly. Regular touchpoints make clients feel known and valued. You collect feedback systematically and use it to remove friction before it costs you customers.

## **Things to Think About**

Where does friction appear in your client journey? Map every touchpoint from first contact to project completion. The moments that frustrate you as the business owner are probably frustrating your clients too. Friction hides in plain sight — slow responses, unclear next steps, or having to repeat information.

How fast is "fast" in your business? Every industry has different expectations. In some sectors, 24 hours is lightning speed. In others, 2 hours feels slow. Know your client's definition of fast and build your response times around their expectations, not yours.

Is the first experience structured or improvised? The first 48 hours after someone becomes a client shapes their entire relationship with you. Do they get a clear welcome sequence with next steps, or are they left wondering what happens next? First impressions compound.

Do clients feel known or processed? There is a difference between personalised and automated. Good systems make personalisation scalable — remembering their preferences, using their name, referencing their specific situation. When clients feel like just another account number, they start shopping around.

How are patterns identified? One complaint is feedback. Three identical complaints is a pattern that needs fixing. Are you collecting feedback in a way that lets you spot trends, or are you only hearing the loudest voices?

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Client Journey Mapping — Identify every touchpoint from first contact to completion and spot where friction appears. You cannot improve what you cannot see.

Play 2: Response Standards — Set clear expectations for how quickly you respond to different types of client communication and build simple systems to hit those standards consistently.

Play 3: Onboarding Experience Design — Create a structured first-week experience for new clients that gets them quick wins and sets clear expectations for what happens next.

Play 4: Client Feedback System — Build a simple way to collect, track, and act on feedback so you fix problems before they become patterns and turn insights into improvements.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: List every way clients can contact you. Check each channel right now and respond to anything waiting. Set a daily reminder to check all channels.

This week: Write down the three most common questions new clients ask in their first week. Create a simple FAQ document and send it to every new client on day one.

This week: Text or email your three most recent clients. Ask them: "What is one thing we could have done differently to make working with us even better?" Use their answers to fix something this month.
