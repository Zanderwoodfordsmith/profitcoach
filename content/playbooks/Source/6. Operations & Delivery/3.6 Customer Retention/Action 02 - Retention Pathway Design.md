# Design Your Retention Pathway

## What to Do

Build a deliberate sequence of touchpoints, check-ins, and renewal mechanisms so that staying with you becomes the natural, easy choice. Right now, most of your retention is accidental. Customers stay because they have not got around to leaving. This action replaces that with a designed experience where customers feel valued, see ongoing results, and never have to wonder whether they should stay.

## Why It Matters

Acquiring a new customer costs 5-7 times more than keeping an existing one. Yet most businesses spend 90% of their energy on acquisition and almost nothing on retention. A well-designed retention pathway does not just reduce churn. It turns satisfied customers into loyal ones and loyal ones into advocates. The compound effect of keeping customers even a few months longer is enormous.

## How to Use This

Work through each stage in order. You are building a system, not a one-off campaign. Each touchpoint should be something you can repeat consistently for every customer.

---

## Stage 1: First 90 Days (The Critical Window)

- [ ] **Design your onboarding welcome sequence** - What happens in the first 48 hours after someone becomes a customer? Map every communication, delivery step, and check-in.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Add a personal check-in at Day 7** - A quick call or message asking "How is everything going? Anything you need?" This single touchpoint dramatically reduces early churn.
- [ ] **Set a "first result" milestone** - Identify the earliest moment a customer gets a tangible result from working with you. Ensure your process accelerates them toward it.
- [ ] **Create a 30-day review point** - Formal or informal, check in to confirm they are on track and address any concerns before they become reasons to leave.
- [ ] **Build a 90-day success check** - At the end of the critical window, review what they have achieved. Reinforce the value they have received.

## Stage 2: Ongoing Engagement (Months 3-12)

- [ ] **Schedule regular value touchpoints** - Monthly or quarterly contact that is not a sales pitch. Share insights, check progress, offer help. Put these in a calendar so they actually happen.
- [ ] **Create content or resources exclusively for existing customers** - Give them something prospects do not get. This reinforces the value of being a customer.
- [ ] **Build a community or peer connection** - Even a simple private group or quarterly meetup creates stickiness that a transactional relationship cannot match.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Set up automated anniversary or milestone messages** - "You have been with us for 6 months, here is what you have achieved." Takes 30 minutes to set up, runs forever.

## Stage 3: Renewal and Recommitment

- [ ] **Design your renewal conversation** - Do not let contracts auto-expire and hope people re-sign. Build a deliberate renewal process that starts 30-60 days before the end date.
- [ ] **Create a renewal incentive** - Loyalty pricing, bonus access, or an upgrade offer that rewards staying. Make it genuinely valuable, not a gimmick.
- [ ] **Offer flexible commitment options** - Some customers want annual contracts, others prefer monthly. Offer both and let the relationship quality do the retaining, not the contract lock-in.
- [ ] **Build a win-back sequence for lapsed customers** - Not everyone who leaves is gone forever. A 30/60/90-day re-engagement sequence brings some back.

## Stage 4: Advocacy

- [ ] **Ask for referrals at peak satisfaction moments** - Right after a big result or positive interaction, not at random. Timing matters.
- [ ] **Make leaving a referral easy** - A simple link, a template message, or a "bring a friend" offer removes friction from the referral process.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A mapped, repeatable retention pathway from Day 1 through renewal
- Deliberate touchpoints at every critical stage of the customer journey
- A renewal process that starts well before contract expiry
- Referral and advocacy built into the natural customer experience
- Reduced reliance on new acquisition to maintain revenue

## Coach Notes

The biggest mistake clients make here is designing a pathway that is too complicated to maintain. Start with 5-6 touchpoints they can actually deliver consistently. A simple pathway that runs every time beats an elaborate one that gets abandoned after two months.

Push clients to automate what they can (email sequences, reminders, milestone messages) but keep the high-impact moments personal. An automated "happy anniversary" email is fine. An automated renewal conversation is not. The human touch matters most when money is on the table.
