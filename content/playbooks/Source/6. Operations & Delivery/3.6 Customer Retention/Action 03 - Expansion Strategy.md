# Build Your Expansion Strategy

## What to Do

Identify and create natural opportunities for existing customers to buy more, upgrade, or expand their relationship with you. This is not about being pushy. It is about recognising that your best customers often want more from you but nobody has asked. The easiest sale you will ever make is to someone who already trusts you and has seen results.

## Why It Matters

Most businesses treat each sale as a standalone event. They close the deal, deliver the work, and then go hunting for the next new customer. Meanwhile, their existing customers are buying complementary products and services from other providers. Expansion revenue typically converts at 3-5 times the rate of new business and costs almost nothing to acquire. Ignoring it is leaving money on the table every single month.

## How to Use This

Review each category below and tick off the ones that apply to your business. Not everything will be relevant. Focus on the 2-3 expansion paths that are most natural for your customers and easiest for you to deliver.

---

## Upsell Opportunities

- [ ] **Identify your natural "next step" offer** - What do customers typically need after they finish working with you? If you do not offer it, someone else will.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Review your last 10 completed projects or engagements** - How many customers asked for something extra that you either could not provide or did not offer? That is your upsell gap.
- [ ] **Create a premium tier or enhanced version of your core offer** - More access, faster delivery, additional support, or deeper involvement. Some customers will always want the best option.
- [ ] **Bundle complementary services** - Two things you sell separately that most customers eventually buy together should be offered as a package from the start.

## Cross-Sell Opportunities

- [ ] **Map all the problems your customers face, not just the one you solve** - Which of those adjacent problems could you credibly address?
- [ ] **Survey your best customers** - Ask: "What else do you wish we could help you with?" The answers will tell you exactly what to build next.
- [ ] **Review what your customers buy from other providers** - If they are paying someone else for something you could deliver, that is a cross-sell opportunity hiding in plain sight.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Introduce one complementary recommendation this month** - Even if you do not deliver it yourself, recommending a trusted partner shows you care about their bigger picture. And you can build a referral arrangement.

## Pricing and Packaging for Growth

- [ ] **Offer annual or long-term commitment options** - Give a meaningful discount for longer commitment. You get predictable revenue, they get better pricing. Both sides win.
- [ ] **Create usage-based expansion** - If your service scales with the customer's business (more users, more locations, more volume), build pricing that grows with them naturally.
- [ ] **Introduce a loyalty or rewards programme** - Does not need to be complicated. Even a simple "10% off your next purchase after 12 months" rewards continued business.

## Expansion Conversations

- [ ] **Train your team to spot expansion signals** - Customers mentioning new challenges, growing their team, entering new markets, or asking about capabilities they have not used are all buying signals.
- [ ] **Build expansion into your review rhythm** - Every quarterly or annual review should include "What is coming next for your business?" This naturally surfaces expansion opportunities.
- [ ] **Track expansion revenue separately** - Know exactly how much revenue comes from existing customers buying more versus new customers. Set a target and measure it monthly.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear map of upsell, cross-sell, and expansion opportunities in your business
- At least 2-3 expansion paths identified and ready to offer
- Pricing and packaging that encourages customers to spend more over time
- Your team trained to recognise and act on expansion signals
- Expansion revenue tracked as a distinct metric alongside new business

## Coach Notes

The biggest resistance here is the fear of being "salesy" with existing customers. Reframe it for the client: "You are not selling to them. You are serving them better. If they need something and you can provide it, staying quiet is a disservice."

Start with the lowest-friction option. Usually that is a simple conversation during an existing review meeting. "What else is on your plate right now?" often opens the door without any hard selling. Help the client script these conversations if they are uncomfortable with them.
