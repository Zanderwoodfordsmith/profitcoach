# Install Your Satisfaction Monitoring System

## What to Do

Set up early warning systems that spot dissatisfied customers before they leave. By the time a customer tells you they are unhappy, the relationship is usually beyond saving. This action builds feedback loops, behavioural triggers, and regular check-ins so you catch problems early, when they are still fixable.

## Why It Matters

Research consistently shows that for every customer who complains, 26 leave without saying a word. They just quietly stop buying, stop engaging, and eventually stop responding. A satisfaction monitoring system does not just measure happiness. It gives you a window of opportunity to intervene. Catching a problem at week 2 is a quick conversation. Catching it at month 6 is a rescue mission.

## How to Use This

Work through the steps in order. Start with the quick feedback mechanisms, then layer on the behavioural monitoring and formal survey process.

---

## Step 1: Quick Feedback Mechanisms

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Add a one-question check-in to your delivery process** - After each interaction, project milestone, or session, ask: "How was that for you? Anything we should adjust?" Simple, fast, and surprisingly revealing.
- [ ] **Set up a Net Promoter Score (NPS) survey** - One question: "How likely are you to recommend us to a colleague?" Run it quarterly. Track the trend, not the absolute number.
- [ ] **Create a "traffic light" system for your team** - After every customer interaction, team members flag the customer as green (happy), amber (something off), or red (problem). Takes seconds and builds a real-time picture.
- [ ] **Make it easy to give feedback** - A link in your email signature, a feedback button in your portal, or a QR code on printed materials. Remove every barrier between the customer and their honest opinion.

## Step 2: Behavioural Warning Signs

- [ ] **Define your engagement baseline** - How often does a healthy customer interact with you? Log in? Open emails? Attend sessions? You need to know what "normal" looks like before you can spot a drop.
- [ ] **Identify your early warning triggers** - Map the specific behaviours that precede churn. Common ones: stopped opening emails, missed two sessions in a row, stopped using the product, or went quiet after a complaint.
- [ ] **Set up alerts for trigger behaviours** - Use your CRM, email platform, or a simple spreadsheet to flag when a customer hits a warning trigger. Do not rely on memory.
- [ ] **Build a re-engagement protocol for each trigger** - When a customer goes quiet, what happens? Define the specific outreach: who contacts them, when, and what they say.

## Step 3: Formal Survey Process

- [ ] **Design a short quarterly satisfaction survey** - 5-7 questions maximum. Mix rating scales with one open-ended question. Keep it under 3 minutes to complete.
- [ ] **Ask the right questions** - Cover: overall satisfaction, likelihood to continue, perceived value, responsiveness, and one open-ended "what could we do better?"
- [ ] **Close the loop on every response** - Anyone who gives negative feedback gets a personal follow-up within 48 hours. Not an automated reply. A real conversation.
- [ ] **Share results with your team** - Monthly or quarterly review of satisfaction trends. Celebrate improvements. Address declining areas immediately.

## Step 4: Act on What You Learn

- [ ] **Create a "save" protocol for at-risk customers** - When monitoring flags a problem, what are the specific steps? Define who owns the recovery, what they offer, and what the timeline looks like.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Call your 3 most at-risk customers this week** - You probably already know who they are. A proactive call before they decide to leave is worth more than any survey.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Multiple feedback channels running so customers can tell you how they feel
- Behavioural triggers defined and monitored so you spot disengagement early
- A quarterly survey process that takes minutes to run and gives you actionable data
- A clear protocol for intervening when a customer shows warning signs
- Fewer surprise departures and more opportunities to fix problems before they become cancellations

## Coach Notes

Clients often overcomplicate this. They want to build a perfect feedback system before launching anything. Push them to start with one mechanism this week - the one-question check-in is the fastest to implement and gives immediate signal.

Watch out for "survey fatigue" as an excuse to avoid asking for feedback at all. The real issue is usually fear of hearing negative things. Remind the client that problems exist whether you measure them or not. Measuring them just means you can actually fix them.

For clients with small customer bases (under 50), skip the formal survey and rely on direct conversations. At that scale, you should know every customer by name and be able to read the room.
