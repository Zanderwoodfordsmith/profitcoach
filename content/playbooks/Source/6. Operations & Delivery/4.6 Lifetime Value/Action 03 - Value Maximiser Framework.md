# Value Maximiser Framework

## What to Do

Systematically increase customer lifetime value by working three levers: frequency (how often they buy), monetary value (how much they spend per transaction), and retention (how long they stay). Instead of trying to grow revenue only through new customers, this framework focuses on getting more value from customers you already have. You will assess your current position on each lever and identify the highest-impact opportunities.

## Why It Matters

Acquiring a new customer costs 5-7 times more than keeping an existing one. Yet most businesses spend the vast majority of their marketing budget on acquisition and almost nothing on maximising the value of customers they already have. A 10% improvement across all three levers (frequency, value, retention) compounds to a 33% increase in lifetime value, with no additional acquisition cost. This is the most capital-efficient growth strategy available to any business.

## How to Use This

You need your Lifetime Value Formula (Action 2) completed. Work through each lever below and identify 2-3 specific actions per lever.

---

## Lever 1: Increase Purchase Frequency

- [ ] Calculate your current average purchase frequency (purchases per customer per year)
- [ ] Identify what triggers repeat purchases in your business. Is it a schedule, a need, a prompt, or a habit?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Send a re-engagement message to every customer who has not purchased in the last 60 days. A simple "How is everything going?" often triggers a conversation that leads to a sale.
- [ ] Consider adding a recurring element to your offering: retainer, subscription, maintenance plan, membership
- [ ] Create a natural buying rhythm: quarterly reviews, annual renewals, seasonal check-ins
- [ ] Design a "next purchase" prompt that happens automatically after each sale (e.g. "Your next session should be in 4 weeks. I have held a slot for you.")
- [ ] Review whether your current customers know about all your products and services. Many do not.

## Lever 2: Increase Monetary Value (Average Spend)

- [ ] Calculate your current average order value / average revenue per customer per transaction
- [ ] Identify upsell opportunities: premium versions, add-ons, extended services
- [ ] Identify cross-sell opportunities: complementary products or services the customer might need
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: At your next customer meeting, ask: "Is there anything else we could help with?" This question alone surfaces revenue opportunities in at least 30% of conversations.
- [ ] Review your pricing. When did you last increase prices? If it has been more than 12 months, you are probably undercharging.
- [ ] Create tiered offerings: good, better, best. Most customers choose the middle tier, which should be higher than your current single offering.
- [ ] Bundle complementary services together at a slight discount to the individual price (higher total spend, better perceived value)

## Lever 3: Increase Retention (Customer Lifespan)

- [ ] Review your churn data from the Customer Lifespan Calculator (Action 1). Where do most customers leave?
- [ ] Build an early warning system: what signals indicate a customer is about to leave? (Reduced engagement, missed meetings, delayed payments, complaints)
- [ ] Create an intervention plan for at-risk customers: who reaches out, what do they say, what do they offer?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Call your 3 most at-risk customers this week. A personal call from the business owner often saves relationships that automated systems cannot.
- [ ] Design "sticky" moments in the customer journey: milestones, celebrations, exclusive access, results reviews
- [ ] Invest in onboarding quality. The first 30-90 days determine whether a customer stays long-term.
- [ ] Ask departing customers why they left. Track the reasons and fix the top two.
- [ ] Create switching costs (positively): the deeper a customer is embedded in your service, the harder it is to leave. Integrations, shared history, personalised service.

## Prioritise Your Actions

- [ ] For each lever, rate your current performance: strong, average, or weak
- [ ] Focus first on the weakest lever, as it has the most room for improvement
- [ ] Choose 2-3 specific actions to implement this quarter
- [ ] Set a measurable target: what LTV number are you aiming for?

## Expected Outcome

- 2-3 specific improvement actions identified per lever

- A quarterly LTV target set
- A plan to implement the highest-impact actions first

## Coach Notes

Clients instinctively reach for the acquisition lever (more new customers) because it feels like growth. The value maximiser framework redirects energy to where the returns are highest. The frequency lever is usually the quickest win for service businesses - most clients simply do not ask existing customers to buy again. The retention lever has the biggest long-term impact but requires patience. Walk clients through the maths: show them what a 10% improvement in each lever does to their total revenue. The compounding effect is what makes this framework powerful. Help clients pick one action per lever and execute it well rather than trying to do everything at once.
