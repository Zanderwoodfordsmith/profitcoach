# Monthly Reconciliation Process

## What to Do

Build a simple monthly process to make sure your books match your bank accounts. Reconciliation means comparing what your accounting software says happened with what your bank says happened, then finding and fixing any differences. Do this every month, within the first week of the following month.

## Why It Matters

Unreconciled books are unreliable books. A missed transaction, a duplicate entry, or an incorrect categorisation might be small on its own, but these errors compound over time. After 6 months of unreconciled books, your P&L could be thousands of pounds off reality. Monthly reconciliation catches errors when they are small and easy to fix, gives you confidence that your numbers are trustworthy, and keeps your accountant happy at year-end.

## How to Use This

Set a monthly reconciliation date and work through the checklist below. Most accounting software has a built-in reconciliation tool that makes this straightforward.

---

## Monthly Reconciliation Checklist

### Preparation (5 minutes)

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Set a recurring calendar event for the 5th of each month: "Monthly Bank Reconciliation." Thirty minutes. Non-negotiable.
- [ ] Download or access your bank statements for the previous month (all business accounts)
- [ ] Open your accounting software's reconciliation tool

### Reconcile Each Account (15-30 minutes)

- [ ] Start with your main business current account
- [ ] Compare the opening balance in your software with the bank's opening balance - they should match
- [ ] Work through each transaction: does your software match the bank? Tick off matched items.
- [ ] Investigate unmatched items:
  - Transaction in the bank but not in your software? It needs entering and categorising.
  - Transaction in your software but not in the bank? It may not have cleared yet, or it may be an error.
  - Amounts that do not match? Check for data entry errors (transposed digits are the most common cause)
- [ ] Check the closing balance: your software and bank should show the same number once all items are reconciled
- [ ] Repeat for any additional accounts (savings, credit cards, PayPal, etc.)

### Resolve Issues (10-15 minutes)

- [ ] Enter any missing transactions
- [ ] Correct any miscategorised items (wrong account, wrong category)
- [ ] Note any recurring issues (same type of error each month) and fix the root cause
- [ ] If you cannot resolve a discrepancy, flag it for your bookkeeper or accountant rather than leaving it

### Close the Month

- [ ] Confirm all accounts are reconciled and balanced
- [ ] Run a quick P&L report for the month - does the profit/loss figure look reasonable?
- [ ] Note anything unusual: "Revenue was £X below average because..." or "Expenses spiked because of one-off purchase"
- [ ] Save/lock the period if your software supports it (prevents accidental changes to reconciled months)

---

## Expected Outcome

- Books reconciled to the bank every month, within the first week
- Errors caught and corrected while they are small
- Confidence that your financial reports are accurate and trustworthy
- Year-end accounting becomes simple because every month was already clean
- A 30-minute monthly habit that prevents hours of painful catch-up

## Coach Notes

Reconciliation is where many clients disengage because it feels like "accounting admin." Reframe: it is quality control on the numbers you use to make every financial decision. If they have a bookkeeper, this should be the bookkeeper's job - but the owner should still review the reconciliation summary monthly. The most common issue: transactions that appear in the bank but were never entered. These are often direct debits, automatic payments, or small card transactions that slip through. Bank feeds in modern accounting software have largely solved this, so if the client is entering transactions manually, push them to connect their bank feeds.
