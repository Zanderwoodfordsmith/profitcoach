# Current Records System

## What to Do

Get your bookkeeping current and keep it current. Whether you do it yourself, use a bookkeeper, or rely on software, establish a rhythm that ensures your financial records are never more than 1-2 weeks behind reality. Current books are the foundation of every good business decision.

## Why It Matters

Outdated books mean outdated decisions. If your last entry is 6 weeks old, you are running your business blind - spending money you may not have, pricing based on margins you have not checked, and missing problems that accurate numbers would have caught weeks ago. Getting current is a one-time effort. Staying current is a weekly habit that takes 30 minutes and saves hours of panic at month-end and tax time.

## How to Use This

Assess where your records stand today, catch up if needed, then establish the weekly rhythm that keeps them current going forward.

---

## Assess Current State

- [ ] Open your accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent, etc.) or your records
- [ ] Check the date of the last entry. How far behind are you?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: If you are less than 2 weeks behind, block 1 hour today to catch up completely. If you are months behind, book a half-day this week or brief your bookkeeper.
- [ ] Check if bank feeds are connected and pulling transactions automatically (if not, set this up - it cuts data entry by 80%)

## Catch-Up Plan (If Needed)

- [ ] Gather all receipts, invoices, and bank statements for the period you are behind
- [ ] Process transactions in chronological order: categorise each one properly
- [ ] Do not rush the catch-up by dumping everything into "miscellaneous" - take the time to categorise correctly
- [ ] If you are more than 3 months behind and cannot face doing it yourself, hire a bookkeeper for a one-time catch-up (typically £200-500)
- [ ] Once caught up, set the date: "From today, records will never be more than 2 weeks old"

## The Weekly Rhythm

- [ ] Block 30 minutes every week (same day, same time) for bookkeeping. Friday afternoon or Monday morning works well.
- [ ] Each week:
  - Review and categorise new transactions from bank feeds
  - Enter any cash transactions or expenses not captured automatically
  - Check that all invoices sent this week are recorded
  - File or scan any paper receipts
  - Glance at the bank balance vs. your records - do they roughly match?
- [ ] Set a calendar reminder so the weekly session becomes automatic
- [ ] If someone else does your bookkeeping, set an expectation: records current within 7 days of month-end

## Choose Your System

- [ ] If you do not have accounting software yet, choose one: Xero, QuickBooks, or FreeAgent are the three most common for UK SMEs
- [ ] Connect your bank accounts for automatic transaction feeds
- [ ] Set up recurring transactions (rent, subscriptions, salaries) so they auto-categorise
- [ ] If you spend more than 2 hours per week on bookkeeping, consider hiring a part-time bookkeeper (typical cost: £150-300/month for a small business)

---

## Expected Outcome

- Financial records current within 1-2 weeks of real time
- A weekly bookkeeping rhythm established and protected
- Bank feeds connected for automatic transaction capture
- Clean, categorised records that produce useful reports
- The confidence to make financial decisions based on accurate, up-to-date data

## Coach Notes

Getting current is the hardest part. Many clients have a backlog they have been avoiding for months, which creates shame and avoidance that makes the problem worse. Be non-judgmental and practical: "Let's just get it done. How far behind are we?" If the backlog is significant, strongly recommend hiring a bookkeeper for the catch-up rather than letting it block progress. Once current, the weekly rhythm is easy to maintain. The key accountability question each session: "Are your books current?" If not, it needs addressing before other financial work (cash flow forecasting, cost control, pricing) can produce reliable results.
