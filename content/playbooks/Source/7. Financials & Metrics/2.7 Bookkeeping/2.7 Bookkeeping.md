## **What This Is**

Your books are the foundation of every business decision you make. When they are current and accurate, you can see what is actually happening. When they are messy or behind, every decision is a guess.

Bookkeeping is not glamorous. It does not directly bring in revenue or delight customers. But it creates the clarity that makes everything else possible. Good books tell you whether you are making money, what you can afford to spend, and whether your business model is working.

This playbook is about getting your financial records organised, current, and trustworthy so you can run your business on facts instead of hope.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Your books are weeks or months behind. You have no idea what your actual profit was last month because the numbers are not ready yet. Personal and business expenses are mixed together in ways that make your accountant cry. You make financial decisions based on your bank balance instead of your P\&L. Tax season is a scramble to find receipts and figure out what happened.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

Your bookkeeping gets done but it takes forever to get the numbers. There are usually discrepancies between your records and bank statements that take time to figure out. You get basic reports but they often raise more questions than they answer. You know roughly how you are doing but not precisely enough to make confident decisions.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Your books are current within a week of month-end. Bank reconciliations happen monthly and discrepancies are rare and quickly resolved. Personal and business finances are completely separate. You get clean P\&L and balance sheet reports that you understand and trust. Financial decisions are made based on accurate data, not bank balances or gut feel.

## **Things to Think About**

How current are your financial records right now? If someone asked you for last month's P\&L today, how quickly could you produce it? The gap between "month-end" and "numbers ready" is a measure of how much you are flying blind. A week is good. A month is dangerous.

Are your bank accounts talking to your books? Monthly reconciliation is not optional bookkeeping admin — it is quality control. If your records do not match your bank statements, one of them is wrong. Finding out which one (and why) prevents small errors from becoming big problems.

Can you draw a clear line between personal and business money? Mixed finances are not just messy — they create tax complications, legal risks, and decision-making confusion. If you cannot instantly tell what belongs to the business and what belongs to you, that line needs fixing.

Are your invoices creating clean data or adding to the mess? Inconsistent invoicing creates inconsistent records. When invoice formats, payment terms, and descriptions vary every time, your books become harder to read and your cash flow becomes harder to predict.

Do you know what questions your numbers should answer? Books are not just for compliance — they are for decisions. Can you quickly see which products are profitable? Which months are strongest? Where money is going? If your reports do not help you run the business, they are not doing their job.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Current Records System — Get your bookkeeping current and keep it current. Whether you do it yourself or hire someone, establish the rhythm that keeps your numbers fresh and your decisions informed.

Play 2: Monthly Reconciliation Process — Build a simple monthly process to make sure your books match your bank accounts. Catch errors early before they compound into bigger problems.

Play 3: Business & Personal Separation — Draw clear lines between business and personal finances. Separate accounts, separate cards, separate everything. This protects you legally and makes your numbers infinitely cleaner.

Play 4: Clean Chart of Accounts — Organise your income and expense categories so your reports actually make sense. Every transaction should have an obvious home that helps you understand what is happening in your business.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Open your accounting software and check the date of the last entry. If it is more than two weeks old, block time this Friday to catch up. Current books start with current data.

This week: Separate one thing that is currently mixed. Move your business banking to a dedicated business account, or get a separate business credit card. One separation is better than none.

This week: Run a P\&L report for last month. Even if the numbers are not perfect, look at it and ask: does this help me understand my business or raise more questions? That tells you how much work needs doing.
