# Value Driver Assessment

## What to Do

Score your business across the key factors that drive valuation: financial performance, growth potential, market position, management team, systems and processes, and scalability. This assessment reveals exactly which areas are adding value and which are holding your valuation back, so you can focus improvement where it matters most.

## Why It Matters

Business valuation is not just about profit. Two businesses with identical earnings can have wildly different valuations because of their underlying drivers. A business with strong systems, a management team that runs independently, and a growing market is worth multiples more than a business with the same profit but no systems, total owner dependency, and a shrinking market. Understanding your value drivers lets you build the business that is worth the most, not just the one that earns the most today.

## How to Use This

Score yourself honestly on each value driver, identify your weakest areas, and create an improvement plan.

---

## Score Your Financial Performance (1-10)

- [ ] **Revenue growth trend** - Has revenue grown consistently over the last 3 years? (1 = declining, 5 = flat, 10 = strong consistent growth)
- [ ] **Profit margin** - Are your margins healthy for your industry? (1 = breakeven or loss, 5 = average, 10 = industry-leading)
- [ ] **Revenue predictability** - What percentage of revenue is recurring or contracted? (Use your score from the Revenue Predictability Audit)
- [ ] **Customer concentration** - Does any single customer represent more than 15% of revenue? (1 = one customer dominates, 10 = highly diversified)

## Score Your Growth Potential (1-10)

- [ ] **Market size and trajectory** - Is your market growing, stable, or shrinking? (1 = shrinking, 5 = stable, 10 = rapidly growing)
- [ ] **Product pipeline** - Do you have new products or improvements in development? (1 = nothing planned, 10 = strong pipeline with validated demand)
- [ ] **Expansion opportunities** - Could you enter new markets, geographies, or customer segments? (1 = maxed out, 10 = multiple untapped opportunities)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Check if your revenue has grown in each of the last 3 years. Consistent growth, even modest, scores much higher than volatile revenue.

## Score Your Market Position (1-10)

- [ ] **Competitive differentiation** - How clearly do customers understand why you are different? (1 = commodity, interchangeable with competitors, 10 = unique position that competitors cannot easily replicate)
- [ ] **Brand recognition** - Are you known in your market? Do customers seek you out? (1 = unknown, 10 = recognised authority)
- [ ] **Customer loyalty** - What is your retention rate and repeat purchase rate? (1 = high churn, 10 = customers stay for years and refer others)

## Score Your Management and Team (1-10)

- [ ] **Owner dependency** - Could the business operate for 4 weeks without you? (1 = would collapse immediately, 10 = runs smoothly without you)
- [ ] **Management depth** - Do you have capable people who can make decisions independently? (1 = you make every decision, 10 = strong management team in place)
- [ ] **Team retention** - Do good people stay? (1 = constant turnover, 10 = stable, loyal team)

## Score Your Systems and Scalability (1-10)

- [ ] **Process documentation** - Are key processes documented so anyone could follow them? (1 = everything is in people's heads, 10 = fully documented and maintained)
- [ ] **Technology infrastructure** - Do your systems support growth or hold it back? (1 = manual and fragile, 10 = automated and scalable)
- [ ] **Scalability** - Could you double revenue without doubling costs? (1 = costs scale linearly with revenue, 10 = significant operating leverage)

## Calculate and Interpret

- [ ] **Average your scores across all categories** - This is your overall Value Driver Score.
- [ ] **Identify your 3 lowest-scoring areas** - These are the constraints on your valuation. Fixing these will have the biggest impact.
- [ ] **Create an improvement plan** - For each of your 3 weakest areas, define one specific action to improve the score over the next 6 months.
- [ ] **Set a reassessment date in 6 months** - Track your progress and see which scores have improved.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A scored assessment across all major value drivers
- Clear visibility on which factors are strengthening and weakening your valuation
- Your 3 highest-priority improvement areas identified
- A 6-month improvement plan with specific actions
- A repeatable assessment you can use to track progress over time

## Coach Notes

Clients often score themselves too generously on the first pass. Push for honesty by asking concrete questions. "Can the business run for 4 weeks without you?" is more revealing than "how dependent is the business on you?" Use specific scenarios to calibrate scores.

The most common weak areas are owner dependency and process documentation. These are also the areas most within the owner's control to fix, which makes them good coaching targets. Revenue growth and market position take longer to change.

Remind clients that no business scores 10 across the board. The goal is not perfection. It is systematic improvement on the things that matter most for valuation. Even moving your weakest area from a 3 to a 6 can meaningfully increase what the business is worth.
