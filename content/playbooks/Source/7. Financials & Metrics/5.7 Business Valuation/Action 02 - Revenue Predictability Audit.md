# Revenue Predictability Audit

## What to Do

Analyse how much of your revenue is recurring, contracted, or predictable versus one-off or unpredictable. Then build a plan to increase the predictable portion. Predictable revenue is the single biggest driver of business valuation because it reduces risk for any future buyer and gives you, the owner, more stability and confidence.

## Why It Matters

A buyer will pay significantly more for a business where next month's revenue is already locked in than for a business where every month starts from zero. John Warrillow's "Built to Sell" framework makes this clear: recurring revenue businesses sell for 3-5x higher multiples than project-based businesses. But this is not just about selling. Predictable revenue changes how you run the business day to day. You can plan hiring, invest in growth, and sleep at night because you are not wondering whether next month will be a good month or a disaster.

## How to Use This

Categorise all your revenue, calculate your predictability score, and identify specific moves to shift more revenue into predictable categories.

---

## Categorise Your Revenue

- [ ] **Pull your last 12 months of revenue data** - Every invoice, payment, and transaction. Group by customer and by product/service.
- [ ] **Sort every revenue line into one of four categories:**
  - **Recurring** - Subscription, retainer, or membership. Customer pays regularly without a new sales conversation each time. (Score: 4)
  - **Contracted** - Customer has signed a contract for future work, even if not subscription-based. Committed but with an end date. (Score: 3)
  - **Repeat** - Customer buys from you regularly but has not committed to future purchases. They could stop at any time. (Score: 2)
  - **One-off** - Single transaction with no expectation of repeat. New customer each time. (Score: 1)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Add up the percentage of revenue in each category. Most small businesses are shocked to discover that 60-80% of their revenue is one-off or repeat, not recurring or contracted.

## Calculate Your Predictability Score

- [ ] **Multiply each category's revenue by its score (1-4)** - Then divide the total by your overall revenue and multiply by 25 to get a percentage score.
- [ ] **Interpret your score:**
  - 80-100% = Highly predictable (strong valuation driver)
  - 60-79% = Moderately predictable (room for improvement)
  - 40-59% = Low predictability (significant valuation drag)
  - Below 40% = Unpredictable (biggest priority to fix)
- [ ] **Compare to your industry** - Some industries naturally have more recurring revenue (SaaS, coaching, maintenance contracts). Others are inherently project-based. Know what "good" looks like for your sector.

## Identify Shifts You Can Make

- [ ] **Review your one-off revenue** - Can any of these customers be converted to a repeat or recurring relationship? What would you need to offer?
- [ ] **Review your repeat customers** - Can you offer them a contract, retainer, or subscription that locks in their spending and gives them a benefit (discount, priority, guaranteed capacity)?
- [ ] **Explore new recurring revenue models** - Maintenance plans, membership tiers, advisory retainers, subscription add-ons, annual contracts with monthly billing. What could work for your business?
- [ ] **Calculate the impact** - If you shifted 20% of your one-off revenue to recurring, what would your new predictability score be? What would that mean for valuation?
- [ ] **Pick one shift to implement this quarter** - Do not try to change everything. Move one revenue stream toward greater predictability and prove the concept.
- [ ] **Set a 6-month review to remeasure your predictability score** - Track the trend over time.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear picture of how your revenue breaks down across predictability categories
- A calculated predictability score with benchmark comparison
- Specific opportunities identified to shift revenue toward recurring or contracted
- One concrete action chosen to implement this quarter
- A baseline score to measure improvement against

## Coach Notes

This audit often triggers a strategic rethink. Clients who have always sold projects start asking "how do I create a retainer?" Clients who have been undercharging start seeing the value of annual contracts. The insight alone is powerful.

The easiest shift is usually from repeat to contracted. If customers already buy from you regularly, a simple annual agreement with a small discount can lock in that revenue. Start there because it requires no new products, just a new structure.

Watch for clients who try to force recurring revenue where it does not fit. Not every business can be a subscription model. The goal is to increase predictability, not to pretend you are a SaaS company. Even shifting from 30% predictable to 50% predictable makes a meaningful difference to valuation.
