# Transferability Review

## What to Do

Audit how dependent your business is on you personally across four areas: sales, operations, strategy, and relationships. Then create a roadmap to reduce that dependency so the business could operate, and eventually be sold, without you at the centre of everything.

## Why It Matters

A business that cannot function without its owner is not really a business. It is a job with overhead. Michael Gerber made this point in The E-Myth decades ago, and it remains the number one reason small businesses sell for less than they should, or cannot sell at all. Every task, decision, relationship, and process that depends on you personally reduces your business's value. A buyer is not buying you. They are buying a machine that produces profit. If the machine breaks the moment you leave, it is not worth much.

## How to Use This

Work through each dependency area, identify the critical risks, and build a transfer plan for each one.

---

## Audit Sales Dependency

- [ ] **Who generates new leads?** - If the answer is primarily you (your network, your reputation, your LinkedIn), that is a dependency risk. Score 1-10 for how well the business generates leads without you.
- [ ] **Who closes deals?** - If customers buy because of their relationship with you personally, the sales process is not transferable. Score 1-10.
- [ ] **Is there a documented sales process?** - Could a competent salesperson follow your approach and get similar results? Or is it all instinct and personal rapport?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Record your next 3 sales conversations (with permission). Transcribe the key parts. This is the fastest way to start documenting a sales process that currently lives in your head.

## Audit Operations Dependency

- [ ] **What operational decisions require your approval?** - List them. If more than 5 decisions per week need you personally, operations are too dependent.
- [ ] **Could your team deliver your core product or service without you for 30 days?** - If no, identify exactly which parts of delivery depend on your involvement.
- [ ] **Are your key processes documented?** - Check against your work in the Processes playbook. Undocumented processes are untransferable processes.
- [ ] **Do you have single points of failure in your team?** - Any role where one person leaving would cause serious disruption is a transferability risk, even if that person is not you.

## Audit Strategy Dependency

- [ ] **Who sets the direction of the business?** - If every strategic decision requires your input, the business has no strategic independence. This is normal for small businesses but reduces transferability.
- [ ] **Is there a documented strategy or business plan?** - Even a simple one-page plan means someone else could understand where the business is heading and why.
- [ ] **Do your team members understand the "why" behind major decisions?** - If they only know the "what," they cannot make good decisions when you are not there.

## Audit Relationship Dependency

- [ ] **List your 10 most important business relationships** - Key customers, suppliers, partners, referral sources, advisors.
- [ ] **How many of these relationships are with you personally rather than with the business?** - If you left, would these people stay?
- [ ] **For each at-risk relationship, identify a team member who could build a parallel relationship** - Start introducing them now. Relationship transfers take time.
- [ ] **Check your supplier agreements** - Are contracts in the business name or yours personally? Personal guarantees and informal handshake deals are transferability risks.

## Build Your Transfer Roadmap

- [ ] **Rank your dependency risks from highest to lowest** - Which dependencies would cause the most damage if you were suddenly unavailable?
- [ ] **For your top 3 risks, define a specific transfer action** - Document the process, hire or train a replacement, introduce a team member to the relationship, or create a decision-making framework.
- [ ] **Set a 12-month timeline** - Transferability does not happen overnight. Plan to reduce your top 3 dependencies over the next year, one per quarter.
- [ ] **Test it** - Take a week away from the business. Not a holiday where you check email. A real test where the business runs without you. The gaps that appear are your remaining dependencies.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear picture of where the business depends on you personally
- Dependency scores across sales, operations, strategy, and relationships
- Top 3 dependency risks identified and prioritised
- A 12-month roadmap to reduce owner dependency
- A plan for testing transferability with real time away from the business

## Coach Notes

This is one of the most uncomfortable actions in the entire Profit System. Most owners intellectually agree they should be less involved but emotionally resist actually stepping back. They enjoy being needed, or they believe nobody else can do it as well as they can.

The coaching opportunity is reframing dependency as a liability, not a strength. "The business needs me" sounds positive but actually means "the business is fragile." Help clients see that building transferability is building strength, not losing control.

Start with operations. It is the easiest area to transfer because processes can be documented and trained. Sales is hardest because it often involves the owner's personal reputation. Tackle that one last but start thinking about it early.
