# Know Your Numbers

## What to Do

Learn and calculate the 6 numbers every business owner must know without checking a spreadsheet. These are the vital signs of your business — if you do not know them, you are making decisions blind.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners can tell you their revenue but not their profit. They know what is in the bank but not their break-even. They have a "feeling" about whether the business is healthy but cannot back it up with a single number. Michalowicz puts it simply: if you cannot see the numbers, you cannot manage the business. You do not need an accounting degree. You need 6 numbers and the habit of checking them.

## How to Use This

Work through each number below. If you do not know it, find it. If you cannot find it, that itself is important information — it means your financial visibility is broken.

---

## The 6 Numbers

### 1. Monthly Revenue

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **What was your total revenue last month?** — Not a rough guess. The actual number from your accounts. If you cannot answer this within 60 seconds, your financial tracking needs work.
- [ ] **What is the trend — growing, flat, or declining over the last 6 months?** — A single month means nothing. The trend tells you whether the business is heading in the right direction.

### 2. Monthly Fixed Costs

- [ ] **List every cost that stays the same regardless of how much you sell** — Rent, salaries, insurance, software subscriptions, loan repayments, utilities. Add them up. This is your minimum monthly burn.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Can you state this number from memory?** — You should know your fixed cost base the way you know your phone number. It is the most important number in your business because it tells you how much you must earn before you make a single pound of profit.

### 3. Break-Even Point

- [ ] **Calculate: Fixed Costs ÷ Gross Margin % = Break-Even Revenue** — If your fixed costs are £15K/month and your gross margin is 60%, your break-even is £25K/month. Below that, you are losing money. Above it, you are profitable.
- [ ] **How many clients/jobs/sales does that represent?** — Convert the number into something tangible. "I need 12 clients per month at £2,000 to break even." That is more useful than a revenue figure.

### 4. Gross Margin

- [ ] **Calculate: (Revenue − Direct Costs) ÷ Revenue × 100** — Direct costs are what it costs to deliver your product or service (materials, subcontractors, direct labour). Everything else is overhead. Your gross margin tells you how much of every pound earned is available to cover overheads and generate profit.
- [ ] **Is it above 50%?** — For service businesses, a gross margin below 50% is a warning sign. It means more than half of every pound you earn goes directly to delivering the work, leaving little for the rest of the business.

### 5. Cash Runway

- [ ] **How many months could you operate if revenue stopped today?** — Cash in bank ÷ monthly fixed costs = months of runway. Below 1 month is a crisis. 1-2 months is fragile. 3+ months gives you breathing room to make strategic decisions rather than desperate ones.
- [ ] **What is your target runway?** — For most £200K-£5M businesses, 3 months of fixed costs in reserve is the minimum. Set this as a savings target.

### 6. Revenue Per Employee (or Per Head)

- [ ] **Calculate: Annual Revenue ÷ Total Number of People** — Include everyone: employees, regular contractors, part-time staff. This tells you how efficiently your team generates revenue.
- [ ] **Is it improving or declining?** — If revenue per head is dropping as you hire, your growth is creating complexity faster than value. This number should hold steady or improve as you scale.

## Expected Outcome

- 6 critical numbers you can state from memory
- A clear picture of whether your business is financially healthy or just busy
- Early warning signals visible (low margin, short runway, declining revenue per head)
- Confidence in financial conversations with your accountant, bank, or business partner
- The foundation for Reading Your P&L and Break-Even Analysis

## Coach Notes

Start every coaching session with "What are your numbers this month?" It takes 2 minutes and sets the tone for a data-driven conversation rather than a feelings-driven one.

The most common revelation: clients who think they are profitable discover their gross margin is lower than expected, or their break-even is higher than they thought. This is not bad news — it is useful news. You cannot fix what you cannot see.

If a client cannot find these numbers, the first action is not financial literacy — it is getting their bookkeeping sorted (which is a Level 2 action). For now, even rough estimates are better than nothing.
