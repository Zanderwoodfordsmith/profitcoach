# Break-Even Calculator

## What to Do

Calculate your exact break-even point — the minimum revenue you need each month just to keep the doors open. Then use it as your baseline for every financial decision: hiring, spending, pricing, and growth.

## Why It Matters

Break-even is the single most clarifying number in your business. Above it, you are making money. Below it, you are losing money. Most business owners cannot state this number, which means every spending decision is based on hope rather than maths. Once you know your break-even, a question like "can I afford to hire?" becomes simple arithmetic instead of anxiety.

## How to Use This

You need two inputs: your monthly fixed costs and your gross margin percentage. If you have done the Know Your Numbers action, you already have both.

---

## Calculate Your Break-Even

- [ ] **Enter your total monthly fixed costs** — Everything that gets paid regardless of sales: rent, salaries, insurance, subscriptions, loan repayments, utilities. This is the number from Know Your Numbers.
- [ ] **Enter your gross margin percentage** — (Revenue − Direct Costs) ÷ Revenue × 100. If your revenue is £50K and direct costs are £20K, your gross margin is 60%.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Calculate: Fixed Costs ÷ Gross Margin % = Monthly Break-Even Revenue** — Example: £15,000 fixed costs ÷ 0.60 gross margin = £25,000 break-even. You need £25K in revenue every month before you earn a penny of profit.
- [ ] **Convert to units** — How many clients, projects, or sales is that? £25K break-even ÷ £2,000 average sale = 12.5 sales per month. Now you know exactly how many deals you need.

## Stress Test It

- [ ] **What happens if you lose your biggest client?** — Subtract their revenue. Are you still above break-even? If one client loss puts you below break-even, you have a concentration risk problem.
- [ ] **What happens if you hire one person?** — Add their full cost (salary + NI + pension + equipment) to your fixed costs. Recalculate. How much additional revenue does the hire need to generate just to cover themselves?
- [ ] **What happens if your margin drops 10%?** — Recalculate with a lower margin. This shows how sensitive your break-even is to pricing or cost changes. Some businesses have so little margin cushion that a small change makes them unprofitable.
- [ ] **What happens if you raise prices 10%?** — Recalculate. How many fewer sales would you need? Often the maths reveals that a price increase with a small volume drop is more profitable than the current situation.

## Set Your Targets

- [ ] **Your break-even is your floor, not your target** — Profitable businesses aim for revenue at least 20-30% above break-even. That buffer funds growth, builds reserves, and absorbs surprises.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Write your break-even number on a sticky note and put it where you see it daily** — Not to create anxiety. To create awareness. Every deal you close above this number is profit. Every month below it is a withdrawal from your future.
- [ ] **Review quarterly** — Your fixed costs change (new hires, rent increases, new tools). Your margin changes (pricing, efficiency, supplier costs). Recalculate every quarter to keep the number accurate.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Your exact monthly break-even revenue, stated with confidence
- The number of clients or sales you need per month to stay profitable
- Clarity on the financial impact of hiring, pricing, and spending decisions
- A stress-tested view of how resilient your business is to changes
- A baseline that makes every financial decision simpler

## Coach Notes

This is one of the most powerful 15-minute exercises in the entire Profit System. Clients who have never calculated their break-even often have a moment of genuine clarity — either relief ("we're healthier than I thought") or alarm ("we're closer to the edge than I realised"). Both are valuable.

Use the stress tests to make strategic conversations concrete. Instead of "should I hire someone?" the conversation becomes "hiring adds £3,500/month to fixed costs, which raises break-even by £5,800/month. Can you generate that?" Maths beats gut feel.

If a client's break-even is very close to their current revenue, that is an urgent signal. They have no margin for error. Address pricing or costs before pursuing growth — growing a business with no margin just creates a bigger problem.
