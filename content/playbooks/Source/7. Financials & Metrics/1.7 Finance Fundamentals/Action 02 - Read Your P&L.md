# Read Your P&L

## What to Do

Open your latest Profit & Loss statement and learn to read it — what each section means, what to look for, and how to spot problems or opportunities in under 5 minutes.

## Why It Matters

Your P&L tells the story of your business — where money comes in, where it goes, and what is left. Most business owners either ignore it entirely (leaving it to the accountant) or glance at the bottom line without understanding the detail. Learning to read your P&L is not about becoming an accountant — it is about having a 5-minute conversation with your numbers every month that could save you thousands.

## How to Use This

Get your latest P&L from your bookkeeper, accountant, or accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, FreshBooks). Open it in front of you and work through this action step by step.

---

## Understand the Structure

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Find these 4 lines on your P&L** — Revenue (top line), Cost of Sales / Direct Costs, Gross Profit, and Net Profit (bottom line). These are the only 4 lines you need to understand to start.
- [ ] **Revenue** — Everything your business earned in the period. Check: does this match what you expected? Any surprises up or down?
- [ ] **Cost of Sales / Direct Costs** — What it cost to deliver your product or service. Materials, subcontractor fees, direct labour. These go up and down with revenue.
- [ ] **Gross Profit** — Revenue minus Cost of Sales. This is what you have left to run the business after paying for delivery. If this number is thin, no amount of cost-cutting elsewhere will save you.
- [ ] **Overheads / Operating Expenses** — Everything else: rent, admin, marketing, software, salaries (non-delivery), insurance. These are relatively fixed.
- [ ] **Net Profit** — What is left after everything. This is what the business actually earned.

## The 5-Minute Monthly Check

Do this every month when your P&L is ready:

- [ ] **Is revenue up, down, or flat versus last month?** — And versus the same month last year if you have the data. One bad month is noise. Three declining months is a trend.
- [ ] **Is gross margin holding?** — Calculate Gross Profit ÷ Revenue. Compare to last month. If gross margin is dropping, you are either charging less or your delivery costs are rising. Both need attention.
- [ ] **Are any overhead categories growing faster than revenue?** — Scan the expense categories. If marketing spend doubled but revenue did not increase, that is a signal. If software costs creep up every month, something is being added without being questioned.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Circle the 3 largest expense lines** — Are they earning their place? Could any be reduced without affecting quality or revenue? Often the biggest expenses are the ones that go longest without being questioned.
- [ ] **Is net profit where you expected?** — If not, the explanation is always in the lines above. Either revenue was lower, costs were higher, or overheads grew. The P&L tells you which one.

## What to Watch For

- [ ] **Revenue growing but profit shrinking** — This means your costs are growing faster than your revenue. Common during expansion if not managed. Growth without profit is just more expensive chaos.
- [ ] **Gross margin below 50%** — For most service businesses, this is a warning. It means more than half of every pound goes to delivery before you pay for anything else.
- [ ] **One client representing more than 25% of revenue** — Not always visible on a P&L, but worth checking. If one client disappears, how bad is the damage? Concentration risk is a hidden threat.

## Expected Outcome

- Ability to read your P&L in under 5 minutes and know whether the business is healthy
- A monthly habit of reviewing your numbers that catches problems early
- Confidence in conversations with your accountant — you understand what they are telling you
- Clear signals about where to focus: pricing, costs, overhead, or revenue growth

## Coach Notes

Many clients are embarrassed that they do not understand their P&L. Normalise this — most business owners were never taught financial literacy. The coaching value is making it simple, not making them feel stupid.

Do this together in a session with their actual P&L on screen. Walk through each line. Ask them what surprises them. The conversation that follows will reveal more about their business health than an hour of general discussion.

If their P&L is a mess or months out of date, the problem is bookkeeping, not financial literacy. Flag it and address it separately — but do not let "my books aren't ready" become an excuse to avoid learning this.
