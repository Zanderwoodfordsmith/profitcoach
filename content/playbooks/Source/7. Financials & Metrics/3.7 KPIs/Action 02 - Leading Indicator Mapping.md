# Map Your Leading Indicators

## What to Do

For each of your critical lag indicators (revenue, profit, retention), identify the leading indicator that predicts it. Lag indicators tell you what already happened. Leading indicators tell you what is about to happen. This exercise builds your early warning system so you can act before problems show up in the financial results.

## Why It Matters

Revenue this month is a result of actions taken weeks or months ago. By the time your revenue drops, the damage was done long before you saw it. Leading indicators give you a chance to intervene early. If your pipeline is thinning, you know revenue will dip in 60 days. If customer engagement is dropping, you know churn will rise next quarter. The businesses that react fastest are the ones watching the right leading indicators.

## How to Use This

Take each lag indicator from your Critical Metrics Audit and work backwards through the cause chain to find the earliest measurable signal.

---

## Revenue Leading Indicators

- [ ] **Map your revenue cause chain** - Work backwards: Revenue comes from closed deals. Closed deals come from proposals. Proposals come from discovery calls. Discovery calls come from leads. What is the earliest point in that chain you can measure?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Identify the one number that best predicts next month's revenue** - For most businesses this is pipeline value, proposals sent, or booked calls. Pick one and start tracking it this week.
- [ ] **Calculate your conversion rates between stages** - Leads to calls, calls to proposals, proposals to closed. These ratios tell you whether your pipeline is healthy or just full.
- [ ] **Determine the typical time lag** - How many days or weeks between a lead entering and revenue arriving? This tells you how far ahead your leading indicator can see.

## Profitability Leading Indicators

- [ ] **Identify what drives your costs before they hit the P&L** - Overtime hours, project scope changes, supplier price increases, or hiring decisions. Which of these can you spot earliest?
- [ ] **Track gross margin per project or engagement in real time** - Do not wait for month-end accounts. If you know margin is slipping on active work, you can adjust before it becomes a quarterly problem.
- [ ] **Monitor your pricing win rate** - If you are winning every deal, your prices are too low. If you are losing most, they are too high. A sudden change in win rate signals a margin problem coming.

## Customer Health Leading Indicators

- [ ] **Define what "engaged" looks like** - Login frequency, session attendance, support ticket volume, email open rates, or purchase frequency. Pick the behaviour that best predicts whether a customer will stay.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Flag every customer whose engagement dropped in the last 30 days** - This is your at-risk list. These customers are more likely to churn unless someone reaches out.
- [ ] **Track complaint or issue volume** - A spike in support tickets or complaints is an early signal that something is wrong with your product, service, or delivery.

## Capacity Leading Indicators

- [ ] **Monitor your team's workload pipeline** - How much committed work is coming in the next 2-4 weeks? If capacity is filling up, you need to act now, not when deadlines start slipping.
- [ ] **Track your recruitment pipeline** - If you anticipate needing to hire in 3 months, the leading indicator is whether you are building a candidate pipeline today.
- [ ] **Document your leading indicator dashboard** - Write each leading indicator alongside the lag indicator it predicts, the data source, and the threshold that triggers action.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Every critical lag metric paired with at least one leading indicator
- Clear understanding of the time lag between leading and lagging signals
- An early warning system that gives you weeks of notice before problems appear in results
- Specific thresholds defined for when a leading indicator requires intervention
- The ability to take action proactively instead of reacting to bad news

## Coach Notes

This is one of the most powerful exercises in the entire system, but it requires the client to think causally, not just descriptively. Most business owners are used to looking at results. This forces them to think about what creates those results.

A common trap is picking leading indicators that are too far removed from the outcome. "Social media posts per week" is technically a leading indicator for revenue, but the connection is so loose it is not useful. Push for indicators with a direct, measurable link. "Discovery calls booked this week" is far more predictive of next month's revenue than "blog posts published."
