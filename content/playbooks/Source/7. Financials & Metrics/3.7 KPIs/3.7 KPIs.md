## **What This Is**

Numbers drive behaviour. When you measure what matters, people start paying attention to what matters. But most businesses track the wrong metrics, track too many metrics, or track nothing consistently. They drown in data but starve for insight.

This playbook is about choosing the right numbers to measure and creating the visibility that drives performance. Not a dashboard with fifty charts that nobody looks at. A focused set of KPIs that everyone understands, checks regularly, and acts on when they move in the wrong direction.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You have no idea which metrics actually predict success in your business. You measure things because they are easy to measure, not because they matter. Different people track different numbers and nobody can agree what "good" looks like. You only discover problems weeks after they happened when it is too late to fix them.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You track some metrics but they change constantly and nobody is really accountable for moving them. You have data but struggle to turn it into decisions. Your team knows some numbers but not why they matter or what happens when they hit their targets.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You have 3-5 core metrics that everyone understands and checks weekly. Each KPI clearly predicts business success and each person knows exactly which numbers they own. When metrics move in the wrong direction, you spot it quickly and act immediately. The numbers drive behaviour because they drive decisions.

---

## **Things to Think About**

Which 3-5 numbers actually predict your success? Most businesses could run on five metrics or fewer. Revenue, gross margin, customer acquisition cost, customer retention rate, cash flow. Pick the ones that move first when your business gets healthier or sicker. Everything else is noise.

Do your metrics predict the future or report the past? Leading indicators tell you what is coming. Lag indicators tell you what already happened. You need both, but leading indicators are more valuable because they give you time to act. What are the early warning signs in your business?

Does everyone know the score? Metrics only work if the people who can influence them actually see them. Your team should know the key numbers as well as they know the weather. Is the information visible, current, and impossible to ignore?

Are your targets specific and time-bound? "Increase revenue" is not a KPI. "Grow monthly recurring revenue from £50k to £60k by end of Q2" is a KPI. Vague targets get vague results. Specific targets get specific actions.

When and how do you review the numbers? Metrics without rhythm become wallpaper. Whether it is daily, weekly, or monthly, you need a consistent time when everyone looks at the numbers, discusses what they mean, and decides what to do about them.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Critical Metrics Audit — Review everything you currently measure and identify the 3-5 numbers that actually predict business success. Cut everything else.

Play 2: Leading Indicator Mapping — For each lag indicator you track, identify the leading indicator that predicts it. Build your early warning system.

Play 3: KPI Scorecard — Create a simple, visual scorecard showing your core metrics, targets, and current performance. One page that tells you everything you need to know.

Play 4: Team Visibility System — Make sure every person knows which metrics they influence and can see their numbers in real time. Transparency drives performance.

Play 5: Review Rhythm — Install a weekly rhythm for looking at the numbers, discussing trends, and making adjustments. Metrics without action are just data.
