# Create Your KPI Scorecard

## What to Do

Build a simple, visual one-page scorecard that shows your core metrics, targets, and current performance at a glance. This is the single document you look at every week to know whether the business is on track. No logins required, no complex dashboards, no data analysis. One page that tells you everything you need to know.

## Why It Matters

The best metrics in the world are useless if nobody looks at them. Most businesses have data scattered across multiple tools - accounting software, CRM, marketing platforms, spreadsheets. The KPI scorecard pulls the critical numbers into one place so they cannot be ignored. When the numbers are visible, they get discussed. When they get discussed, they get improved.

## How to Use This

Take the 3-5 critical metrics from your audit and leading indicators from your mapping exercise. Build them into the scorecard format below.

---

## Scorecard Design

- [ ] **Choose your format** - A spreadsheet works fine. Google Sheets for shared access, Excel if you prefer. Do not buy new software for this. The simplest tool you will actually update is the right one.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Set up a single row for each KPI with these columns** - Metric name, owner, target, actual (this week), actual (last week), trend (up/down/flat), and status (green/amber/red). That is all you need.
- [ ] **Add your 3-5 critical lag indicators** - Revenue, profit margin, retention rate, or whatever you identified in your Critical Metrics Audit.
- [ ] **Add your leading indicators beneath each lag indicator** - Pipeline value beneath revenue, engagement score beneath retention. This shows cause and effect at a glance.
- [ ] **Set clear green/amber/red thresholds for each metric** - Green: on or above target. Amber: within 10% of target. Red: more than 10% below target. Define these before you start tracking so there is no debate later.

## Making It Visual

- [ ] **Use conditional formatting or colour coding** - The scorecard should be readable in 30 seconds. Colours tell you where to focus without reading every number.
- [ ] **Add a 4-week trend line or sparkline for each metric** - A single number is a snapshot. A trend tells a story. Is this metric improving, stable, or declining?
- [ ] **Include a "notes" column** - Space for brief context. "Pipeline down due to bank holiday week" prevents overreaction to normal fluctuations.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Print it out or display it on a screen in your workspace** - Physical visibility beats digital accessibility. If it is on the wall, it gets looked at.

## Ownership and Updates

- [ ] **Assign one person to update the scorecard weekly** - This is a 10-minute task, not a project. The same person updates it every week on the same day.
- [ ] **Set a fixed update day** - Monday morning or Friday afternoon works best. Pick one and stick to it.
- [ ] **Build the scorecard into your weekly meeting agenda** - The first 5 minutes of your weekly meeting should be a scorecard review. Numbers first, then discussion.
- [ ] **Version-control your targets** - When you change a target, keep the old one visible so you can see progress over time. Do not just overwrite.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A single-page scorecard showing every critical metric, target, and current status
- Visual colour coding that makes problems impossible to miss
- Trend data showing whether metrics are moving in the right direction
- Clear ownership for updating and maintaining the scorecard
- A scorecard that is reviewed weekly and drives real decisions

## Coach Notes

The most common failure mode is building a beautiful scorecard that nobody updates after week 3. The key is making the update process painless. If it takes more than 10 minutes to update, it is too complicated. Strip it back.

Push the client to start ugly and iterate. A Google Sheet with 5 rows and manual data entry is better than a complex dashboard that needs API connections. Get the habit of weekly review established first, then improve the tools later.

If the client already has dashboards in their CRM or accounting software, do not duplicate that data. The scorecard should only contain the 3-5 numbers that are not already visible in one place. The goal is consolidation, not another layer of reporting.
