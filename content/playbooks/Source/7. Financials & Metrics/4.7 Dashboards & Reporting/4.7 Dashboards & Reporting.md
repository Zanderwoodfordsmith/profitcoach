## **What This Is**

Visibility protects performance. When you cannot see the business clearly, problems hide until they become crises. When you can see everything at a glance, you catch issues early and make decisions based on facts, not feelings.

This playbook is about building the dashboards, scorecards, and reports that give you complete visibility into business health. Not just numbers for the sake of numbers — the right metrics, presented clearly, that help you lead without having to dig into spreadsheets every time someone asks "how are we doing?"

This sits at Level 4 because you need working systems and a team before dashboards become essential. If you are still doing everything yourself, you already know what is broken. When you are leading through others, visibility becomes survival.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You get surprised by problems that have been building for weeks. You ask "how are we doing?" and nobody can answer without spending an hour pulling numbers from different systems. You make decisions based on gut feel because the data is either missing, wrong, or impossible to access. Monthly financials arrive weeks after month-end and by then it is too late to change anything.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have some reports but they are manual, inconsistent, or take too long to produce. Key metrics exist but they are scattered across different places and you have to hunt for them. Your team knows the numbers exist but they do not check them regularly. You react to trends after they have already changed direction.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You can assess the health of your business in under five minutes every morning. Key metrics are automatically collected and presented in clean, visual dashboards. Your team refers to the numbers when making decisions and can spot problems before they escalate. You review trends weekly and adjust quickly. Exception alerts flag anomalies so nothing falls through the cracks. You lead with confidence because you see everything clearly.

---

## **Things to Think About**

Can you assess business performance in under five minutes? That is the test of good dashboard design. If it takes longer than that to see where you stand, your dashboards are either too complex or measuring the wrong things.

Do your metrics tie back to your strategic intent? Every number you track should connect to something you are trying to achieve. If a metric does not drive a decision, it is clutter. Ruthless focus on what matters versus what is interesting to know.

Is your data collection automatic or manual? Manual reporting dies the moment you get busy. If someone has to remember to update a spreadsheet, it will be wrong within a month. Automation turns reporting from a chore into a competitive advantage.

Would your team make different decisions if they saw the numbers daily? The best dashboards change behaviour, not just inform. If your numbers exist in a vacuum, they are not working. They should drive action at every level.

Do you spot problems while you can still fix them? The purpose of tracking is not historical record-keeping — it is early warning. Trends matter more than snapshots. Exception reporting matters more than routine updates.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Executive Dashboard Design — Build a single-screen view of the business that answers "are we winning?" in seconds. Revenue, profit, cash, customers, team — the vital signs that matter most to the owner.

Play 2: Department Scorecards — Create focused dashboards for each area of the business so team members can see their performance and take ownership of their results. Sales, operations, marketing, finance — each gets their own scorecard.

Play 3: Exception Reporting System — Set up alerts that flag anomalies before they become problems. Automatic notifications when key metrics move outside normal ranges so you can act fast.

Play 4: Weekly Review Rhythm — Install a weekly cadence to review trends, discuss variances, and adjust course. Turn dashboard data into decisions through structured review meetings.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Pick your five most important business metrics — the ones that would change how you run the business if they moved significantly. Write them down and check where you can find each one today.

This week: Set up one simple automated report that saves you time. Even if it is just a weekly email with key numbers from your accounting system, automation starts building the habit.

This week: Look at your key metrics from the last 12 weeks. Identify one clear trend — up, down, or flat. Ask yourself what decision that trend should drive.
