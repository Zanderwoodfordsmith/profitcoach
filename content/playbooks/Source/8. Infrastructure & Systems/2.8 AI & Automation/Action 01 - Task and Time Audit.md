# Task and Time Audit for Automation

## What to Do

Map exactly where your time goes on manual, repetitive work and identify the highest-impact automation opportunities based on time saved and effort to implement. This is similar to the Time & Energy audit (2.0) but focused specifically on finding tasks that technology can handle instead of you.

## Why It Matters

Automation works best when you automate the right things. Most businesses either automate nothing (because it feels overwhelming) or automate something complex and impressive that saves 20 minutes per month. The biggest wins are usually the boring, repetitive tasks you do every day without thinking about them. This audit identifies those high-frequency, low-value tasks so you automate for maximum impact.

## How to Use This

List your recurring tasks, score them on automation potential, and prioritise the best candidates.

---

## Task Inventory

- [ ] List every task you or your team does on a recurring basis (daily, weekly, monthly). Aim for 20-30 items.
- [ ] For each task, note: how often it happens, how long it takes each time, and who currently does it
- [ ] Calculate the total time per month for each task (frequency x duration)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Identify the task you personally do most often that involves copying data from one place to another. This is almost certainly automatable.

## Score Each Task for Automation Potential

Rate each task on three criteria (1-5 scale):

- [ ] **Repetitiveness:** Is the task the same every time (5) or different every time (1)?
- [ ] **Rule-based:** Can the task be described with clear rules and if/then logic (5) or does it require judgment and creativity (1)?
- [ ] **Digital:** Does the task happen on a computer using digital tools (5) or is it physical/relationship-based (1)?

Calculate: Repetitiveness + Rule-based + Digital = Automation Score (out of 15)

- [ ] Tasks scoring 12-15: Strong automation candidates
- [ ] Tasks scoring 8-11: Possible automation with some setup
- [ ] Tasks scoring 7 or below: Better suited for delegation than automation

## Prioritise by Impact

- [ ] Sort your strong candidates (12-15 score) by monthly time saved
- [ ] Estimate the setup effort for each: Quick (under 1 hour), Moderate (1-4 hours), or Complex (4+ hours)
- [ ] Create your priority list: high time saved + quick setup = do first
- [ ] Pick your top 3 automation candidates to focus on

## Common Automation Opportunities

Review this list and check any that apply to your business:

- [ ] New enquiry notifications and auto-responses
- [ ] Data entry from one system to another (email to CRM, form to spreadsheet)
- [ ] Sending follow-up emails after specific events (meeting booked, invoice sent, onboarding started)
- [ ] Social media posting from a scheduled queue
- [ ] Invoice creation and sending
- [ ] Report generation and distribution
- [ ] Calendar scheduling and reminders
- [ ] File organisation and backup
- [ ] Customer feedback collection at set intervals

## Expected Outcome

- A complete inventory of recurring tasks with time estimates
- Each task scored for automation potential
- A prioritised list of the top 3 automation opportunities
- A clear picture of how much time automation could reclaim per month
- A starting point for the workflow automation work in Actions 3 and 4

## Coach Notes

This audit often reveals that the owner spends 5-10 hours per week on tasks that could be fully automated. The emotional response is usually a mix of frustration ("I have been doing this manually for years") and overwhelm ("I do not know how to automate anything"). Reassure them: start with one. The first automation win builds confidence and momentum. Common first wins: auto-responding to enquiries, scheduling social media, and automating invoice reminders. These can each be set up in under an hour with tools like Zapier, Mailchimp, or built-in features of their existing software.
