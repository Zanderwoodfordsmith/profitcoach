# CRM Implementation

## What to Do

Set up a simple customer relationship management system so all client data, communication history, and next actions live in one place instead of your memory, your inbox, and scattered spreadsheets. This does not need to be expensive or complex. A basic CRM used consistently beats a sophisticated one that nobody touches.

## Why It Matters

When client information lives in your head, three things happen: you forget things (follow-ups, preferences, conversation history), nothing is transferable (if you are unavailable, nobody else can help the client), and you cannot see patterns (which clients are at risk, which prospects need chasing, where deals are getting stuck). A CRM solves all three. It is your business's memory system. And unlike your memory, it does not lose things after a busy week.

## How to Use This

Choose a CRM, set it up with the basics, migrate your existing data, and build the daily habit of using it.

---

## Choose Your CRM

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: If you have fewer than 50 contacts and need something today, start with a well-structured Google Sheet or Notion database. A simple system you use beats a fancy system you do not.
- [ ] For a proper CRM, evaluate based on your needs:
  - **Simple and free:** HubSpot Free, Zoho Free, or Notion
  - **Simple and paid:** Pipedrive, Close, or Capsule (£15-30/month)
  - **All-in-one:** HubSpot, GoHighLevel, or Keap (includes email marketing, automation)
- [ ] Choose based on: ease of use (will you actually open it daily?), integration with your email and calendar, and cost
- [ ] Do NOT over-research. Pick one that seems right and commit for 90 days. You can switch later.

## Initial Setup (2-3 Hours)

- [ ] Create your pipeline stages (match your actual sales process):
  - Example: New Lead > Contacted > Meeting Booked > Proposal Sent > Negotiation > Won > Lost
- [ ] Set up your contact fields: Name, Company, Email, Phone, Source (how they found you), Status, Last Contact Date, Next Action, Notes
- [ ] Import your existing contacts: export from your spreadsheet, phone, or email and import into the CRM
- [ ] Connect your email if the CRM supports it (auto-logs email conversations)
- [ ] Connect your calendar if supported (auto-logs meetings)
- [ ] Create a simple dashboard view: what needs attention today?

## Build the Daily Habit

- [ ] Start every working day by opening your CRM and checking: who needs a follow-up today?
- [ ] After every client call, meeting, or significant email: update the contact record (2 minutes max)
- [ ] Log the key point from every conversation: what was discussed, what was agreed, what is the next action
- [ ] Move deals through pipeline stages as they progress
- [ ] Every Friday: review your pipeline. Which deals are stuck? Which leads have gone cold? Who needs chasing?

## Make It Useful

- [ ] Set up reminders for follow-ups so nothing falls through the cracks
- [ ] Use tags or categories to segment contacts: Hot Lead, Current Client, Past Client, Partner, etc.
- [ ] Track how leads found you (Source field) so you can measure which channels produce results
- [ ] After 30 days, review: is the CRM helping you stay on top of relationships? What is missing?
- [ ] After 90 days, consider adding automation: auto-emails when a deal moves to a new stage, reminder sequences for cold leads, etc.

---

## Expected Outcome

- All client and prospect data in one accessible place
- Communication history logged and searchable
- A clear pipeline showing where every opportunity stands
- Follow-ups happening on time because the system reminds you
- The ability to hand off client relationships to team members without losing context

## Coach Notes

CRM resistance is common: "I already know my clients." Challenge: "What happens when you go on holiday? What happens when you hire someone? What if a client calls and you cannot remember what you discussed 3 months ago?" The daily habit is everything - a CRM is only as good as the data in it. The first 2 weeks are the hardest as the habit forms. Check in every session: "Are you updating your CRM daily?" If not, diagnose why. Usually it is too complex (simplify) or too slow (switch tools). For most businesses under £1M, Pipedrive or HubSpot Free are the sweet spot.
