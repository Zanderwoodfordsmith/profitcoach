# AI Content and Analysis Tools

## What to Do

Implement AI tools for content creation, customer analysis, and routine decision support. This is about using AI as an accelerator - not a replacement for thinking, but a tool that gives you first drafts instead of blank pages, insights instead of raw data, and quick answers instead of hours of research.

## Why It Matters

AI tools have reached the point where they can genuinely save hours per week for a small business owner. Writing a proposal from scratch takes 2 hours; editing an AI-generated first draft takes 30 minutes. Analysing customer feedback manually takes an afternoon; asking AI to identify themes takes 5 minutes. The business owners who learn to use AI effectively will have a significant time and quality advantage over those who do not. This is not about becoming a tech expert. It is about knowing which tools to use and when.

## How to Use This

Identify where AI can help most in your business, choose your tools, and start using them this week.

## Identify Your AI Opportunities

- [ ] Review these categories and tick every task where you could use AI help:

### Content Creation
- [ ] Writing social media posts
- [ ] Drafting emails to clients
- [ ] Creating proposals or quotes
- [ ] Writing blog posts or articles
- [ ] Creating marketing copy
- [ ] Writing job descriptions
- [ ] Producing meeting agendas and summaries

### Analysis and Research
- [ ] Summarising customer feedback
- [ ] Analysing competitor offerings
- [ ] Researching industry trends
- [ ] Reviewing financial data for patterns
- [ ] Identifying themes across client conversations

### Communication and Response
- [ ] Drafting responses to common questions
- [ ] Creating template emails for recurring situations
- [ ] Preparing for difficult conversations
- [ ] Writing standard operating procedures

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Pick the one task from the list above that you do most frequently. Use an AI tool for it today. Even a basic prompt like "Write a first draft of [thing]" will show you the potential.

## Choose Your Tools

- [ ] **General AI assistant:** ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini (for writing, analysis, brainstorming, and research)
- [ ] **Email AI:** Built-in features in Gmail or Outlook, or Superhuman (for faster email drafting and responses)
- [ ] **Content AI:** Jasper, Copy.ai, or just ChatGPT/Claude (for marketing copy, social posts, and long-form content)
- [ ] **Meeting AI:** Otter.ai, Fireflies, or Fathom (for automatic meeting transcription and summaries)
- [ ] Start with ONE general tool (ChatGPT or Claude) and learn to use it well before adding specialised tools

## Build the AI Habit

- [ ] Set a rule: "Before I start writing anything from scratch, I ask AI for a first draft"
- [ ] Save your best prompts: when you find a prompt that produces great results, save it for reuse
- [ ] Create prompt templates for your recurring tasks:
  - "Write a follow-up email to a client who [situation]. Keep it professional and [tone]."
  - "Summarise these meeting notes into action items with owners and deadlines."
  - "Draft a social media post about [topic] for [audience]. Keep it under 200 words."
- [ ] Spend 15 minutes per week experimenting with new ways AI can help

## Quality Control

- [ ] Never send AI output without reviewing and editing it - AI is your starting point, not your finish line
- [ ] Check facts: AI can generate plausible-sounding but incorrect information. Verify anything important.
- [ ] Maintain your voice: edit AI drafts to sound like you, not like a robot
- [ ] Be transparent where appropriate: some contexts require disclosure that AI was used
- [ ] Track time saved: after 30 days, estimate how many hours AI has saved you per week

## Expected Outcome

- AI tools integrated into your daily workflow for content, analysis, and communication
- First drafts produced in minutes instead of hours
- Routine analysis and summarisation handled in seconds
- A library of reusable prompts for your most common tasks
- Measurable time savings that free you for higher-value work

## Coach Notes

AI adoption varies hugely by client. Some are already using ChatGPT daily; others have never tried it. Meet them where they are. For beginners, start with one simple use case: "Use ChatGPT to draft your next client email." Let them experience the time saving before expanding. For more advanced users, push toward prompt engineering and integration (using AI within their existing tools). The most important principle to reinforce: AI generates first drafts, humans create final versions. Clients who skip the editing step produce generic, impersonal content that can damage their brand. The sweet spot is AI speed with human judgment and personality layered on top.
