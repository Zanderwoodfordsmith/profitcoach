# Requirements Audit

## What to Do

Question every business requirement and eliminate those that cannot be justified. Every process step, approval layer, report, meeting, and practice gets challenged with one question: "Why do we do this?" If the answer is "because we always have" or "I'm not sure," that requirement is a candidate for elimination. This is the starting point of optimisation - before you can make things better, you need to remove things that should not exist.

## Why It Matters

Businesses accumulate requirements like houses accumulate clutter. A process step gets added to fix a problem that no longer exists. An approval layer gets introduced after one mistake and stays forever. A report gets created for a manager who left two years ago. Over time, these requirements slow everything down, frustrate your team, and cost money without producing value. Elon Musk's first principle of engineering applies: "The best part is no part. The best process is no process." Elimination is always more powerful than optimisation. There is no point making a useless step faster.

## How to Use This

Work through your major business areas and challenge every requirement you find.

---

## Challenge Your Process Requirements

- [ ] **Pick your 3 most time-consuming processes** - The ones that eat the most hours each week. List every step in each process.
- [ ] **For each step, ask: "What would happen if we stopped doing this?"** - If the honest answer is "nothing bad," the step should go. If the answer is "something might go wrong," ask how often that something actually happens.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Identify one step in a daily process that everyone knows is pointless but nobody has removed. Remove it today. The time savings compound instantly.
- [ ] **Challenge every handoff** - Each time work passes from one person to another, there is a delay and a risk of error. Can any handoffs be eliminated by letting one person complete the full task?
- [ ] **Challenge every approval layer** - Who needs to approve what? Is the approval adding value or just adding delay? If someone is approving things and never saying no, the approval is theatre.

## Challenge Your Reporting Requirements

- [ ] **List every report your business produces** - Weekly reports, monthly reports, dashboards, status updates, client reports.
- [ ] **For each report, ask: "Who reads this and what decision does it drive?"** - If nobody reads it, or if nobody changes behaviour based on it, the report is waste.
- [ ] **Check for duplicate data** - Are multiple reports showing the same information in different formats? Consolidate or eliminate.
- [ ] **Ask your team: "Which reports do you create that feel like a waste of time?"** - They know. They have just not been asked.

## Challenge Your Meeting Requirements

- [ ] **List every recurring meeting** - Weekly stand-ups, monthly reviews, team meetings, client calls, board meetings.
- [ ] **For each meeting, ask: "Could this be an email, a message, or a 5-minute check-in instead?"** - Most recurring meetings were created for a reason but have outlived that reason.
- [ ] **Check attendance lists** - Are people in meetings where they add nothing and gain nothing? Let them leave.
- [ ] **Challenge meeting length** - If a meeting is scheduled for an hour, could it achieve the same outcome in 30 minutes with a tighter agenda?

## Challenge Your Administrative Requirements

- [ ] **Review every form, template, and document your team fills in** - Are all the fields necessary? Are all the documents still relevant?
- [ ] **Check for "just in case" requirements** - Tasks done "just in case" something goes wrong. Calculate the actual frequency of the risk. If it happens once a year, doing a daily prevention task is disproportionate.
- [ ] **Review compliance and regulatory requirements** - Some requirements are genuine and non-negotiable. But many businesses over-comply because nobody has checked what is actually required versus what someone assumed was required.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A list of process steps, reports, meetings, and admin tasks that have been challenged
- Unjustified requirements identified and marked for elimination
- Immediate time savings from removing obvious waste
- A leaner business that moves faster because it has less unnecessary work
- A mindset shift: the team starts questioning "why" before adding new requirements

## Coach Notes

The biggest resistance is fear. Clients worry that removing a step will cause problems. Help them see that most requirements were added reactively (in response to one incident) and never reviewed. The question is not "could this ever be useful?" but "does this justify the time it costs every single week?"

Start with the easy wins. Every business has at least 3-5 things that everyone knows are pointless. Eliminating these builds confidence for the harder decisions later.

Watch for clients who challenge requirements in theory but struggle to actually remove them. Set a target: eliminate at least 5 requirements this quarter. Make it concrete and track it. The habit of elimination is as valuable as any individual change.
