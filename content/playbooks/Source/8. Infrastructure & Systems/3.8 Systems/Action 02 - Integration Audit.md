# Audit Your Integrations

## What to Do

Review every tool and platform your business uses, identify where information should flow automatically but currently requires manual input, and prioritise the integrations that would save the most time. Most small businesses are running 10-20 software tools that barely talk to each other. The result is hours of manual data transfer every week, errors that nobody catches, and a team that spends more time feeding systems than serving customers.

## Why It Matters

Every time someone copies data from one system to another, three things happen: it takes time, it introduces the chance of error, and it creates a delay. Multiply that by dozens of manual transfers happening every week and you have a significant hidden cost. Integration is not about buying more technology. It is about making the technology you already have work together so your team can focus on the work that actually matters.

## How to Use This

Start with a complete inventory of your tools, then map the data flows between them to find the gaps.

---

## Tool Inventory

- [ ] **List every software tool and platform your business uses** - CRM, accounting, email marketing, project management, communication, file storage, scheduling, invoicing, analytics. Include everything, even the small ones.
- [ ] **For each tool, note: who uses it, what data it holds, and what it costs** - This often reveals forgotten subscriptions and duplicate tools doing the same job.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Identify any tools you are paying for but nobody uses regularly** - Cancel them today. Most businesses have 2-3 zombie subscriptions costing money for nothing.
- [ ] **Flag any tools where the same data exists in multiple places** - Customer contact details in your CRM, email tool, and invoicing software. That is three places the same information can be wrong.

## Data Flow Analysis

- [ ] **Map how data moves between your tools** - When a new lead comes in, what happens? Does it get entered into the CRM automatically, or does someone type it in? When an invoice gets paid, does your accounting software know, or does someone reconcile manually?
- [ ] **Identify every manual data transfer** - Each one is a candidate for automation. List them all with an estimate of how long each takes per week.
- [ ] **Mark the transfers that cause the most errors** - Where do things go wrong? Mismatched customer details, missed invoices, leads that fall through the cracks. These are your priority fixes.
- [ ] **Check what native integrations already exist** - Most popular tools connect natively or through Zapier, Make, or similar platforms. Before building anything custom, check what is already available out of the box.

## Prioritise and Plan

- [ ] **Rank integrations by time saved per week** - A transfer that takes 5 minutes daily (25 minutes per week) multiplied by 50 weeks is over 20 hours per year. That adds up fast.
- [ ] **Rank integrations by error risk** - Some manual transfers are low-risk (copying a name). Others are high-risk (entering financial data). Prioritise the high-risk ones even if they do not save the most time.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Pick the single easiest integration to set up this week** - Usually connecting your form tool to your CRM, or your invoicing tool to your accounting software. One Zapier connection, 15 minutes, done.
- [ ] **Create a phased integration plan** - Not everything needs to happen at once. Quick wins this week, medium-effort integrations this month, complex ones this quarter.
- [ ] **Estimate costs for each integration** - Some are free (native connections). Some need middleware (Zapier at £20-50/month). Some need custom work. Know the cost before you commit.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A complete inventory of every tool and platform in the business with costs
- A clear map of where data flows automatically and where it requires manual input
- Zombie subscriptions identified and cancelled
- A prioritised list of integrations to build, ranked by time saved and error risk
- At least one quick-win integration set up and running

## Coach Notes

This action often triggers "shiny object syndrome" where the client wants to replace everything with one perfect system. Redirect them. The goal is not to change tools. It is to connect the tools you have. New tools come with migration costs, learning curves, and their own integration problems.

Start with the integration that annoys the team the most. That builds momentum and buy-in. If the first integration saves someone 30 minutes a week of tedious data entry, they will champion the next one.

For very small businesses with only 3-5 tools, this audit may only take 30 minutes. That is fine. The exercise is still valuable because it forces the owner to see the full picture of their tech stack and make deliberate choices about what stays.
