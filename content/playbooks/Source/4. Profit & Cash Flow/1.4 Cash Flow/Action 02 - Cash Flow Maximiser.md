# Cash Flow Maximiser

## What to Do

Work through this checklist to find cash your business is losing, leaving on the table, or waiting too long to collect. You don't need to do everything — pick the items that apply to your business and commit to the ones with the biggest impact.

## Why It Matters

Most businesses doing £200K–£5M have £20K–£100K+ in recoverable cash hiding in plain sight — in slow invoices, forgotten subscriptions, weak payment terms, or pricing they haven't reviewed in years. This isn't about cutting costs to the bone. It's about stopping the leaks and speeding up the flow.

## How to Use This

1. Go through each section below
2. Mark each item: **Not Relevant** / **To Do** / **Done**
3. Pick your top 3–5 actions and do them this month
4. Review quarterly — your business changes, and new opportunities appear

---

## Lever 1: Speed Up Cash Coming In

Getting paid faster for work you've already done or sold.

- [ ] **Review your payment terms** — If you're on 30-day terms, test moving to 14-day or 7-day for new clients. Most won't push back.
- [ ] **Invoice on completion, not end of month** — Every day between finishing work and sending the invoice is a day you're lending money for free.
- [ ] **Add a "pay now" button to invoices** — Stripe, GoCardless, or similar. Remove friction between "I'll pay this" and actually paying.
- [ ] **Offer a small discount for early payment** — 2-3% for payment within 7 days. You'll often collect weeks faster. Do the maths — it's usually worth it.
- [ ] **Chase overdue invoices the day they're overdue** — Not a week later. Day 1. A simple "just checking this landed" email is enough. Automate if possible.
- [ ] **Require deposits or upfront payment for new work** — 50% upfront is standard in most industries. 100% upfront is increasingly normal for smaller projects.
- [ ] **Set up recurring billing for ongoing clients** — Direct debit or subscription billing removes the "waiting to be paid" problem entirely.
- [ ] **Review your longest-paying clients** — Who consistently pays late? Have the conversation or change their terms. One client paying 60 days late on a £5K invoice costs you real money.

## Lever 2: Reduce Cash Going Out

Not about cutting everything — about cutting what isn't earning its place.

- [ ] **List every subscription and recurring payment** — Log into your bank, list them all. Most businesses find 3-5 they forgot about or no longer use.
- [ ] **Cancel or pause anything unused for 30+ days** — If nobody noticed it was there, nobody will notice it's gone.
- [ ] **Renegotiate your top 3 supplier contracts** — Especially if you've been with them 12+ months. Ask for better terms, bulk pricing, or extended payment windows. The worst they say is no.
- [ ] **Review staff costs against revenue per head** — Not to cut people, but to know your ratio. If revenue per employee is declining, that's a signal.
- [ ] **Check your insurance, utilities, and telecom** — These auto-renew at higher rates. A 30-minute comparison can save thousands annually.
- [ ] **Stop paying for premium tiers you don't fully use** — Software, memberships, services. Downgrade to what you actually use.
- [ ] **Audit your ad spend against actual return** — Which campaigns are generating revenue and which are generating vanity metrics? Pause the vanity ones for a month and see what happens.

## Lever 3: Improve Your Pricing

The fastest way to increase cash flow without selling more.

- [ ] **When did you last raise prices?** — If it's been over 12 months, you're almost certainly undercharging. Costs have risen. Your prices should too.
- [ ] **Calculate your effective hourly rate per service** — Total revenue from the service ÷ total hours spent (including admin, prep, revisions). Some "profitable" services are loss-makers when you count the real time.
- [ ] **Identify your most profitable service or product** — Sell more of that. Actively push it. Make it easier to buy.
- [ ] **Identify your least profitable service or product** — Either raise its price, reduce the delivery cost, or stop offering it.
- [ ] **Test a price increase on your next 10 new clients** — If they all say yes without hesitation, you're still too cheap.
- [ ] **Add a premium or fast-track option** — Some clients will pay 20-50% more for faster delivery or priority access. Let them.

## Lever 4: Manage Your Cash Cycle

How quickly your business turns effort into cash in the bank.

- [ ] **Calculate your cash conversion cycle** — Days from spending money (supplier payment, wages) to receiving payment from the customer. The shorter this number, the healthier your cash flow.
- [ ] **Negotiate longer payment terms with suppliers** — If clients pay you in 14 days but you pay suppliers in 7, you're funding the gap. Flip this where you can.
- [ ] **Reduce work-in-progress (WIP)** — Unfinished work is cash tied up. Finish and invoice projects faster rather than starting new ones.
- [ ] **Bill in milestones, not on completion** — For larger projects, bill at each stage rather than waiting until the end. This keeps cash flowing throughout the project.
- [ ] **Review stock or inventory levels** — If you hold physical stock, are you holding too much? Every unit on a shelf is cash you can't use. Aim for just-in-time where possible.

## Expected Outcome

After your first pass through this checklist, you should have:

- A clear list of 3–5 high-impact actions to take this month
- At least one quick win you can action today (a subscription to cancel, an invoice to send, a price to test)
- A sense of where your biggest cash leaks are — and a plan to close them
