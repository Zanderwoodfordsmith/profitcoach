**Level 1 · Overwhelm · Profit & Cash Flow**

---

## **What This Is**

Cash flow is the oxygen of your business. It is not a nice-to-have metric, it is survival. When cash flow is broken, everything feels broken. When cash flow is stable, you can think clearly, make better decisions, and stop running your business from your bank balance.

This playbook is about one thing: knowing exactly where your cash is, where it is going, and whether you can pay your bills. Not next year. Next month.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You check your bank balance before every payment and hope the number is high enough. You have no idea what is coming in next week, let alone next month. Invoices go out late. Customers pay whenever they feel like it. You have taken on debt or deferred your own pay just to cover wages. You lie awake wondering if you can make payroll. Every unexpected cost feels like a crisis because there is no buffer. You are making business decisions based on fear, not strategy.

### **🟠 When this is partial**

You have a rough sense of your cash position but no rolling forecast. Some months are fine, others catch you off guard. You chase invoices when things get tight but not before. You know you should be tracking this more closely but you have not built the habit. There is no reserve, if a big client delayed payment by 30 days, you would feel it.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You know exactly how much cash you have right now, what is coming in over the next 13 weeks, and what is going out. You review it weekly, it takes 15 minutes. Your invoicing is prompt and your collection process means customers pay on time. You have a cash reserve that covers at least one month of fixed costs. You make spending decisions with confidence because you can see the impact before you commit. Cash is not something you worry about, it is something you manage.

## **Things to Think About**

These are the questions that sit behind this playbook. Be honest with yourself.

**Do you know your break-even number without checking?** This is the amount of cash that needs to come in every month just to keep the lights on, before you pay yourself, before profit, before growth. If you do not know this number off the top of your head, start there.

**Can you see the next 13 weeks?** A rolling 13 week cash flow forecast is the single most important financial tool for a business at this stage. It turns "I think we are OK" into "I know we are OK", or "I know we need to act now." Not next month. Now.

**How quickly do you convert work into cash?** The gap between doing the work and getting paid is where businesses suffocate. Every day a customer takes to pay you is a day you are funding their business with your cash. How tight is your invoicing? How firm are your payment terms? How consistently do you follow up?

**What expenses survive purely by inertia?** Most businesses are paying for things they no longer need, no longer use, or never questioned. Subscriptions, legacy contracts, overstaffed shifts, inefficient suppliers. Cash leaks quietly, until it doesn't.

**How many months of runway do you actually have?** If all revenue stopped tomorrow, how long could you survive? One month? Two? The answer tells you how fragile your business really is — and how urgently you need a cash reserve.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find the concrete actions to fix this:

**Play 1: The 13 Week Cash Flow Forecast**. 

Build a simple rolling forecast so you always know what is coming. Review it every Friday. 15 minutes.	

**Play 2: The Cash Flow Maximiser**

A checklist covering five levers you can pull to improve cash flow immediately: decrease expenses, increase revenue, speed up collections, reduce inventory days, and extend payment terms. You work through it with your coach and pick the actions that apply to your business.

**Play 3: Cash Collection System**

Put a process in place so invoices go out on time, payment terms are enforced, and overdue accounts get chased before they become a problem. Stop being the business that is too polite to ask for money.

## **Quick Wins**

**This week:** Write down every fixed cost you pay monthly. All of them. Then circle the ones you have not actively reviewed in the last 6 months. Cancel or renegotiate at least one.

**This week:** Send every outstanding invoice that is overdue. Today. Not tomorrow.

**This week:** Open a spreadsheet and list your expected cash in and cash out for the next 4 weeks. It does not need to be perfect. A rough forecast is infinitely better than no forecast.
