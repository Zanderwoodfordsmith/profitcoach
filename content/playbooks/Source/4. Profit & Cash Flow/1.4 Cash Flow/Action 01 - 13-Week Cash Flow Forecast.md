# Build Your 13-Week Cash Flow Forecast

## What to Do

Set up a simple 13-week rolling forecast that shows exactly what's coming in, what's going out, and whether you'll have cash problems before they arrive. Review it every Friday in under 5 minutes.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners check their bank balance and hope for the best. A 13-week forecast turns "I think we're fine" into "I know we're fine" — or "I can see a problem in week 8 and I've got 7 weeks to fix it." It's the difference between reacting to cash crises and preventing them.

## How to Use This

Download the Cash Flow Forecaster worksheet (spreadsheet). Follow the steps below to set it up. Then keep it alive with a 5-minute weekly review.

---

## Setup Checklist

- [ ] **Download or open the Cash Flow Forecaster worksheet** — Use the provided spreadsheet template. Don't build your own — it's already structured.
- [ ] **Enter your opening cash balance** — What's in your business bank account(s) right now? Use today's actual number.
- [ ] **Fill in confirmed cash in for the next 4 weeks** — Invoices sent, recurring payments due, any confirmed incoming. Use real numbers, not hopes.
- [ ] **Estimate cash in for weeks 5-13** — Based on your pipeline, averages, or contracts. A rough number is infinitely better than blank.
- [ ] **Enter all fixed cash out** — Rent, wages, loan repayments, subscriptions — anything that goes out every month regardless. Put each in the week it's due.
- [ ] **Enter known variable cash out** — Supplier payments, VAT quarters, one-off expenses you know about. Put them in the right week.
- [ ] **Check the running total row** — This is your projected cash position each week. If it goes negative at any point, you now know about it.
- [ ] **Flag any week where cash drops below your comfort level** — Your "comfort level" is the minimum you'd want in the account. For most £200K–£1M businesses, that's 1-2 months of fixed costs.

## Weekly Review Checklist (Every Friday, 5 Minutes)

- [ ] **Update actuals for the week just gone** — Replace estimates with what actually happened.
- [ ] **Roll the forecast forward one week** — Add a new week 13 at the end.
- [ ] **Adjust upcoming estimates** — Anything changed? New invoice? Lost client? Delayed payment? Update it.
- [ ] **Check the running total** — Any weeks turning negative or getting uncomfortable? Act now, not then.
- [ ] **Note one thing you learned** — Over time, you'll spot patterns: "We always run low in March" or "Client X always pays 2 weeks late."

---

## Expected Outcome

- You know your exact cash position right now
- You can see 13 weeks ahead — no surprises
- Problems become visible with weeks of lead time, not days
- You make spending decisions with confidence instead of anxiety
- Friday review becomes a 5-minute habit that pays for itself

## Coach Notes

This is the first tool for any client who scores red or amber on cash flow. Set it up together in the first session — don't just assign it as homework. Walk them through entering their numbers. The emotional impact of seeing their forecast for the first time is powerful and builds trust.

After setup, hold them accountable to the weekly review. If they're not updating it, nothing else in cash flow will work.
