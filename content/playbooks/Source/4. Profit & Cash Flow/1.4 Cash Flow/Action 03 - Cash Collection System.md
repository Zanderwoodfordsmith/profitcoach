# Set Up Your Cash Collection System

## What to Do

Build a simple, repeatable system that ensures invoices go out on time, payment terms are clear, and overdue accounts get chased promptly — without you having to remember or manage it manually.

## Why It Matters

Most small businesses leave thousands on the table every year simply because invoices go out late, terms aren't enforced, and nobody chases until it's awkward. A cash collection system isn't about being aggressive — it's about being consistent. The business that invoices on Tuesday and follows up on Thursday gets paid before the business that invoices "when I get around to it."

## How to Use This

Work through the steps in order. Each one builds on the last. You're setting up a system, not doing a one-off task — so take the time to get it right.

---

## Step 1: Get Your Invoicing Right

- [ ] **Set a fixed invoicing day** — Pick a day (e.g., every Tuesday, or on project completion). Invoices go out on that day, no exceptions.
- [ ] **Standardise your invoice format** — Every invoice should include: clear description of work, payment terms, due date, and a direct payment link or bank details.
- [ ] **Add a "pay now" link to every invoice** — Stripe, GoCardless, PayPal, bank transfer link — whatever removes friction. The fewer clicks to pay, the faster you get paid.
- [ ] **State your payment terms clearly** — "Payment due within 14 days" is clear. "Payment due on receipt" is clearer. "Net 30" is jargon most small business clients don't understand.

## Step 2: Set Up Your Follow-Up Sequence

Build this once, then it runs automatically (or with minimal effort).

- [ ] **Day of invoice: Confirmation** — "Hi [name], invoice [number] for [amount] has been sent. Due by [date]. Let me know if any questions."
- [ ] **3 days before due: Gentle reminder** — "Just a heads-up that invoice [number] is due on [date]. Payment link: [link]."
- [ ] **Due date (if unpaid): Direct follow-up** — "Invoice [number] was due today. Can you confirm when payment will be made?"
- [ ] **7 days overdue: Firm follow-up** — "Invoice [number] is now 7 days overdue. Please arrange payment by [date] or let me know if there's an issue."
- [ ] **14+ days overdue: Escalation** — Phone call or direct message. At this point, email isn't working. Have the conversation.
- [ ] **Automate what you can** — Most invoicing tools (Xero, QuickBooks, FreshBooks) can send reminders automatically. Set them up once.

## Step 3: Handle Your Current Overdue Invoices

Before the system runs forward, clean up what's behind you.

- [ ] **List every overdue invoice right now** — Amount, client name, how many days overdue.
- [ ] **Send a follow-up to every one today** — Not next week. Today. A simple "I'm tidying up our accounts and noticed this is outstanding — can you confirm when payment will be made?"
- [ ] **Flag anyone over 30 days** — These need a phone call, not another email.
- [ ] **Decide your policy for chronic late payers** — Options: move to upfront payment, add a late payment fee, stop work until paid, or fire the client. Pick one and communicate it.

## Step 4: Protect Future Cash

- [ ] **Require deposits for new clients or projects** — 50% upfront is standard. 100% for projects under £1,000. State this in your proposal, not after they've agreed.
- [ ] **Add payment terms to your contracts or proposals** — Don't rely on invoice terms alone. Terms should be agreed before work starts.
- [ ] **Review your debtor days monthly** — Average number of days between invoicing and receiving payment. Track this number. If it's rising, your system needs attention.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Invoices go out consistently and on time
- You have a clear follow-up sequence that runs without you thinking about it
- Overdue invoices get chased promptly, not awkwardly after 6 weeks
- New clients start with clear payment expectations
- Your debtor days (time between invoice and payment) start dropping

## Coach Notes

This is often the most uncomfortable action for clients — chasing money feels awkward. Reframe it: "You've done the work. You deserve to be paid on time. A system means you don't have to have awkward conversations — the process handles it."

Help the client draft their follow-up email templates in the session. If they leave with the templates written, they'll actually use them. If they leave with "I'll write them later," they won't.

For clients with significant overdue invoices, this action alone can unlock thousands in cash within a week.
