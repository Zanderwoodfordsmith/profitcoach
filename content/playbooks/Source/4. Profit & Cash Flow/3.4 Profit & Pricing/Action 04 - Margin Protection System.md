# Margin Protection System

## What to Do

Identify every place where your profit margins are being eroded - through discounting, scope creep, undercharging, complexity, or operational waste - and then install specific guardrails to protect them. This is a defensive action. You have done the work to set the right prices and understand your profitability. Now you need to stop the margin from leaking away through a hundred small concessions.

## Why It Matters

Michalowicz's Profit First principle is that profit is not what is left over after expenses. It is what you take first and protect. Most businesses lose 10-30% of their potential margin through preventable leaks: discounts given too easily, projects that expand beyond the original scope, invoices sent late, and clients who consume more time than they pay for. Each one seems small. Together, they are the difference between a business that grows its profit and one that works harder every year for the same return.

## How to Use This

Review this checklist and tick the items that apply to your business. You do not need all of them - pick the ones that address your biggest margin leaks first.

---

## Discounting Guardrails

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Set a maximum discount policy** - Define the maximum discount anyone in the business can offer (e.g., 10%) and require approval for anything beyond it. Write it down. Share it with the sales team.
- [ ] **Eliminate casual discounting** - If "I'll do you a deal" is a standard part of your sales conversation, you are training clients to expect it. Remove discounting from the default sales process.
- [ ] **Replace discounts with added value** - Instead of cutting price, add a bonus that costs you little but has high perceived value. This protects margin while still giving the client something.
- [ ] **Track every discount given** - Run a monthly report showing total discount value. When the team sees the aggregate number, behaviour changes.

## Scope Creep Guardrails

- [ ] **Define scope in writing for every engagement** - Proposals and contracts must specify what is included and what is not. Vague scope is an open invitation for extras.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Create a change request process** - When a client asks for something outside scope, respond with "Absolutely, let me put together a quick quote for that." This is not adversarial. It is professional.
- [ ] **Track "free" extras** - Every time you or your team does something outside scope without charging, log it. Review monthly. This number is usually eye-opening.
- [ ] **Set boundaries on client communication** - Unlimited access to you via WhatsApp, evenings, and weekends is not a feature. It is a margin leak. Define response times and channels in your engagement terms.

## Operational Margin Protection

- [ ] **Review supplier and subcontractor costs annually** - Costs creep up. Renegotiate or shop around at least once a year. Even a 5% reduction on a major supplier flows straight to profit.
- [ ] **Identify your most time-consuming clients** - Some clients consume 3x the attention of others at the same price. Either restructure their engagement, increase their price, or let them go.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Tighten payment terms** - If you are on 30-day terms, move new clients to 14-day or payment-on-completion. Faster payment improves cash flow and reduces the cost of chasing.
- [ ] **Automate or eliminate low-value admin** - Every hour your team spends on admin that could be automated is an hour that is not generating revenue. Audit for quick automation wins.
- [ ] **Set a minimum engagement size** - Small projects often have disproportionate setup and management costs. Set a floor below which you do not take work, or charge a minimum project fee.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A clear picture of where margin is leaking in your business
- Written policies for discounting, scope management, and payment terms
- Guardrails installed that protect profit without damaging client relationships
- A monthly tracking habit that makes margin erosion visible
- Higher effective margins on both new and existing engagements

## Coach Notes

This is one of the most practical actions in the entire system because the results show up almost immediately. A client who stops giving 15% discounts on a £100K revenue line just added £15K to their bottom line.

The most common pushback is "my clients will leave if I enforce boundaries." They will not. The clients who leave over reasonable scope management and payment terms are usually the ones eroding your margins the most. Losing them is profitable.

Start with the biggest leak. For most service businesses, it is scope creep. For most product businesses, it is discounting. Fix the biggest one first, then work down the list. Do not try to install all guardrails simultaneously or the team will feel like everything changed overnight.
