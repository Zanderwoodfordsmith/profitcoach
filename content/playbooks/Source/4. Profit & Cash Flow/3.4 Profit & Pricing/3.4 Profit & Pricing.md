## **What This Is**

Profit does not happen by accident. It is not what is left over after all the bills are paid. It is designed into every price, every offer, every client relationship from day one. At this stage, you have cash flow under control. Now it is time to engineer profit into the machine.

This playbook is about understanding where you actually make money and where you do not. It covers margin analysis by offer and client, pricing architecture that makes sense strategically, and protecting profit from the complexity that slowly erodes it. Revenue feels good. Profit pays for growth.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You can tell them your revenue but not your margin. Some clients and projects seem profitable but you are not sure which ones. Pricing is inconsistent — sometimes based on cost, sometimes on competition, sometimes on what feels right. Discounting happens more often than you would like to admit. The business feels busy but bank balance growth does not match the effort.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have a rough sense of which offers make more money than others but no clear data to prove it. Pricing has some logic to it but exceptions creep in regularly. You know discounting hurts profit but struggle to say no when clients push back. Some months feel profitable, others feel thin, and you are not always sure why.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You know exactly which offers and which clients generate real profit and which do not. Your pricing is structured strategically with clear logic that ties to value delivered. Discounting has rules and happens rarely. Every new offer is designed with margin in mind before it goes to market. You can see profit trends month to month and make decisions based on data, not gut feel.

## **Things to Think About**

Which offers actually make you money? Not revenue — profit. If you stripped out all the hidden costs, complexity, and time that different offers require, which ones would you do more of and which would you stop tomorrow? Without this clarity, you are building a business on hope.

Is your pricing telling the right story about value? Every price sends a message about what the customer is buying and how much it is worth. Pricing too low suggests low value. Pricing without logic confuses the buyer. Your pricing architecture should make the decision easy for the right customers.

Are you pricing effort or outcomes? Hours and costs are about you. Outcomes and transformations are about them. The shift from "this costs me X to deliver" to "this is worth Y to them" is where real profitability lives. What are you actually selling — time or results?

When you discount, is it strategic or habitual? Every discount should have a clear strategic reason — to win a big contract, to move volume, to test a new market. If you find yourself discounting to close most deals, the problem is not price sensitivity. It is value clarity.

Where does complexity kill your margins? Every custom solution, every special exception, every "just this once" adds cost that rarely gets priced in properly. The simplest offers to deliver often have the best margins. Where are you making things harder than they need to be?

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Profit by Offer Analysis — Map the true profitability of each service or product you offer. Include all costs — direct, indirect, and opportunity. Some offers that feel profitable will surprise you.

Play 2: Pricing Architecture Review — Build logical, strategic pricing that connects to value delivered. Good pricing architecture makes it easier to sell and harder for clients to negotiate down.

Play 3: Value-Based Pricing Framework — Shift from cost-plus pricing to outcome-based pricing. Learn to price what it is worth to them, not what it costs you to deliver.

Play 4: Margin Protection System — Identify where discounting and complexity are eroding profit, then install the guardrails to protect margins going forward.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: List your top 5 offers. Next to each one, write your gut feel of which makes the most profit per hour. Then calculate the actual numbers. See where your instincts were right and where they were wrong.

This week: Pick one offer where you regularly discount. Define the minimum margin you need to make it worthwhile and the specific circumstances where discounting makes strategic sense.

This week: Review your last 10 clients. Circle the ones where delivery was smooth and simple. Put a square around the ones that were complex or custom. Compare the profit margins and see the pattern.
