## **What This Is**

Revenue grows ego. Cost discipline grows profit.

Revenue expansion can disguise inefficiency. When you are growing, it is easy to ignore the fact that costs are creeping upward and margin is eroding. You feel successful because the top line is moving, but the bottom line tells a different story.

This playbook is about installing the discipline to control what goes out so more stays in. Not penny-pinching or cutting everything to the bone — smart cost management that strengthens resilience and increases your reinvestment capacity. Revenue gets the headlines. Cost control gets the profits.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You have no idea what you are actually spending each month until you see the bank statement. Costs have crept up gradually over months or years, and you are not sure where the money is going. Subscriptions and services pile up on autopay. You make spending decisions in the moment without knowing if you can afford them. Profit margins feel squeezed but you cannot put your finger on why.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You review expenses occasionally and cut obvious waste when you remember to. Some costs are controlled, others have grown without oversight. You know roughly what the big expenses are but have not tested whether you are getting value for money. You suspect there are leaks but lack the time or system to plug them properly.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You know exactly what you are spending and why. Every expense is categorised as fixed or variable, and you understand which costs grow with revenue and which do not. Major expenses are regularly reviewed for ROI. Supplier contracts are renegotiated periodically. Spending approval thresholds are clear and respected. You cut waste systematically, not reactively. Your cost base supports growth rather than draining it.

---

## **Things to Think About**

Can you separate your fixed costs from your variable costs? Fixed costs hit you every month regardless of revenue — rent, salaries, software subscriptions. Variable costs move with sales — materials, commissions, transaction fees. Until you know which is which, you cannot predict what happens to profit as you grow.

Which expenses have you never properly evaluated? Most businesses carry costs that made sense two years ago but no longer deliver value. The software you bought but never implemented. The service contract you have not reviewed since you signed it. What is quietly draining margin without contributing to results?

Do you know the ROI on your major expenses? That expensive consultant, the premium software package, the upgraded office space — what measurable return are these generating? If you cannot answer this question for your biggest costs, you are spending on hope rather than data.

Who can authorise what level of spend? Without clear approval thresholds, costs creep up through a thousand small decisions. If everyone can spend anything, everything gets bought. If only you can approve expenses, you become a bottleneck. The sweet spot is clear rules that protect cash without slowing decisions.

When did you last test the market on your biggest contracts? Your biggest suppliers know you are comfortable with them. That comfort costs you money. Loyalty should be earned with competitive pricing and great service, not rewarded with lazy procurement.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Cost Categorisation — Sort every expense into fixed, variable, and semi-variable buckets so you understand what drives your cost base and how it behaves as revenue changes.

Play 2: ROI Evaluation Matrix — Review your major expenses against measurable returns using a simple framework that separates essential costs from nice-to-haves and identifies what is delivering value versus what is consuming it.

Play 3: Supplier Contract Review — Systematically renegotiate your biggest contracts using market benchmarking and relationship leverage to reduce costs without damaging service quality.

Play 4: Spending Authority Framework — Set clear approval thresholds and processes so routine expenses flow smoothly while protecting against budget creep and unauthorized spending.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: List every recurring payment that hits your bank account automatically. Circle any you have not consciously reviewed in the last 12 months.

This week: Call your three biggest suppliers and ask for their latest pricing. Tell them you are reviewing all contracts and need their best rates to stay competitive.

This week: Add up what you spent on software subscriptions last month. Cancel anything you have not used in the last 90 days or find a cheaper alternative that does the same job.
