# Supplier Contract Review

## What to Do

Systematically review and renegotiate your biggest supplier contracts using market benchmarking and relationship leverage. Most businesses overpay their suppliers simply because they have never asked for better terms. This action gives you a structured process for reducing costs without damaging service quality.

## Why It Matters

Your suppliers are businesses too - they have flexibility in their pricing that they will not volunteer. The rate you agreed 2 years ago is probably not the best they can offer today. Market conditions change, competitors emerge, and your volume may have grown enough to warrant a volume discount. A single afternoon of supplier negotiations can save thousands per year. Most SME owners leave this money on the table because the negotiation feels uncomfortable or they assume the price is fixed.

## How to Use This

List your biggest supplier relationships, research alternatives, then contact each supplier with a clear ask. This is not aggressive negotiation - it is informed business management.

---

## Preparation

- [ ] List your top 5-8 suppliers by annual spend
- [ ] For each, note: what they provide, your annual spend, contract terms, renewal date, and how long you have been with them
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Check if any contracts are on auto-renew in the next 60 days. Mark these for immediate attention - you have more leverage before renewal than after.
- [ ] For each supplier, research one alternative provider and their pricing (even a rough estimate is useful)

## Market Benchmarking

- [ ] For your top 3 suppliers by spend, get at least one competing quote
- [ ] You do not need to switch - the quote is leverage for the negotiation
- [ ] Check online for typical market rates in your industry for these services
- [ ] Note any areas where your current supplier provides value beyond price (reliability, relationship, expertise, flexibility)
- [ ] Calculate the gap: are you paying market rate, above, or below?

## The Negotiation Conversations

For each supplier you are reviewing:

- [ ] Call (do not email) your account manager or main contact
- [ ] Frame the conversation positively: "We value the relationship and want to continue working together. We are reviewing all our contracts to make sure we are getting the best value."
- [ ] Share that you have been looking at the market: "We have seen competitive quotes that are [X]% lower for comparable service."
- [ ] Ask directly: "What can you do on pricing to keep us as a customer?"
- [ ] If they cannot move on price, negotiate other terms: payment terms, additional services, volume discounts, longer notice periods in your favour
- [ ] Get any agreed changes in writing

## Review Outcomes

- [ ] Document the outcome of each conversation: price change, new terms, or no change
- [ ] Calculate total annual savings from successful negotiations
- [ ] For any supplier where negotiation failed and the gap is significant, seriously consider switching
- [ ] Set calendar reminders 60 days before each contract renewal to repeat this process
- [ ] Build a supplier review into your quarterly or annual business rhythm

---

## Expected Outcome

- Your top supplier contracts reviewed against market rates
- Negotiation conversations completed with each major supplier
- Measurable cost savings achieved or alternative suppliers identified
- A renewal calendar set up so contracts never auto-renew without review
- A repeatable process for keeping supplier costs competitive over time

## Coach Notes

Many clients feel uncomfortable negotiating with suppliers they have good relationships with. Reframe it: this is not confrontation, it is good business management. Their suppliers expect it. The script above is designed to be firm but friendly. Common wins: 10-20% reduction on software and SaaS contracts (especially if you mention cancelling), better payment terms on professional services, and volume discounts for growing businesses. The auto-renewal check alone often saves money - many businesses pay for contracts they forgot to cancel or renegotiate. This is one of the fastest ways to improve profit without changing anything about revenue.
