# Spending Authority Framework

## What to Do

Set clear rules for who can approve what level of spending in your business. Define thresholds, approval processes, and boundaries so routine expenses flow smoothly while protecting against budget creep and unauthorised spending. The goal: your team can spend where needed without everything coming through you, but significant costs get proper scrutiny.

## Why It Matters

Without clear spending authority, one of two things happens. Either you approve every purchase (which makes you a bottleneck and slows everything down) or everyone spends freely (which leads to creeping costs and unpleasant surprises). A simple framework solves both problems. The team knows what they can approve themselves, what needs a manager, and what needs the owner. Decisions happen faster and costs stay controlled.

## How to Use This

Design your framework based on your business size, team structure, and risk tolerance. Start simple - you can always add complexity later.

---

## Design Your Thresholds

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Set three spending levels right now. For example: Under £100 = team member decides. £100-£500 = manager approval. Over £500 = owner approval. Adjust the numbers to your business.
- [ ] Define the thresholds for each level of your organisation:
  - **Self-approve:** Individual team members can spend up to £___ without asking anyone
  - **Manager approve:** Spending between £___ and £___ needs a manager's sign-off
  - **Owner approve:** Spending above £___ requires owner approval
- [ ] Set separate thresholds for recurring expenses (monthly subscriptions) vs. one-off purchases - recurring costs have a bigger long-term impact

## Define the Rules

- [ ] Create a short list of spending categories that always need owner approval regardless of amount (new hires, new contracts, capital equipment, etc.)
- [ ] Define what "approval" means: verbal OK, email confirmation, or formal sign-off? Keep it proportional to the amount.
- [ ] Set rules for emergency spending: what if someone needs to spend above their threshold urgently and the approver is not available?
- [ ] Clarify budget vs. unbudgeted spending: if an expense was already included in the plan/budget, does it still need approval?
- [ ] Define what happens with personal expenses and reimbursements: what is covered, what is not, and what is the process?

## Communicate and Implement

- [ ] Write up the framework in one page (keep it simple enough to fit on a single sheet)
- [ ] Share it with every team member and explain the reasoning: "This is about speed AND control"
- [ ] Make the approval process as frictionless as possible - a quick Slack message or email is fine for most levels
- [ ] Set clear expectations: spending within your authority is encouraged, not just permitted. The goal is to remove bottlenecks, not create fear.
- [ ] Define consequences for spending outside the framework - not punishment, but a conversation about why it happened

## Monitor and Adjust

- [ ] Review all expenses monthly against the framework: is it working? Are approvals happening smoothly?
- [ ] Track whether the thresholds are right: too low and you create bottlenecks, too high and costs creep
- [ ] Adjust thresholds as people prove reliable - increase authority as trust builds
- [ ] Watch for workarounds: splitting purchases to stay under thresholds is a sign the limits are too low or the approval process is too slow

---

## Expected Outcome

- Clear spending authority levels that the whole team understands
- Routine expenses flowing without owner involvement
- Significant expenses getting proper review before commitment
- Faster decision-making at every level
- Cost control built into the culture rather than dependent on the owner catching everything

## Coach Notes

For solo operators or very small teams (2-3 people), this framework might seem unnecessary. It is not. Even setting rules for yourself ("I will not commit to any recurring expense above £200 without sleeping on it") creates discipline. For growing teams, this is essential infrastructure - without it, the owner becomes the approval bottleneck for every purchase, which kills both speed and trust. The most common mistake: setting thresholds too low out of anxiety. If you trust someone to manage a client relationship worth £50K, you can probably trust them to approve a £200 software purchase. Start generous and tighten only if problems emerge.
