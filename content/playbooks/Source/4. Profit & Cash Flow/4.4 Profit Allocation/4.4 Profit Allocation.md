## **What This Is**

Profit is not something that happens to you — it is something you decide what to do with. When profit comes in, most owners either spend it reactively or leave it sitting in the bank account where it gets absorbed by the next month's expenses. This playbook changes that.

Profit allocation is about deliberately deciding where every pound of profit goes before you make it. Owner distributions, business reserves, reinvestment, innovation funds, tax — each gets its own bucket so you are building resilience, funding growth, and rewarding yourself according to a plan, not a mood.

This is not budgeting. This is not expense management. This is about taking the profit you have earned and directing it towards the outcomes that matter most: a sustainable business, strategic growth, and personal reward.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Profit disappears as quickly as it arrives. You make a good month, then wonder where the money went when the next month's bills come in. Owner distributions are random — you take money when things feel good and nothing when they do not. There are no reserves, so every setback feels like a crisis. Growth happens by accident, not intention, because there is no dedicated fund for investment.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have some structure around profit but it is inconsistent. You set aside money for tax but other allocations happen on gut feel. Owner distributions are better than before but still feel arbitrary. You talk about reinvesting in the business but never quite know how much or when. Reserves exist but they are not systematic — sometimes healthy, sometimes depleted.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Every pound of profit has a destination before you make it. You have clear percentages that go to owner distributions, business reserves, reinvestment, and tax. Reserves are automatically built to your target level and stay there. Growth investments are funded from a dedicated allocation, not by borrowing from other buckets. You take consistent distributions without guilt because they are part of the plan, not an extra.

## **Things to Think About**

What percentage of profit should you actually keep in the business? Most owners reinvest everything and wonder why they feel broke despite having a profitable company. Others take everything out and wonder why growth stagnates. The right reinvestment ratio depends on your growth plans, your personal needs, and your risk tolerance.

How much should sit in reserves before you feel safe? Different businesses need different cushions. A consultancy might need three months of expenses. A manufacturer might need six. The number matters less than having a deliberate target and a plan to reach it.

Are you funding innovation or just talking about it? Growth requires investment in new capabilities, new offerings, and new systems. If you do not allocate profit specifically for innovation, it gets crowded out by immediate needs. Every good month becomes an opportunity lost.

What does reward look like in your business model? Owner distributions are not just about personal reward — they are about business sustainability. Take too little and you resent the business. Take too much and you starve future growth. The allocation needs to reflect both what you need personally and what the business needs to thrive.

How do you balance today's needs with tomorrow's opportunities? The tension in profit allocation is always between now and later. Reserves feel safe but do not grow the business. Investment feels exciting but leaves you exposed. The right allocation acknowledges both without sacrificing either.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Profit Allocation Framework — Set up the allocation percentages that work for your business and personal needs. Define how much goes to owner distributions, reserves, reinvestment, and tax so every decision is made once, not every month.

Play 2: Reserve Fund Targets — Calculate the right reserve levels for your business and create the automatic process to build and maintain them. Reserves should grow when times are good and protect you when they are not.

Play 3: Growth Investment Budget — Create a dedicated fund for strategic investments in the business — new systems, new capabilities, new opportunities. When the budget is set, investment decisions become about priorities, not whether you can afford it.

Play 4: Owner Distribution Rules — Establish clear rules for when and how much to distribute to yourself. Consistent distributions that are sustainable for both your personal needs and the business health.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: Calculate last quarter's profit and decide what percentage should have gone to each bucket: you, reserves, reinvestment, tax. Use this as your starting allocation framework.

This week: Open separate bank accounts for reserves and growth investment if you do not have them already. Physical separation makes allocation real.

This week: Set a target reserve amount based on 3-6 months of your fixed costs. This becomes your minimum safety level before distributions or investments get increased.
