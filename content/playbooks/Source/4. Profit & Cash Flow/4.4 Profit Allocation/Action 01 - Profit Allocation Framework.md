# Profit Allocation Framework

## What to Do

Set up fixed percentages that determine where every pound of revenue goes before you spend it. You will create allocation buckets for profit (owner distributions), operating expenses, tax, and reserves. Once set, this becomes a standing decision that runs every month without renegotiation.

This is the Profit First principle in practice. Instead of Revenue - Expenses = Profit, you flip it: Revenue - Profit = Expenses. You take your profit first, then run the business on what remains.

## Why It Matters

Without predetermined allocations, profit is whatever happens to be left over at the end of the month. For most business owners, that means profit is zero or close to it because expenses expand to fill available revenue. Fixed percentages create discipline. They force spending decisions within a constraint. They also remove the monthly debate about "can we afford to take profit this month?" The answer is always yes, because the percentage is set.

## How to Use This

Pull your last 6 months of revenue and expense data. You need to know your current reality before setting target percentages. Work through the checklist below to build your allocation framework.

---

## Assess Your Current Reality

- [ ] Calculate your average monthly revenue over the last 6 months
- [ ] Calculate what percentage currently goes to: owner pay, operating expenses, tax provisions, and actual profit retained
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Check your bank balance right now. If profit is supposed to be there but is not, that tells you everything about why allocation matters.
- [ ] Note any months where you took zero profit or skipped tax provisions
- [ ] Identify your current effective tax rate (ask your accountant if unsure)

## Set Your Target Allocation Percentages

- [ ] Choose your profit allocation percentage (start with 5% if you are currently at 0%, target 10-20% over time)
- [ ] Set your owner pay percentage (what you need personally, typically 30-50% depending on business size)
- [ ] Set your tax reserve percentage (based on your effective rate plus a small buffer, typically 15-20%)
- [ ] Set your operating expense percentage (whatever remains after profit, owner pay, and tax)
- [ ] Confirm all four percentages add up to 100%
- [ ] Compare your target percentages against your current reality. If the gap is large, plan a phased transition over 3-6 months.

## Create Your Allocation System

- [ ] Open separate bank accounts for each bucket: Profit, Tax, Owner Pay, Operating Expenses
- [ ] Set up automatic transfers that run on the 1st and 15th of each month (twice monthly keeps amounts manageable)
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Open the separate accounts this week. Even before you set final percentages, the act of separating money changes behaviour immediately.
- [ ] Write down your allocation percentages somewhere visible so they are not forgotten
- [ ] Set a calendar reminder to review percentages quarterly

---

## Expected Outcome

- Four clear allocation percentages that account for 100% of revenue
- Separate bank accounts for each allocation bucket
- Automatic transfers scheduled twice monthly
- A clear gap analysis between current state and target state
- A transition plan if percentages need to shift gradually

## Coach Notes

Most clients resist this at first because they feel they cannot afford to take profit. That is exactly why they need it. Start with small percentages and increase over time. The psychological shift matters more than the exact numbers. Mike Michalowicz's Profit First framework is the foundation here, but adapt it to each client's reality. Do not let them set aspirational percentages they cannot sustain - that leads to raiding the profit account and losing trust in the system. Connect this directly to the Cash Flow Forecast (1.4) so they can see how allocation affects monthly cash position.
