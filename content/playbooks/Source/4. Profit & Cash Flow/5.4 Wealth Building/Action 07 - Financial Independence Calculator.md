# Financial Independence Calculator

## What to Do

Define exactly how much passive income you need to fund your ideal life, calculate how much capital is required to generate it, and track your progress with clear milestones. Financial independence is not a vague aspiration - it is a specific number. This action helps you find that number and build a roadmap to reach it.

Work through three stages: define your required income, calculate the capital needed, and set quarterly milestones to track progress.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners have no idea what financial independence actually costs for them personally. They work toward "enough" without defining what enough means. The result is either working far longer than necessary (because they never realise they have already arrived) or retiring and discovering they do not have enough (because they never calculated the real requirement).

JL Collins' "Simple Path to Wealth" principle applies: the number is simpler than you think, and the path is more achievable than you fear. Once you know the number, every financial decision becomes clearer. Every pound invested is measurable progress. Every unnecessary expense is visible delay.

## How to Use This

Work through the three sections in order. The calculations are straightforward - you do not need a financial adviser for this part. What you need is honesty about your lifestyle costs and discipline about tracking progress.

---

## Stage 1: Define Your Required Income

- [ ] **Calculate your current annual living expenses** - Not your income, your actual spending. Go through 12 months of bank statements. Include everything: mortgage/rent, bills, food, travel, leisure, children, insurance, subscriptions.
- [ ] **Adjust for your ideal lifestyle** - Your Life Vision Canvas (see [[5.0 Life Design]]) describes the life you want. Does it cost more or less than your current lifestyle? Be realistic. Adventure, travel, and freedom often cost more than people estimate.
- [ ] **Add a buffer of 20%** - Life surprises you. Healthcare costs rise. Inflation erodes purchasing power. Children have unexpected needs. Build in a margin.
- [ ] **Calculate your annual Financial Independence Income** - Current expenses, adjusted for ideal lifestyle, plus 20% buffer. This is the annual income your investments need to generate.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Write down your best guess of annual living costs before checking the bank statements** - Then compare. Most people underestimate by 20-30%.

## Stage 2: Calculate Required Capital

- [ ] **Choose a safe withdrawal rate** - The standard guideline is 4% (the "Trinity Study" rule). This means you need capital of 25x your annual income requirement. More conservative planners use 3.5% (roughly 29x). Choose your comfort level.
- [ ] **Calculate your Financial Independence Number** - Annual income needed multiplied by 25 (at 4%) or 29 (at 3.5%). This is the total investment capital you need to be financially independent.
- [ ] **Subtract your current investment portfolio value** - What you already have invested (excluding business equity and primary residence). The gap between this and your FI number is what you still need to build.
- [ ] **Factor in pension income** - State pension and any defined benefit pensions reduce the capital you need. Calculate the income these will provide and subtract it from your annual requirement before calculating the capital number.
- [ ] **Factor in business sale proceeds (conservatively)** - If you plan to sell the business, estimate net proceeds after tax. This reduces the gap. But use a conservative valuation - do not count on the best-case scenario.

## Stage 3: Build Your Roadmap

- [ ] **Calculate how much you need to invest annually to reach your FI number** - Based on the gap, your timeline, and assumed investment returns (use 5-7% real returns for a balanced portfolio). Online compound interest calculators make this easy.
- [ ] **Set quarterly milestones** - Break the annual target into quarterly investment contributions. Track whether you are on pace.
- [ ] **Define "levels" of financial independence** - Level 1: 6 months' expenses in cash (security). Level 2: Annual expenses covered by investments (breathing room). Level 3: Full FI number reached (freedom). Celebrate each level.
- [ ] **Track net worth quarterly** - Total assets minus total liabilities. Plot the number on a simple chart. Watching it grow is motivating.
- [ ] **Review and recalculate annually** - Lifestyle costs change. Investment values fluctuate. The number is not fixed - it is a living target that you update regularly.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Calculate your FI number right now using rough estimates** - Even a rough number is infinitely more useful than no number. Refine it later.

## Expected Outcome

- Specific annual income requirement for financial independence
- Clear capital target (your Financial Independence Number)
- Understanding of the gap between where you are and where you need to be
- Quarterly milestones and tracking system
- Motivating progress chart that makes abstract wealth building feel tangible

## Coach Notes

This is one of the most motivating actions in the system. When a client sees a specific number and a realistic path to it, the conversation shifts from "I hope I can retire someday" to "I am X% of the way there and I know exactly what to do next."

The most common mistake is using gross income instead of actual expenses as the starting point. Push clients to use real spending data, not salary. Many discover they need less than they thought, which means their FI number is more achievable than expected.

Watch for the client who calculates a very large number and feels discouraged. Help them see the role of business sale proceeds, pension income, and compound growth. Also introduce the "levels" concept - they do not need to reach full FI to feel the benefit. Even Level 1 (6 months in cash) dramatically reduces financial anxiety and improves decision-making.
