# Estate Planning Foundations

## What to Do

Structure your affairs so wealth transfers efficiently to your beneficiaries, minimising tax and complexity. Estate planning is not just about writing a will - it covers how assets are owned, how business interests transfer, how inheritance tax is managed, and how your wishes are documented clearly enough to be executed without dispute.

This action covers the foundational elements. Complex estate planning requires professional advice, but every business owner should understand and complete these basics.

## Why It Matters

Without proper estate planning, the default outcome is determined by law, not by your wishes. In the UK, intestacy rules decide who gets what, and the result is often not what the owner would have chosen. Business assets can be frozen during probate. Tax liabilities can force the sale of assets at unfavourable prices. Family members can end up in disputes that destroy relationships and wealth simultaneously.

For business owners, the stakes are higher than for employed people. Business interests, shareholdings, directorships, and intellectual property all need specific handling. A business owner who dies without adequate estate planning does not just leave a mess - they may destroy the business they spent decades building.

## How to Use This

Work through this checklist to assess your current position. Engage a solicitor who specialises in business owner estate planning for anything beyond the basics. This is not an area for DIY.

## Foundational Documents

- [ ] **Ensure you have a current, comprehensive will** - Covering all personal and business assets. If your circumstances have changed since the last will (marriage, divorce, new children, business growth), it needs updating.
- [ ] **Set up lasting power of attorney for finances** - If you lose mental capacity, someone needs legal authority to manage your financial affairs. Without an LPA, your family faces a costly, slow court process.
- [ ] **Set up lasting power of attorney for health** - Separate from financial LPA. Appoints someone to make health decisions if you cannot. Essential, often neglected.
- [ ] **Write a letter of wishes** - Not legally binding but extremely valuable. Explains the reasoning behind your will, provides guidance on business decisions, and helps executors understand your intentions.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Check whether your LPAs are in place and registered** - If not, download the forms from gov.uk or instruct a solicitor. Registration takes weeks, so start early.

## Business Asset Planning

- [ ] **Clarify what happens to your shares on death** - Does your shareholder agreement have a buy-sell provision? Cross-option agreement? Or will your shares pass through your estate with no plan for what happens next?
- [ ] **Review Business Property Relief eligibility** - Shares in qualifying trading companies can pass free of inheritance tax. Ensure your structure qualifies and document it.
- [ ] **Consider a cross-option agreement with co-shareholders** - If you have business partners, a cross-option ensures your shares can be bought at agreed value rather than left in limbo.
- [ ] **Assess the impact of your death on business operations** - Who takes over? How are decisions made? How is the transition funded? Your estate plan should dovetail with your succession plan.
- [ ] **Ensure key documents are accessible** - Shareholder agreements, partnership deeds, insurance policies, bank mandates. Your executors need to find these quickly. Document locations clearly.

## Inheritance Tax Planning

- [ ] **Calculate your potential IHT exposure** - Total estate value minus allowances (nil-rate band, residence nil-rate band, BPR). Know the potential liability before planning how to mitigate it.
- [ ] **Review how assets are owned** - Joint ownership, sole ownership, trust ownership. How assets are held affects both IHT and probate. Get advice on the optimal structure.
- [ ] **Consider lifetime giving** - Gifts made more than 7 years before death are typically free of IHT. Regular gifts from surplus income can also be exempt. These strategies require planning and record-keeping.
- [ ] **Review trust structures** - Trusts can be useful for IHT planning, asset protection, and control over how wealth is distributed. But they are complex and have their own tax implications. Professional advice is essential.
- [ ] **Ensure life insurance is in trust** - If your life insurance pays into your estate, it increases the taxable value. Putting policies in trust means the payout goes directly to beneficiaries, outside the estate.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Check whether your life insurance policies are written in trust** - If not, your insurance provider can usually arrange this with a simple form. Potentially saves your family tens of thousands in IHT.

## Expected Outcome

- All foundational legal documents in place and current (will, LPAs, letter of wishes)
- Business asset transfer planned and aligned with succession strategy
- Inheritance tax exposure calculated and mitigation strategies identified
- Key documents accessible and locations documented for executors
- Professional advice engaged for complex elements

## Coach Notes

Estate planning is the topic most owners know they should address but actively avoid. The reasons are understandable - it involves confronting mortality, family dynamics, and legal complexity. The coach's role is to make it feel manageable by breaking it into the specific, practical steps in this checklist.

Start with the quick wins. Getting LPAs registered and checking life insurance trusts are high-impact, low-effort actions. Once those are done, the momentum makes the bigger items feel less overwhelming.

Never provide specific tax or legal advice. The coach's value is in ensuring the client engages with the topic, prioritises the work, and follows through with professional advisers. If a client says "My accountant handles all that," ask them to name three specific things their accountant has done for estate planning in the last year. If they cannot, the work is not being done.
