# Wealth Monitoring Dashboard

## What to Do

Build a simple system to track your net worth, investment performance, and progress toward financial independence goals. You do not need complex software. A spreadsheet updated monthly is enough. The goal is visibility - seeing your complete financial picture in one place so you can make informed decisions and track progress over time.

Set it up once, then update it monthly. The 15 minutes it takes each month is the most valuable financial time you will spend.

## How to Use This

Build the dashboard using a spreadsheet (Google Sheets or Excel). Follow the structure below. Populate it with current values, then set a recurring monthly calendar reminder to update it.

## Why It Matters

Peter Drucker's principle - "What gets measured gets managed" - applies powerfully to personal wealth. Most business owners can tell you their business revenue to the penny but have no idea what their personal net worth is, how their investments performed last quarter, or whether they are on track for financial independence.

Without a dashboard, wealth building happens by accident. Money sits in wrong accounts, underperforming investments go unnoticed, and progress toward goals is invisible. With a dashboard, you see the truth every month. That visibility drives better decisions - where to invest, what to cut, whether to accelerate or relax.

## Dashboard Structure

### Section 1: Net Worth Tracker
- [ ] **List all assets with current values** - Business equity, property, investments, pensions, cash, other. One row per asset. Update monthly.
- [ ] **List all liabilities** - Mortgage, business loans, personal loans, credit cards, other debts. One row per liability. Update monthly.
- [ ] **Calculate total net worth** - Assets minus liabilities. This is your headline number. Track it monthly and plot it on a chart.
- [ ] **Calculate liquid net worth** - Total net worth minus illiquid assets (business, property). This is what you could access in a crisis. Track it separately.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Create a simple spreadsheet with two columns (Asset/Value and Liability/Value) and fill it in right now** - You will have a net worth figure within 30 minutes.

### Section 2: Investment Performance
- [ ] **Track each investment account's value monthly** - ISAs, SIPPs, general investment accounts, property investments. Simple value tracking, not detailed transaction analysis.
- [ ] **Calculate total investment return** - Compare current total to contributions plus previous month's value. You do not need exact IRR calculations - a simple "Am I up or down, and by how much?" is sufficient.
- [ ] **Compare your allocation to your target** - Your Asset Allocation Model sets target percentages. Check monthly whether you have drifted. If any class is 5%+ off target, schedule a rebalance.
- [ ] **Track investment contributions** - How much did you add this month? Are you on track with your annual contribution target?

### Section 3: Financial Independence Progress
- [ ] **Display your FI number prominently** - The total capital target from your Financial Independence Calculator. This is the finish line.
- [ ] **Calculate percentage progress** - Current investment portfolio (excluding business and primary residence) divided by FI number. Express as a percentage. Watch it grow.
- [ ] **Estimate time to FI** - Based on current portfolio, monthly contributions, and assumed returns. This number will fluctuate but gives you a rough timeline.
- [ ] **Track your "FI level"** - Level 1 (6 months' expenses in cash), Level 2 (annual expenses covered), Level 3 (full FI). Which level are you at?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Calculate your current FI percentage** - Investment portfolio divided by FI number, times 100. Even 5% is progress worth celebrating.

### Section 4: Monthly Review Checklist
- [ ] **Update all asset and liability values** - Takes 10-15 minutes with online banking and investment platform access.
- [ ] **Note any significant changes** - Large expenses, windfalls, business distributions, investment gains or losses. Context helps you understand the numbers.
- [ ] **Check for actions needed** - Rebalancing, contribution increases, insurance renewals, policy reviews. The dashboard should trigger action, not just record numbers.
- [ ] **Compare to previous month and same month last year** - Monthly comparison shows short-term progress. Annual comparison shows the trend. Both matter.

- Monthly tracking rhythm that takes 15 minutes
- Clear visibility of progress toward financial independence
- Investment performance monitoring against target allocation
- Data-driven financial decisions instead of guesswork

## Coach Notes

The biggest barrier to building a dashboard is perceived complexity. Many owners think they need sophisticated financial software or a detailed accounting system. They do not. A simple spreadsheet with current values, updated monthly, is more than 90% of business owners have. Start simple and add complexity only if needed.

The first time a client fills in the dashboard is often a significant moment. Seeing their complete financial position in one place - sometimes for the first time ever - frequently triggers important realisations. Common reactions: "I did not realise how much was tied up in the business," "My liquid position is much weaker than I thought," or "I am actually further along than I expected."

Hold clients accountable to the monthly update. It is easy to set up and then neglect. Build it into the coaching rhythm: "Before we discuss business performance, show me your wealth dashboard." Making it a standing agenda item ensures it gets done.

If a client finds the monthly update genuinely impossible (it should not be), suggest automating it. Tools like Moneyhub, Emma, or a simple portfolio tracker can pull investment values automatically. But even a manual spreadsheet works if it is maintained.
