# Tax-Efficient Wealth Transfer

## What to Do

Structure how money moves from your business to your personal investments in the most tax-efficient way possible. Every pound you extract from the business gets taxed. The difference between a well-structured and poorly structured extraction strategy can be worth tens or hundreds of thousands of pounds over a business lifetime.

This is not about avoidance. It is about using legitimate structures, allowances, and timing to keep more of what you have earned.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners take money out of their business in the simplest way possible - salary and dividends - without considering whether a more structured approach would save them significant tax. HMRC provides numerous legitimate reliefs, allowances, and structures specifically designed for business owners. Not using them is leaving money on the table.

The compound effect of tax efficiency is enormous. An owner who saves 5% more of their extraction each year, invested over 20 years, accumulates significantly more wealth than one who does not. Tax efficiency is not a one-time win - it is a permanent advantage that compounds.

## How to Use This

Work through this checklist to identify opportunities you may be missing. Then engage a qualified accountant or tax adviser to implement the ones that apply to your situation. This action provides awareness and direction - professional advice is essential for implementation.

---

## Salary & Dividend Strategy

- [ ] **Review your salary level** - Are you taking the tax-optimal salary? In most cases, this is at the National Insurance threshold. Above that, dividends are usually more tax-efficient.
- [ ] **Optimise your dividend strategy** - Use your annual dividend allowance fully. Consider timing dividends across tax years to stay within lower tax bands.
- [ ] **Consider your spouse or partner's tax position** - If they are a shareholder, their lower tax bands may offer additional extraction efficiency. Ensure any arrangement is commercially legitimate.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Check whether your salary is set at the optimal NI threshold for the current tax year** - This is the single most common missed optimisation. Five minutes with your accountant can save thousands annually.

## Pension Contributions

- [ ] **Maximise employer pension contributions** - Company pension contributions are a corporation tax deduction and do not attract NI or income tax. The annual allowance is generous - make sure you are using it.
- [ ] **Consider carry-forward rules** - If you have unused pension allowance from the previous 3 tax years, you can carry it forward. This allows larger one-off contributions in profitable years.
- [ ] **Review your pension fund structure** - SIPPs offer investment flexibility. Ensure your pension is invested in line with your Asset Allocation Model, not sitting in a default fund.
- [ ] **Plan for the lifetime allowance implications** - Although the lifetime allowance charge has been removed, keep tracking total pension value in case rules change. Tax policy is not permanent.

## Business Asset Structures

- [ ] **Review company ownership of assets** - In some cases, holding property or investments through the company is more tax-efficient than personal ownership. In others, it is not. Get specific advice for your situation.
- [ ] **Consider a holding company structure** - If you have significant retained profits, a holding company can allow tax-efficient reinvestment and provide asset protection. Not suitable for every business, but worth evaluating.
- [ ] **Review Business Asset Disposal Relief (formerly Entrepreneurs' Relief)** - Understand the current rules and qualifying criteria. This relief can significantly reduce CGT on business sale, but it has conditions and limits.
- [ ] **Plan for inheritance tax implications** - Business Property Relief can shelter business assets from IHT, but the rules are specific. Ensure your structure qualifies and plan ahead.

## Extraction Timing

- [ ] **Plan profit extraction around tax year boundaries** - Timing dividend declarations and bonus payments to span two tax years can keep income within lower bands.
- [ ] **Build a multi-year extraction plan** - Rather than taking large sums in one year, plan a 3-5 year extraction strategy that smooths income and minimises peak tax rates.
- [ ] **Consider retained profits strategy** - Not all profits need extracting immediately. Retained profits grow within the company and can be extracted more efficiently over time or via eventual sale.
- [ ] **Review annually with your accountant** - Tax rules change every budget. What was optimal last year may not be optimal this year. Book an annual tax planning meeting, not just a compliance review.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Ask your accountant: "Am I extracting profits in the most tax-efficient way?"** - If they have not proactively raised this, you may need a more advisory-focused accountant.

## Expected Outcome

- Clear understanding of current extraction strategy and its tax efficiency
- Identified opportunities for more tax-efficient extraction
- Professional advice engaged for implementation
- Multi-year extraction plan that maximises wealth transfer from business to personal investments
- Annual review rhythm that keeps the strategy current with changing tax rules

## Coach Notes

This action sits at the intersection of coaching and professional advice. The coach's role is to ensure the client is thinking about tax efficiency and engaging the right professionals. The coach should not provide specific tax advice - that is for accountants and tax advisers.

Many business owners have a compliance-focused accountant who files returns accurately but does not proactively advise on tax planning. If a client cannot remember the last time their accountant suggested a tax-saving strategy, they may benefit from a more advisory-focused relationship. Raising this question diplomatically is part of the coaching value.

Watch for the owner who avoids this topic because it feels complicated or "not my area." Tax efficiency is not optional at Level 5 - the sums involved are too significant. Push them to engage, even if they delegate the detail to a professional.
