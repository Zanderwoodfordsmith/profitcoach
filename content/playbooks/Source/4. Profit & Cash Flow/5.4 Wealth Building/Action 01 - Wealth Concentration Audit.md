# Wealth Concentration Audit

## What to Do

Map exactly where your wealth sits and identify concentration risks. List every asset you own, its approximate value, and what percentage of your total net worth it represents. Most business owners are shocked by how much of their net worth depends on a single asset - the business itself.

This is not about judging your financial position. It is about seeing it clearly so you can make informed decisions about diversification and risk.

## Why It Matters

Thomas Stanley's research in "The Millionaire Next Door" found that most wealthy business owners have 60-80% of their net worth tied up in their business. That is not wealth - that is concentration risk dressed up as success. If the business suffers a downturn, gets disrupted, or fails to sell at expected value, decades of work can evaporate.

Nassim Taleb's concept of antifragility applies here. A portfolio that is 80% dependent on one asset is fragile. One bad event - a market shift, a key client leaving, a health crisis - can wipe out most of your net worth. The audit is the first step toward building a more resilient financial position.

## How to Use This

Gather your financial information (bank statements, investment accounts, property valuations, pension statements, business valuation estimate) and work through the mapping exercise. Be honest about values - use conservative estimates rather than optimistic ones.

---

## Asset Mapping

- [ ] **Estimate the current value of your business** - Use a conservative multiple of normalised EBITDA, or the price you would realistically accept if someone offered to buy today. Not the aspirational price - the realistic one.
- [ ] **List all property assets with current values** - Primary residence, investment properties, commercial property. Use recent comparable sales, not purchase price or peak value.
- [ ] **List all investment accounts and balances** - ISAs, general investment accounts, stocks, bonds, funds. Include crypto or alternative investments if applicable.
- [ ] **List all pension provisions and current values** - Personal pensions, SIPPs, workplace pensions, defined benefit entitlements. Include estimated values.
- [ ] **List all cash and savings** - Current accounts, savings accounts, fixed-term deposits. Include business cash reserves if you own 100% of the business.
- [ ] **List any other significant assets** - Vehicles, collectibles, intellectual property, loans owed to you, partnership interests.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Write down your best guess of business value as a percentage of total net worth before doing the detailed mapping** - Then compare after. The gap between perception and reality is usually the most powerful part of this exercise.

## Concentration Analysis

- [ ] **Calculate the percentage of net worth in each asset class** - Business equity, property, investments, pensions, cash, other. Express each as a percentage of total.
- [ ] **Flag any single asset representing more than 40% of net worth** - This is your primary concentration risk. It deserves specific attention and a plan to reduce over time.
- [ ] **Assess liquidity of each asset** - How quickly could you convert each asset to cash? Business equity and property are illiquid. Investments and cash are liquid. Know the difference.
- [ ] **Identify correlated risks** - If your business is in property, your commercial property and business equity may be correlated. A property downturn hits both simultaneously. Look for hidden correlations.
- [ ] **Calculate your "liquid net worth"** - Total net worth minus illiquid assets (business, property). This is what you could actually access in a crisis. For many owners, this number is uncomfortably small.

## Risk Assessment

- [ ] **Scenario test: What happens to your net worth if the business value drops by 50%?** - How much of your total wealth would you lose? Can you afford that outcome?
- [ ] **Scenario test: What if you could not sell the business at all?** - Some businesses are worth a lot to the owner but very little on the open market. What would your net worth look like without the business value?
- [ ] **Identify your single biggest financial vulnerability** - The one event that could damage your wealth most severely. Name it and start thinking about how to protect against it.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Check whether your personal finances could survive 12 months with zero income from the business** - If not, building a personal cash buffer becomes an immediate priority.

---

## Expected Outcome

- Complete map of all assets with realistic valuations
- Clear picture of wealth concentration, especially business dependency
- Identification of liquidity gaps and correlated risks
- Understanding of vulnerability to specific scenarios
- Foundation for building a diversification strategy

## Coach Notes

This exercise often produces discomfort. Owners who feel wealthy discover that most of their wealth is illiquid and concentrated. That is not a failure - it is normal for business owners. The value is in seeing it clearly and deciding to do something about it.

Be careful with business valuations. Owners tend to use the number they heard from a broker or read about comparable sales. Push for conservative estimates. A business that "could be worth" a certain amount is not the same as one that has a firm offer at that price.

The emotional response to this audit varies widely. Some owners feel motivated to diversify immediately. Others feel defensive ("The business IS my best investment"). Both reactions are valid. The coach's role is to ensure the data is accurate and the risks are understood, then let the owner decide their comfort level.
