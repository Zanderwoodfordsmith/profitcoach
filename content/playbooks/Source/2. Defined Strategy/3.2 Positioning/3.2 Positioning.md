## **What This Is**

Positioning is how your customers think about you compared to everyone else they could choose. It is not what you say about yourself — it is what they believe about why you are different and better. When positioning is sharp, price becomes less important and competition becomes less scary.

Most businesses compete on effort: "We work harder" or "We care more." That puts you in a race to the bottom where the cheapest wins. Positioning lets you compete on value instead. It answers one simple question: why would someone choose you over a credible alternative?

This playbook is about getting deliberately different. Not just better — different. Because being 10% better gets you 10% more customers. Being different gets you 100% of the customers who want that difference.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

You compete mostly on price and find yourself saying "we are different because we care more." Prospects compare you to everyone else and you cannot explain why they should pay more for you. You win some and lose some, but you are not sure what makes the difference. Your sales conversations feel like negotiations where lower price is the main way to close deals.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You know you are different but struggle to explain it clearly to prospects. Sometimes your differentiation lands and people get it immediately. Other times you sound like everyone else. You have some happy customers who love what makes you special, but new prospects do not always see it. Your positioning works in person but gets lost in your marketing.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Prospects understand immediately why they would choose you over the competition — not just because you are better, but because you are different in a way that matters to them. You rarely compete on price because your value is obvious. You attract customers who want specifically what you offer, not just anyone looking for your category of service. Your team can explain your positioning consistently without thinking about it.

## **Things to Think About**

What do you do that no credible competitor does? This is your unique mechanism — the specific thing that delivers the outcome your customers want. Not "we care more" but "we use this method" or "we focus on this aspect." If a good competitor could copy it tomorrow, it is not positioning.

What category are you actually competing in? Are you a web designer competing against other web designers, or are you competing against doing nothing? Sometimes the real competition is not who you think it is. Sometimes you are competing against the status quo, not other providers.

Who loses when you win? Every positioning choice means saying no to someone. If your positioning appeals to everyone, it compels no one. Being the cheapest loses you quality buyers. Being the premium loses you price buyers. What are you willing to lose to win bigger?

Does your differentiation actually matter to buyers? Being different is not enough — being different in a way that customers care about is positioning. The best technical solution loses to the solution that solves the problem customers actually want solved.

Can you prove your positioning is real? Anyone can claim to be "the experts" or "the innovators." Positioning that sticks comes with evidence: case studies, specific outcomes, named methods, clear proof that this difference exists and delivers value.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Competitive Intelligence — Map your real competitive landscape so you understand exactly who you are up against and where the gaps are. Not just direct competitors, but alternative solutions your prospects might choose instead.

Play 2: Unique Advantage Audit — Identify what you genuinely do differently that delivers measurable value. This goes beyond features to find the underlying approach, method, or focus that makes your results different.

Play 3: Category Strategy — Decide whether to compete within an existing category or create a new one. Sometimes being the best in category wins. Sometimes creating a new category wins bigger.

Play 4: Positioning Statements — Craft clear, compelling language that explains your differentiation in terms prospects immediately understand and care about. Test different angles until you find what resonates.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: List three credible competitors and write down exactly how a prospect would choose between you and them. If the answer is mostly price, your positioning needs work.

This week: Ask your last three happy customers: "Why did you choose us over the alternatives?" Their actual words matter more than what you think your positioning is.

This week: Read your website homepage as if you were shopping for your service. Circle anything that sounds exactly like what your competitors would say. That is where your positioning is weakest.
