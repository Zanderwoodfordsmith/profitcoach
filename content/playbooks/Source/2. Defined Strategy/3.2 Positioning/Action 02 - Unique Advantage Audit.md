# Unique Advantage Audit

## What to Do

Identify what you genuinely do differently that delivers measurable value to clients. This goes far beyond listing features or services. It means uncovering the underlying approach, method, experience, or focus that makes your results different from what a client would get elsewhere.

Most businesses think they are different but cannot explain why in terms a prospect cares about. This audit fixes that.

## Why It Matters

"We provide great service" is not a unique advantage. Every competitor says the same thing. If your differentiation is not specific, provable, and meaningful to your target audience, you have no positioning. You are a commodity. And commodities compete on price.

Real unique advantage is not about being better at everything. It is about being genuinely different in a way that matters to a specific type of client. Southwest Airlines is not better than Emirates. It is different in a way that matters to budget-conscious travellers. Your job is to find your version of that difference.

## How to Use This

Work through the layers below from surface level to deep. Most real advantages hide in the deeper layers where competitors do not bother to look.

---

## Surface Layer: What You Offer

- [ ] **List every service and product you provide** - Everything a client can buy from you. Be comprehensive.
- [ ] **Mark which of these your competitors also offer** - Be honest. If three competitors offer the same service, it is table stakes, not an advantage.
- [ ] **Identify anything only you offer** - Any service, feature, guarantee, or inclusion that no competitor provides. If nothing stands out, that is an important finding.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Ask yourself: "If I removed our company name, would a prospect know this was us?"** - If your marketing could belong to any competitor, your differentiation is not visible enough.

## Method Layer: How You Do It

- [ ] **Document your process step by step** - How do you actually deliver results? From first contact to final outcome, what is your method?
- [ ] **Identify where your process differs from the standard approach** - Do you include something others skip? Do you sequence things differently? Do you spend more time on a particular phase?
- [ ] **Name your method** - If your approach is genuinely different, give it a name. A named method feels proprietary even if individual elements are common. "The Profit System" is a named method. "Business coaching" is a commodity.
- [ ] **Document the results your method produces** - Specific outcomes, timeframes, client transformations. Numbers beat adjectives.

## Experience Layer: Why You See Things Differently

- [ ] **What relevant experience do you have that competitors lack?** - Industry background, career history, personal experience, failures you have learned from. This shapes perspective, and perspective shapes results.
- [ ] **What pattern do you consistently see that others miss?** - After years of doing this work, what do you notice that less experienced providers overlook?
- [ ] **What counter-intuitive belief drives your approach?** - The most powerful positioning often comes from disagreeing with conventional wisdom. What does everyone else believe that you think is wrong?
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Complete this sentence: "Most [your industry] providers believe X. We believe Y instead, which is why we..."** - This is often the seed of your strongest positioning statement.

## Proof Layer: Evidence That It Works

- [ ] **Collect your strongest client outcomes** - Specific numbers, transformations, before-and-after stories. The more concrete, the more credible.
- [ ] **Identify your best case study** - One story that perfectly illustrates your unique advantage in action. This becomes your signature proof point.
- [ ] **List credentials, awards, or recognition** - Not to boast, but to reduce perceived risk. Third-party validation matters to cautious buyers.
- [ ] **Note any guarantees you can offer** - A guarantee that competitors cannot match is a powerful differentiator. Only offer what you can deliver.

## Synthesise Your Unique Advantage

- [ ] **Write a single paragraph describing your unique advantage** - Combine the best elements from each layer. Method + experience + proof = compelling advantage.
- [ ] **Test it against the "so what?" filter** - Read your paragraph from the prospect's perspective. Does it answer "why should I choose you?" If not, sharpen it.
- [ ] **Validate with 3 existing clients** - Ask them: "Why did you choose us over the alternatives?" Their language is often better than yours for describing your advantage.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A layered understanding of what makes you genuinely different
- A named method or approach that prospects can remember and refer to
- Specific proof points that back up your claims
- A clear, one-paragraph description of your unique advantage
- Client-validated language for describing your differentiation

## Coach Notes

Most clients start this exercise believing they are more differentiated than they are. The surface layer often reveals that they offer the same services as five competitors. That is not a problem to hide from. It is the starting point for finding deeper differentiation.

The method layer is where most real advantages live. Two accountants can offer "tax planning" but the one who has a named 5-step process for reducing tax by 20%+ feels like a specialist while the other feels generic. Help clients name their method, even if it feels uncomfortable. Imposter syndrome often kicks in here. "Who am I to name my process?" Anyone who has developed a consistent way of delivering results, that is who. Push past the discomfort. The "counter-intuitive belief" question is often the most powerful. It takes courage to articulate, but it is what makes positioning truly distinctive.
