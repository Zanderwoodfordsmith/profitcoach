## **What This Is**

Exit strategy is about building optionality. Not necessarily leaving your business, but creating the choice to leave — which paradoxically makes the business more valuable whether you stay or go.

Most owners avoid thinking about exit because it feels like giving up or because it seems decades away. But preparing for exit makes you a better business owner today. It forces you to reduce dependencies, strengthen systems, and build real value. Every exit-ready business is a better business to run.

This playbook is not about retirement planning. It is about building a business so valuable and independent that selling becomes a strategic choice rather than a desperate necessity.

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

The business cannot survive without you for more than a few weeks. All major decisions flow through you, key relationships are tied to your personal connections, and critical processes exist only in your head. The idea of selling feels impossible because there is nothing to sell beyond a job that requires you to show up every day. Your business has no value independent of your labour.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

The business can run without you for short periods but struggles with bigger decisions or unexpected situations. Some processes are documented and systems exist, but key relationships and knowledge are still concentrated around you. You have thought about eventual exit but have not taken concrete steps to prepare for it.

### **🟢 When this is working**

Your business could be sold within 24 months if needed. Clear exit routes have been identified and evaluated. The business generates strong cash flows independently of your daily involvement, systems and teams handle operations, and value exists in transferable assets rather than personal relationships. You have genuine options — sell, scale, or step back — because optionality has been deliberately built.

## **Things to Think About**

What would a buyer actually be purchasing? If someone bought your business tomorrow, would they be buying systems, processes, and cash flows — or would they be buying a job that requires them to work as hard as you do? Real value lies in what works without you.

How many realistic exit routes do you have? Trade sale to a competitor, management buyout by your team, transition to passive income, or strategic partnership — each requires different preparation. Most owners assume there is only one way out when there are actually several paths worth exploring.

Could your business survive and thrive if you disappeared for six months? This is the ultimate test of exit readiness. If the answer is no, your first job is not finding a buyer — it is building a business that does not need you for daily decisions.

What drives value in your industry? Revenue multiples, profit margins, customer retention, market position — different industries value different things. Understanding what buyers pay for in your sector tells you exactly what to build and optimise.

Do you know what your business is actually worth? Most owners dramatically overestimate their business value because they confuse revenue with value or effort with worth. Getting a proper valuation early tells you what gaps need closing before any exit conversation.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Exit Route Assessment — Map and evaluate all realistic exit options for your business. Different routes require different preparation timelines and value different assets. Know your options before you need them.

Play 2: Value Driver Analysis — Identify what actually drives value in your industry and business model. Focus your efforts on the specific factors that buyers pay premiums for rather than generic business improvements.

Play 3: Dependency Audit — Systematically identify and eliminate your personal dependencies. Map every decision, relationship, and process that currently requires you and create plans to transfer or systematise each one.

Play 4: Exit Readiness Scorecard — Assess your current readiness across financial, operational, legal, and strategic dimensions. Track progress over time and identify the biggest gaps blocking a potential sale.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: List every decision you made in the last month that required your personal approval. Circle the ones that could have been made by someone else with clear criteria.

This week: Research three businesses similar to yours that have been sold in the last two years. What did the buyers highlight as valuable? What drove the purchase price?

This week: Book a conversation with a business broker or M\&A advisor in your industry. Not to sell, but to understand what buyers are looking for and what your business would need to be attractive.
