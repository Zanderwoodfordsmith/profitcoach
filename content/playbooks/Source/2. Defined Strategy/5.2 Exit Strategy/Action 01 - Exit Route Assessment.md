# Exit Route Assessment

## What to Do

Map and evaluate all realistic exit options available to you. Different exit routes require different preparation timelines and value different business assets. Before you can prepare for an exit, you need to understand your options clearly - including routes you may not have considered.

Work through each exit type, assess its viability for your specific business, and identify what preparation each route would require. You are not committing to a route yet. You are building a menu of informed options.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners have a vague idea about "selling the business someday" but have never mapped out what that actually means. John Warrillow's research for "Built to Sell" found that 75% of owners who put their business on the market fail to sell. The primary reason is not that the business is unsaleable - it is that the owner did not prepare for the specific route they eventually chose.

Different exits create radically different outcomes. A trade sale might maximise price but eliminate your brand. A management buyout preserves culture but may limit valuation. An ESOP builds loyalty but is complex to structure. Knowing the options and their trade-offs lets you prepare intentionally, not reactively.

## How to Use This

Work through each exit route assessment honestly. Some routes will not be viable for your business - that is useful information. For the ones that are viable, note what preparation they would require and how long it would take.

## Exit Routes to Evaluate

### Trade Sale (Acquisition by Another Company)
- [ ] **Identify 5-10 potential acquirers in your industry** - Competitors, complementary businesses, private equity portfolio companies. Who would benefit from owning your business?
- [ ] **Assess what they would value most** - Client base? Technology? Team? Market position? IP? Different buyers care about different things.
- [ ] **Estimate preparation timeline** - Trade sales typically require 2-3 years of preparation to maximise value. Where are you on that timeline?

### Management Buyout (MBO)
- [ ] **Assess whether your leadership team has the capability and desire to buy** - Not everyone wants to be an owner. Have honest conversations.
- [ ] **Consider funding options** - Can the team fund the purchase through earn-out, seller financing, or external investment? Most MBOs require creative structuring.
- [ ] **Evaluate whether the business can sustain the debt** - An MBO often means the business borrows to fund the purchase. Can cash flow support that?

### Employee Ownership (ESOP/EOT)
- [ ] **Research the tax advantages of Employee Ownership Trusts (EOTs)** - In the UK, selling to an EOT can be CGT-free. Significant financial benefit worth understanding.
- [ ] **Assess cultural readiness** - Does your team want ownership responsibility? Employee ownership works best in cultures that already share information and accountability.

### Private Equity Investment
- [ ] **Determine whether PE is a realistic option for your size and sector** - PE firms typically look for businesses with EBITDA above a certain threshold. Know whether you qualify.
- [ ] **Understand what PE investors want** - Scalable model, strong management team, growth potential, clean financials. Assess your gaps honestly.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Talk to one business owner in your network who has taken PE investment** - First-hand experience is worth more than any article.

### Family Succession
- [ ] **Assess whether family members are willing and capable** - Willingness without capability is dangerous. Capability without willingness is pointless.
- [ ] **Consider the impact on non-family team members** - Key people may leave if they see family succession as a ceiling on their growth.

### Lifestyle Hold (Keep and Optimise)
- [ ] **Evaluate the "do nothing" option honestly** - What if the best exit is not to exit? A business that generates strong cash flow, runs without you, and funds your life may be the best asset you can own.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Calculate your annual return on equity if you kept the business forever** - Compare that to what you would earn investing the sale proceeds. Sometimes keeping is better than selling.

## Expected Outcome

- Clear understanding of all viable exit routes for your specific business
- Honest assessment of preparation required for each route
- Identification of the 2-3 most attractive options to explore further
- Understanding of timelines and what needs to happen next
- Foundation for making an informed exit strategy decision

## Coach Notes

Many owners have never seriously considered anything beyond "sell the business." Expanding their thinking to include MBOs, EOTs, PE, and lifestyle holds often changes their perspective significantly. The best exit is not always the most lucrative - it is the one that serves their life vision.

Watch for emotional attachment distorting the assessment. Some owners overvalue their business because it is their life's work. Others undervalue it because they are tired and want out. Neither perspective produces good decisions. Push for objective assessment - ideally backed by professional valuation.

This is also a good time to introduce the concept of preparation timeline. If the owner wants to exit in 3 years, some routes are already off the table because they require longer preparation. Clarity on timing narrows the options helpfully.
