# Value Driver Analysis

## What to Do

Identify what actually drives value in your industry and business model, then assess how your business performs against each driver. Buyers pay premiums for specific characteristics. This action identifies those characteristics and shows you where to focus your preparation effort for maximum impact on valuation.

This is not about inflating numbers. It is about understanding what makes a business genuinely valuable and building those qualities intentionally.

## Why It Matters

John Warrillow identified eight key drivers of company value in "Built to Sell." Businesses that score well across these drivers sell for 3-6x more than comparable businesses that do not. The difference is rarely about revenue - it is about the quality and sustainability of that revenue.

Most owners focus on growing revenue when preparing for exit. But a buyer looking at two businesses with the same revenue will pay dramatically more for the one with recurring contracts, diversified clients, documented systems, and a strong management team. Value drivers are the multiplier, not the multiple.

## How to Use This

Score your business against each value driver honestly (1-5 scale). Identify the 3 drivers with the biggest gap between current score and potential. Focus your preparation effort there.

## Core Value Drivers

### Revenue Quality
- [ ] **Calculate your recurring revenue percentage** - What share of revenue comes from contracts, subscriptions, or retainers versus one-off projects? Higher recurring revenue = higher valuation. Score yourself 1-5.
- [ ] **Measure revenue concentration** - What percentage comes from your top 3 clients? If any single client represents more than 15% of revenue, that is a risk factor buyers will discount.
- [ ] **Assess revenue predictability** - Can you forecast next quarter's revenue within 10%? Predictable businesses command premium multiples.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Calculate your top-client concentration right now** - Revenue from top 3 clients divided by total revenue. If it is above 30%, this becomes a priority to address.

### Growth Trajectory
- [ ] **Document your 3-year revenue trend** - Consistent growth is more valuable than spiky performance. A buyer wants to see a pattern they believe will continue.
- [ ] **Identify untapped growth opportunities** - New markets, products, or channels you have not yet pursued. These represent upside a buyer can capture.
- [ ] **Assess the sustainability of current growth** - Is it dependent on your personal relationships, a single marketing channel, or market conditions that could change?

### Operational Independence
- [ ] **Score your owner-dependency (1-5)** - 1 = nothing works without you. 5 = the business runs smoothly when you are away for a month. Be honest.
- [ ] **Assess management team depth** - Could your current leadership team run the business for 12 months without you? If not, what is missing?
- [ ] **Review system documentation** - Are your key processes documented and repeatable? Or do they live in people's heads?

### Financial Health
- [ ] **Clean up your financial statements** - Ensure P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow statements are accurate, up to date, and professionally prepared. Messy financials kill deals.
- [ ] **Identify and adjust owner-specific expenses** - Personal expenses running through the business distort profitability. Identify them and calculate "normalised" EBITDA.
- [ ] **Assess working capital requirements** - Businesses that require heavy working capital are worth less than asset-light models. Know your position.

### Competitive Position
- [ ] **Define your competitive moat** - What makes it hard for competitors to replicate what you do? Brand, IP, relationships, data, network effects? If the answer is "nothing," that is a problem for valuation.
- [ ] **Assess market position** - Are you a leader, a strong challenger, or a commodity player? Position affects multiple.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **List your top 3 competitive advantages and ask: "Would these survive without me?"** - If they depend on you personally, they are not value drivers for a buyer.

## Expected Outcome

- Honest scorecard of your business across all major value drivers
- Clear picture of where the biggest valuation gaps exist
- Prioritised list of 3 value drivers to improve over the next 12-24 months
- Understanding of what buyers in your industry actually pay premiums for
- Foundation for a focused exit preparation plan

## Coach Notes

The hardest part of this exercise is honesty. Owners tend to score themselves generously, especially on operational independence ("My team could handle it" when they have never actually tested this). Push for evidence, not feelings.

Revenue quality is almost always the highest-impact area to improve. Converting project revenue to recurring revenue, or diversifying away from a concentrated client base, can dramatically increase valuation. These changes take time, which is why starting early matters.

If an owner scores low across most drivers, the conversation may need to shift from "How do I prepare for exit?" to "Is the business ready to be valuable at all?" That is a harder but more useful conversation. Sometimes the best exit preparation is spending 2-3 years building a genuinely better business.
