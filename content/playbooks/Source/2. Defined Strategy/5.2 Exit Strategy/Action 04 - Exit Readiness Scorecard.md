# Exit Readiness Scorecard

## What to Do

Assess your current readiness for exit across four dimensions: financial, operational, legal, and strategic. Score each area honestly, identify gaps, and track progress over time. This is the dashboard that tells you how close you are to being able to exit on your terms.

Update the scorecard quarterly to track progress and ensure preparation stays on track. A score below 3 in any dimension means that dimension would either block a deal or significantly reduce valuation.

## Why It Matters

Exit readiness is not binary - you are not simply "ready" or "not ready." It is a spectrum across multiple dimensions, and weakness in any single area can derail an otherwise strong exit. Owners who track readiness over time prepare more effectively than those who try to get ready in a rush when an opportunity appears.

The owners who achieve the best exits are those who started preparing 3-5 years before they sold. Not because the preparation takes that long, but because the changes that increase value - reducing dependency, diversifying revenue, building management depth - compound over time. This scorecard keeps that long-term preparation focused and measurable.

## How to Use This

Score each item 1-5, where 1 is "not started" and 5 is "fully ready." Calculate an average for each dimension. Any dimension averaging below 3 is a priority. Review and rescore quarterly.

## Financial Readiness

- [ ] **Financial statements are clean, accurate, and professionally prepared (score 1-5)** - A buyer's accountant will scrutinise every number. Messy accounts either kill deals or invite aggressive price reductions.
- [ ] **3+ years of consistent or growing revenue documented (score 1-5)** - Buyers want evidence of a trend, not a single good year. Consistency matters more than peak performance.
- [ ] **Owner compensation is at market rate (score 1-5)** - If you are overpaying or underpaying yourself, normalise it. Buyers calculate value from normalised earnings.
- [ ] **No significant off-balance-sheet liabilities or tax issues (score 1-5)** - Outstanding tax disputes, pending claims, or undisclosed liabilities will surface in due diligence and destroy trust.
- [ ] **Working capital requirements are understood and manageable (score 1-5)** - Can you clearly explain how much cash the business needs to operate? Buyers need this for their financial model.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Ask your accountant: "If someone wanted to buy this business, what would concern you about our financials?"** - Their answer becomes your financial readiness to-do list.

## Operational Readiness

- [ ] **Business operates without daily owner involvement (score 1-5)** - Can you be absent for a month without performance declining? If not, you are the product, and that limits value.
- [ ] **Key processes are documented and repeatable (score 1-5)** - A buyer needs to see that operations are systematic, not dependent on tribal knowledge.
- [ ] **Management team is capable and retained (score 1-5)** - Strong leadership with contracts, incentives, and commitment to stay through a transition. Key person risk is a major buyer concern.
- [ ] **Technology and systems are current and scalable (score 1-5)** - Outdated systems or technical debt reduce valuation. Buyers factor in the cost to modernise.
- [ ] **No single points of failure in operations (score 1-5)** - One person, one system, one supplier that everything depends on. Any single point of failure is a risk buyers will discount.

## Legal Readiness

- [ ] **All contracts are current, documented, and transferable (score 1-5)** - Client contracts, supplier agreements, leases, partnerships. Are they written, signed, and assignable?
- [ ] **IP is properly protected and owned by the company (score 1-5)** - Trademarks, patents, copyrights, domain names. Owned by the company, not by you personally.
- [ ] **Employment contracts and policies are compliant and up to date (score 1-5)** - Including non-compete clauses, IP assignment, and data protection compliance.
- [ ] **No outstanding or potential legal disputes (score 1-5)** - Pending litigation, regulatory issues, or unresolved disputes. Buyers walk away from legal risk.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Check whether your key client contracts have a "change of ownership" clause** - Some contracts allow clients to terminate on ownership change. Know this before a buyer finds it.

## Strategic Readiness

- [ ] **Clear growth story for the next buyer (score 1-5)** - A buyer is purchasing the future, not the past. Can you articulate a compelling growth plan they can execute?
- [ ] **Customer base is diversified (score 1-5)** - No single client represents more than 10-15% of revenue. Concentration risk is one of the most common deal-killers.
- [ ] **Competitive position is defensible (score 1-5)** - The business has something that makes it hard to replicate: brand, data, network, IP, or market position.
- [ ] **Market conditions favour a sale (score 1-5)** - Is your industry growing or contracting? Are multiples in your sector trending up or down? Timing matters.

## Expected Outcome

- Quantified readiness score across four dimensions with specific item-level detail
- Clear identification of the weakest areas requiring immediate attention
- Quarterly trend data showing preparation progress over time
- Prioritised action plan for closing the biggest gaps
- Confidence in understanding exactly where you stand and what remains to be done

## Coach Notes

The scorecard is most valuable when used honestly over time. Encourage clients to score conservatively - it is better to be pleasantly surprised during due diligence than to discover gaps you did not know existed.

Quarterly reviews should be short and focused: "What improved? What did not? Why?" If scores are not improving, the preparation plan is not working and needs adjustment.

The legal dimension often gets neglected because owners find it boring and expensive. But legal issues are the most common reason deals collapse in due diligence. Push clients to engage a solicitor early, even if exit is 2-3 years away. A legal health check now is far cheaper than a failed deal later.

If a client's overall score is below 2.5, they are likely 3+ years from a strong exit. Frame that honestly but constructively - it means there is time to build real value, not just cosmetic improvements.
