# Partnership Opportunity Audit

## What to Do

Identify potential partners who could accelerate your growth through referrals, joint ventures, channel partnerships, or strategic alliances. This is a systematic scan of your business ecosystem to find organisations and individuals who serve the same customer you serve but do not compete with you directly. The goal is a prioritised shortlist of partnership opportunities worth pursuing.

## Why It Matters

Partnerships can be the fastest and cheapest growth lever available to a small business. Instead of building an audience from scratch, you access someone else's established trust and distribution. Ryan Deiss calls these "before and after" partners - businesses that serve your customer either before they need you or after you are done with them. A single strong partnership can deliver more qualified leads than months of cold outreach. But most business owners either ignore partnerships entirely or pursue them randomly. A structured audit changes that.

## How to Use This

Map your customer journey, identify the gaps where partners could add value, then build a prioritised target list.

---

## Ecosystem Mapping

- [ ] **Map your customer's journey before they reach you** - What problem do they solve before yours becomes urgent? Who helps them with that problem? Those businesses are potential referral partners.
- [ ] **Map what happens after you finish** - What does your customer need next? Who provides that? These are your "after" partners.
- [ ] **Identify complementary service providers** - Who serves the same customer with a different service? Accountants and solicitors. Marketing agencies and web developers. Your combination will be different, but the logic is the same.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **List 5 businesses that already send you referrals informally** - These are your warmest partnership opportunities. Formalising what already happens is far easier than building from scratch.

## Partnership Types

- [ ] **Referral partnerships** - Simple introductions in both directions. Low commitment, low complexity, easy to start. Score each potential partner on: quality of their audience, strength of their relationship with that audience, and their willingness to refer.
- [ ] **Joint ventures** - Collaborative projects, co-created content, shared events, or bundled offerings. Higher commitment but higher value. Only pursue with partners whose quality and values match yours.
- [ ] **Channel partnerships** - A partner sells or distributes your service as part of their offering. Higher complexity and requires clear agreements on pricing, delivery, and quality control.
- [ ] **Strategic alliances** - Deep, long-term relationships where both businesses invest in shared outcomes. Rare but transformative when they work. Only appropriate with partners who share your strategic direction.

## Opportunity Assessment

- [ ] **Create a target list of 10-15 potential partners** - Combine names from your ecosystem map, existing informal relationships, and industry connections.
- [ ] **Score each partner on three criteria** - (1) Audience overlap: Do they serve your ideal customer? (2) Reputation quality: Would you be proud to be associated with them? (3) Reciprocity potential: Can you offer genuine value back?
- [ ] **Rank your top 5** - These are your priority targets. Focus your energy here rather than spreading across all 15.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Reach out to your number one prospect this week** - A simple message: "I have been thinking about how we could help each other's clients. Fancy a coffee?" Do not overthink it.
- [ ] **Identify what you bring to the table** - Before approaching any partner, be clear about what value you offer them. If the answer is "we would like their referrals," that is not a partnership. That is asking for a favour.

## Risk Check

- [ ] **Assess reputational risk for each partner** - If this partner has a public failure, how does it affect your brand? Only partner with businesses whose quality you trust completely.
- [ ] **Check for competitive overlap** - Could this partner become a competitor? If they are building capability in your space, a partnership might help them more than it helps you.
- [ ] **Evaluate dependency risk** - If a single partner accounts for more than 25% of your leads, you are too dependent. Diversify.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A mapped ecosystem showing where partnership opportunities exist
- A scored and ranked list of 10-15 potential partners
- A prioritised top 5 with clear reasoning for each
- At least one outreach conversation initiated this week
- A clear understanding of what you offer partners in return

## Coach Notes

Many clients have untapped referral relationships sitting right in front of them. The quick win of formalising existing informal referrals almost always produces immediate results. Push them to act on it before building complex new partnerships. The most common failure in partnerships is one-sidedness. If the client cannot clearly articulate what value they bring to the partner, the partnership will not last. Spend time on this before any outreach. Also watch for clients who want to pursue too many partnerships at once. Partnerships require relationship investment. Three well-managed partnerships beat ten neglected ones.
