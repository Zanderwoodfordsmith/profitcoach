# Growth Metrics Dashboard

## What to Do

Build a focused dashboard that tracks whether your growth initiatives are delivering strategic value or just consuming resources. This is not a comprehensive business dashboard. It is specifically designed to measure the health and performance of your growth efforts, so you can double down on what works and cut what does not before it drains your business.

## Why It Matters

Peter Drucker's principle holds: what gets measured gets managed. But the inverse is equally true - what gets measured badly gets managed badly. Most businesses either track too many metrics (drowning in data but starving for insight) or track vanity metrics that look impressive but do not connect to value. For growth specifically, you need leading indicators that tell you what is about to happen, not just lagging indicators that tell you what already happened. A well-designed growth dashboard gives you early warning signals and clear evidence for decision-making.

## How to Use This

Select metrics that match your specific growth initiatives. Build the simplest possible tracking system. Review weekly. Act on what you see.

---

## Metric Selection

- [ ] **Define 3-5 growth-specific metrics** - These should directly measure the performance of your active growth initiatives. Not general business health metrics. Growth-specific. Examples: new customer acquisition rate, expansion revenue from existing customers, partnership-sourced leads, new market conversion rate.
- [ ] **Include at least one leading indicator per initiative** - A leading indicator predicts future results. Pipeline value predicts revenue. Website traffic predicts lead volume. Customer satisfaction predicts retention. Leading indicators give you time to act.
- [ ] **Include at least one efficiency metric** - Growth that costs more than it returns is not growth. Track customer acquisition cost, revenue per employee, or margin on new business. This prevents the trap of growing revenue while shrinking profit.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Pick the single most important metric for your top growth initiative** - If you could only track one number, what would tell you whether the initiative is working? Start there.
- [ ] **Set targets for each metric** - What does good look like? Be specific. "More leads" is not a target. "15 qualified leads per month from partnership channel" is.

## Dashboard Design

- [ ] **Keep it to one page** - If your growth dashboard does not fit on one page (or one screen), it is too complex. Simplicity forces focus.
- [ ] **Use a simple tracking tool** - A spreadsheet is fine. A whiteboard is fine. Do not let tool selection delay the work. The best dashboard is the one you actually look at.
- [ ] **Show trends, not just snapshots** - Display at least 12 weeks of history for each metric so you can see direction, not just current position. A metric that is below target but trending up tells a different story than one that is below target and falling.
- [ ] **Include a red/amber/green status for each metric** - Green: on or above target. Amber: within 10% of target. Red: more than 10% below target. Simple visual status makes weekly reviews fast and focused.

## Review Rhythm

- [ ] **Review weekly in 15 minutes or less** - Look at each metric. Note the status. Identify anything that needs action. Do not turn the review into a meeting about the meeting.
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: **Add a 5-minute growth metrics check to your existing weekly meeting** - Do not create a separate meeting. Bolt it onto what you already do.
- [ ] **Act on red metrics within one week** - If a metric is red, something needs to change. Either adjust the initiative, increase resources, or reconsider whether the initiative is viable.
- [ ] **Monthly deeper analysis** - Once a month, spend 30 minutes looking at the trends. Are your growth initiatives collectively delivering the results you planned? Is the cost of growth justified by the returns?

## Evolution

- [ ] **Retire metrics that stop being useful** - If a metric has been green for 12 consecutive weeks, it might not need weekly tracking anymore. Replace it with something that needs attention.
- [ ] **Add metrics as new initiatives launch** - Every new growth initiative should come with its own metrics from day one. If you cannot define how you will measure success, you are not ready to start.
- [ ] **Connect growth metrics to financial outcomes** - At least quarterly, trace your growth metrics back to revenue and profit impact. Activity metrics are only valuable if they connect to financial results.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A one-page growth dashboard with 5-8 metrics maximum
- Clear targets and red/amber/green status for each metric
- A weekly review habit that takes 15 minutes or less
- Early warning signals that allow you to adjust before problems become crises
- Evidence-based decisions about which growth initiatives to continue, expand, or stop

## Coach Notes

The most common mistake is tracking too many metrics. Clients want to measure everything because it feels thorough. Push them to choose the vital few. Ask: "If this metric changed significantly, would you do something different?" If the answer is no, drop it. The second mistake is building the dashboard but not reviewing it. Build the review habit first, even before the dashboard is complete. A quick verbal check of three numbers every Monday is more valuable than a beautiful dashboard that nobody opens. Watch for clients who focus exclusively on lagging indicators (revenue, profit, customer count). These are important but tell you what happened last month, not what will happen next month. Push for at least one leading indicator per growth initiative.
