## **What This Is**

Your business model is the machine that turns effort into profit. When it is broken, growth becomes exhausting — more hours, more stress, more chaos just to stay afloat. When it is right, revenue becomes predictable and growth becomes manageable.

Most owners never step back to look at how money flows through their business. They focus on getting customers and delivering work but ignore the structure underneath. This playbook helps you design a model that scales without scaling your effort. It is about working smarter, not harder.

This is not about complex business theory. It is about asking five simple questions: Where does money come from? How reliable is it? What breaks if your biggest client leaves? Can you grow without burning out? Where can you build predictability?

---

## **What It Looks Like**

### **🔴 When this is broken**

Every sale feels like starting from scratch. Revenue swings wildly month to month. You are completely dependent on a few big clients who could leave any time. Growth means working more hours, not making better decisions. You are trapped in a cycle of effort — stop working and the money stops.

### **🟠 When this is OK**

You have some revenue predictability but it is patchy. Some months are strong, others catch you off guard. You know you rely too heavily on certain clients or revenue streams but you are not sure how to change it. Growth is possible but it feels like pushing a boulder uphill — constant effort for modest gains.

### **🟢 When this is working**

You have multiple revenue streams that balance each other out. A significant portion is predictable — subscriptions, retainers, or repeat clients. You could lose your biggest client and survive comfortably. Growth comes from better systems and pricing, not just more hours. Your income is steady and your margins are healthy.

---

## **Things to Think About**

Which revenue stream actually pays the bills? Most businesses have multiple income sources but only one or two that matter. Identify your reliable core. Everything else is a bonus until it becomes consistent enough to count on.

What happens if your largest client disappears tomorrow? If losing one client would break you, your model is fragile. True business health means no single relationship controls your survival. Spread the risk or accept that you are running on borrowed time.

Does your pricing reward scale or punish it? Some pricing structures get better as you grow — higher volumes, lower costs, better margins. Others trap you in a linear exchange of time for money. The difference determines whether growth energises you or exhausts you.

Can your delivery expand without expanding you personally? Look at how work gets done. If every new client means more personal involvement from you, growth is capped by your capacity. Sustainable models let you serve more customers without working more hours.

Where can you build predictability into the chaos? One-off projects create feast-or-famine cycles. Subscriptions, retainers, maintenance contracts, memberships — any revenue that repeats creates stability. Even a small recurring base changes everything.

## **The Plays Inside This Playbook**

When you open this playbook in the Profit System, you will find:

Play 1: Revenue Stream Analysis — Map all your income sources and identify which ones are reliable, growing, or vulnerable. Know exactly where your money comes from so you can make informed decisions about where to focus.

Play 2: Client Dependency Assessment — Calculate how much of your revenue comes from your top clients and assess the risk. Create a plan to reduce dangerous dependencies before they become a crisis.

Play 3: Pricing Structure Review — Examine whether your pricing model supports growth or limits it. Identify opportunities to build recurring revenue, improve margins, or reward scale.

Play 4: Scalability Audit — Assess which parts of your delivery can expand without expanding your personal involvement. Find the bottlenecks that cap your growth and plan to remove them.

## **Quick Wins**

This week: List every revenue stream and calculate what percentage each represents. Circle any that make up more than 40% of your income — these are dependencies to watch.

This week: Identify one client relationship or service where you could introduce recurring revenue. A maintenance contract, ongoing support, or monthly retainer. Ask yourself: what do they need regularly that I could provide?

This week: Look at your three most profitable clients. What made those relationships profitable? Can you replicate that model with new clients or existing ones?
