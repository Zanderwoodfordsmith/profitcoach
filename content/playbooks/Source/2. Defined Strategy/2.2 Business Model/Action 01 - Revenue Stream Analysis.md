# Revenue Stream Analysis

## What to Do

Map every source of income in your business, calculate the percentage each represents, and assess which streams are reliable, growing, or vulnerable. This gives you a clear picture of where your money actually comes from so you can make informed decisions about where to focus, what to protect, and what to build.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners have a rough sense of their revenue mix but have never mapped it precisely. When you actually run the numbers, surprises appear. A stream you thought was 20% of revenue turns out to be 40%. A service you spend a lot of time on generates almost nothing. The analysis reveals where your business is strong, where it is fragile, and where the best growth opportunities sit.

## How to Use This

Pull your revenue data for the last 12 months (or as much as you have). Break it down by source and work through the framework below.

---

## Revenue Mapping

- [ ] List every distinct revenue stream in your business (products, services, retainers, project work, subscriptions, affiliate income, etc.)
- [ ] For each stream, calculate the total revenue for the last 12 months
- [ ] Calculate the percentage of total revenue each stream represents
- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Circle any stream that makes up more than 40% of your total revenue - this is a dependency risk that needs attention
- [ ] Rank the streams from largest to smallest

## Stream Assessment

For each revenue stream, answer:

- [ ] **Reliability:** Is this income predictable month to month, or does it fluctuate? Rate 1-5 (1 = completely unpredictable, 5 = highly recurring)
- [ ] **Growth trajectory:** Is this stream growing, flat, or declining over the last 6-12 months?
- [ ] **Effort required:** How much of your personal time does this stream demand per £1,000 of revenue? Rate 1-5 (1 = very time-intensive, 5 = mostly passive)
- [ ] **Margin:** What is the approximate profit margin on this stream? (High >50%, Medium 20-50%, Low <20%)
- [ ] **Vulnerability:** If your biggest customer or channel in this stream disappeared, what would happen?

## Revenue Quality Score

- [ ] Create a simple table with each stream and its scores across the five criteria above
- [ ] Highlight your strongest streams: high reliability, growing, low effort, high margin, low vulnerability
- [ ] Highlight your weakest streams: unreliable, declining, high effort, low margin, or highly vulnerable
- [ ] Identify the single stream with the most growth potential and the single stream that is the biggest risk

## Action Planning

- [ ] For your strongest stream: what would it take to grow this by 20-30% in the next 12 months?
- [ ] For your weakest stream: should you fix it, reduce it, or eliminate it?
- [ ] For any stream over 40% of revenue: what is your plan to reduce that dependency?
- [ ] Is there a revenue stream you should be building that does not exist yet? (Recurring revenue, productised service, digital product, etc.)
- [ ] Prioritise: which one change to your revenue mix would have the biggest impact on business stability and growth?

---

## Expected Outcome

- A complete map of every revenue stream with exact percentages
- Each stream assessed on reliability, growth, effort, margin, and vulnerability
- Clear identification of strengths to build on and risks to address
- A prioritised action plan for improving your revenue mix
- Data-driven decisions about where to focus instead of gut feel

## Coach Notes

This is one of the most eye-opening exercises for Level 2 owners. Many discover that their most profitable stream gets the least attention, or that a stream consuming 30% of their time generates only 10% of revenue. Use the results to challenge their time allocation (connects to 2.0 Time & Energy). The vulnerability question is particularly powerful for owners dependent on 1-2 big clients. If losing one client would cause a crisis, that is not a business - it is a job with extra risk. Push for concrete actions to diversify.
