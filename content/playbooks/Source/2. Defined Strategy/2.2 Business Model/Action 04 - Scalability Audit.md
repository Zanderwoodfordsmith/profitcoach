# Scalability Audit

## What to Do

Assess which parts of your business can grow without growing your personal involvement. Find the bottlenecks that cap your growth and create a plan to remove them. This is about answering one question honestly: if you doubled your revenue tomorrow, what would break?

## Why It Matters

Many SMEs hit an invisible ceiling because the owner is the bottleneck in every process. More clients means more of the owner's time, which means the owner works harder but the business does not actually scale. A scalability audit reveals where growth is capped by your personal capacity so you can redesign those areas before they become crises. The goal is not to remove yourself entirely - it is to ensure growth comes from better systems, not longer hours.

## How to Use This

Walk through each area of your business and honestly assess what happens if volume doubles. The bottlenecks will become obvious.

---

## The Doubling Test

For each area below, answer: "If we doubled our volume tomorrow, what would break?"

### Sales & Marketing

- [ ] Could you generate 2x the leads with your current marketing approach? If not, what is the constraint?
- [ ] Could you handle 2x the sales enquiries without you being involved in every conversation?
- [ ] Is your sales process dependent on your personal relationships, or could someone else follow a defined process?
- [ ] Rate scalability (1-5): 1 = completely dependent on you, 5 = could scale without you

### Delivery & Operations

- [ ] Could you deliver to 2x the clients with your current team and processes?
- [ ] Which parts of delivery require your personal involvement and which could someone else handle?
- [ ] Are your processes documented enough that new team members could follow them?
- [ ] Rate scalability (1-5)

### Finance & Admin

- [ ] Could your back-office systems handle 2x the invoices, expenses, and financial complexity?
- [ ] Is financial management automated or manual?
- [ ] Rate scalability (1-5)

### Team & Management

- [ ] Could your current team handle 2x the workload, or would you need to hire?
- [ ] If you need to hire, do you have the recruitment and onboarding systems to do it quickly? (See [[2.9 Recruitment]])
- [ ] Are there enough management layers, or does everything still report to you?
- [ ] Rate scalability (1-5)

## Bottleneck Identification

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win: Look at your ratings above. The area with the lowest score is your primary bottleneck - the thing that will break first as you grow. Name it.
- [ ] List the top 3 bottlenecks in order of severity
- [ ] For each bottleneck, identify the root cause: Is it a people gap (need to hire)? A process gap (need to document and systematise)? A technology gap (need tools)? A trust gap (need to learn to let go)?
- [ ] For each bottleneck, describe what "fixed" looks like: what would need to be true for this area to handle 2x volume?

## Removal Plan

- [ ] Prioritise: which bottleneck, if removed, would unlock the most growth?
- [ ] Define the first step to address it (not the whole solution, just the next action)
- [ ] Set a timeline: when will this bottleneck be addressed?
- [ ] Identify dependencies: does fixing this bottleneck require other changes first?
- [ ] Set a review date to re-run the doubling test after changes are made

---

## Expected Outcome

- An honest assessment of where your business cannot scale without you
- Your top 3 growth bottlenecks identified and ranked
- Root causes diagnosed for each bottleneck
- A prioritised removal plan with specific next steps
- A clear picture of what needs to change before your next growth phase

## Coach Notes

This is a strategic conversation, not a tactical one. Resist the urge to dive into solving each bottleneck in the session. The goal is the diagnosis. Most Level 2 owners discover that they ARE the bottleneck in 2-3 areas, which can be confronting. Frame it positively: "The good news is the bottleneck is something you control." The most common bottlenecks for £200K-£1M businesses: owner involved in every sale, no documented delivery processes, and no management layer between owner and team. Connect to Delegation (2.0 Action 8), Processes (1.8), and Team Foundations (1.9).
