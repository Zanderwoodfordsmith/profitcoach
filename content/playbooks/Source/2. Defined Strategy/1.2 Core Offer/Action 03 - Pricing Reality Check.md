# Pricing Reality Check

## What to Do

Price your core offer based on the value it delivers, not the cost to deliver it or what competitors charge. Then validate that price against real market feedback before committing to it.

## Why It Matters

Most business owners price by looking at competitors and going slightly lower, or by calculating their costs and adding a margin. Both approaches cap your income and position you as a commodity. Value-based pricing starts from a different question: "What is the result worth to my client?" If you help a business owner find £50K in unrealised revenue, charging £5K for that is a bargain — regardless of how many hours it took you.

Hormozi's Value Equation applies directly here: the more clearly you can define the dream outcome, prove it works, deliver it fast, and make it easy — the more you can charge.

## How to Use This

Work through the calculator, then validate with real prospects. Do not skip the validation — your price needs to survive contact with actual buyers.

---

## The Value-Based Price Calculator

- [ ] **What is the measurable result your client gets?** — Revenue gained, costs saved, time freed, risk reduced. Put a number on it. If you helped your last 3 clients, what was the average financial impact? Even a rough estimate works.
- [ ] **What multiple feels fair?** — A common rule of thumb: charge 10-20% of the value you create. If your work generates £50K in value, £5K-£10K is the range. If it generates £200K, you can charge £20K-£40K. Where does your offer land?
- [ ] **What are you currently charging?** — Write down your actual price. Now compare it to the value-based calculation above. Most business owners discover they are charging 50-80% less than the value would justify.
- [ ] **What would you need to charge to build the business you want?** — Calculate backwards: desired annual income ÷ number of clients you can serve per year = minimum price per client. If this number is higher than your current price, you have a pricing problem.

## The Competitor Sanity Check

- [ ] **List your 3 closest competitors and their pricing** — Not to copy them, but to understand the market's reference points. Your price needs to make sense relative to alternatives.
- [ ] **Identify what you offer that they do not** — This is what justifies a premium. Speed? Depth? Hands-on delivery? Guarantee? Better results? If you cannot name the difference, you cannot charge more.
- [ ] **Position your price as an investment, not a cost** — "It costs £5K" versus "For £5K, you get a system that generates £50K in additional revenue." Same number. Completely different conversation.

## The Validation Test

- [ ] ⚡ Quick Win · **Present your new price to your next 3 prospects** — Do not discount. Do not apologise. State the price with confidence and observe the reaction. If all 3 say yes immediately, you are still too cheap.
- [ ] **Track the responses** — Immediate yes (too cheap), yes after thinking (about right), or no with price as the reason (either too expensive or value not communicated clearly). You want a mix — some easy yeses and some pushback means you are in the right zone.
- [ ] **Adjust based on feedback, not fear** — If 2 out of 3 say yes, your price is working. If all 3 say no, check your value communication before dropping the price — the problem is usually how you explain the value, not the number itself.

---

## Expected Outcome

- A price based on value delivered, not hours worked or competitor pricing
- Confidence in stating your price because you can explain the ROI
- Real market validation from actual prospect conversations
- Clarity on whether your pricing problem is the number or the communication
- A price that supports the business you want to build, not just the business you happen to have

## Coach Notes

Pricing is emotional territory. Most clients have deep-rooted beliefs about what they are "worth" that have nothing to do with business economics. The value calculator helps make it logical rather than emotional.

If a client cannot articulate the measurable result they create, go back to Value Definition before pricing. You cannot price what you cannot quantify.

Watch for the client who drops their price at the first sign of resistance. That is a confidence problem, not a pricing problem. Role-play the pricing conversation in the session — let them practice stating their price without flinching.

The "test it on your next 3 prospects" step is critical. Theory means nothing without market contact. Hold them accountable to actually doing it and reporting back.
