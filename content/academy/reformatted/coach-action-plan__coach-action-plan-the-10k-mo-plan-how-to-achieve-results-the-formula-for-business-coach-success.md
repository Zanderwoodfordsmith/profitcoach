## Understanding Volume and Conversion

Let's break these two concepts down into their core definitions and then build a strategy from there.

### **Understanding Volume**

Volume is about how many people you reach out to with your coaching offer. This isn't about selling right away; it's about seeing if they're interested in solving a pain/problem they have. The more people you reach out to, the better your chances of finding potential clients. Volume has two key parts:

1. **Outreach Rate:** This is how many people you contact in a given time period, like daily or weekly. It's important to be aware of the outreach limits on platforms you are using. For example, on LinkedIn you can only send 100-200 connection requests per week. You can't suddenly send 5,000 requests, so your outreach should align with these capacity limits — and this is why the next concept becomes important.
2. **Consistency:** This means how regularly you keep up your outreach efforts. For example, if you reach out to potential clients every day, you're being consistent. So you want to be consistently maximising the outreach rate to the capacity available — i.e. sending 100-200 connection requests week after week (and other methods to increase capacity).

Together, these create your overall Volume:

## Outreach Rate x Consistency = Volume

By focusing on both how many people you reach out to and how often you do it, you can increase your chances of success in finding new clients. See the image below as a representation of the difference.

### **Understanding Conversion**

Conversion is the rate at which your outreach efforts turn into paying clients. For example, if you send out 1,000 connection requests on LinkedIn and gain one client, your conversion rate is 0.1%. This is a good target to aim for. Another coach might need to send 10,000 requests to get one client, resulting in a 0.01% conversion rate. Our goal is to achieve the highest conversion rate possible.

**Different Levels of Conversion:**

1. **Macro Conversion:** This is the big goal — turning a prospect into a paying client. It's the ultimate success metric for your outreach efforts.
2. **Milestone Conversions:** These are the key steps along the way to a macro conversion. For example, moving an interested lead to a booked call, then to a discovery call, and finally to a sales call. Each milestone is important for guiding prospects through the sales journey.
3. **Micro Conversions:** These are the small actions that lead to milestone conversions. For instance, for a prospect to become interested, they need to see and accept your connection request, then reply to express interest. For them to book a call, they need to read your message, click a link, and schedule the call.

Understanding these levels helps you see where you can improve each step of the process to boost your overall conversion rate.

#### **What Influences Conversion?**

Conversion is influenced by two main factors: **Pain** and **Confidence**. These are the driving forces that motivate a potential client to take action.

1. **Pain:** This represents the problem or need your potential client is experiencing. The greater the discomfort or urgency they feel, the more likely they are to seek a solution.
2. **Confidence:** This is the trust and belief that your potential client has in your ability to solve their problem. The more confidence they have in you and your offer, the more likely they are to take action.

You can think of it like this:

> (Pain Level + Confidence Level) / 2 = Conversion (Likelihood of Action)

If a person's pain and confidence levels are high enough to cross their action threshold, they're more likely to become your client. By increasing the perceived pain of staying the same and boosting confidence in your solution, you enhance the chance of conversion.

## Relating Volume and Conversion

Explore how volume and conversion rates work together to achieve your client goals. This section will illustrate how different approaches can lead to the same result, using real-life examples.

**Case Study Comparisons:**

* **Ashley Maile's Approach:** With a deep understanding of his industry and strong credibility, Ashley achieved a high conversion rate. By speaking to only 6-10 prospects, he secured 6 clients, demonstrating how expertise and trust can minimise the need for high outreach volume.
* **John McCarthy's Strategy:** In a more sceptical industry, John needed to build credibility from the ground up. He sent out 6,000 outreaches to achieve a 0.1% conversion rate, ultimately securing 6 clients. This highlights how increasing volume can compensate for lower conversion rates.
