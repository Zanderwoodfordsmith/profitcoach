### **What Is This?**

This lesson will teach you how to set the right pricing strategies for your coaching packages, ensuring that you get the best value for your services while meeting the needs of your clients.

### **Why Is This Important?**

* **Optimise Revenue:** Proper pricing can maximise your revenue and profitability.
* **Client Commitment:** Different pricing strategies can affect client commitment and retention.
* **Cash Flow Management:** Upfront payments improve cash flow management by reducing financial uncertainty.
* **Value Perception:** Clients often associate higher prices with higher value, which can enhance your brand reputation.
* **Flexibility:** Offering various payment plans can accommodate different financial situations, making your services more accessible.

## How To Do It

### **Step 1: Understand Your Value**

Before setting any prices, understand the value you provide to your clients. Think about:

* The transformation and outcomes your clients can expect.
* Your qualifications and experience.
* Unique methodologies or tools you offer.

**Nothing Is Set In Stone.** Some coaches, with their experience and industry, have immediately started charging more than £5,000/mo. Others have combined their coaching with consulting work for the first 3 months and then rolled them into pure coaching after that, charging £15,000/mo for those first 3 months.

### **Step 2: Choose Your Pricing Models**

There are really two primary ways to position your prices. You are selling the same thing — it is just how you package it.

#### **Model 1: Monthly Packages**

**Example: Starting at £1,450/mo**

1. **Starter Price:** Begin by setting your monthly rate at £1,450 (higher if you wish, but we found this is a good starting point).
2. **Price Increases:** Plan to increase your price by £500 every 2-4 clients you bring on, so £1,950/mo, £2,450/mo etc.

**Client Justification:** Understand that with this model, you'll need to justify the value delivered every month to retain clients.

#### **Model 2: 6-Month Packages**

We think 6 months is a good length — it's not too much cash upfront but is enough time for people to see transformation. You can always try for 12 months, but that's usually with clients you have worked with and built trust. If you have lots of authority (which is what we show people how to build in our top programme) these longer engagements can happen sooner.

**Example: Starting at £7,800 or £9,600 for 6 months**

1. **Upfront Payment Benefits:**
   * **Higher Cash Flow:** Get more cash upfront, improving financial stability.
   * **Reduced Monthly Justification:** You won't need to justify your value each month; instead, focus on the overall transformation over 6 months.
   * **Value Perception:** Clients are likely to see a higher upfront cost as indicative of high value.
2. **Payment Plans:**
   * **Full Payment:** Offer the full 6-month package at £7,800 or £9,600.
   * **2, 3 or 4 pay options:** We have the payment plans mapped out for you in the sales training.
   * **Monthly Payment Plan:** Equivalent to the monthly fee, providing flexibility and a consistent revenue stream.

**Which Is Better?** Honestly, the most appropriate answer is... it depends.

We don't have enough data to definitively say, and I suspect it would vary massively by coach, client, industry, business stage, and available cash. It also depends on personal preference and who you ask:

* **Pam** prefers monthly because she is very confident in the value she adds and is excellent at retaining clients. She believes you just get them signed up at the start and they'll stay for years, and they don't need to keep thinking about it.
* **Zander** prefers upfront because he is used to scaling through ads, so cash upfront is important, and he likes that you can just staircase down with payment plans and end up at the monthly option anyway with the 6 pay. But he has also been used to selling online programmes that have a more defined end, whereas Pam keeps clients 4, 5, 6 years (her longest was over 9).
* **Alex Hormozi** prefers as long as possible upfront because of what he calls "The Look Back Window": "**Customers determine whether a purchase was good based on the last purchase they made.**" So if you do great the first month and terrible the second month, they think about cancelling — versus billing upfront, where they look at the overall improvement during that time. ### **Step 3: Assess and Adjust**

Based on performance and feedback, be ready to adjust your pricing strategy. Incremental price increases every 2-4 clients can help you stay competitive while growing your revenue.

### **Step 4: Communicate Value**

Pricing is always relative to value.

* If you offer massive value in excess of your pricing, then **you are leaving money on the table**.
* If the value you deliver is only slightly more than the price, then **you will have a hard time making sales**.

The numbers we have shared in this lesson are what works well for most coaches and business owners, but always take into consideration your industry, market, experience etc. We will show you exactly how to position your price in the sales training, and we have some responses further down in this lesson if people ask you early in the process.

## What To Say If Someone Asks You The Price

**Before the Sales Call & Demonstrated Value**

If someone asks you early on in the marketing process, then **you don't want to avoid the question**. Otherwise it makes them feel like you are hiding something. However, you also don't want to give a fixed amount before demonstrating value, so we recommend saying this:

> "Thanks for asking — our top coaching clients invest up to £50,000 a year, but you can start for under £1,000/mo. It depends on your situation and what makes sense. I could do a call on Monday or Wednesday to look at what makes sense for you. Which day works best?"

The reason we do it like this is:

1. You don't want to be evasive and avoid the question.
2. We want to anchor a very high price in their mind at the start, so what we say next seems less.
3. We then want to alleviate concerns that they can't get started. Even if your minimum fee is £2,000/mo, I'd say the £1,000 and, at the end of the call, if they remember what you said, just say that was for once-per-month coaching.
4. You want to take the lead and assume it makes sense to have the call, then just ask which day works best — they are more likely to just pick one.

**What You Say At The End of The Sales Call**

> For the BOSS Coaching that gets you to X/year revenue... (then choose either / or)
>
> 1. It's an upfront investment for the first 6 months of £9,600.
> 2. It's a monthly investment of £1,450 per month.
>
> And for that you get... (then we list out what they get)
