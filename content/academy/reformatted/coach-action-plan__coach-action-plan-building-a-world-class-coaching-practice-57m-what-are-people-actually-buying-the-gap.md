### **What Is This?**

This section explains what business owners are truly purchasing when they hire a coach. It's not just about the coaching itself; it's about bridging the gap between their current and ideal business.

### **Why Is This Important?**

Understanding what clients are actually buying will help you:

* Communicate your value more effectively.
* Tailor your services to meet their real needs.
* Close sales more efficiently by addressing their pain points.
* Create long-term client relationships by focusing on their ultimate goals.

By truly understanding this concept it answers 1,000 other decisions you'll make when growing your coaching business.

## Understanding What Clients Are Really Buying

The 1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣ of why clients buy.

### **One Concept: The Gap**

Clients aren't buying coaching services; they're buying a solution to bridge the gap between their current business and their ideal business. Your role is to provide the knowledge, accountability, and outside perspective needed to close this gap.

**What Does Bridging the Gap Mean in Practice?** Below are examples of the improvements business owners may want, broken down by the original 6 pillars of the PROFIT System.

**They Aren't Buying All Of Them... Yet.** When you market to a client they may only have 1, maybe 2, of these in mind, but over time this is what you will improve in the business.

#### **Profit & Cash**

* **Improved profitability:** Helping them increase their profit margins and overall revenue.
* **Cost reduction:** Identifying opportunities to reduce expenses, such as negotiating better deals with suppliers.
* **Better financial control:** Providing support in budgeting, financial forecasting, and managing cash flow.

#### **Revenue & Marketing**

* **Quality leads:** Generating higher-quality leads to fuel sustainable business growth.
* **Enhanced business strategy:** Crafting strategies that align with their long-term vision and goals.
* **Market penetration:** Assisting in expanding their presence within existing markets or entering new markets effectively.

#### **Ops & Product**

* **Customer retention:** Developing strategies to improve customer satisfaction and loyalty.
* **Operational efficiency:** Identifying and eliminating bottlenecks to ensure smoother day-to-day operations.
* **Improved supply chain:** Optimising supply chain management to reduce costs and improve delivery times.
* **Enhanced productivity:** Training team members on best practices and processes.

#### **Financials & KPIs**

* **Clear KPIs:** Setting clear financial and non-financial KPIs to measure the success of the business.
* **Data-driven decisions:** Using data and analytics to make informed decisions about the business direction.
* **Regular monitoring:** Setting up systems to monitor and report on KPIs regularly.
* **Risk management:** Implementing measures to mitigate business risks.

#### **Innovation & Management**

* **Better team performance:** Assisting in building and managing a more effective and cohesive team.
* **Culture of innovation:** Fostering a culture of continuous learning and improvement.
* **Professional development:** Providing opportunities for professional growth and training.
* **Supportive environment:** Creating a supportive and collaborative work environment.

#### **Team & Leadership**

* **High-performing team:** Investing in employee well-being and engagement.
* **Team building:** Providing opportunities for team building and collaboration.
* **Positive work environment:** Fostering a positive and inclusive work culture.
* **Open communication:** Communicating openly and transparently with team members.

By addressing these aspects, you help clients move closer to their ideal business, making your coaching services invaluable.

### **Two Dimensions of Improvement**

The world's economy runs on people closing the gap. The 4 main categories are health, wealth, relationships and happiness. However, when it comes to being a business coach it's best to think about it in 2 dimensions of closing the gap:

1. **Business Performance:**
   * This includes metrics like profit, revenue, lead generation, system efficiencies, and team performance.
   * Clients want tangible improvements in these areas.
2. **Individual Well-Being / Personal Performance:**
   * Clients also seek to improve their own skills, mindset, and productivity & lifestyle — e.g. if you get them home for dinner with their kids (and that's important to them) that is huge value.
   * A better-performing business often depends on the personal growth of the business owner. Sometimes it has been affecting their health.

Different coaches will gravitate towards a different focus (and it can even vary on a client-by-client basis). For example:

* **Business Focus** — Our Global Coach of the Year 2020-2022 Ashley Maile is very focused on driving the business and even says he isn't a great coach but an excellent mentor. He drives the business performance and wants clients who aren't interested in work-life balance at this stage.
* **Individual Focus** — Whereas John McCarthy, 2023 Global Coach of the Year, even though he still helps clients build incredible businesses, most of his transformations talk about the health of his clients, them cutting down work hours and other aspects of getting their lifestyle back.

**There is no right or wrong. You just want to understand how you add value.**

### **Three Key Reasons Clients Will Buy Your Coaching**

But why don't people just close this gap on their own? Many reasons, but it usually comes down to:

1. **Lack of ability:** They don't know how to reach their goals on their own.
2. **Speed of achievement:** They believe they can achieve their goals faster with your help.
3. **Ease and stress reduction:** They feel it will be less stressful and easier with a coach guiding them.

It can either be one reason or multiple.

### **Personal Trainer Analogy**

Think of it like hiring a personal trainer. People looking to lose weight aren't interested in hiring a personal trainer; they're interested in bridging the gap between their current body and their ideal body. Here's how it plays out with the 1-2-3 framework:

**1. The Gap — Current Body vs. Ideal Body:** Just like business owners want to transform their current business into their ideal business, individuals want to transform their current physique into their ideal body.

**2. The Two Dimensions of Improvement:**

1. **Nutrition:**
   * Knowing what to eat and when to eat it.
   * Understanding macronutrient ratios and caloric intake.
2. **Exercise:**
   * Knowing which exercises to perform for maximum efficiency.
   * Understanding the correct form and technique.

**3. The Three Key Reasons People Hire Personal Trainers:**

1. **Lack of knowledge:** They don't know the right workout routines or what to eat. Similar to business owners who don't know how to improve their business on their own.
2. **Speed of achievement:** Correcting exercises and optimising workout routines helps them achieve results faster. Just like a coach helps business owners reach their goals more quickly.
3. **Ease and stress reduction:** Having someone to keep them accountable makes it easier to stick to their gym routines. Similarly, business owners find it easier to implement strategies and maintain focus with a coach's guidance — as long as the coach is challenging them and not allowing them to get pulled into the day-to-day putting out fires.
