### **What Is This?**

This guide explains how to structure your coaching offer effectively. It emphasises selling outcomes rather than hours and suggests strategies to make your services more appealing and easier to manage.

### **Why Is This Important?**

Structuring your offer correctly will help you:

* Give you clarity on what you are offering.
* Communicate value beyond just time spent.
* Make it difficult for clients to calculate an hourly rate.
* Focus on delivering measurable outcomes.
* Manage your time efficiently by stacking sessions.

---

## How To Structure Your Coaching Offer

### **Focus on Outcomes, Not Hours**

**Avoid Hourly Rates:** Selling coaching by the hour commoditises your time and limits your earning potential. Instead, focus on the outcomes you help clients achieve.

**Package-Based Approach:** We recommend positioning coaching for the long-term that emphasises the results clients will get, and they pay in 12, 6, 3 month packages in advance or ongoing monthly coaching. Always focused on bridging the gap talked about previously. We set up the coaching structure in a way that makes it harder for them to compare your services based on hourly rates.

---

### **Best Frequency for Coaching**

Should you be doing it weekly, monthly, quarterly?

* **Weekly sessions** can be too intense, giving clients little time to implement changes.
* **Monthly sessions** are too far apart, offering only 12 opportunities per year to course-correct.
* **2 x per month** strikes the perfect balance, providing ample time for action and reflection. However, during the first 4-6 weeks, weekly sessions are ideal to achieve quick wins and build momentum.

### **Ideal Session Structure**

**2 x 90-Minute Sessions Per Month:** This frequency makes it harder for clients to calculate an hourly rate and keeps them engaged without overwhelming them.

You do not have to do it this way. For example, Ashley Maile does 1 x half day per month with a short call in between. He found that worked better for his engineering clients, so he just combined the two slots together.

---

### **Stack Clients for Efficiency**

**Blocking similar tasks together** is a time management strategy that enhances productivity. By stacking all clients on the 1st and 3rd Tue or Wed of each month, you manage your time effectively and create a predictable routine. This approach leaves other days free for marketing activities or enjoying your lifestyle; it even allows for taking two-week holidays without affecting client sessions.

If you're still in a job, you can start by stacking sessions on Saturday mornings when your calendar allows.

While not essential, this practice is incredibly useful for maintaining a balanced and efficient schedule.

### **The Approach To Working With Clients**

> "As long as you are in business we are going to be working together."

At the start of the marketing and sales process you will focus on alleviating immediate problems and the pain those problems are creating, but during the sales call and in the first sessions you will always paint the long-term picture of working together. This helps retention.

### **Quick Wins for Early Engagement**

**First 4-6 Weeks:** Work with clients **weekly** to achieve some quick wins. This initial period is critical for building momentum and demonstrating the value of your coaching. It helps clients implement immediate changes and see early results, keeping them motivated and committed to the programme.

### **Ongoing Coaching**

Transition into ongoing coaching with a balance of long-term plan and short-term execution on 2 x per month sessions.

Some coaches like to sell a 6-month package upfront. This timeline is long enough to achieve significant results but short enough to maintain client engagement. At the end of 6 months, the coach sells another 6-month package or switches to ongoing monthly payments. Some coaches prefer just ongoing monthly from the start.

---

### **Summary**

By structuring your coaching offer around outcomes rather than hours, you create a more compelling and valuable proposition for your clients. Using a package-based approach with a focus on achieving specific results within a set period can help you maximise your revenue and manage your time more effectively.

Start the coaching weekly for the first 4-6 weeks to gain quick wins and build momentum, then transition into 2 x 90 per month coaching.
