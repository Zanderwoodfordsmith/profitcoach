# Foreword to Day Zero: Going PRO (19m)

#### **📚 Summary:**

 

This lesson introduces the concept of Day Zero and Going Pro, explaining the reasons behind the term and its significance in setting the foundation for success in business. The speaker emphasizes the need to address vulnerabilities and eliminate distractions before building a coaching business. The lesson also highlights the importance of maintaining a day one mentality and continuously striving for results. Learners are encouraged to focus on personal mastery and efficiency to increase their output and achieve their goals.

### **Going Pro Core Principles**

Notice that all these “productivity” hacks are about eliminating not adding. Whenever someone tells you “do these things that you’re not doing to have more time.” Be skeptical. Best way to make more time is to stop doing shit that doesn’t matter.

**Get More Focused Time**

Prioritise 4 hours a day to work uninterrupted. Early morning and late night work well since other people are usually clocked out and won’t bother you.

Have a block of your day that NEVER gets interrupted.Example: I don’t talk to people/take meetings until noon.That gives me 4-7hrs uninterrupted per day. Works for me.

Treat weekdays and weekends the same. Go to bed at reasonable hour. Wake up at reasonable hour. Work if you want to, it's not illegal\! We just got 52-104 more days of productivity per year.

 

**Focus On What Matters**

Outsource personal activities/chores.This gives you time BACK. Cooking. Cleaning. Picking stuff up. Errands. Etc.Human-ing. You are a high-value business coach charging $500+ an hour...ACT LIKE IT\!

Audit or remove meetings ruthlessly. See what stuff you can stop attending, attend less frequently, get an email update instead, or send someone in your place.

Look at your overwhelming to do list and put names next to people who can handle the items. Not all, but most, can be delegated. Then give a deadline for them. Then set calendar reminder for you to check on what you just delegated.

Stop replying to people who only ask for shit from you. Eventually, they’ll just stop.

Don’t check email until AFTER you’ve done what you need to get done.

**Environment**

Get a workspace without distractions. Notifications are the enemy. Remove all, EVERYWHERE...Your phone, your desktop, external distraction, internal distractions (deal with you limiting patterns)

Make sure you get your partner onboard by helping them understand what you are trying to achieve. You don't want a spouse to make you feel guilty for working. Cheerleaders don’t ask the quarterback to leave the game when the game’s on the line.

 

**Consumption**

Cut or Cycle stimulants. Don’t let them use you.If you can’t function without them (E.g. coffee), they’ve stopped conferring a benefit.Be able to stop them and only use them when you really know you want that extra push. On off on off is typically good enough for most, or eventually you won't need them at all.

Limit alcohol consumption. It affects sleep and mood over the next few days.It’s just a net negative from a money perspective. Not saying it’s not fun. Just saying it’s not productive. And productive is the goal.

1. Day Zero represents a fresh start and a temporal landmark for making positive changes.  
2. Day Zero in software refers to identifying vulnerabilities that can hinder business growth.

4. Personal mastery and efficiency are key to building a successful coaching business.

#### **✅ Actions & Application:**

 

1. Establish a Day Zero mindset by setting clear goals and committing to personal growth.  
2. Identify and address vulnerabilities in your business or personal life that may hinder success.  
3. Prioritize deep work and minimize distractions to increase productivity.

# Selecting the Right Business Structure (2m)

### **What Is This?**

This lesson will guide you through the process of choosing the most suitable business structure for your coaching practice, focusing on Sole Trader and Limited Liability Company (LLC or Ltd) options.

### **Why Is This Important?**

Choosing the right business structure will help you:

* **Protect against liabilities:** Understand your personal risk.  
* **Optimize taxation:** Ensure you are taxed appropriately.  
* **Manage administrative overhead:** Understand the complexity of running your chosen structure.

Legally we cannot advise on business structures, you need to speak with your accountants. However, the majority of our coaches trade as a limited company in the UK or LLC in the USA.

### **Business Structure Comparison**

| Aspect | Sole Trader | Limited Liability Company (LLC or Ltd) |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Definition** | A sole trader is an individual running a business on their own. | A separate legal entity from its owners. |
| **Liability** | Personal liability for all business debts and obligations. | Personal assets are protected from business liabilities. |
| **Taxation** | Simplified tax process; profit is taxed as personal income. | Separate tax entity; potential tax benefits and complexities. |
| **Administrative Overhead** | Easier and less costly to set up and run. | More complicated and costly to set up and maintain. |

### **Steps to Choose Your Business Structure**

1. **Evaluate Your Needs:**  
   * Assess your need for liability protection.  
   * Consider tax implications and benefits.  
   * Think about the administrative burden you can handle.  
2. **Utilize Government and Professional Resources:**  
   * Visit government websites for definitions and implications of each structure.  
   * Use professional advisory sites to get a comprehensive understanding.

---

### **Consult Recommended Professionals**

**UK:**

* **Accountants:** Seek advice on whether to choose Sole Trader or Ltd.  
* **Resources:** HMRC website, professional advisory organizations.

**USA**

* **Tax Advisors:** Get insights into LLC structuring and implications at state and federal level.  
* **Resources:** IRS website, Small Business Administration (SBA).

### **Practical Tips**

* **Document Your Findings:** Keep notes on pros and cons relevant to your situation.  
* **Ask Specific Questions:** In consultations, ask about specific scenarios or concerns.  
* **Plan for the Future:** Consider how your business might grow and how that affects your choice.

### **Summary**

Choosing the correct business structure is not a big decision and can be changed later if needed. DO NOT let it hold up you making progress through the Academy. By researching your options and consulting with professionals, you can make an informed decision that protects you from liabilities, optimises your taxes, and balances administrative demands.

This sets you up for long-term success and enables you to focus on what you do best: coaching your clients to achieve their goals.

Securing the right insurance will help you:

* Protect against potential claims and liabilities.  
* Ensure your business is compliant with legal requirements.  
* Provide peace of mind for you and your clients.

**Legally we cannot recommend what insurance is right for your business.**

### **Understanding Business Insurance**

#### **Essential Insurance Policies**

| Type of Insurance | Definition | Why It's Important |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Professional Indemnity Insurance** | Covers claims against your business for professional negligence or mistakes. | Protects against financial losses due to claims of inadequate service or advice. |
| **Public Liability Insurance** | Covers claims made by members of the public for accidents or injuries that occur on your business premises. | Protects against legal and compensation costs if someone is injured or their property is damaged. |
| **Employers’ Liability Insurance** | Required if you have employees; covers claims from employees who are injured or become ill while working for you. | Protects against compensation costs and legal fees from employee claims. |

### **Steps to Secure Business Insurance**

1. **Research Insurance Needs:**  
   * **Professional Indemnity:** Essential for any business offering professional advice or services.  
   * **Public Liability:** Important if you interact with the public or clients in person.  
   * **Employers’ Liability:** Mandatory if you employ staff.  
2. **Consult with Insurance Brokers:**  
   * **BCA Recommendations:** Consult with the Business Coach Academy team for the latest recommendations on trusted insurance brokers.  
   * **Get Quotes:** Obtain multiple quotes to compare coverage options and costs.  
3. **Understand Policy Details:**  
   * Review the terms, conditions, and exclusions of each policy.  
   * Ensure the coverage limits are adequate for your business needs.  
4. **Regularly Review Your Coverage:**  
   * As your business grows, periodically review and update your insurance policies to ensure continued adequate protection.

### **Summary**

Securing the right business insurance is crucial for protecting your coaching practice from potential claims and liabilities. By understanding the different types of insurance available and consulting with professionals, you can ensure that your business is well-protected and compliant with legal requirements.

# Understand Tax Implications (2m)

### **What Is This?**

This lesson covers the key tax obligations and considerations for your chosen business structure, focusing on both the UK and USA.

### **Why Is This Important?**

Understanding tax implications will help you:

* Stay compliant with tax laws.  
* Optimise your tax strategy.  
* Avoid unexpected tax liabilities.  
* Make informed decisions about your business finances.

We legally cannot give tax advice but we can recommend people if you wish.

### **Key Tax Obligations**

#### **🇬🇧 UK Tax Obligations**

| Tax Type | Description | Why It's Important |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Value Added Tax (VAT)** | A tax on the value added to goods and services, applicable if your turnover exceeds the VAT threshold (at time of writing 90K in 12 month period) | Ensures compliance with tax laws and avoids penalties for underpayment. |
| **Corporation Tax** | A tax on the profits of a limited company. | Required for all limited companies to remain compliant with UK tax laws. |
| **Salary vs. Dividends** | Distinction between drawing a salary and taking dividends from your company’s profits. | Different tax treatments can impact your overall tax liability and financial planning. |

#### **🇺🇸 USA Tax Obligations**

| Tax Type | Description | Why It's Important |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Federal and State Taxes** | Income taxes applicable at both federal and state levels. | Ensures you meet all your tax obligations and avoid penalties. |
| **Self-Employment Tax** | Taxes for self-employed individuals to cover Social Security and Medicare contributions. | Necessary for compliance and securing future Social Security and Medicare benefits. |
| **S Corporation Benefits** | Potential tax benefits available if you elect to be taxed as an S Corporation. | Can minimize your overall tax liability through salary and dividend distributions. |

### **Steps to Understand and Manage Tax Implications**

1. **Research Tax Obligations:**  
   * **UK:** Understand VAT, Corporation Tax, and the distinctions between salary and dividends.  
   * **USA:** Learn about federal and state tax obligations, self-employment tax, and the benefits of S Corporation status.  
2. **Consult with Professionals:**  
   * **Schedule Consultations:** Book meetings with accountants or tax advisors to clarify tax strategies and obligations specific to your business.  
   * **Tailored Advice:** Get advice tailored to your specific business structure and financial situation.  
3. **Implement Tax Strategies:**  
   * **Optimize Tax Payments:** Use the information and advice from professionals to minimize your tax liabilities.  
   * **Stay Organized:** Keep accurate records and documentation to ensure smooth tax filing and audits.  
4. **Regular Reviews:**  
   * **Update Strategies:** Periodically review and update your tax strategies as your business grows or tax laws change.  
   * **Ongoing Consultations:** Maintain regular communication with your tax advisor to stay informed about any new tax regulations or opportunities.

### **Summary**

Understanding the tax implications of your business structure is crucial for staying compliant and optimizing your tax strategy. By researching tax obligations, consulting with professionals, and implementing effective tax strategies, you can ensure your business remains financially healthy and compliant with all tax laws.
